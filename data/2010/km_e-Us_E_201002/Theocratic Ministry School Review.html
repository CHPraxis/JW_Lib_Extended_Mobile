Theocratic Ministry School Review

The following questions will be considered at the Theocratic Ministry School during the week beginning February 22, 2010. The school overseer will conduct a 20-minute review based on material covered in assignments for the weeks of January 4 through February 22, 2010.

1. What factors may have caused some Israelites to delay in taking possession of the Promised Land, and what lesson can we learn from this? (Josh. 18:2, 3) [it-1 p. 359 par. 5]

2. What undoubtedly moved Joshua to say what he did at Joshua 24:14, 15, and how should this affect us? [w08 5/15 pp. 17-18 pars. 4-6]

3. What is one way that Baal worshippers and their form of worship became snares and lures to the Israelites? (Judg. 2:3) [w08 2/15 p. 27 pars. 2-3]

4. What lesson can we learn from Ehud’s courageous use of the sword? (Judg. 3:16, 21) [w97 3/15 p. 31 par. 4]

5. What encouragement do we receive when we study the manner in which Jehovah delivered Gideon and his 300 men? (Judg. 7:19-22) [w05 7/15 p. 16 par. 8]

6. In what way did Jehovah’s soul become “impatient because of the trouble of Israel”? (Judg. 10:16) [cl pp. 254-255 pars. 10-11]

7. Did Jephthah have in mind making a human sacrifice when he made his vow? (Judg. 11:30, 31) [w05 1/15 p. 26 par. 1]

8. Was Samson’s power literally in his hair? (Judg. 16:18-20) [w05 3/15 p. 28 pars. 5-6]

9. How can we be helped by understanding Samson’s amazing deed described at Judges 16:3? [w04 10/15 pp. 15-16 pars. 7-8]

10. Concerning the human soul, what can be learned from the statement found at Judges 16:30? [w90 9/1 p. 5 par. 5; sp pp. 13-14]
