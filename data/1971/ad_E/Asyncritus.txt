ASYNCRITUS
(A·synʹcri·tus) [incomparable, unlike].
A Christian in Rome to whom the apostle Paul sent a greeting in his inspired letter to the Romans written from Corinth about 56 C.E. (Rom. 16:14) Further information on Asyncritus is not provided in the Scriptures; however, archaeological evidence in inscriptions and papyri indicates that the name Asyncritus was used to some extent at that time.
