Theocratic Ministry School Review
The following questions will be considered orally at the Theocratic Ministry School during the week beginning February 23, 2004. The school overseer will conduct a 30–minute review based on material covered in assignments for the weeks of January 5 through February 23, 2004. [Note: Where there are no references after the question, you will need to do your own research to find the answers.​—See Ministry School, pp. 36-7.]
SPEECH QUALITIES
 1. What can we do to ensure that the practical value of the material being presented stands out to our audience and truly benefits them? [be p. 158 pars. 2-4]
 2. Why should we choose our words carefully? [be p. 160 par. 1 and second box]
 3. What fundamental requirement of good speech is highlighted at 1 Corinthians 14:9, and how can we apply this principle in our teaching? [be p. 161 pars. 1-4]
 4. As seen at Matthew 5:3-12 and Mark 10:17-21, what is one outstanding characteristic of Jesus’ teaching that we can imitate? [be p. 162 par. 4]
 5. In our ministry or when commenting at congregation meetings, why should we be interested in using words that convey vigor, feeling, and color? (Matt. 23:37, 38) [be p. 163 par. 3–p. 164 par. 1]

 8. Among all the principles contained in the Bible, which are the weightier ones? [w02 2/15 p. 5 pars. 1, 4, 6]

10. How does Proverbs 11:11 apply to congregations of Jehovah’s people? [w02 5/15 p. 27 pars. 1-3]
