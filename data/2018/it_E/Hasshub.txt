HASSHUB
(Hasʹshub) [One Who Is Accounting (Considering)].
1. Son of Pahath-moab; one of those who did repair work when the wall of Jerusalem was being rebuilt under Nehemiah’s direction.​—Ne 3:11.
2. One who repaired a section of the wall of Jerusalem, evidently a section in front of his house.​—Ne 3:23.
3. One of the heads of the people whose descendant, if not he himself, attested by seal the “trustworthy arrangement” contracted during Nehemiah’s governorship.​—Ne 9:38; 10:1, 14, 23.
4. A Merarite Levite; son of Azrikam and father of Shemaiah.​—1Ch 9:14; Ne 11:15.
