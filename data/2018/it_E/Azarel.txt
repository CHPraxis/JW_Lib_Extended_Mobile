AZAREL
(Azʹar·el) [God Has Helped].
1. One of the mighty men who joined David at Ziklag.​—1Ch 12:1, 6.
2. Head of the 11th of the 24 divisions of temple singers in David’s time; also called Uzziel.​—1Ch 25:1, 4, 18.
3. Son of Jeroham and prince of the tribe of Dan under David’s rule.​—1Ch 27:22; 28:1.
4. One of the descendants of Binnui who, at Ezra’s urging, sent away their foreign wives and sons.​—Ezr 10:19, 38-41, 44.
5. Father or ancestor of the Amashsai who dwelt in Jerusalem under Governor Nehemiah. He was of the priestly house of Immer.​—Ne 11:1, 13.
6. A musician in the procession headed by Ezra as they walked atop Jerusalem’s rebuilt wall at its inauguration. Perhaps the same as No. 5 above.​—Ne 12:31, 36.
