ANAIAH
(A·naiʹah) [Jah Has Answered].
1. One of the men who stood at Ezra’s right hand when he read the Law to the people, on the first day of the seventh month. Probably a priest or prince.​—Ne 8:2, 4.
2. One of the headmen of the people whose descendant, if not he himself, attested to the confession contract of Nehemiah.​—Ne 10:1, 22.
