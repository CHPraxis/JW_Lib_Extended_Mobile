BOHAN
(Boʹhan) [possibly, Thumb].
1. A descendant of Reuben after whom a boundary stone for the territory of Judah was named.​—Jos 15:6; 18:17.
2. “The stone of Bohan” served as a boundary marker for the tribes of Benjamin and Judah. (Jos 15:6; 18:17) It lay near the NW corner of the Salt Sea and evidently toward the foot of the plateaulike Low Plain of Achor.
