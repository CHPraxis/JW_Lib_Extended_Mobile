NOADIAH

(No·a·diʹah) [Jah Has Kept Appointment; Jah Has Met by Appointment].

1. Son of Binnui; a Levite who, on the fourth day following Ezra’s arrival in Jerusalem, 468 B.C.E., helped inventory the silver, gold, and utensils for the temple.​—Ezr 8:32, 33.

2. A prophetess who, in 455 B.C.E., was singled out by name as one who tried to stop the rebuilding of Jerusalem’s walls by instilling fear in Nehemiah.​—Ne 6:14.
