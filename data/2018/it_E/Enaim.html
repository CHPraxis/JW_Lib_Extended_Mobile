ENAIM

(E·naʹim) [probably, Two Fountains; Two Springs].

A site near which Tamar, disguised as a prostitute, cohabited with Judah, resulting in the birth of Perez and Zerah. (Ge 38:14-16, 21, 29, 30) Enaim was apparently situated between Adullam and Timnah. (Ge 38:12) While considered by some geographers to be the same as Enam in the Shephelah (Jos 15:34), its exact location is today unknown.
