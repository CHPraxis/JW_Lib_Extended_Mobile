BEZAI
(Beʹzai) [shortened form of Bezalel].
1. An Israelite whose descendants numbering over 300 returned to Jerusalem with Zerubbabel in 537 B.C.E.​—Ezr 2:17; Ne 7:23.
2. One by this name, or a representative of such a family group, who attested to Nehemiah’s “trustworthy arrangement.”​—Ne 9:38; 10:1, 14, 18.
