The light shining upon the Kingdom privileges and requirements is such that daily the issue assumes a clearer and deeper meaning. A few years back the examination of our progress was considered almost entirely from the standpoint of the amount of literature placed, ft became evident, as the issue clarified, that this could not be the determining factor, for two reasons: First, it was not entirely Scriptural; and, second, it was not reasonable, and our God is both consistent and reasonable. The fact was gradually borne home to us that we are Jehovah’s witnesses, not his book agents in an;1 sense of the word.

The work of a witness is to give testimony; and that is why the Kingdom work daring recent years is forecast in testimonies rather than on the amount of literature placed. As members of the anointed, we can and should hold ourselves responsible for the number of testimonies we give; but we cannot hold ourselves responsible for the amount of literature placed, because that is affected by too many tilings beyond our control. The Apostle Paul admonishes the Lord’s people to present their bodies a living sacrifice, wholly and acceptable unto God. This means being his witnesses by giving testimony on the question at issue, and the apostle says this is our reasonable service. It is a reasonable service. It is up to us whether we are doing this to the extent of our ability or not. However, giving testimony is not a mechanical proposition. The anointed arc living sacrifices; indicating that their faculties are alive and alert to the requirements of the work assigned to them. The manner and method of giving the testimony will manifest the degree of faithfulness on the part of the creature.

Still another may deliver the testimony convincingly and feel that that is all that is required. All these methods lack the vital spark and indicate that to some extent at least those using them do not really know Jehovah. What Is “Business Territory”?


Manifestly it is nut only to the interests of the work in general for all territory to be thoroughly covered, but to the interests of the pioneers themselves, as they will not have to move so often. Business districts are part of pioneer assignments, and we encourage those who hold assignments not in. company territory to thoroughly cover the business sections before the assignment is turned back to this office. Business districts have proven to be a fruitful field for many pioneers.

In company and sharpshooter territory the business districts are open for pioneers who cannot go to isolated territory. Business districts are not to be covered by the company workers unless the service director or sharpshooter makes a special request stating that the company workers can cover the business sections, and receives the business district assignment from this office. Auxiliaries wishing to work generally in business districts should get a special assignment from this office, as do the pioneers.

Business districts refer not only to retail stores, but also to offices, factories, water fronts, in fact, all but the residential territory of a city. Tn many places there are streets of stores with apartments above. The rows of stores are business districts; the apartments above are residential. Often, away ftota the main, downtown businesses, arc groups of business houses of all kinds, and these also are included in the term “business districts”, and are pioneer territory unless the company specially requests them. What about the isolated neighborhood stores, those located by themselves throughout the residential sections? The service directors should see that these are witnessed to, and the best way would be for the worker having the . adjoining residences to take these little stores as he comes to them. Sometimes directors report that business districts are covered, when only some of the first-floor stores are entered, offices, etc., above being untouched; but these professional men are included in business districts.

THE NEW CATALOG Can Be Used

in Witness Work

About January 15 the Society' will have completed the publication of a revised catalog. It will be practically the same as the one used last year, with a few additions for the new books. A new colored insert will appear in the middle, which will give a better display of the books published by the Society than the previous one.

Many of the friends have found this catalog a real help in placing the literature by using it the same as they would a prospectus. Some of the sisters and older brothers find it impossible to carry a full set of the ten bound books and all the booklets when witnessing from door to door. They have found that by carrying a few of the books with this catalog they have been able to place complete sets of ten.

The Society publishes the catalog primarily for answering radio inquiries. All listeners desiring to know more about our work and the literature we publish are mailed a copy of this catalog, and it has proved effective in. this manner. It can be used not only in answer to radio inquiries, but as a prospectus, and the Society is pleased to offer these booklets to the friends at cost of production. They can again be had at two for 5c.

We should very much appreciate it if the director of each company would order at one time the number the workers desire for their use, with some order for books, so as to save shipping charges. The same would be well for the pioneers and auxiliaries also.

A Convenience for Every Worker A request has been made several times for a testimony to be printed in different languages. It may be necessary to use one or all of these methods under certain circumstances, and they would really be a testimony under those conditions, if nothing else could bo done; but Jehovah says that we are his witnesses that he is God. Therefore our ultimate object, the ideal testimony, should be to reveal Jehovah, his word, his name and his purposes to our listeners in the most complete way possible. The oral testimony reveals him to a degree. Placing booklets reveals him to a greater degree; and the bound books, which explain his Word to a greater extent, are undoubtedly the best testimony. Therefore the hast witnesses of Jehovah are not satisfied to simply read, recite or deliver their testimony, or even to leave a few booklets. Their great objective always is to leave the bound books in the hands of the people. However, if the oral testimony is all that they can leave, well and good; and if they can place a few booklets, so much the better; but their real objective is to testify that Jehovah is God in the best way possible, and this can be best accomplished by leaving the bound books in the hands of the people.

Therefore, let us all be witnesses of Jehovah and bear testimony on the great issue, “Who is God?’’ Let us always strive for the ideal testimony, that is, leaving the bound books, and be thankful for what we are able to accomplish by the Lord’s grace, whether it be an oral testimony, a booklet, or a complete set of bound books.
