One of the Big Features

There are certain features in every year’s work that are outstanding, and certain objectives that the Lord’s anointed will earnestly strive to accomplish. The purpose of the organization which the Lord has formed is to give a testimony to the nations of the earth concerning God’s purposes. He has blessed us with the great privilege of being his witnesses that he is Jehovah and that his kingdom shall shortly he established in all the world for the blessing of those who love righteousness. Due to the fact that the remnant are commissioned to give the testimony, it is appropriate tiiat the Society should set as its goal each year a quota in testimonies. In arranging for the quota, which is set at 22,500,000 testimonies given in the United States, the number of company workers, of pioneers, of auxiliaries and of sharpshooters has been carefully considered. The time they are putting into the service has been used as a basis for the quota, as well as the number of testimonies they are delivering each hour. There has been a marvelous increase in the number of workers this year. This is due mostly to those of good will, the Jonadab class, who are taking in the water of life freely and then in turn passing it on to others. These have been richly blessed, and should be aided hy all of the Lord’s people, throughout the coming year, to continue in this privilege of honoring Jehovah’s name in the earth.

The Workers

It is estimated that during the fiscal year 1934 there will be an average of 14,000 company workers and sharpshooters engaging in the field service and reporting monthly. We hope that this will be increased, however, due to the interest shown by the Jonadab class, which results would be included in company results. It is hard to estimate this figure. In considering the number of pioneers that might be expected in the service, the estimate is 1,850 reporting each month. There are more than 2,000 on our list, but, for some reason or other, between 150 and 200 always fail to send in reports. We hope that during the coming year this will be overcome. Even though pioneers are ill or unable to work during the month, a report should be sent in, so that we shall be in close contact with every individual in the pioneer service. The pioneers are granted the greatest privilege, because they cau spend all of their time in giving testimony, which is the commission to them from Jehovah. During the year their experiences in the work as reported to this office have been very gratifying.

The Auxiliaries

There has not been much of an increase in the auxiliary list, but here is a point we hope that every company worker will consider. Our estimate is 850 for the year; but we hope that hy the end of the year this will be far short, because the number in the auxiliary work should be at least more than 1,000 reporting monthly. Many of the company workers are working only part-time or Short hours, many on a 5-hour day, others 4 days a week ; and with such hours for their secular employment which is necessary to take care of their obligations, it would be easily possible for many of these individuals to arrange their time so as to spend at least two or three hours a day in the field work. If this be done, in a month’s time they could easily pnt in 55 hours in the work, and, in that way, be enrolled as auxiliaries, making reports direct to the Society. Special rates are granted those on the auxiliary list. In spending a few extra hours a day in this branch of the service it will be especially pleasing to the Lord, and much witnessing can be accomplished by going at it in a systematic manner such ub this. We hope that every company director will bring this to the attention of the company every short while, and then it will be up to each individual to decide whether he has the time or can arrange to engage in the auxiliary branch of the service.

How you are able to arrange your time to engage in this work will be of interest to this office, as your experience may be an aid to others to get into the auxiliary work.
