Feed the Hungry


THERE is now a famine in the land for the hearing of the Word of the Lord, even as his prophet foretold. —Amos 8:11.

There are, ■ we believe, millions on earth who are really hungry for the truth and know not where to find it. It is a blessed privilege to point them to the food and to feed them.

Lift up a Standard for the People ,

The enemy (Satan and his cohorts) has come in like a flood in his desperate attempt to turn the people away from God, his Word and his kingdom. Those who have the spirit of the Lord will now, in obedience to the command, lift up a standard against him. (Isaiah 59: 19) That standard is the message of truth. It is the message that must be given to those of the world who desire to know the true God and his righteous ways. It is the food upon which the hungry must be fed. It is the balm of consolation that is needed by those who mourn.             .

Our Commission

The anointed of the Lord are the ones on the earth to carry this message and feed the hungry. To them the Lord has said: ffYe are my witnesses . . . and my servant whom I have chosen. . . . Ye are my witnesses, saith the Lord, that I am God.”—Isaiah 43:10-12.

Thy God Reigneth

This is a plain divinely-given commission to the saints this side the vail to represent the Lord and to. keep before the people that Jehovah is God, that his kingdom is at hand; and that he, through Christ, comforts all those who desire to be comforted and feeds those who desire to be fed. .

During the past few years the Church has been specially advertising the King and his kingdom. Great numbers have learned something about the truth by seeing it advertised and hearing public addresses. There are millions who have not heard, many of whom are hungry. There is a great work to do. Each one who has the spirit of the Lord will have a part in it according to the Lord’s command.

METHODS OF WORK

Larger opportunities present themselves with the Spring and Summer weather. With the more favorable season of the year for work, 1935 has the advantage of finding the people more ready to read our literature. In past years for the most part our literature has been purchased by church workers or people interested in the Bible, but with the calamities and trouble upon the nations thus far experienced in 1935, together with the emphasis laid upon, this year by Christian people generally, we find a wider interest not only in the meetings but also in the literature.

Canvass for Sets

To take advantage of this, we recommend that a new combination be introduced in connection with your canvassing, and that the primary canvass be for the seven volumes and the Harp Bible St^idy . Course. We recommend canvassing for these volumes because'our experience has demonstrated that there are a greater number of sales made, and a larger number of homes reached.

It should be remembered that the signs indicate that our work will not long continue; and the people by having the 8 volumes in their homes have a library on Bible matters for reference after you, as a worker, can no longer serve them. Tailing, however, to place the 8 volumes, we .recommend canvassing for the Harp Bible Study Course, and failing to place the Harp Bible Study Course, offer the three booklets. Desirable Government, World Distress and Comfort for the People, at 25y. If no sale has been made then offer the Comfort booklet at 10^.

This combination we recommend only, however, if the class has a large stock of Desirable Government and World Distress booklets on hand. Otherwise we recommend substituting the Hell booklet, and Our Lord’s Beturn together with the Comfort booklet for 25(1.

8 Volume Offer Increases Sales

The sale of the eight-volume combination, reports we receive indicate, will exceed this year the distribution of Studies in the Scriptures of the past year. Experience in canvassing for the eight-volume combination naturally increases one’s ability to place them in the hands of the people, and surely the complete library is what should be left with them.

“Be Thou Faithful Unto Death”

Though all may not be situated so as to devote more than one day a week to the work; yet the persistent devoting of time every week will increase our ability to present the Truth properly to the people. The following letter is a testimony of what one sister did, and it holds before us what is possible when there is an earnest determination to engage in field service:

“I write you of the ‘faithfulness unto death’ of our dear Sister Wollam (Mrs. Z. A. Wollam) who finished her course February 6th. She was an auxiliary colporteur, averaging about twenty hours canvassing'each week, besides caring for her home and husband. She was sixty-seven years of age, iiitd of small stature, weighing about ninety pounds. But away she would go with her two heavy bags of books, day after day, as cheerful and happy as one could imagine. 'J’lio .joy of the Lord was her strength.

“Her memory was not especially strong, but her faithfulness and loving obedience to li^r heavenly Father were glorious! Sometimes she would get up at 3 a. m. so as to get her work done in time to .go out for the day with a canvassing auto party. For the year 1924 she sold more books than any other member of our Riverside Ecclesia, 1321 books; more than the 30 other members of the class put together, not counting the three colporteurs. While coming home in the rain, after a half day’s witnessing, about 6:30 p. m. February 6th, she was struck down by an auto and killed. ‘Precious in the sight of the Lord is the death of his saints.’

“Director at Riverside, Calif."
