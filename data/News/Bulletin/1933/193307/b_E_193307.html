ALL RECORDS EXCELLED International Thanksgiving Period the Greatest Ever Held World-wide Report Shows 3,920,190 Books and Booklets Placed


6,682,439 Persons Receive Testimony

Brother Rutherford Heard by Vast Audience

4The figures for The Remnant’s

Thanksgiving Period, April 8;16, are ready iit last, and here they are, a whole page . of them. Reports have been received from 76 different countries and all bear out the same conclusion, that this was the most wonderful service period ever enjoyed. Everywhere the brethren responded to the call to service as never before. Appreciating the tremendous importance of the message to he brought to the people, and that they were in a covenant with the Lord to do this, tlie brethren simply could not stay at home, for it was as a ‘fire in their bones’ and stirred their zeal for the Lord to a fever pitch.

During the spring Thanksgiving Period of 1932, when an international report of the results was tabulated, we rejoiced greatly as we beheld 38,585 workers in the field delivering 2,447,840 testimonies and placing 840,340 pieces of literature in the hands of 710,638 persons. These workers reported 350,833 hours in the service. That was a mighty effort and a wonderful period. The figures for the fall Thanksgiving Period (Kingdom Testimony Period) of the same . year showed a decrease in number of workers to 37,411, total literature 677,1.22, ob-tainers 4.90,522, testimonies 2,202,-979, but those who participated worked diligently and reported 384,-190 hours. With these figures as a basis the quota of 3,000,000 testimonies and 1,000,000 pieces of literature was set at first for The Remnant’s Thanksgiving Period of April 8-16. As it became known that more and more countries were going to be ahle to have the Crisis booklet for the campaign we became more hopeful for bigger results. The various printing plants of the Society rushed the translation of the booklet and its publication in as many languages as possible, and as a result 52 different countries, included in this report, had The Crisis in some language to place with the people.

And then the reports began to come in. Tn a very short time the million mark, in pieces of literature placed, was passed. The figures climbed steadily toward the two-3million mark. Incoming reports kept saying “It’s the best period yet”; “More workers than ever before, more zeal and joy in the service, and splendid results in the placement of literature.” But even at that, when the figures showed that 3,920,190 books and booklets had actually been placed and 6,682,439 testimonies given within a short i>eri-od of nine days, we could hardly believe our eyes. That means that almost 4.7 times as many pieces of literature were placed as during the same period of the preceding year, and about 5.8 times as many as during the Kingdom Testimony Period of October 1-9, 1932.

This amazing result was possible because of the Lord’s blessing upon the efforts of his people who stood ready to take advantage of every opportunity? His time had come for the people to be informed concerning the meaning of “The Crisis”, and they were. To Him is due all the honor and glory. Instead of around 38,000 workers, the number participating rose to 58,804, an increase of almost 53 percent, and these averaged better than 12 hours each in the witness work. The number of pioneers, auxiliaries and company workers all increased as each one seemed to appreciate the privilege and responsibility of being in the work and making his report. No doubt many of these new workers are of the Jonadtib class, which is making itself more and more manifest. There are still a great many who participate in .the Memorial emblems who do not appreciate their true significance, as is evidenced by the 84,179 reported for Memorial, compared with this 58,804. . This Memorial report shows a slight increase over the preceding year, at which time 81,778 were reported.

Then, to cap the climax, on the night of April 16, at the close of this glorious period, when nil the other workers had returned from the field, Brother Rutherford delivered a stirring lecture on “The Way of Escape”, over a large network of radio stations. During the week millions of radio folders announcing the lecture were distributed in the United States and Canada and every effort was made to see that the people had an opportunity to listen to this important message. Shortly after the lecture thousands of letters and telegrams were received expressing appreciation and requesting a copy of the address.

It was surely a wonderful Thanksgiving Period and the brethren throughout the earth give tha.rks and praise to Jehovah for these inspiring results accomplished. But for His spirit, His blessings and His protection, none of this would have been possible. Knowing that you will delight in hearing a word from some of the various countries as to how they got along in this campaign, we give you some excerpts from their reports.

Great Work to Be Done During Next Three Months

Summer Vacations Mean More Time to Witness


Finish Rural Territory by September 30

Vindication and Preservation to Be Stressed

The manner in which Jehovah is going before his anointed and literally plowing, breaking and watering the earth with the three stirring radio speeches, first, over a national network of about 60 radio stations, next a nation wide broadcast over 170 stations, and following that over hundreds of portable transcription machines in every nook and corner of the country, Indicates the importance of this summer’s activities. The King of Eternity is riding majestically Into actiou. The position of the anointed now is that of witnesses and heralds of the King. Every highway and byway, every city, town and hamlet must be told of the significance of the time in which we are living. God’s remnant are prepared for that purpose. Every one who would be faithful to Jehovah at this time must use his every power to that end. As Brother Rutherford very definitely stated in his speech “The Way of Escape”, the rulers have had their witness and we are now calling upon the people that those who love righteousness may escape and that the issue may be squarely presented to every creature, giving them an opportunity to take their stand on the Lord’s side and have his protection or to reject the Lord’s provision and suffer the consequences.

To do this properly, this summer must witness the greatest house-to-house campaign ever staged. Every section of the rural territory held by companies should be finished before September 30, and where the portable transcription machine meetings are held this territory should be witnessed to twice: once before the meeting, doing the double work of witnessing and inviting the people you call upon to attend the meeting; and the second time within a week or ten days after the transcription meeting, so that advantage may be taken of the interest developed by the meeting.

The literature to be stressed is the Vindication and Preservation books and .the booklets, with our objective being that of leaving at least a Crisis in every home, but be sure always to present the bound books first. This is particularly necessary in rural territory, where much timo, energy and money is spent in reaching the people. The responsibility devolves upon each service director and service committee, pioneer and sharpshooter to plan this summer’s work so that the greatest possible witness will be given. The service director is particularly charged with the responsibility of seeing that adequate provisions are made for every one in the company to have a part in these campaigns. The KINGDOM Is the big thing In the lives of the anointed now. It is not a matter of 10 percent, 20 percent, or 50 percent, but each one must be 100 percent for the Lord and his cause, first, last aud all the time.

In many companies there are a great many interested who love the Lord and his cause and who do not claim to be of the anointed, who are termed the “Jonadab” class. Provisions should be made so that every one of these can avail himself of the opportunity of demonstrating his love for the Lord and. his cause by participating in this activity to the extent of his ability.

During the next three months there will be 'vacations for those who are employed in secular pursuits and because, of this work are unable to devote all of their time to the Lord's kingdom service. What is going to be done with this vacation ? Is it going ,to be used to the praise of Jehovah’s, name or to gratify some personal desire along other lines? This is the question each one must face and answer before the Lord. Then there are Labor Day and the previous Saturday and Sunday, during which practically every one is released from all secular employment. How is this time to be used? Will it simply mean a drive to some divisional campaign, work 2-j or 3 hours and come home, or will it mean a real witness for Jehovah? These are serious questions for each one to ask himself or herself; for the issue is put squarely before us by Jesus in these words, “Herein is my Father glorified, that ye bear much fruit.” Fruit-bearing is carrying the Kingdom message to the people to the extent of our ability. Therefore, during the Labor Day week-end at least six hours should be devoted to field service by company workers aud sharpshooters, with auxiliaries increasing their time proportionately. The divisional leader, In arranging for territory for September 3 and 4 divisional campaign, is to allow at least 400 homes for each car. This does not need to be in one city, of course. The campaign can be extended to include sufficient territory to provide that many homes for each car in order that a thorough witness may be given. See to it, however, that the territory is within the company and sharpshooter assignments of your division. In this way adequate provisions will be made for all to have part in the campaign and also to give a real witness to the praise of Jehovah during this period.

THE UNITED STATES

The Remnant’s Thanksgiving Period was the greatest witness yet for the United States. Never before has there been such a response to a call to service as during this special period. During the same period last year 13,628 participated in the work. This year from the United States and territory under its direct supervision reports were received from 20,902, or an increase of 7,274 workers. With such a big increase in number of workers engaging in the service for tlie first time, we might expect tlie general averages of time and placements to be considerably less; but the results were just to the contrary. During the Kingdom witness week of September 1931, when the Kingdom booklet was offered, 732,852 books and booklets were left with the people.
