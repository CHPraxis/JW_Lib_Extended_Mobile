Identification Card Never Be Rude to People In many cases where the friends are stopped by local police officials the police claim that they do this because they are not certain that the friends are bona fide representatives of the Society. They claim that there i.s such a wave of lawlessness everywhere that they have to take this precaution and insist on stopping the friends until they have established the fact that they are really what they claim to be.

To overcome this difficulty and avoid the embarrassment that arises out of such circumstances, tlie Society is prinfing a neat identification cord signed by Brother Rutherford. This card identifies the worker and slates his mission. The cards will be sent to the classes on the basis of the number of Bulletins they are now receiving. Pioneer and auxiliary colporteurs and sharpshooters will receive their card filled out by this office. The director is to fill in the name of each worker in tlie space provided or have some other competent member of the class do so. The worker will then keep this card, and if he Is stopped he Is to use it as his means of identification as a representative of the Society.
