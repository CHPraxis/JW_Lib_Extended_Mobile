TRAINING ANO TEACHING IN 1958

Better and More Ministers Our*Goal


  • 1 Christians know the value of goals. “I am pursuing down toward the goal,” said Paul. Fixing our eyes on the right kind of goals is a course of wisdom: “Wisdom is before the face of the understanding one, but the eyes of the stupid one are at the extremity of the earth.” Last year’s service year showed the wisdom of keeping goals before our eyes. We had seven of them. Their value is evident from the blessed results. What progress we made!—Phil. 3: 14; Prov. 17:24.

  • 2 Now with the new service year we have a goal of immense importance. It is the twin goal of better and more ministers. The means of reaching this goal? Training and teaching in 1958.

  • 3 This means training new ministers so that they are more effective in their ministry. Helping others to maturity is a right goal: “You must assist those who are weak.” Training in 1958 also depends on our individually living up to the command: “Be training yourself with godly devotion as your aim.”—Acts 20:35; 1 Tim. 4: 7.

  • 4 To reach our training goal, then, we need to be willing to participate in the training program. Before coming to a knowledge of the truth, how many Kingdom ministers ever thought of being house-to-house teachers of the good news? Few Indeed. But no matter; we can train ourselves with godly devotion as our aim. It is not impossible to attain the maturity needed for freeness of speech, both in house-to-house and Bible study work. “For all things,” said Paul, “I have the strength by virtue of him who imparts power to me.”—Phil. 4:13.

“Adequately Qualified to Teach Others”

  • 5 So that we can teach householders from house to house and through Bible studies we must become “qualified to teach.” How vital this is! We do not want to be like those of whom Paul said: “You ought to be teachers in view of the time.” Instead, we have this Scriptural objective in view: “The things you heard from me with the support of many witnesses, these things commit to faithful men who in turn will be adequately qualified to teach others.” (2 Tim. 2:24; Heb. 5:12; 2 Tim. 2:2) Do you know that only one third of the publishers conduct Bible studies now? Why not make it two thirds by December? Be a teacher in home Bible study activity.

    Every service meeting should have a theme. This outline provides the theme for the first service meeting of the month. Congregations will select their own theme for the other meetings. How should a theme be selected? There are several ways. Is there a particular weakness in the congregation? A Scripture text or idea that suggests correcting that weakness might be made the theme. When a feature of Kingdom ministry is highlighted in your congregation, a theme in harmony with the stressed activity could be used. Another source of ideas is the daily text. The Scripture text or com


    ment at the bottom of the front and back pages of Kingdom Ministry suggests theme ideas, especially for the meetings when comments are to be made on them.

    Having a theme is of little value if the brothers on the program neglect it. It is most valuable when each participant says a few words about the theme, weaving it into his part, whether it be a talk, question-and-answer part or a demonstration. The introducing of the next brother on the program furnishes opportunity to weave in a brief statement on the theme.

SCENE I (7 min.) The setting is in the home of a young couple who have given up the pioneer work and have become involved in worldly pursuits. Husband sitting in living room sympathizes with wife who is complaining about work in office and house being too much for her. Husband admits strain, but bills must be paid. He tells wife of a raise offered him, but it will require several nights a week of extra work. She brightens up and sees it as a means of paying off debts and getting few extras. She encourages him to take job and agrees to help him do his evening work.

Wife suddenly remembers this is service meeting night. It is late —7:15 p.m. Wife asks husband if he does not have a student talk to give. He says he was so busy that he asked the instructor to give it to someone else. Both decide to stay home. Husband tells wife that the congregation servant wanted to arrange for a book study in their home and was considering recommending him for study conductor. Asks wife what she thinks of the idea. She bemoans being tied down and reminds him that he would have to take the lead in all features of the work. Can’t see how he could manage and still keep up with his secular job. He admits it would crowd him. Wife consoles him with the thought that they are regular publishers, averaging three hours a week. Husband’s conscience is soothed with that, sits back to watch television.

SCENE II (13 min.) While enjoying TV there is a knock at the door. A young couple and their son whom the first couple helped to get the truth happen by. Since their service meeting has been changed to a different night they were out making back-calls. They saw lights on and decided to stop in. They have exciting news to tell, but are puzzled to find the first couple at home. These try to explain, but are obviously embarrassed. The second couple tell about their plans to pioneer, to work only two or three days a week and devote the rest to the service. They tell of the joy of having a congregation book study in their home and how the husband has been made conductor and how his group is making progress. His wife tells of so much interest that the only way to take care of them is to pioneer. They speak excitedly of the 1958 convention. The first couple say they could not spare the money. The husband says his wife could go but she thinks it selfish to go without him. The, other couple call attention to the article in the “Kingdom Ministry” urging all to attend. If one part of the family could attend it would prove more beneficial than none going. They tell how they had planned on getting a new car and color TV, but when the convention was announced they decided to go to it instead, and how happy they are that they so decided. They mention the rich young ruler and how he lost out because he was reluctant about giving up luxuries. They do not want to end up like him. They talk about the joy of presenting “The Watchtower” at the doors, of the excellent articles. The first couple had no time even to read them. The enthusiastic couple warmly discuss the ministry school and the tests and speak about the joy of knowing the truth.

SCENE III (5 min.) After their friends have gone, the first couple take a new look at themselves. They now see how far they have fallen behind. They are determined to do something about it. They plan to sell some of their extras to pay up the bills. The husband decides to reject advancement offer. Instead he will turn his house into a service center, and is determined to do his best if appointed as conductor. Their consciences feel better. They talk of re-entering the pioneer work. The scene closes with the brother saying he appreciates Jehovah’s waking him up.

Pioneering

—a Blessed Privilege of Service

1A door to a new life with bountiful blessings stands before you. Can you open it? Perhaps you can if you try. The door is pioneering, and it is the same door that the apostle Paul stepped through. He said: “A large door that leads to activity has been opened to me.”—1 Cor. 16: 9.

  • 2 He overcame the obstacles opposing him. Nothing could hold him back from expressing his joy.

  • 3 Do you have this same abounding joy for the truth? If you do you will want to follow Paul’s example and not “put out the fire of the spirit.” (1 Thess. 5:19) Let it flame up within you. Let it move you.

  • 4 Like Paul you can travel to isolated territory, and perhaps even to a foreign land and there, like a luminary, radiate the truth that has brought joy to your heart. Think what a blessing you would be to the good-will persons there.

  • 5 Of course, if you are unable to work in territory where the need is great, that does not mean the door to pioneering is closed to you. Not in the least. You can work with a congregation, perhaps your own.

  • 6 Even those who are unable to pioneer can experience the joy pioneers have of working isolated territory or with small congregations who need help. They can find work in such territory and move there.

  • 7 But whatever your age, whatever your circumstances, consider pioneering. Perhaps you can follow Paul’s example by opening its door and enjoying its blessings.

DEVOTE MORE HOURS TO PREACHING

  • 1 Often a new or immature publisher will want to stop after an hour of telling the good news, but the mature companion should encourage him to keep on. In one country where the mature and full-time publishers are doing this the average for congregation publishers is from twelve to thirteen hours a month. It is a case of the strong helping the weak ones.

  • 2 To help increase hours let congregations arrange for weekday group witnessing, not overlooking afternoons for those unable to get out in the morning. You pioneers can help, especially by taking the lead in weekday morning house-to-house witnessing. And you mature publishers who regularly tell the good news in the evening, encourage weaker ones to work with you.

  • 3 The more hours we devote to the service the more praise to Jehovah.


Essential Qualities of Sermons

  • 1 It is written that “the tongue of the wise ones does good with knowledge.” (Prov. 15: 2) How much good are you doing? You have a tongue; you have knowledge; you have territory. Here are the means for doing much good. But perhaps you feel that more good should come from your efforts. It can. And Kingdom Ministry is going to show you how. Beginning with this issue Kingdom Ministry will consider twelve essential qualities of sermons. Here is a wonderful opportunity to increase your productiveness.

  • 2 The first essential quality of a sermon is a theme—a Scriptural Kingdom theme. That means you must choose one simple point to stress in your sermon. It may be Jehovah’s name or world peace or world unity or something else. But the point you choose must be the point you talk on and around which you build supporting arguments and proofs. Without a theme your sermon will have little more effect than if you spoke to the air. “For truly, if the bugle sounds an indistinct call, who will get ready for battle? In the same way also, unless you through the tongue utter speech easily understood, how will it be known what is being spoken? You will, in fact, be speaking into the air.” (1 Cor. 14 : 8, 9) Use a theme and be understood.

  • 3 A theme gives value to your words, and when associated with the Kingdom and Scriptural quotations your words become like “apples of gold in silver carvings.” (Prov. 25; 11) It is important that you associate your theme with the Kingdom, for the Kingdom is our hope. It is what we yearn for and pray for. The thrilling blessings it will bring gives our sermons strong appeal. It gets listening ears.

  • 4 Carefully select pointed scriptures to support your theme, and then read them from the Bible each time you give your sermon. What better way can we impress upon people that what we say is not our own opinion but what God’s Word says? By reading from the Bible you encourage people to be like the Be-roeans who examined “the Scriptures daily as to whether these things were so.”—Acts 17:11.

  • 5 As you repeatedly work youi- territory with pointed messages from the Bible, the people will come to respect what you say. For persons of good will your tongue will be like the tongue mentioned at Proverbs 10: 20: “The tongue of the righteous one is choice silver.” But this will be only if you put your thoughts across, if you make yourself understood. You cannot be understood if you say too much. Be satisfied to put one simple point across, a point they will remember. That point is your Scriptural Kingdom theme.

  • 6 Now that you know what the first essential quality of a sermon is you will want to think about it. Examine your sermon. See if you have it. Make use of it at the doors, watching carefully that you do not wander from it. Keep to it as to a fixed point. Its use is the first step toward reaping more good from the use of your tongue.
