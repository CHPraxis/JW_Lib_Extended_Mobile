How Magazine-conscious Are You?

  • 1 Regarding the publishers’ magazine placements in the United States, the Society may well quote Paul’s words at 1 Thessalonians 4:10: “In fact, you are doing it . . . But we exhort you, brothers, to go on doing it in fuller measure.”

  • 2 Thus in June, 1955, congregation publishers were averaging 6.5 magazines monthly, pioneers 49.5 and special pioneers 61.9. But during 1957 the congregation publishers averaged 12.4 magazines, almost twice as many; pioneers averaged 99.8 and special pioneers 135.6, both more than doubling their magazine placements in two years. In 1955, in the United States alone, we placed just a little more than 18 million magazines, but in 1957 almost 36 million. It is no wonder Brother Knorr said that the Society was very much pleased with the excellent magazine work done and that we should continue to stress it!

  • 3 Let all, therefore, continue to support the magazine activity zealously. Do so on Magazine Day, in going from store to store and from house to house and in the street work; do so in the prestudy evening work and on Sundays by offering the two magazines, if the householder does not accept the regular offer. And especially in December we should all be magazine-conscious, because of the opportunity that the holiday presents.

  • 4 Schedule your affairs so that magazines will not pile up at your home. If for any reason you have a few left after the next issue comes out, offer one with the current magazine until they are used up. And, of course, we do not want to have magazines piling up at the Kingdom Hall, either. Therefore, let each one regularly take the number of magazines he stipulated he would and upon which the magazine-territory servant based his order from the Society. By watching these points our magazine activity for 1958 can be even better than it was for 1957!

“Awake!” Results

We have cause for rejoicing despite the flu during the Awake! campaign. Simplicity and Practical Application

  • 1 When Jesus preached he did not use the language of the scholars and philosophers of his day. He spoke the language of the common people, using expressions familiar to them. Because he used simplicity of speech he could be understood by all. Our speech at the doors should have the same simplicity.

  • 2 Simplicity means clearness, unaffectedness and freedom from complexity. If you speak with simplicity you will not use theocratic terms in your sermons without proper explanation. Terms such as remnant, theocracy, Armageddon, great multitude, goats, other sheep, Jonadabs and so forth have no meaning to outsiders unless an explanation is given. If you use the term “battle of Armageddon,” qualify it by saying, “that is, when God will destroy this present wicked world or system of things.” Make sure each statement is understood before you make another one; then you will be speaking with simplicity. “Unless you through the tongue utter speech easily understood, how will it be known what is being spoken?” (1 Cor. 14:9) It takes a conscious effort to express oneself in terms that people can understand. That means we must be sensitive to the words and terms we use at the door, making sure they are clear to the householder. We should also have simplicity in our theme. It should be a simple point, which is clear, logical and easily grasped.

  • 3 When Jesus preached he made frequent use of illustrations. “Then he told them many things by illustrations.” (Matt. 13:3) He told about the sower that went out to sow seed; about the Kingdom being like a mustard grain, like yeast in dough and like a dragnet. He told many other things that were common to the people’s experience. Uis simple and direct illustrations helped to impress on the minds of the people the truths he preached. We can make the same good use of illustrations.

  • 4 Since people are generally interested in themselves, you will need to point out how the subject you are speaking about affects them and how they can benefit. If you are speaking about new-world blessings, ask If they would like to live in a world where they and their children would never be sick. Then explain how they can. To people living in large cities it might be pointed out that it is unsafe to be on the streets at night, but under the rule of God’s kingdom there will be no danger. There may be some local condition troubling a community that will lend itself well for making practical application of your sermon. Be alert in applying such things.

  • 5 When Jesus spoke the people listened “with pleasure,” not only because he spoke the truth but also because he spoke in a way they could understand. He used simplicity of speech with good illustrations, making practical application of what he said. Let us follow his example and improve the effectiveness of our sermons.
