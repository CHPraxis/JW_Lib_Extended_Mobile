Walk by Faith amid Materialism's Deceptions

1 As we begin the new 1964 service year this month, all of us will want to determine to continue improving our service to Jehovah. We are better equipped to do so now than we were a year ago. Not only have we had one more year to progress toward maturity, but on September 8 we will have concluded the stimulating Around-the-World Assembly. How this has enriched us spiritually! Yes, Jehovah continues to provide what we need to aid us toward further growth in the ministry so we can continue walking by faith amid the materialistic deceptions of this old world. —Matt. 13:22.

Objectives for New Year

2 What is our primary objective for the new service year? It is to take advantage of every opportunity to praise Jehovah and make known the “good news of the kingdom.” All publishers are encouraged to arrange their schedules to shoulder their individual responsibilities as ministers. Included in this is the aiding of others so they too may progress toward Christian maturity. Is there a publisher in the congregation who would be strengthened and helped to grow to maturity if you took a personal interest in him, perhaps helping him to improve his personal study and encouraging him to comment regularly in meetings? Of course there is! Lovingly take the initiative to offer that help. Servants will be glad to offer suggestions on where the most good can be done.

31964 can be another year of fine increase, as the brothers return to their congregations encouraged by the recent assemblies, eager to put into practice the things they have learned. All publishers will be interested to know the 10-percent goal for increase in their congregations for the coming year. The new chart will be put up at the end of this month and will show this suggested congregation goal. Take a personal interest in it. Know what your congregation is working toward and then work constructively to help someone with whom you are studying to be included among those who start in the service this year.

4 The summer is almost past and children will be going back to school. This means that many sisters will have time to vacation pioneer. Also, with people back from vacation, Bible studies discontinued during the summer months should be resumed. Call on all who were studying before the summer and who, for one reason or another, stopped the study. Their study of God’s Word is vital if they are to meet Jehovah’s approval and be put on the way to life. In addition, try to start new studies with other interested ones. Let each one of us be Bible-study conscious and endeavor to conduct one or more studies regularly during the 1964 service year.

Ten-Hour Goal

  • 6 Another goal that should receive attention is that of devoting a minimum of ten hours to the field ministry each month. Every publisher who can will want to arrange a reasonable schedule for this. The goal is surely within reach for many who have not yet attained it, especially when we consider that It means just two and a half hours a week in the ministry. Much fine assistance and encouragement can be rendered by servants and study conductors who will patiently and lovingly help newer and weaker ones to progress toward the goal. They will keep in mind that each publisher has his own routine for the week, and what may be a practical schedule for one may not be so for another.

  • 7 Many publishers are already devoting more time to the ministry than the suggested ten hours, and that is fine. Wherever possible, work diligently toward that reasonable goal. Where it is not possible because of infirmity, old age, family responsibilities or various hindrances, remember, our service to Jehovah is acceptable when we give what we can with the available strength we have. Earnest service, even though limited by circumstances beyond our control, is pleasing to Jehovah, as was the widow’s whole-souled contribution of two small coins. (Luke 21:1-4) However, while it may not be possible for some of us to grow in the quantity of our service, we still want to grow in the quality, increasing in our devotion and integrity.

Dedicated Publishers, Shoulder Your Responsibility

  • 1 Having dedicated our lives to Jehovah and the doing of his will, there is only one direction in which we want to go and that is forward. Having received a commission from God, each one of us should have an overwhelming desire to fulfill it. That this duty falls upon each one individually Paul indicates when he says, “Each one will carry his own load.”—Gal. 6: 5.

  • 2 Before us lies the new service year with all its grand prospects for expansion. What an appropriate time not only to determine how we can individually shoulder more responsibility but also to make definite plans for doing so! What should those plans include? For each member of the family it should mean participation in door-to-door work with effective sermons and sharing fully in magazine work so that the territory is covered adequately and the meek are given the opportunity to hear. Further, it becomes our grand privilege to call back on all interested persons found and cultivate this interest so that Bible studies can be started. Finally, it means conducting Bible studies with a purpose; teaching so that accurate knowledge is imparted and new ministers begin serving.

  • 3 Does shouldering your own responsibility mean receiving no help or encouragement? Not at all. In fact, assisting others is an additional responsibility to shoulder. How happy we should be to receive loving assistance from our brothers from time to time I Therefore, always welcome the assistance of the circuit servant, the district servant, the congregation servant and others in regard to making up a practical schedule.

Every year sees advancement and increase in the New World society, and Jehovah’s blessing continues richly upon his people for progress. With this prospect before us, may we individually shoulder our responsibilities during the 1964 service year, resulting in increased praise to Jehovah and continued growth toward maturity individually.

WHAT HAPPENED HERE IN JULY?

While many were attending- the “Everlasting- Good News” Assemblies in Milwaukee and New York during July as well as enjoying vacation periods, the Society was pleased to see that the country as a whole averaged 9.1 hours per publisher. This was fine in view of the busy schedule so many families had during this period, and we commend you for your faithful activity.

Now, of course, we will want to get back into a pattern of regular field ministry and resolve to have a workable weekly schedule so we can make the ten-hour goal each month. Regular and special pioneers will want to work out a practical schedule that will enable them to spend 100 and 150 hours respectively in field service each month. If you are having some difficulty in working out a schedule, whether a publisher or a pioneer, do not hesitate to talk to your circuit servant, congregation overseer or study conductor about receiving some help in this regard. These brothers will be more than happy to help you, and time and again it has been proved that a good schedule, adhered to on a weekly basis, produces results. May Jehovah continue to bless you individually as you put Kingdom interests first in your lives.
