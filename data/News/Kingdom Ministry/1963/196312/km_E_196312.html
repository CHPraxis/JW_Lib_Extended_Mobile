ARE YOU ANSWERING THE CALL TO PIONEER?

This supplement should be kept because each of the five parts will be considered separately on the service meeting during the months of December 1963 through April 1964. It will be conducted by an enthusiastic brother, one qualified to handle this material. It will be conducted like a Watchtower study, with each paragraph being read after considering the questions on the paragraph. Study the material and come prepared for a lively, informative discussion.

  • 1 During the 1963 service year there were, on an average, 280,052 publishers preaching the Kingdom message every month in the United States. This was 12,616 more ministers in the field every month than during the previous year. Truly, as Jesus said, “the harvest is great.” As great as it is—and it is marvelous in our eyes—there Is a vast multitude not being reached regularly with the message. In the United States there are still some counties that were last worked before 1958, and 782 counties still not assigned to congregations. Some congregations covei- their territory once a year or less and many other congregations have territory that needs more regular coverage. In addition to this, many congregations need mature servant leadership. Are you seriously considering answering the appeal, “Step over into Macedonia and help us”?—Matt. 9:37; Acts 16:9.

  • 2 In view of these needs the Society has regularly sent out a call for more pioneers. As you consider the above facts you will see that they point to the great work that needs to be done before Armageddon strikes in execution of divine judgment. Many more persons are to be reached with the message so they may take flight to safety. Does your desire to reach these people move you to respond to the call to pioneer? In Jesus’ day a similar situation existed. “On seeing the crowds he felt pity for them, because they were skinned and thrown about like sheep without a shepherd.” To be sure, there are many persons in the world, the field, that are “skinned and thrown about” and need shepherds to guide them. We too feel tender affection for them and see the need for more full-time “workers,” more pioneers.—Matt. 9: 36.

  • 3 The call Is so urgent that Jesus said: “Beg the Master of the harvest to send out workers into his harvest.” (Matt. 9:38) The Master is thus implored to stimulate the desire in the hearts of many to respond to the plea and become “workers” in the harvest. By the term “workers” is meant persons who would be so concerned that they would be anxious to pitch in to get the harvest work done before unfavorable weather sets in. Each day that goes by, the time left to accomplish this work is reduced. Energetic persons with a vision of the need are required to be at work “urgently,” as Paul admonished at 2 Timothy 4: 2. The tremendous task at hand demands willing workers I

  • 4 Individually, then, we have a responsibility to examine ourselves. Are you holding back from the pioneer service when you could serve in this capacity? Have you personally analyzed your circumstances? If you are in position to pioneer, what is preventing you from answering the call? It may be necessary for you to put up a hard fight for the faith in order to overcome the materialistic philosophy of life peculiar to this generation, which philosophy prevents some from being spiritually-minded.

  • 5 Unquestionably, there are many in the New World society who can and should give more serious consideration to the call to pioneer. To these we say: Do not delay! Take positive action toward answering the call for more pioneers. Do not take the negative approach and say, ‘One does not have to pioneer to get into God’s new order.’ Actually, each person is judged on the basis of what he can do, and Jehovah expects one to increase or give more attention to the Kingdom interests to the extent that he can. (For further information on this please see the Kingdom Ministry supplement for December, 1962.) So why rationalize and miss out on the joys, privileges and responsibilities that can be yours if you are in position to pioneer? We urge you to give this matter thorough consideration.

  • 6 Those of you who cannot become regular pioneers, make wise use of your time and energies. Jehovah is aware of what you are able to do. Perhaps you cannot respond to the call to become a regular pioneer now, but you can enjoy the vacation pioneer service. Whatever your circumstances, keep in mind the Importance and urgency of preaching. The dedicated Christian will endeavor to share fully in the harvesting work. The call is urgent. Will you be one of these willing workers who can and will increase his privileges of service by responding to the Master’s call? Happy are those who respond to the call to pioneer. —Ezek. 3:17; 1 Cor. 9:16.

PARENTS, ARE YOU ANSWERING THE CALL TO PIONEER?

1 In the United States there are some 125,000 publishers under sixteen years of age. It is most gratifying to see this large number of young ministers, many of whom regularly share in the ministry ten hours or more each month. It is also most gratifying to see many of these vacation pioneering during school vacations and entering the regular pioneer service upon graduating. Parents are to be commended for giving their children such guidance in their early years. This has resulted in aiding children to grow to greater maturity and to shoulder responsibilities in the New World society. We encourage all you parents to keep up the good work.

3 Parents should be very proud to see their children serve in the pioneer ministry. Wise parents will hold this goal before their children during their formative years. Directing the thinking and youthful aspirations of children toward pioneering helps them to grow up with this in mind. To them pioneering will be the natural thing to do in later years. How can you parents best do this? By inculcating the desire for a career as a pioneer in the hearts and minds of your children at every opportunity. Could there be a greater trust that you could give to your children? Could they have a finer career this side of Armageddon? Obviously not. Much can be done along this line by scheduling family Bible studies, field service and meeting attendance together and by helping your children to serve as vacation pioneers during the year. Some parents are now serving as regular pioneers, while not neglecting family responsibilities, and in this way they are setting the finest possible example. Many other parents very commendably serve as vacation pioneers as often as possible. At other times these parents display a fine example by doing well as publishers, spend-

ing ten or more hours each month in the field service. This is a fine way to cultivate the pioneer spirit in your children.

  • 3 Parents are reminded that the responsibility to prepare their children for the future rests upon them. Parents are to “train up a boy according to the way for him.” Youth must be prepared for the future, and parents must cultivate in them the mature view of what constitutes spiritual security. Keep in mind that your children are no exception to the divine statement that “the inclination of the heart of man is bad from his youth up.” Because this is true you should exert your influence and at times your parental authority and discipline to direct the minds of your children so they will "keep Jehovah’s way” before them. Be diligent so your children do not grow up to be unaware of “Jehovah’s way,” indifferent, irresponsible spiritual delinquents. —Prov. 22:6; Gen. 8:21; 18:19; Deut. 6: 7.

  • 4 It is right for you parents to prepare your children for adult life and marriage. However, this preparation should include giving emphasis to spiritual matters and helping them to find their place in the New World society. This is best done by parents’ instilling in their hearts the desire for a career as a pioneer. It would be very wise for parents to encourage their children to devote at least a few years to the pioneer service upon completion of high school. They could have no finer training. Shouldering the responsibilities of the pioneer service will help them to grow to maturity and prepare them to handle additional responsibilities of life, including, if they desire, marriage at an appropriate time. If they choose to marry, they will, as a result of having had proper spiritual education and values of life taught them, never regret the time spent in pioneer service and will benefit by the contribution it has made toward their happiness, contentment and maturity. Many will even continue to pioneer with their marriage mates, devoting all their efforts to the advancement of Kingdom interests.

  • 5 In view of the urgent call for more pioneers, it is important for parents to reflect now on the way they are directing their children. Are your children going the way they should go or the way they want to go? Are they being properly directed, or being misdirected? Speaking of direction, it is a fine thing to require your children to make good use of the public educational facilities by going through high school and thus cultivate certain abilities for future use—if possible, learning a trade during these school years. On the other hand, is it not a matter of misdirection for parents to encourage children to attend the colleges and universities of the day where materialistic philosophies and ideologies are taught and disbelief in God and his Word is fostered? (See The Watchtower issues of February 15, 1952, pages 117-120 and May 15, 1956, pages 313-315.) To prepare your children reasonably for worldly vocations and so-called “security” is proper, but to neglect the spiritual aspect in life is to misdirect them. To provide them with many material things and a bank account but to neglect their spiritual education is to endanger their welfare and is not following the “authoritative advice of Jehovah.” The authoritative advice from Jehovah through his organization is to respond favorably to the call for more pioneers.—Eph. 6: 4.

6 Remember, parents, the best instruction you can give is by your own example. How are you responding to the call to pioneer? In many cases you can answer it yourselves by serving as regular or vacation pioneers. If you do not find this possible, then diligently serve as congregation publishers, shouldering your responsibilities and being a good example to your children. You can also answer the call to pioneer by cultivating the pioneer spirit in your children. Your doing so will bring Jehovah’s blessings.

    New Theocratic Ministry School Schedule


  • 1 A few adjustments in the theocratic ministry school schedule for 1964 are planned to give us all a more active part. Each week all of us will be assigned a portion of the Bible to read. We will do this at home. In due time we will read the entire Bible. Our home reading assignments are scheduled in the same Bible book that will be discussed that week in the school. We will concentrate more direct attention on the Bible itself through the use of our thrilling new book “Ml Scripture Is Inspired, of God, and, Beneficial” (si).

  • 2 Each school session will be a symposium of five talks or demonstrations, all of them usually being based on the same Bible book. The first is the instruction talk. It will cover material designed to give us better background knowledge of the Bible book itself. The speaker should select a subject theme around which to develop his talk. He should amplify the material as necessary, doing any required research in the Bible and the Society’s publications. He should concentrate particularly on material not assigned to students or, at the most, treat such portions in a more general way, leaving details to the student.

  • 3 The first of the four student talks, or talk number’ two, will generally be simply a narration of some outstanding portion of the Bible book. The objective is to give the Bible account, not to explain its meaning or apply the principles embodied in it. This talk will replace the reading assignment.

  • 4 The other student talks, numbers 3 to 5, will usually be more than mere summations of the assigned material. They will be explanatory or interpretive talks, making application of the scriptures to show how and why they are beneficial. Not all the portion assigned need be read or discussed. Whenever possible we will use those key texts that bear most directly on the subject theme assigned. The rest of the scripture assignment is given primarily for background so that the scriptures discussed will not be taken out of context. Neither should the talk be simply a discussion of different isolated texts from the assignment. We should get the central theme in mind and tie all parts of the talk together. In some cases the verses covered by two student talks will be the same, but, as indicated by the assigned themes, the talks will be given from completely different viewpoints.

  • 5 In especially the last three student talks we want to have in mind the particularly beneficial nature of the material assigned. Try to develop settings that will highlight the profitable use of these texts in everyday activity. This will be of more value to the rest of the school, since it will demonstrate application of the texts and how they can be used in a practical way.

  • 6 The written review is scheduled to follow a completed study of a portion of the Bible rather than at regular intervals as heretofore. To prepare, complete your assigned home Bible reading and review the questions in the book “All Scripture Is Inspired of God and Beneficial.” Material from both will be covered by the questions but only the Bible may be used while you are taking the review.

  • 7 The new schedule should allow the school servant a minute or so to introduce the session effectively and perhaps another minute or so to conclude with a brief sum-up or to whet the appetites of all for the following week.

  • 8 We will want to encourage all interested persons to join with us in this inspiring consideration of God’s Word. Since our home Bible reading assignment is a vital new provision of the school, let us diligently keep up with our assignments, reading our Bible daily, rejoicing in the blessings we will receive.

Shoulder Your Responsibility —Helping New Ones in the Service

  • 1 What a joy to see new ones start out in the preaching work! Our joy continues and, in fact, grows as they keep on, step by step, progressing in knowledge and effectiveness in service. Of course, this does not just happen; rather, it takes place when we shoulder our responsibility toward those who are new in the service.

  • 2 We should not be reluctant to invite those with whom we study to go with us in the service when it appears they are ready. Instead, we will take the initiative and then allow God’s spirit to operate upon them. Preparation, of course, is necessary on their part, and so we will rehearse a magazine presentation or the simplified sermon with them, even writing the words out for them if this appears necessary to build up their confidence.

  • 3 In the service we will want to be sure that they have a share either by making the presentation themselves, if qualified, or allowing them to assist in our presentation. When they only listen, progress will be slower and interest may lag. If our presentation has been simple and we have avoided long discussions, they will be built up and encouraged by the experience. Should they be counseled? Commendation at this point will serve a far better purpose, though some counsel, offered in a kind, tactful way, may be appropriate.

  • 4 Field service will be a new thing in their daily routine so it will be good to allow time for them to make this adjustment in their lives. Usually it is best to keep them out in the field for shorter periods of time initially, gradually lengthening this, lovingly taking into account their physical strength and other factors. Some have become tired and discouraged by two or three hours in the field when still new. Others have stopped altogether, feeling that too much is required.
