The other day Brother Knorr told us that there were over 68,000 persons baptized last year and that worldwide there are now, on an average, more than a million publishers proclaiming the Kingdom good news every month. All of us were glad to hear this, and we are anxious to get our copies of the 1965 Yearbook to read more about what was accomplished this past year.

Frequently our brothers ask us, “What do members of the Bethel family do? What kind of life do they lead?” Well, let’s start with Monday and we’ll tell you. A bell rings at 6: 30 in the morning to get us up, and by seven o’clock all the family are expected to be at the breakfast table. Arrangements are made so that twenty persons are prepared to comment on any questions asked on the text; then Brother Knorr, or the one assigned in his absence, makes his comment. A brother is called on to represent the family in prayer, and then breakfast is served. This gets the day off to a good start.

By eight o’clock everyone is at his assignment of work, either in the factory or the home. Some work on machines, others take care of office work or rooms or the laundry, and the kitchen force prepares the food. The morning goes fast.

At five minutes before twelve the bell rings again, and it is time to go to dinner. We all sit in our regularly assigned places, and a hot meal is served. This is our big meal for the day. Back at work after dinner, we stay with our assignments until 5 : 40 p.m.

Our Monday evenings are set aside for the Bethel family to study The Watchtower together for an hour, beginning at 7:25. It is a lively discussion and the hour goes by quickly; then we have a ministry school for the family. With ten groups meeting in different parts of the home for the last four talks, we are able to have forty student talks every Monday night.
