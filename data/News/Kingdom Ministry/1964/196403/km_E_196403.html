Display Conduct Worthy of the Good Hews

1 Our fine Christian conduct is a recommendation for the good news. Do we not represent the established kingdom of God? As representatives of God’s glorious kingdom, may we all conduct ourselves worthily of the good news, so recommending it to others. “Only behave in a manner worthy of the good news about the Christ.” (Phil. 1:27; 2 Cor. 6:3-7) As we reflect credit upon the good news by our actions and our speech, we do well to examine the progress we have made in advancing the good news thus far during the Watchtower campaign.

Our Subscription and Bible Study Goals

  • 2 With the Watchtower campaign entering its third month, publishers should have obtained at least one subscription, pioneers ten and special pioneers fifteen. If any of us have not reached our goal yet, we would do well to reconsider the many helps we have for obtaining subscriptions. They are: Being out regularly in the house-to-house ministry ; having a schedule that will enable us to spend ten or more hours a month in the ministry; being well acquainted with the sermon “Will Sincerity Alone Please God?” ; remembering that The Watchtower is available in sixty-six languages, when we meet a householder who does not read English; making effective use of the information on page two, “The Purpose of ‘The Watchtower’ ”; being diligent to follow up subscription promises and ex-pired-subscription slips. Many publishers have found that offering the Watchtower and Awake! subscriptions together has met with success. One brother recently said: “Out of the first several houses Sunday morning four subscriptions were obtained as the result of offering both subscriptions. People are often inclined to take both as readily as one.”

  • 3 Reaching our subscription goal, of course, is only part of our objective in helping others to learn the good news. Jehovah has commanded us, not only to find, but also to feed sheeplike. ones. Since the best way to feed these persons is through a home Bible study, we will want to put forth earnest effort to start a study with those persons who subscribe and others who show interest. Our goal is one study with the public for each publisher. Are you conducting a study? If some publishers are starting more studies than they can conduct, they can notify the Bible study servant so he can transfer a study to a publisher who is not presently conducting one. Help Them to Meetings and Memorial

  • 4 Since March is Memorial month, it is most fitting that we concentrate this month on aiding all interested persons to get to the meetings. How can we really expect a new one to be zealous in the ministry if he does not attend meetings? Only by going to the meetings can new ones be spiritually alive and so want to conduct themselves worthily of the good news. Servants and others will call on all inactive and irregular publishers and those who need encouragement. Help them from the outset of the month, inviting them to attend congregation meetings. Put forth special effort to invite them to Memorial on Saturday, March 28.

Then help them to keep on coming to meetings by encouraging them not to miss the stimulating special talk on April 5, “What Will ‘God’s Kingdom Come’ Mean to You?” Especially do we want interested ones to attend the service meetings during the month so they will know what they can do to share the good news with others during March and April. All of us who regularly attend meetings will want to help others attend also so they will be anxious to share in the ministry and be spiritually alive in April.

  • 5 So it would be well for all publishers to think of interested persons to invite during March, encouraging them to attend Memorial and the special talk, as well as the other meetings. To help us, the accounts servant will list on pieces of paper the names and addresses of all subscribers shown on the Subscription Record form since January 1963, giving the names to the publishers who obtained the subscriptions. In addition to subscribers, there are undoubtedly many others we would like to invite to the regular meetings and Memorial and the special talk.

  • 6 In harmony with our special goal of helping all to attend meetings, there is something we should give serious thought to: Can we help others with transportation? Let us think now of those interested persons or brothers who do not have autos or easy access to public transportation systems. Are some unable to get to meetings simply because they live on the outskirts of the city? Are there elderly persons who find it difficult to use public transportation? Be alert to give help. It will take some of our time, yes, but is not this display of love in providing transportation conduct worthy of the good news? Indeed it is!

Shoulder Your Responsibility —As a Young Publisher

  • 1 Are you one of the more than 65,000 publishers in the United States under fifteen years of age? How happy we older ones are to be associated with you in the New World society I How marvelous it is to see your devotion, your zeal, your good example!

  • 2 One of the big problems that confronts youthful ministers is: How can I be regular in the service, meet the goals set by the Society and still keep up with my schoolwork? The answer lies in having a schedule for weekly service. Many of our younger publishers have been aided by their parents in this respect. And what splendid examples in zeal and faith there are to copy! Think of Samuel, Jeremiah and Jesus, to mention just a few. (1 Sam. 2:18, 19, 26; Jer. 1: 4-10; Luke 2: 40-50) What made these youths outstanding? The fact that they listened to the counsel of Jehovah and paid more than the usual attention to it.

  • 3 A good way to be introduced to the ministry is in the magazine work. Many youthful publishers are placing three or more magazines each week and are reaching the goal of twelve magazines per month. Some set aside an hour or so each week to engage in the magazine work after* school hours, under the direction of a mature brother. Have you tried this yet? Perhaps overseers can make such arrangements in the congregations, if advisable. After training is received in magazine work, help can be obtained in giving the simplified sermons. From this point it is only a small step ahead to give the full sermon.

  • 4 And what about conducting.home Bible studies? An ever-increasing number of our youthful publishers are finding it possible, with good parental training, to conduct studies with their schoolmates in the Paradise book, and some have been aided to get the truth in this manner. You younger publishers, are you responding to all the fine training offered by your parents? Also, do you have vacation pioneering as your goal from time to time as well as advancing on to the regular pioneer work, missionary work or Bethel service?

  • 5 How true the statement is, “Train up a boy according to the way for him; even when he grows old he will not turn aside from it.” (Prov. 22:6) In this connection, the Society encourages all you young ministers to fortify yourselves for the future by accepting God-given responsibilities now, and may Jehovah bless you richly for this.

WHAT HAPPENED HERE IN JANUARY?

The second-highest number reporting for this service year! That is our fine January report. It was indeed good to see 290,488 share in the preaching work. While that is fewer than the previous month, our drop in publishers from December to January, was only 6,519, whereas last year it was 13,585. All publishers and pioneers are to be commended for continuing to help ones who shared in December service and all will want to continue the fine work. Speaking of pioneers, we are pleased to let you know that another new peak was reached in January, the eighteenth month in a row!

As for subscriptions, it was the best January on record, with 128,783 reported, to compare with 116,247 last January. In addition to getting subscriptions, many new Bible studies are being started. We increased from 218,449 in December to 224,948 in January. Very fine, indeed! Keep up the excellent ministry, brothers, as we •unitedly continue to find, feed and tenderly care for the "other sheep.”
