Shoulder Your Responsibility -—As a Family Head

  • 1 It has been said that good families make good congregations. How vital it is, then, to shoulder your responsibility as a family head. As such you have assigned to you the privilege and responsibility of leading your wife and children in true worship. How are you discharging this responsibility?

  • 2 As a family head, are you taking the lead in arranging for a family home Bible study? Is this conducted at least once a week so that the entire family is spiritually built up? Does your schedule also include time for group study through attendance at all the congregation meetings?

  • 3 Essential too is family prayer. Have you taken time to explain the purpose and value of prayer to your family? Set the example at mealtimes, at meetings and in the evening-before retiring.

  • 4 Regular weekly field service for each member of the family is also of great importance. This calls for good family organization. First, patiently teach your children simplified sermons, how to do magazine work, and then let them gain the joy of advancing to the point of being teachers of others. Arrange a family schedule of field service to put into practice the things they have learned. Take them along with you in all features of the ministry. Your own leadership will provide them with an incentive to serve Jehovah. Regular weekly service, for at least two and a half hours a week, will see the entire family reaching the ten-hour goal.

  • 5 Recently two line theocratic families of four each reported on the excellent improvement they made in their activity after working out a family schedule and sticking to it. Both families nearly doubled their average hours. In one family each member averaged 15.8 magazines before and 30.5 after. In the other family, back-calls increased from 2.6 to 6.8. True, they were both doing well before, but what a fine increase after making a schedule! In the first six weeks these two families started nine new studies. One of the sisters reported that, even with the increased activity, with the schedule she has more time than ever before.

  • 6 Even though no children are involved, you husbands should lead your wives in serving Jehovah. This responsibility is enjoined upon you when you become “one flesh” through marriage. Wisely accept and discharge this responsibility for which you are accountable to Jehovah.
