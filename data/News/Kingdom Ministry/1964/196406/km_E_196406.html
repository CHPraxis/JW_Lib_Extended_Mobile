Shoulder Your Responsibility as a New Publisher

  • 1 Did your career as a publisher of the good news commence recently, possibly in April? Your fellow publishers welcome you warmly into the ranks of active praisers of Jehovah’s name. Now that you have taken this initial but very important step, what will your course be during the months to come? One of constant progress, assuredly, shouldering responsibility even though you may have many things to learn as a new publisher.

  • 2 First of all, learn to appreciate that you are not alone in this grand work. You have many sources of help. Reliance should be placed in Jehovah and not in self, as he points out at Zechariah 4:6, “Not by a military force, nor by power, but by my spirit.” In addition to this help, the one who has been conducting the study with you will be happy to offer assistance.

  • 3 Taking in spiritual food is an unending process and is specially vital now at this point in your preaching career. Do not recommend to your teacher that the home Bible study that has been conducted with you be stopped just because you have started in the field activity and are attending congregation meetings. Continue the study and seek to improve your own personal study habits too. You should also read and study The Watchtower and Awake! regularly along with the Society’s other publications.

  • 4 Association with other active servants of Jehovah is likewise essential. This means that you will want to attend all the meetings, preparing well for them and then participating in them.

  • 5 Having started to serve actively in the field, you will want to preach and teach regularly from this point on, having some share in the service every week, if possible. Mature publishers have a schedule to guide them, and a weekly one is best. Schedules can be discussed with one of the mature brothers.

  • 6 As a new publisher you will be greatly blessed as you shoulder your responsibility in serving God, keeping in mind that this is now a part of your worship.
