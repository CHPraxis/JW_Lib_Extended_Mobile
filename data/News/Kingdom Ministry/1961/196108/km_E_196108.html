Maintain Peace to Bear Fruit

  • 1 Unlike the old world, true Christians are not divided by nationalism, racial strife, social distinctions or family squabbles. Having Jehovah as their teacher, they are at peace. They earnestly endeavor to apply Bible principles so as to maintain peace within the Christian congregation, and as witnesses for Jehovah they are “making peace to be heard.” —Isa. 54:13; 52: 7.

  • 2 Congregations that are participating in unassigned-territory work can reflect the fruitage of peace by good organization and wholehearted co-operation. There is much to be done, and all will want to spend as much time as possible in this service so that an extensive witness can be given. When interest is found, lovingly take time to demonstrate how to study the publications with the Bible. If at all possible, call back to continue the study. If the distance is too great, arrange for qualified publishers—perhaps some who are infirm—to correspond with these newly interested ones.

  • 3 If there is no unassigned territory within reach of your congregation, servants will analyze the congregation’s territory and concentrate efforts on the least-worked sections. Emphasize calling on not-at-homes and following up on all interest, to assure thorough coverage of the assignment. See to it that everyone in your territory hears the message of peace.

  • 4 All can help to maintain peace by showing consideration as to service arrangements. Out of thoughtfulness for the others in your service group, as well as for the sake of receiving the counsel needed for effectiveness in your own ministry, make it a habit to be on time at meetings for field service. After the meeting is dismissed, conserve time by leaving promptly for the territory. If you are in charge of a service group, be sure that each one has enough territory to work during the entire period he plans to engage in house-to-house work so no time is lost in waiting for another assignment. The most can usually be accomplished if each one works alone at the doors, unless training is being given. Show your Christian maturity by being willing to do this. Yes, all can contribute to peaceable, efficient service arrangements.

  • 5 Through his organization, Jehovah has marvelously equipped us for the ministry. The sermon “Does God Really Care?” enables us to direct the attention of interested ones to His loving provisions for a peaceful new world. Use it, and others as appropriate. Patiently help those you meet to understand God’s way. Even if they are not interested, maintain peace by being kind, demonstrating both by words and deeds that the message really is one of peace. Be a Teacher at Meetings for Field Service

  • 1 Jehovah’s servants are always alert to ‘buy out the opportune time’ to teach. A golden opportunity for instructing our brothers is at the meetings for field service. In a year a regular Kingdom publisher spends many hours at these meetings. Since what is learned there can be immediately passed on to many others in the territory, what an appropriate time it is to teach!—Eph. 5:16.

  • 2 This grand opportunity to teach can so easily be lost by lack of preparation. So whet the appetite of your brothers in advance. Let them know what they can do to prepare. Encourage them to be there and on time by showing them that this is an opportunity provided by Jehovah to be taught by Him. When they arrive, give them a warm welcome.

  • 3 The possibilities for teaching at meetings for service are almost limitless. Publishers assigned well in advance may demonstrate the current sermon. Where weaknesses have been noted in these sermons, demonstrations may be arranged and presented so as to emphasize how improvement can be made. Objections commonly met in your territory should be considered. The various essentials for effective sermons found in the September, 1957, to November, 1958, issues of Kingdom Ministry may be discussed. A week or two might be spent on each essential. Enthusiasm on the part of the one in charge and a variety of approaches that draw all the publishers into the discussion are required for stimulating and instructive meetings for service.

  • 4 At meetings for magazine service the essentials for good magazine presentations found in the April, 1960, Kingdom Ministry may be reviewed. Talking points from the current magazines may also be discussed, and demonstrations prepared in advance may be used to illustrate how these can be used in the field. Experiences that illustrate a good point or principle of presentation might be shared.

  • 5 A morning meeting for service should begin with a brief discussion of the daily text, drawing out comments that apply the text to the circumstances and ministry of those in the group, and before departing for the field service Jehovah’s direction should be petitioned. The entire morning program should not exceed fifteen minutes. Meetings for service at other times should be cut considerably shorter by dispensing with the discussion of the text.

CONGREGATION ORGANIZATION


Ordering Magazines

  • 1 Some congregations find it difficult to maintain an ample, but not excessive, supply of magazines. When too few are ordered, the publishers do not have enough. At other times the magazines accumulate and the account with the Society becomes delinquent. How should the order for distributors’ copies be made up?

  • 2 Rather than simply estimating the congregation’s magazine requirements, the magazine-territory servant should check with each publisher and pioneer to ascertain their regular needs. Perhaps a publisher wants four copies of each issue of The Watchtower and four copies of each issue of Awake!; the servant will make a note of the publisher’s, name and the quantity of each issue desired. To determine how many magazines the congregation should order, simply add up the individual orders submitted. For ordering, the congregation servant should use the magazine Distributors’ Order blank; ordering, if possible, each magazine in tens for orders of thirty or more.

  • 3 Each publisher should recognize that when he places an order for a regular- supply of magazines, those magazines become his responsibility, not that of the congregation. If you still have on hand magazines of previous issues, do not tell the magazineterritory servant that you do not want the new supply; they are yours, obtained at your request. Likewise, if you are going to be away from the congregation for a week or for several weeks, perhaps on a trip, remember that your magazines will continue to come through for you at the Kingdom Hall, just as other mail accumulates at your home. And even if you should move away on short notice, accept the responsibility for your supply of magazines until such time as the cancellation of your order goes into effect with the Society.

  • 4 In order to place all the magazines you order, a schedule is important. If you have a specific time set aside for magazine work and share in it regularly, if you always offer the magazines when the regular literature offer is not accepted, if you are using them on your back-calls and in incidental witnessing, the magazines will not accumulate. Even if you should not be able to share in the service as fully as usual for a short time owing to unforeseen circumstances, resourcefulness on your part will make it possible to place your magazines. Offer them to those who call at your home. Look at the titles of the articles and consider who there is in your community that would be particularly interested in those articles and make an effort to get the magazines to those persons. If, after considering your own possibilities for placing the magazines, you feel that it still will not be possible to use all of a particular issue, while they are still current tell the magazineterritory servant that you have extras, and then if a new publisher who does not yet have an order asks for magazines or some other publisher finds that he is placing more than usual of that particular issue and asks for more, the magazine-territory servant can suggest that he see you to get copies.

3 On certain occasions one knows that he will be able to use an extra supply of magazines; for example, to vacation pioneer, for special activity during the week of the circuit servant’s visit and on worldly holidays, also when one of the magazines carries advance notice of an article that will go well in a particular territory. In these cases place an order with the magazine-territory servant as early as possible for extra copies of the particular issue or Issues that you will need, and the congregation can place a special order with the Society for these issues without changing your regular order for each issue. It is very important that all special orders and changes in order be handled promptly by the servants.

  • 6 If for some reason you find that older magazines have accumulated, do not destroy them; they contain information that can lead persons to life. While we distribute only magazines of recent issue from house to house, some have had excellent results in visiting hospitals, city, county, or state poor farms and old-age homes and, as ministers, with the permission of the ones in charge, talking to the patients and leaving free copies of these upbuilding Bible journals with those who will read them.

  • 7 A tremendous witness is being given through the use of the magazines. Plan carefully to be sure that you have the supplies that are needed and that all magazines received are used effectively in the ministry.
