Your Letterhead

A number of congregations have been printing letterheads, and they may do so if they think it necessary. Some congregations have printed letterheads with just the words “New-World Society.” This is meaningless, as it does not connect the Society with Jehovah’s witnesses. It could be any kind of a society. It is suggested that, if congregations are going to print letterheads for their use, a more explicit form be followed. For example, it could read: “New York, N.Y., Congregation of Jehovah’s Witnesses, Brooklyn Heights Unit.” Then on the right-hand side of the letterhead it could have “Kingdom Hall, 136 Columbia Heights, Brooklyn 1, New York.” On the lefthand side the meeting times could be shown, the hour and the day.

Letterheads printed in this manner advertise the name of the congregation and Jehovah’s witnesses, as well as directing people to the Kingdom Hall. This has meaning and identification.

Continue to Help New Ones

  • 1 New publishers that have been helped in April to go out in the service should not be forgotten nor neglected during May. Even though they have been aided in the field once or twice, they are still very new and need much training and encouragement before they become sturdy, regular, mature ministers of God. Our desire should be to build these new ones up until they “all attain to the oneness in the faith and in the accurate knowledge of the Son of God.” We do not want them to remain babes, being tossed about by doubts and tricked by crafty men. See, then, that they grow up strong in faith, rich in love.

  • 2 For over three years Jesus taught and trained his apostles and disciples how to carry on the ministry. It had its effect, because after Pentecost people recognized by the apostles’ outspokenness “that they used to be with Jesus.” We should desire that our training be as thorough. Our heart should be set on developing ministers of quality, publishers that are efficient in every feature of the ministry. This takes time—sometimes many months—depending on the ability, appreciation and zeal of the new one. If we conscientiously assume our responsibility as teachers and preachers, the new publishers generally come along and do their part. So let us help them grow to Christian maturity through love.—Acts 4:13; Eph. 4:16.

  • 3 What Is the best way to aid the new publisher to maturity? By gradually increasing his theocratic privileges and responsibilities. If he started to serve in the magazine work, keep up with that feature of service with him but Increase his privileges by asking him to accompany you in the house-to-house work, then the back-call and the Bible study work. Teach him to give the three- to eight-minute sermon. Train him how to overcome objections and refute falsehoods. See that he continues to study the publications by studying with him.

  • 4 Preparing for the international assembly, no doubt, will keep you busy. But do not let this cramp your service to Jehovah. Just hustle a little more. Stay close to your studies and help them in the field. Let your advancement be manifest to all persons. “Pay constant attention to yourself and to your teaching. Stay by these things, for by doing this you will save both yourself and those who listen to you.”—1 Tim. 4: 15,16.


Essential Qualities of Sermons Part 8: Friendly, Conversational and Direct

1 Jehovah’s people have the most interesting subject in the world—the good news of the Kingdom. However, even though our sermon may have a good theme with excellent material, how does it appeal to the listener? Not only is an intellectual appeal necessary but we need to stir our listener emotionally. Otherwise he will not be moved to action. To achieve this the minister needs to be friendly, conversational and direct.

  • 2 First of all, we need to examine our attitude. Does each home contain potential sheep until you find out otherwise? Then do you get off to a good start with the householder? Avoid undue controversy by starting with a point of common interest. This will gain a listening ear. Further, be friendly disposed toward the people, just as Jesus was. A long face or an unhappy expression has no place with our message. Good news makes one radiate with gladness, thus showing the effect the truth has upon us. Remember- friendliness often begets friendliness and has much persuasive power.

  • 3 To make your sermons appealing never lecture to the people. Converse with them and you will get better results. Conversation, like teaching, is an art. Therefore it must be cultivated. It is sometimes defined as a friendly interchange of views. Hence, do not do all the talking; it is only natural that the householder will want to say something. By being a good listener you may learn just what is needed to put your sermon across. Also, remember to include your companion in the conversation. He should pay attention to what is being said and not appear indifferent or preoccupied. This would detract from the sermon.
