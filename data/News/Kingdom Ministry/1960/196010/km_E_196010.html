Teaching at the Theocratic Ministry School

  • 1 The theocratic ministry school is provided by Jehovah to help us become effective teachers. Taking the lead as a teacher at this meeting is the school servant, who must always strive to help all to develop greater teaching ability.

  • 2 The servant should analyze the teaching ability of each student and constantly seek to improve it. When offering suggestions on overcoming weaknesses, he should be sure that the student understands the counsel given and appreciates its value. As opportunity affords, do more; offer private assistance.

  • 3 Counsel should be designed to stimulate constant progress. However, when a student makes some improvement on a major weakness, it is not always advisable to pass on quickly to other weaknesses. This may slow down progress being made by focusing his attention elsewhere. In some cases it may do more good simply to commend the student for solid improvement, emphasize the value of the good quality he is developing and encourage him to continue to give it attention. The student will get a sense of achievement as he overcomes this weakness. If weaknesses are always highlighted no matter how well he does, he may get discouraged.

  • 4 When counseling, bear in mind the type of talk being delivered. For example, in the first student talk, counsel on qualities and methods employed in talks given from the platform. On the other hand, counsel given on house-to-house sermons and back-call presentations should be designed to develop both speakingability and the teaching techniques used in these features of the service, as discussed in Kingdom Ministry. Students should be encouraged to deliver these sermons just as they would in the field service. In fact, it would be beneficial for them to use their sermons in the field ministry, either from house to house or on back-calls, before and after giving them in the school. In this way the theocratic ministry school will provide the most practical instruction for the ministry.

  • 5 It is good for the school servant to suggest that the students bring along their textbooks to the meeting. It may be helpful to mark important points in the books in preparation for the written review, as well as for use in the service. With both servant and students working to cultivate teaching ability, all can develop greater effectiveness in the ministry.—2 Tim. 2 ; 2.
