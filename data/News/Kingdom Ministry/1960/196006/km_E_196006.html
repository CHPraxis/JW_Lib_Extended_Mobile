Teach New Ones How to Study

  • 1 Progress to maturity is directly affected by one’s diligence in personal study. For that reason we should encourage those with whom we study to prepare their lesson in advance right from the start. Show them that the sincerity of their prayer to God for knowledge of his purposes is demonstrated by their earnestness in studying his Word.

  • 2 In most cases it is necessary to do more than encourage new ones to study; we must teach them how to do it. Show them how you mark your answers, selecting only key words and phrases. Stress the importance of looking up all scriptures cited and ascertaining their relation to what is in the paragraph. Encourage them to reason on the material in the lesson, seeking reasons for the statements made. Their understanding will be enlarged if they learn to think about the relationship of new points learned to truths already known. At the end of any study period they will find it helpful to review.

  • 3 Their progress will be stimulated when they fully appreciate the reason for study. Point out that the acquiring of knowledge is not the only goal toward which they are striving, but that they should have in mind that Christians should be ‘ready always to make a defense before everyone that demands a reason for the hope in them.’ (1 Pet. 3:15) They should also be encouraged to analyze the material studied in relation to their own lives, asking themselves : What bearing might this have on personal decisions I might have to make? What does it call on me to do now?

  • 4 When new ones ask Bible questions, alert publishers often seize this opportunity to help them learn how to study. Instead of simply giving the answer, they may take time to help the new one to find the answer. In this way they teach him how to use a concordance and the indexes in the Society’s publications, and the benefit of the library at the Kingdom Hall.

  • 5 It is important to have a regular schedule for personal study and then to stick to it. At first their schedule may involve simply setting aside a specific time to prepare for the home Bible study. Progressively encourage them to expand that program.

  • 6 Never forget, too, that we need Jehovah’s blessing when we study, and we should pray for it. Point this out to new ones. It will strengthen their heart appreciation of the truth and call forth Jehovah’s blessing upon their efforts.

No Place for Commercialism

It has come to the attention of the Society that some brothers have been taking advantage of their theocratic association to approach brothers and persons of good will to sell their products. As has been brought out in The Watchtower, it is entirely improper for congregation servants, pioneers or publishers to sell or make contacts for selling products at the Kingdom Hall, at book studies or at assemblies. Brothers should not use their position as a servant, a pioneer or as a publisher to call upon other brothers or persons of good will to sell their' products. If a person wishes to engage in selling for a living, it is his personal business, but never should he misuse theocratic contacts and friendship as an outlet for the products he sells. This announcement is to answer the many inquiries received and to put all brothers on guard against these who would inject commercialism into the theocratic organization.
