Pursue Peace in the Love of Life

1 We want other people to enjoy life in God’s new world; so as Christians we pursue peace in a most positive way. How? By “always having plenty to do in the work of the Lord.” With our 10-percent increase in view, December is the month to put forth extra efforts as we “pursue peace with all people.”—1 Cor. 15: 58; Heb. 12:14.

2 What a tremendous help is this month’s offer in aiding new ones to pursue peace with God I Both books are easy to place and so appropriate for starting studies. Here is a letter from a fourteen-year-old boy: “I was baptized Catholic, but for a long time I did not believe what my church taught. I read your book From Paradise Lost to Paradise Regained and have found what I have been searching for. Please tell me where meetings are held -where I live.” How urgent the need to follow up all placements! If we really ‘do our utmost to present ourselves approved to God,’ a Bible study can be arranged. “I’ve been in the truth several years now,” writes one publisher, “and even though having much encouragement to share in Bible study work, failed to do so until recently. After hearing so many fine experiences of different publishers starting studies when placing literature, I decided to try it and am now happy to say—I have my first Bible study. Wish I had tried the suggestion sooner.”

Reaching the 10-Percent Goal

3 Our goal for December is a 10-percent increase, or 255,895 publishers. What can we do to work toward this goal? Let every publisher try to be out in the field ministry by December 4. Those not out by then should be given kind encouragement, as the assistant congregation servant gives a list to all study conductors each week thereafter of ones who have not reported. Study conductors will make definite arrangements to assist all in their

service centers. Publishers not reporting by December 20 should be assisted by the congregation servant or assistant congregation servant. The overseer’s having a meeting with all servants and study conductors the last few days of November will help to get December off to a fine start. Let each congregation’s aim be “100 percent reporting.”

FIRST MEETING IN DECEMBER

Theme: Pursuing Peace in the Love of Life.

5 min: Welcome, text, comments.

10 min: Talk on “Pursuing Peace in the Love of Life.’’ (See “Watchtower,” December 15, 1959, pp. 741-755, and December 1, 1960, p. 733.)

15 min: Question-and-answer discussion of article “Pursue Peace in the Love of Life.”

23 min: “Help All to Preach in December.”

(3 min.) Overseer acting as chairman, talks on importance of zealous share in ministry, so honoring Jehovah and saving ourselves and those who listen to us. If we do this regularly, it is only reasonable to see increase, and our goal for December is 10 percent. He expresses conviction goal will be reached locally. Special effort stimulates all of Jehovah's people. It builds faith, strengthens integrity and enlarges appreciation for service. During December strong, mature publishers put forth even greater effort through helping others; publishers usually putting in only a few hours are activated to put in more time; irregular ones are reacquainted with joys of the ministry and many new ones have their first taste of preaching the Kingdom good news. What practical means will be arranged for all to share in reaching the 10-percent goal in December?

(8 min.) Assistant congregation servant first outlines the potential congregation has for meeting 10-percent goal. Reveals number of Publisher’s cards in the file. Zealousness on the part of all of these required if goal to be met. Each week he will give lists of publishers not out in field service to study conductors. They will follow through by themselves aiding some of these to share in the field service and by assigning mature publishers to assist others. Next he discusses with audience what they can individually do to share in the field service and help others. They variously express themselves on supporting meetings for field service, adjusting their personal schedule to include more time for field service, gladly assisting others as assigned, etc. Some with health problems can use telephone, write letters, oi’ spend few minutes each week in regular feature of service, so accumulating hours of service. Every publisher can be active in December.

(9 min.) Bible study servant discusses with audience the need of helping persons with whom they are studying to appreciate the privilege and urgency of sharing in the field service. He will assist by calling on all publishers who have been conducting a study for at least six months and check with them on the possibility of aiding some of these students to go in the field service in December. (In larger congregations study conductors can share in contacting publishers who have had studies six months or more.) What will publishers do to help new ones? Audience responds with suggestions: Be service-minded; the more service-minded one is, the sooner the student will be ready to share in the field service. Make definite arrangements for new ones to go with you. Help them prepare what they will say, such as giving a magazine presentation or reading a scripture in the sermon as you give it at the door. Aid them to prepare a complete sermon; the better prepared they are the sooner they will share in teaching others.

Bible study servant invites all to come to him for help in starting new ones in service.

(3 min.) Overseer concludes with rousing talk outlining program to be followed and showing the great joy to be had by all in fully participating in tremendous December effort for 10-percent goal.

7 min: Concluding comments. (Include comments on “October Report” and slogan on bottom of page 1, based on 1 Thess. 5: 15.)

CHAIRMAN (2 min.) Millions of persons have copies of the “Paradise” book, “Let God Be True” or other Society books, but only a small portion of these have studies in their homes. Many of them have shown a receptive heart condition, but not all are being cared for. We locate many of these as we go from door to door and find people who say: ‘I already have that book.’ Will we give them the help they need or will we be like this publisher?

SCENE I (3 min.) Publisher giving sermon takes out “Paradise” book after Gen. 1: 28 as suggested. Householder states he already has it. Publisher is pleased to learn that, asks if he has been able to read it, and how it was enjoyed. On hearing householder’s reply that not much has been read, publisher encourages him to read it regularly so as to benefit from its wonderful material, then offers “Your Will Be Done on Earth,” which householder also has. Publisher encourages him to be sure to study them to gam valuable information.

CHAIRMAN (3 min.) Calls for audience comments on how effective that was and what could be done. Bring out: If you find someone who has the literature, the interest should be cultivated as diligently as if you placed the literature personally, unless another publisher is currently doing this adequately. Do not neglect caring for persons like this; take positive approach. How can such householders be helped?

SCENE II (5 min.) Same publisher giving sermon takes out “Paradise” book as in Scene I. On being advised householder has it, publisher is pleased to know it, inquires as to how much householder has learned about paradise. Do you think God still purposes that there be a paradise? If so, where? Householder is not clear on the matter, so publisher gives sermon and then endeavors to start the study as outlined in June, 1960, “Kingdom Ministry.”

CHAIRMAN (2 min.) Benefits and values pointed out. Someone in audience asks how publisher would handle this call if householder knew about paradise being restored.

SCENE III (10 min.) Same publisher giving sermon takes out book as in Scene I. On learning householder understands paradise is to be restored to earth, publisher uses Matt. 6:9, 10 to establish that Kingdom we pray for will restore the looked-for paradise. It will end all wickedness, as Ps. 37:10, 11 tells us. Turn to Ps. 37:10, 11 in Bible, then to page 222, par. 11, of “Paradise.” After discussing it, publisher comments how book will help one learn more of paradise being restored and how we can live there. Publisher asks to step in to show householder how book can be effectively used in study of Bible. Householder accepts. Publisher steps in, asks householder to get his copy of “Paradise” and Bible, completes back-call sermon (November “Kingdom Ministry”) and starts study.

CHAIRMAN (3 min.) Be always ready to help sheeplike persons where -ever they are found. On initially placing literature the interest should be cared for to help people grow in knowledge. On encountering those who have been neglected, apply these practical suggestions so studies can be held with them.

7 min: Concluding comments. (Include comments on slogan at bottom of page 4, based on Col. 1: 29.)

Be a Teacher of Children—To Study

1 To be teachers of their children, parents must sincerely desire to obey Jehovah’s command: “Train up a boy according to the way for him.” (Prov. 22:6) From infancy the child should hear the discussion of the day’s text and Yearbook comments. From infancy the child should be able to observe his parents’ example of showing diligence, enthusiasm and joy in study.

  • 2 Parents with young children could read them the Paradise book. Progress on to “Let God Be True” and “This Means Everlasting Life.” When they are old enough to read and look up scriptures, a regular established study in these books will ground them in doctrinal truths and proper moral conduct.

  • 3 Hold to a planned schedule of study. Teach children that, though there is a time for play, there is also a time for study. Make a schedule with reasonable study periods. Stick to the schedule as much as possible. Monday nights might be set aside for family study, as is done at Brooklyn Bethel.

  • 4 Open study periods with prayer so children appreciate they are being taught by Jehovah. Make study periods interesting. Explain difficult portions, using illustrations and rephrasing questions. Teach them the mechanics of study: how to underline, pick out key words, etc. Every child who is old enough to read should have his own textbooks and Bible. Teach the child how to locate scriptures. Have him learn the order of the books of the Bible, perhaps simplifying this by dividing the Bible into several sections.

  • 5 Children should also be encouraged to do private study. The schedule of study could include reading the Kingdom Ministry, preparation of the lesson for the congregation book study and the ministry school. Make written reviews something children look forward to.

  • 6 The congregation can help in teaching young ones to study by encouraging their comments at the congregation book study and the Watchtower study. Do not forget children whose parents are not in the truth. Congregation servants could select a qualified brother who has children giving evidence of being well trained and ask if the brother would like to help the child. The child might be invited to the home of the theocratic family when they have their family study. Be a teacher of children, and ‘from infancy they will know the holy writings which are able to make them

‘ wise for salvation.’—2 Tim. 3:15.

A Good Report from Alaska

From the 1961 'Yearbook it is very gratifying to note the progress made in the state of Alaska, which reports separately to the United States branch. Whereas the United States increased 5 percent in average publishers, Alaska enjoyed a 19-percent increase for the year on the average. During May there was a peak of 312 publishers. The 1959 peak was 271. In this land with the short summers and long, hard winters, each publisher on the average has been able to devote 11.0 hours to the service every month, compared with 9.9 in the lower 48 states. There were other good increases too. Magazines increased from 10.3 to 11.8; back-calls from 3.3 to 3.8 and Bible studies have held up to .7, with September’s report showing .8 per publisher. A fine report for the year 1 Publishers native to Alaska and those who moved there to serve where the need is great have been richly blessed during the year, and we rejoice with them.
