Around the World with Jehovah’s Witnesses

Seeing

“The New World Society in Action’* Part I

The New World society is a going concern in all parts of the earth. To show its true magnitude and to enable all of Jehovah’s witnesses to become better acquainted with functions of the organization Jehovah has built up on earth in these last days, the Society sent the film to all countries possible. What are the early results as shown in the 1955 “Yearbook”?

Without exception all who see it are thrilled and feel as do the brothers in Northern Rhodesia (223) and Argentina (84). They now have a much better appreciation of the New World society and the theocratic organization.

If you were a publisher working with a small congregation and never had an opportunity to attend a large assembly of any kind and had no conception of the work done at headquarters, how would you feel upon seeing the film? Probably as the publishers you read about in Chile feel. (118, 119)

Is seeing the film once sufficient? There is so much to see in it that, as expressed by the Belgium report, “This film can be seen two, three, five or ten times. It never becomes monotonous.” (95, 96) Although the showing of it will not go on indefinitely the Society does wish to arrange for as many brothers and persons of good will as possible to see it. Therefore, it will be shown at all circuit assemblies during the remaining part of the 1955 service year. Do not miss seeing it at your next circuit assembly and be sure to take persons of good will with you. Let them see and hear of Jehovah’s expanding New World society.

Effective Witnessing

From house to house Jehovah’s witnesses faithfully go, using Jehovah’s Word skillfully in giving short sermons, in an effort to find and feed the other sheep. Only knowledge and understanding of Jehovah’s purposes will enable people of good will to brace up their minds and join in new-nation activity.

How much more effective is our witnessing when we use the Bible and give sermons? A small town in South Africa was witnessed to over a year ago. The total of book placements was one. And what resulted recently when Bible sermons were used? Not one, but sixty books were placed before the town was completed. (248)

“What else can we say?” was the reply of the occupants of a home in Fiji after listening to an effective sermon. As you use sermons you will find this to be the reaction of many good-will persons. (88)

Sermons are being used world-wide by Jehovah’s witnesses. With interest you will read the reports on Bechuana-land (250) and French Equatorial Africa (76) and see how our brothers are learning how to use sermons in order to become more effective ministers.

Is not a minister expected to give a sermon? Yes, and that is what a householder in Egypt expected on hearing one of Jehovah’s witnesses say he was a minister. He was invited in and the household was called together to listen to his sermon. Such a good sermon was given that the people were deeply impressed. Arrangements were made for return visits. (139, 140)

Are you using the Bible and giving sermons from house to house in order to be most effective in your witnessing?

Working with the Circuit Servant

  • 1 The circuit servant’s visit is something that all congregations look forward to with keen interest. And now that the training program has been instituted his visit becomes even more important to each publisher. Why? Because he will have an opportunity to give personal instruction to the more mature publishers, so they, in turn, can be of greater assistance to the others.—Rom. 15:1,2.

  • 2 Since the circuit servant’s visit comes only twice a year all preliminary arrangements should be directed to the end of receiving the greatest possible benefit during the week's activity. Each day's activity is scheduled. Appointments to work with the publishers are made several weeks in advance in order that all available time set aside for training publishers in the field will be well spent. If one publisher fails to fulfill his appointment with the circuit servant, the time that could have been spent training someone in house-to-house preaching will be lost.

  • 3 Occasionally it is reported that some are hesitant in working with the circuit servant because they do not feel sure of themselves while talking in his presence. This is a natural reaction, just as a speaker has initial nervousness as he begins his public lecture. But this should never cause one to fail in fulfilling one's appointment.

  • 4 Confidently express yourselves in the presence of the circuit servant so that he will be able to observe your presentation of the good news and to offer constructive suggestions for improvement. Then observe his presentation. You will be surprised how much you learn and how quickly any timidity vanishes.

  • 5 Since the appointments for the circuit servant are made ahead of time by the congregation servant, arrange your affairs so that when the time comes for you to work with him you can enjoy this profitable period of training. Under his direction the circuit servant’s wife will work with various publishers that attend contact points. With all cooperating much will be accomplished in “building up the holy ones for ministerial work.”
