“All Nations Advance” Testimony Period Final Month of 1948 Watchtower Campaign


Stress The Watchtower;

Work for Peak of Publishers

Theocracy’s advance is on among the nations! The irresistible onward march of true worship spreads over the earth. With trustworthy assurance that “lie [Jehovah] it is that shall tread down our enemies”, unparalleled numbers of Jehovah's fearless ministers in all nations ‘do valiantly through God’, advancing knowledge and instruction in the interests of the Kingdom. Such advance-minded publishers cherish the opportunity to share in Theocratic progress, and rejoice at the prospects of advancement afforded all publishers of truth in the “Axl Nations Advance” Testimony Period in April.—Psalm 60-: 12.

Advance the “Watchtower” Campaign

April marks the final month of the 1948 Watchtower campaign, during which a year’s subscription for The Watchtower for one dollar, with a premium of eight booklets, is the offer. Older booklets included in combinations should not discourage booklet distribution, for much timely information is to be had in all such publications. During April every truth-advancer will put forth an added effort to forward the Watchtower campaign. Why? A count of subscriptions for the first two months reveals that we are 10,000 subscriptions behind last year, or 5,000 fewer subscriptions per month have 'been obtained this year as compared with 1947. True, severe winter months hampered campaign activity. But the most inclement weather has passed. Therefore, in April we shall make every endeavor to regain the loss incurred. If some publishers feel they can effectively present the Watchtower and Awake! subscriptions together, they are at liberty to do so. With additional effort we can meet the 300,000 subscription quota and give the 1948 Watchtower campaign a fitting climax.

By April many subscriptions that were obtained in 1947 will have expired. Necessarily, the “All Nations Advance” will include following up these expired subscriptions, as well as new ones, and encouraging home Watchtower studies. Back-call servants should make certain that expiration slips received from the Society are cared for immediately. It is not our desire to simply obtain 300,000 new subscriptions, but we hope to have subscribers who have been on the list for the past few years continue Theocratic advance by renewing their Watchtower subscriptions.

Advance the Peak of Publishers

Indeed, a new peak in number of publishers is in keeping with the April advance In all nations. Doubtless, new publishers advancing to active field service during the impetus-giving Memorial week-end in March will desire to continue their Kingdom service. These should be heartily encouraged to extend their activity into April, which can be the biggest month of the year for new publishers. In doing this, servants and regular publishers will co-operate and arrange to offer needed assistance to new publishers, as well as the irregular and inactive. The “All Nations Advance” Testimony Period will be an excellent time to invite good-will interest to progress to public truth-advancing. Tactfully invite them to accompany you in the service work and then aid them in every way possible to continue therein.

Another avenue of advance, which will aid in effecting an all-time high in number of publishers, is the new public meeting series. Together, all will enthusiastically support the new 1948 series of public talks by thoroughly advertising the lectures in the field, attending the meeting and welcoming the stranger. If your company has not already done so, arrange to start the interesting and timely new series of public talks in April.

Further, April is a splendid month to take the forward step to pioneer service. On hearing the new pioneer arrangement outlined by the Society’s president at the Philadelphia convention, many advance-minded publishers joyfully looked forward to spring, when their affairs could be set in order and they could enter the full-time service. Well, April is here! And the Society feels that many expansion-minded ministers, having prayerfully considered their personal pioneer letters, will find it possible to enter the full-time ranks, contributing toward the 10-percent goal, and a hoped-for peak of pioneers in this country. Moreover, those so doing will certainly do their share in reaching a new high in number of company publishers in April. How? Brethren advancing to the pioneer ranks will naturally devote more time to Kingdom service, and hence will be able to assist more individuals in entering field activity. Pioneers can also be a great help in aiding weak publishers in the company.

Let each one of us, therefore, assume a direct interest in a new peak in number of publishers; plan and work toward that end in your company, advancing beyond your company’s present all-time high.

April is superabounding with opportunities to advance Theocratically. In all nations Jehovah’s servants unitedly join in Theocracy’s onward march during the concluding month of the Watchtoicer campaign, the “All Nations Advance” Testimony Period.

Remember Pioneering

Study Third Personal Pioneer Letter

All consecrated witnesses recognize the desirability of devoting full time to the service of Jehovah. Consequently their thoughts dwell upon their own personal circumstances and each day some find it possible to take the forward step into the pioneer service. Even so, the Society feels the same responsibility that Peter did when he wrote, "Wherefore, I will not be negligent to put you always in remembrance of these things, though ye know them.” It is to put you in remembrance of things already known that a third letter of invitation to pioneer service is being sent to each publisher with this Informant. As you individually read it, may it bring to mind the practicability of the pioneer work and possible ways for you to engage therein. It will also be considered by the entire company as were the previous letters.

Spirited discussion awakens new ideas and instills determination in the servant whose circumstances make pioneering possible. Accordingly, in addition to the discussion of the third letter during one service meeting, another service meeting during April should stress the privilege of pioneering. During that meeting, outstanding points from all three pioneer letters can be considered, either by short talks, discussions between two or more individuals, questions and answers, or by demonstrations. Include experiences on pioneering also. These can be personal experiences of local pioneers or some taken from The Watchtower and the 1948 Yearbook. By giving careful thought and diligence in preparation, it should be possible to have a very interesting and helpful service meeting program based on the pioneer work. Perhaps, through this medium, publishers from your company will be aided to advance into the pioneer ministry.

Vacation Pioneers

There is no doubt that the coming of spring will witness an encouraging increase in the number of pioneers. During the summer months, working side by side with these regular pioneers, will be the vacation pioneers, boys and girls who wisely recognize that their early years can most profitably be spent serving their Creator, and adults whose vacation periods permit expanded service. Now is the time for you to start making plans so that when vacation comes there will be no delay. Mature publishers can do much to encourage the young folks and help them get started on a sound basis. Vacation pioneering might also be given some attention in the pioneer service meeting.

All publishers, then, whether old or young, remember the pioneer service. Plan with faith, strive constantly to remedy those things that hinder, and, above all, look to Jehovah for his aid that you may attain to this blessed service.

District Assembly

Atlanta Assemblers Richly Blessed

From ’Wheeling, West Virginia, on the north to Key West, Florida, on the south, and from western Kentucky to the Atlantic ocean, witnesses streamed to the focal point of Atlanta, Georgia, March 12-14, for the first of a series of district assemblies in 1948.

The assembly was held in the spacious Municipal Auditorium. The entire main floor was filled for the opening meetings Friday morning by publishers desirous of reaping full benefits from the assembly. Over 5,000 were present Saturday, and attendance for the public meeting Sunday swelled to 6,665 as people of good-will from Atlanta joined with Jehovah’s witnesses to hear Brother Knorr deliver “The Kingdom Hope of All Mankind”.

From the opening words of the chairman Friday afternoon to Brother Knorr’s concluding remarks Sunday evening, the program was brimful with choice food from the Lord, each portion bringing something new and strengthening as it was presented by the president or other representatives of the Society. The audience gave rapt attention throughout, showing their appreciation with repeated applause.

Practical Instruction

Throughout all sessions notebooks had been very much in evidence as the interested listeners recorded information from the talks, demonstrations and experiences for use by themselves and those at home who had been unable to attend.

The district assembly, in addition to proving most profitable to the publishers, gave an effective witness to the friendly city of Atlanta, one newspaper reporting, "Jehovah’s witnesses Tranquil in a War-torn World.” Happy smiles and joyful voices gave evidence of keen appreciation of the table Jehovah had spread for them. Many expressions such as these were heard: “It was the most practical assembly I have ever attended.” “This is just what our company needed.” Not least among the joyful events were the thrilling experiences of pioneers. The innumerable ways that the Lord provides for these faithful servants to continue in the full-time service was very inspiring.

Yes, Jehovah had gathered his people for a purpose and they had drunk deeply of the refreshing spiritual truths and practical instructions. As they departed for their homes, whether in populous cities, villages or secluded mountain regions, all felt better equipped and anxious to get back to the field to put into practice those things they had learned. Similar provisions await the brethren in other sections as conventions for their districts are held. Plan now to be among them.

“The Tongue of the Wise Is Health” Starting Book Studies to Be Featured During May


Diligence in Every Good Work


'‘Let God Be True” and The Joy of All the People to Be Offered

In spite of all the efforts of men to gain health and life, the human race continues on its downward course. Why? Because they ignore the only means of health and life, a knowledge of Jehovah and his Son, Christ Jesus. Those who have this knowledge and are wise will impart it to others, even as the text for the month of May states: “The tongue of the wise is health. The lip of truth shall be established for ever: but a lying tongue is but for a moment.”—Prov. 12:18,19.

For the tongue to be health, what we say, though the truth, is not enough. How we say it is also important ; our speech must be in accordance with the wisdom that comes from above. It should be friendly, gracious, kind, judicious and persuasive. (See Proverbs 16: 21,23, Moffatt.') We should try to develop that sensitive mental discernment, Theocratic tactfulness, which helps to analyze each situation, and then adjust our speech accordingly. And having struck a spark of interest, hold it by conversing on the level of the people. Remember, they know nothing of the truth, and therefore if they are to grasp what we have to say we must proceed simply and slowly.

Starting Book Studies

In many companies less than a third of the publishers conduct book studies. To improve this situation the starting of book studies will be featured during May. Usually, before we can start a book study we must first place the book with the people, and therefore during the month of May the wise will use their tongues in presenting the book "Let God Be True", together with the booklet The Joy of All the People, on a contribution of thirty-five cents. If unable to place the bound book make a special effort to leave the booklet on their promise to read it. if they are unable to contribute. Every home should have a copy of this joyful and timely message. Should there be some who promised to subscribe for The Watchtower or Awake! during April, but were unable to do so then, you may still give them the benefit of the special offer of the year’s subscription and eight booklets on a contribution of one dollar during May. However, for all regular house-to-house activity the bound book and booklet will be presented.

Having found interest, be sure to make a note of it on your house-to-house record, and then call back so as to give further health-giving information. During May there will also be much back-call work done in connection with those who subscribed during the Watchtower campaign, as well as with those who subscribed a year ago and have allowed their subscription to expire. These expiration slips are now being sent to the companies. Regardless of the occasion of the back-call, be prepared and equipped to discuss a specific subject with them, one in which you have reason to believe they will be interested.

All back-call activity should be engaged in with the goal in mind of starting a book study, for book studies result in publishers. The wise will be tactful in this also. Some are eager to learn. With these a book study can be started on the first or second back-call. Others have only a slight interest, which must first be fanned into a flame before they will appreciate the need of regular study.

Having started a book study, the tongue of the wise will be careful to use tact when difficult situations arise. When an error is stated dogmatically, Instead of flatly contradicting, pose a question, such as: “If that be true, I wonder what the apostle Paul meant when he said ‘Let no man judge you regarding the sabbath’? Such judicious speech will go far in giving health to the hearers.

Other opportunities afforded the wise to let their tongues be health come in calling out appropriate slogans in the street magazine work, extending cheery invitations to passers-by, together with the handbills, giving a hearty welcome to the stranger that comes to hear the talk, and, for some, the added privilege of giving the public talks.

During the month of May, and at all times, let us show our love for Jehovah, for righteousness, for life and for our neighbor by using our tongues wisely in every feature of Kingdom service. Thereby our tongues will be health not only to others, but also to ourselves, for we shall realize the promise that “the lip of truth shall be established for ever”.

The Kingdom Hall

A Clean Place of Pure Worship

More than anything else the people of today need a knowledge of the Kingdom. To partly fill this need, Kingdom Halls have been established in cities and towns from coast to coast. It is at the Kingdom Hall where true ministers, young and old, are thoroughly equipped to expand the Kingdom witness to the ends of their territory. It is at the Kingdom Hall, and not any imposing religious edifice, where good-will persons may assemble with a Christian congregation to learn the way to life under the Kingdom. Indeed, the Kingdom Hall, regardless of size or location, assumes its proper place as the most important structure in the community ; it is directly related to the peace, freedom and eternal life-interests of the people.

In their Kingdom ministry Jehovah’s witnesses deport themselves always in keeping with the Scriptural admonition to be clean in speech, habits and dress. Necessarily, this trait should be reflected in the appearance of their Kingdom Halls. The very name “Kingdom Hall”, in fact, denotes a clean place of pure worship. Does your Kingdom Hall live up to its name? Is it truly clean at all times, comfortable and inviting to publishers, new interest, and the public? What is the impression your Kingdom Hall will make upon a truth-seeker visiting the company for the first time? Surely every company values highly its Kingdom Hall, whether owned or rented, as a provision intended to honor Jehovah's name and bring much benefit to all who enter its doors.

All Features of Service Bring Blessings

There is no boredom in serving Jehovah. Even as he has provided an infinite variety in his inanimate creation, he places a well-balanced, varied service program in the hands of his witnesses, each of which fills a definite need. Diligent servants, upon learning of their service charge, recall the words of the wise man who said, “Whatsoever thy hand findeth to do, do it with thy might.” (Eccl. 9:10) They wisely refrain from slighting any feature of the service.

Our Leader, Christ Jesus, set an excellent example for us in this respect. “Whatsoever” avenue of preaching opened itself to him he seized readily and performed with his might. He took part in the house-to-house work, made back-calls, instructed small groups in the privacy of their homes, shared in public meeting activity and was found magnifying Jehovah's kingdom in the streets and market places. Because he did so he conveyed blessings to persons in all walks of life and was richly rewarded by his Father. We do well to thus participate in every feature of the service, and this we can do through Christ.
