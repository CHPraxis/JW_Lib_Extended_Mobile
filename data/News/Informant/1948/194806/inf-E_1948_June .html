BROOKLYN, N. Y.


“Neighbor Love” Testimony Period


Current Book-and-Booklet Offer Continued


Stress Back-Call and Public Meeting Activity

Love for the Provider of the hope of everlasting life and its attendant blessings prompts sincere persons to learn God’s righteous requirements, and, having learned, to act in harmony therewith. To faithful servants, then, God’s command to ‘‘love thy neighbour as thyself” (Lev. 19:18) is full of meaning. During the month of June, which has been set aside as ‘‘Neighbob Love” Testimony Period, they will make special effort to show love for their neighbors by bringing them God’s truths.

One way to show love for our neighbor in June is by participating in the distribution of “Let God Be True" and The Joy of All the People, on a contribution of 35c. Were we not overjoyed and moved to praise our Creator when we first read these two remarkable publications? Could we honestly say, then, that we love our neighbor as much as ourselves if we fail to present these truth-containing publications to him?

But the showing of love is not fulfilled by simply appearing at the door of the neighbor with the publications. Our presentation of the message should be enthusiastic and motivated by a real concern for- the welfare of the hearer. He undoubtedly has many problems in these days of distress. The answer to every such problem is found in the Bible, and the book “Let God Be True” can assist the inquiring one to find the answer quickly. We can be of greatest assistance to our neighbor by endeavoring to consider his point of view and by being kind and tactful. Then problems once seeming big to him become of little consequence in new-found knowledge of the truth.

Knowledge of the contents of “Let God Be True" and The Joy of All the People will enable the publisher to stress the vital points and accomplish the most in the usually brief time that is available when calling from door to door. Call attention to these main points by referring to the table of contents, questions that you have noted in previous study or the subject and scripture indexes. It would be difficult and time-consuming for the publisher to answer all the questions and objections of the householder without “Let God Be True”. It would be even more difficult for this “neighbor” to gather together this fund of information by himself. It is, therefore, an act of love and unselfishness to get “Let God Be True” and The Joy of All the People into every home possible, and to instruct the obtainers how to use them most effectively.

Show Love by Calling Back

While a study may not result from every placement of “Let God Be True”, let this not be because of our failure to properly show love for our neighbors. Consider each interested person as a prospective study. Diligence will bring heartcheering results. Then, when studies are started, be dependable, calling back regularly, assisting them to advance. Only by so doing can the desired results be expected, that is, an increase in devoted servants of God showing love for their neighbors.

However, these neighbors with whom we are studying will not usually begin to publish without special assistance. Therefore, we must continue to prove that we love our neighbors “as ourselves” by patiently helping them to confess with their mouths, that they may gain life.

So important is the calling back upon our neighbors who have shown ; interest, either by obtaining litera-I ture or otherwise, that a service I meeting in June should be set aside for the consideration of the back-call and book-study work, emphasizing particularly studies in “Let God Be True”. Points that might be included in this meeting are: publisher preparation (such as noting topics the individual is interested in and looking up further information on the subject before calling back), demonstrations on making the initial back-call and converting back-calls into book studies, arrangements to improve local back-call activity, results of consistent back-call and book-study work, experiences, assisting the new ones to publish, etc.

Other Features of the Work Continue

During June, other features of the work will continue to be stressed. We can show love for our neighbors by faithfully participating in the advertising of the public meetings, and then attending ourselves so that we can further assist the people of good-will who attend. Magazine work, as well as the distribution of leaflets on the streets, will now be more effective since the coming warm spring days will bring more people out and the publishers can remain for longer periods of time. Nor should we overlook the advantages and possibilities of outdoor meetings. Many companies have already begun to have outdoor meetings in conjunction with intensified witness in rural districts.

Our desire for life, not only for ourselves, but also for our goodwill neighbor whom we love as ourselves, will motivate us to continued faithful service to our Creator during June, as we bring to a close the two months of distribution of “Let God Be True” and The Joy of All the People and participate fully in the “Neighbor Love” ‘Testimony Period.

Making Remittances

Use New Remittance Form

Included with this Informant is a supply of a new remittance form (S-52), which is to replace the old form, beginning July 1, 1948. To avoid confusion, copies of the old form should be discarded and only the new form used after that date.

Instructions for its proper use appear on the back of the form. It is to be used by both companies and pioneers whenever a remittance is sent to the Society, except when the remittance is for subscriptions only. (Whenever possible pioneers should obtain literature from company stocks and remit to the company at the time of getting literature.) Whenever subscriptions are sent, that remittance should be shown on the Subscription Record sheet (S-10). Do not enter any money on the Subscription Record sheet except that which is for subscriptions. That is, after July 1 the remittance form on the old Subscription Record sheet should not be used to request credits for literature or magazine accounts. If the remittance includes monies for the literature or magazine accounts, the new remittance form should be used and the entire remittance listed on It, including the amount to be applied toward subscriptions, which is also shown on the Subscription Record sheet.

Companies may continue to use their present supply of Order Blanks (S-5), but the remittance form which appears on it will no longer be used. Use the separate new remittance form instead.

The remittance form should be filled out carefully, completely and in duplicate. Write plainly, please. The original is to be sent to the Society with the remittance, and the duplicate copy kept in the company files. Be sure to fill in the date, complete name and address of the company (or pioneer) and the number of the check or money order, in the spaces provided. The moneyorder stub might be pinned to the company’s copy of the remittance form, for a record.

The Society will no longer send credit memos to acknowledge receipt of remittances, nor will remittances be entered at the bottom of the invoice. The Society will henceforth acknowledge receipt of your remittances on the monthly statement only. The company will then have a complete record of credits and debits of its account on one slip, the statement. The remittance will be identified on the statement by the date and by the number of the check or money order, as well as by the amount. Hence, all checks should be numbered and the remittance form dated. When a small slip marked “Duplicate copy of your cash item order” is sent to you with a shipment of cash items, the money you remitted for these cash items will not appear on any statement. Praise Guarantees Salvation


New Booklet, “Permanent Governor of AH Nations,”


to Be Featured

July Suitable for Rural Activity

Jehovah God, the giver of every good and perfect gift, has provided a gracious means whereby men can gain salvation, and that salvation is guaranteed by praise. Hence the psalmist's words resound from our lips: “I will call upon the Loan, who is' worthy to be praised: so shall I be saved from mine enemies.” (Ps. 18:3) Such proper praise is that which upholds the true worship of Jehovah, and thus opens the way for all men of good-will to gain salvation.

How Praise?

During the month of July we will praise Jehovah by going from house to house presenting the book, “The Kingdom Is at Hand”, and the new booklet, Permanent Governor of All Nations, on a contribution of 35c. Most companies will have ordered and received their shipment of this splendid booklet, and, when its distribution begins in July, will be able to include it in every offer. Truly the peoples constantly perplexed by anxiety and unrest need today more than ever before the comforting message of the Kingdom that announces the sure remedy for world distress. Kingdom publishers, then, will review these publications and become familiar with their contents, in order to give a clear presentation of the Kingdom message at the doors. The book, with its effective narrative style, treats the Kingdom subject thoroughly, from the original Edenic promise down to its final fulfillment in the new world. The new booklet can be used to show’ that Jehovah has already provided the King of this new world in his permanent Governor of all nations. His ambassador-publishers will endeavor to leave a copy of the new booklet in every home. At the service meetings, effective demonstrations outlining points the publishers can use in the field will be helpful.

Praise that guarantees salvation includes making back-calls. These should by no means be neglected. Publishers placing the book and booklet in the field should note the interest shown by the recipient and, on the back-call, should endeavor to further his interest. The many bound books that were placed during June provide good ground for book studies. Those not at present conducting a book study should follow up previous placements, and, by diligent effort, have a part in this important work of praise. Experienced study conductors can lend much practical assistance. A book study should be the ultimate goal of each publisher in placing literature.

Do Not Forget the Rurals

Territory previously covered in the Watchtower Campaign and with “Let God Be True” can be covered again with the July offer. But many companies have territory that has not been worked with any of these offers. Concentrate on this territory, especially the rural areas, where, in many cases, the people have not been witnessed to in many months. Servants should take the lead in working this and other territory, that the number of praisers of God’s name may continue to increase.

Summer brings good weather and

opportunity for outdoor public meetings. Jehovah richly blessed the out- j door public meetings last year, and i many people of good-will were aided thereby. Servants should be on the alert to find suitable places to hold these meetings. Thought should be given to the comfort of the people that come. Cool, shady lots with suitable seats should be selected wherever possible. Consideration of the direction of the wind, and providing for sound equipment, if available, will enable the audience to hear better. Good planning and support by the publishers and pioneers are certain to result in Jehovah’s blessing on these meetings.

Rendering praise to Jehovah, Kingdom publishers understand the way to salvation: “And let such as love thy salvation say continually, Let God be magnified.” (Ps. 70:4) During this month we will let God be magnified by rendering praise to him, for continued praise guarantees salvation.

Public Meeting Halls

Where the brethren find it impossible to get adequate facilities or private halls for public meetings, applications can be made to the school boards for permission to hold the public meetings in the school auditoriums. It may be that the authorities will consent to the use of the school facilities on Sunday, when it would not interfere.

In some states the school trustees or boards of education are, by law, required to permit the use of school buildings and school auditoriums for community gatherings. If such is the case, the school may be compelled by law to open up the rooms to Jehovah’s witnesses, if they are used by others for civic purposes or community gatherings. If denied the use of such buildings in states where the law allows the school to be used, communicate with the Society.

Organize Street Witnessing

Co-operation Necessary for

Best Results

The worth-while work being done in the earth today is that related to the reconstruction of true worship. Appreciation of that fact will inspire us with zeal for service in all its various features. However, if our efforts are to be effective and accomplish Jehovah's purpose, our zeal must be accompanied by knowledge and understanding, not only of how to carry on each feature of the work individually, but also of how to work together, co-operating mutually for the best interests of the service.

Among our varied, forms of service to Jehovah is that of street witnessing. The public meeting campaign has resulted in more time being devoted to this form of activity, especially handbill distribution ; but lack of organization in some companies has prevented this added time spent from being as effective as it should be. Organization and co-operation are essential. How, then, can street witnessing be better organized?

Organizing the Work

According to Organization Instructions, the advertising servant is, among other things, responsible for the proper organization of the street witness work, co-operating closely with other servants to see that handbills are properly distributed on street corners and from door to door, as well as for assigning territory for street magazine work. It would be well to indicate not only the location of such assignments but also the best time and the kind of street work to be done. There are certain street corners on which it would be well to have publishers from morning until late at night on Saturdays, one publisher taking over when another leaves.

Both magazine publishers and those distributing handbills can be assigned to the same corner at the same time, co-operating together. By keeping both handbill and magazine distribution going simultaneously, the piling up of unused magazines will be avoided, and it will result in a greater combined witness. Publishers might alternate, engaging in magazine work one week and handbill distribution the next. All should be glad to co-operate with the servants in taking care of assigned territory at designated hours, so that ail time devoted to this work may be most effective.

Having received such an assignment, the publisher should be regular, dependable. When he is engaging in street magazine work he should be alert, stand erect, be friendly and call out appropriate slogans in an appealing manner, giving no suspicion of weariness. He should be quick to note people standing near by and to approach them, giving a brief but effective witness regarding the magazines; and, of course, always be prepared to place the subscription offer. He should also carry a few handbills to give to those manifesting some interest.

Or perhaps he is assigned to distribute handbills. In this activity also he should be alert and show a pleasant friendliness. Additionally, a cheery invitation, perhaps mentioning the subject of the talk, should go with the placing of the handbill. Make them feel that one really wants them to come and that they will be welcome if they do. Such activity will go far in improving the effectiveness of one’s service.

The Kingdom work deserves our best. Let us give it our best by being individually zealous and alert and by mutually co-operating together.
