Keeping Peace by United Praise Book Studies to Phy Important Role


Godliness Means Proper Conduct


“Let God Be True” and Booklet Presented

Neither peace nor unity find their beginning in the praise rendered by this world. Praise is there, to be sure, but praise of men and their varied but imperfect accomplishments ; in fact, praise of almost everything except the Creator, who is worthy to be praised. No wonder they have no peace! However, there is one organization that praises God and seeks to follow the instructions of the apostle, “. . . keep the unity of the spirit in the bond of peace.” (Eph. 4:3) Peace is granted as a reward for this proper praise.

Enjoying the blessings of peace, these servants of God are free to spread peace to others through united praise. During November they will do this by directing the attention of the people to the timely message in the book “Let God Be True” and either the booklet Permanent Governor of All Nations or The Joy of All the People, on a contribution of 35c.

Book Studies Essential

Experience has already proved the practicability of studies, for most new publishers have learned the truth through such studies. Where many studies are conducted the increase in publishers is usually rapid.

The conducting of studies with teachable persons is the individual responsibility of each servant before his master. None should sit back and wait for another to start a study for him, yet those who are more proficient realize that united praise can be effectively demonstrated by helping others to get started in this important field of service. It is another means of proving our integrity and caring for the life interests of our congregation, the meek who live in our territory.

During November, when both a bound book and booklets are being placed, there will be many opportunities to start studies. It is not necessary to wait until a book is placed. Frequently studies can be started in the booklets. They furnish a theme for the publisher and hence serve as an aid to a successful back-call. By being well acquainted with the material, a very interesting and lively discussion can often be carried on. Keep the discussion informal, ask a question, turn to the booklet for the answer, and soon a booklet study may be in operation. A few extra copies of the publication will prove helpful if others are present.

However, since our primary purpose is to start studies in “Let God Be True”, we must be alert for opportunities to switch from the booklet to a book study. Perhaps a point under discussion can be backed up with material from “Let Cod Be True”, thus introducing it and opening the way for continued study.

Share Peace Through United Praise

As soon as a study is started and truths concerning God and his purpose begin to fill the mind of the student, peace replaces former fears, but it is only through uniting with Jehovah’s ministers in active praise that complete enjoyment of peace can be attained. The study is the proper place to start training these new prospective ministers. Let them know of your joys in service; keep them informed of the service program, the public meetings and Watchtower studies; tell of recent assemblies which so clearly illustrate the peace that exists within the organization of Jehovah. The desire to share this peace will motivate many to united praise.

Most, of course, will need a helping hand; an invitation to other studies, special calls to take them to meetings and Introductions to other witnesses. While their forward steps into service must stem from a desire to serve, we can make those steps easier by continued assistance.

If the study attendant will accompany you to another study, help him to participate and later perhaps to assist you in conducting it. They need not be old in the truth to conduct a study. Having attended the one conducted by you they are now equipped to conduct one themselves, for even after only a few lessons they know more about the Bible than most churchgoers.

As the student progresses it may be possible to turn a study over to him, but it would be well for the original conductor to continue to attend from time to time to see that it is being properly cared for, or to further instruct the new conductor.

With the knowledge, then, that all must share in Jehovah’s praise in order to share in his peace, let us give diligence to the starting of book studies with all who show themselves to be teachable, regardless of what, if any, publication they may have obtained. Then, let us make the study a center of Theocratic education where prospective publishers are instructed to assist others to praise Jehovah unitedly until all who live are bound together ‘‘in the unity of the spirit in the bond of peace”,

“Godliness is an everyday way of life. It takes hold on all of life’s relationships, whether one is a father, mother or child, an old person or young, a slave or freeman, an employer or a laborer, an appointed servant ... or just an average member. It tries to copy Jesus Christ . . . It strives to do all things as if they were being done directly to God.”—The Watchtower, September 1, 1948.

Godliness means Theocratic living, 24 hours daily, for the Christian minister. It embraces proper conduct in the field, at company meetings, circuit and district assemblies. It means exemplary behavior in one’s own home, at his place of employment, or wherever his steps may carry him.

In his everyday way of life, the minister will strive to be kind, meek, helpful, clean in habit, dress and speech, applying godly principles. Thus, whether out in the field of ministry or away therefrom, he will be serving God “day and night”, and by his “good works” draw “sheep” to the King and the Kingdom.

Company meetings are vital for our continued life of godliness, with its gains. Attending meetings is important, but attending them on time is also important. Avoid getting into the easily-formed bad habit of coming late to meetings.

Gaining full benefit from meetings will necessitate attentiveness and concentration from start to finish. Distracting practices, such as whispering, candy-eating, etc., should be avoided during meetings. Parental oversight of children is a godly requirement. Needless noise-making and running about should be eliminated. By sitting with children of tender years, parents train them to be attentive and gain benefits from company studies.

Reporting Baptisms

Baptism ceremonies at circuit and district assemblies have proved adequate for the majority of those being baptized in recent years, and those desiring to be immersed are urged to take advantage of this arrangement as far as possible. “Setting the Mark” Testimony Period Work for Peak of Publishers


“Set the Mark” with Book Studies

With tiie complete annihilation of Satan’s abominable world-rule dangerously near, Jehovah’s ministers hasten to obey God’s command: “Go through the midst of the city . . . and set a mark upon the foreheads of the men that sigh and that cry for all the abominations that be done.” (Ezek. 9: 4) No holding back for these ministers now! Rather, ‘through the city’ they go ‘setting the mark’ by preaching the Kingdom message and proclaiming God’s vengeance against this godless generation.

Worldly die-hards set their foreheads like flint against the warning message of his ministers. Nevertheless, there are multitudes of men of good-will toward God that ‘sigh and cry’ and turn with disappointment from this doomed system. Gladly they receive the figurative “mark” of perception and acceptance of Kingdom truth. These are the ones Jehovah’s ministers will seek to assist during “Setting the Mark” Testimony Period, in December, first by presenting the month’s offer “Let God Be True” and one 32-page booklet, preferably Permanent Governor of All Nations or The Joy of All the People, on a 35c contribution.

Reaching a New Peak of Publishers

Not only acceptance of the “mark” of truth, but confession thereof is required. Therefore a new peak of publishers is in keeping with the December Testimony Period. If each company will strive to reach its monthly quota based on a 10-percent increase over last year’s average, a new peak can be reached in December. True, it is a bad month in many parts of the country because of winter's setting in. Still, those having the “mark” will not permit cold weather to chill their burning zeal to witness to the Kingdom.

In order to reach tins new high, all ministers must co-operate in assisting irregular and inactive brethren, as well as new publishers, to "set the mark’. To this end a full month of Theocratic ministry should be arranged by each company. The holiday season provides a splendid opportunity in this respect. Organized house-to-house group witnessing will be arranged. Magazine street work will also be appropriate, for thousands of persons will be engaged in holiday shopping. Also, advertising of public meetings will occupy its rightful place in reaching the peak. See that not one person associated is omitted in the invitation to ‘set the mark’ in December.

Another avenue of increasing the number of publishers locally is through book studies, for from home Bible studies new publishers enter the service. Ministers should not fail to call back on all literature ob-tainers and endeavor to establish a book or booklet study. Additionally, many interested persons with whom publishers have been studying now have the “mark” firmly set in their minds and are themselves prepared for service. But how can the matter of service be presented to book-study interest? Begin laying the groundwork for field activity well in advance. Emphasize points pertaining to service considered in the study; read Watchtower field experiences from time to time and discuss them; and, of course, relate experiences you enjoy in the local territory. Then, wheu the time is ripe, the alert minister can tactfully approach the matter of field service with ease.

“Setting the Mark” Service Meeting

Early in December each company will devote one service meeting to a discussion of reaching a peak of publishers locally. A consideration of the main article in the December Informant will strike the theme of the meeting and can be followed by a practical demonstration on assisting others in the ‘marking’ work. Experiences on aiding book-study interest to enter field service can be related, as well as having new ministers tell what supplied the needed spark that caused them to engage in Kingdom service. Outlining in detail the activity of the company in December will bring to a close the “Setting the Mark” service meeting. Properly planned, prepared and executed, it will give impetus to your efforts to reach a new publisher peak. By each one’s taking advantage of December’s full schedule of Kingdom activity, a peak of publishers can be reached.

Personal Subscriptions

A number of publishers have asked whether they can renew their personal subscriptions for more than one year. This is perfectly all right and in many cases preferable. Renewals for two, three, four or five years save the publisher and the Society the trouble of handling the renewal each year and the expense of sending separate money orders is avoided. Furthermore, the possibility of interruption of magazine service during the process of renewing is eliminated. If the subscription is for more than one year, the number of years should be plainly indicated in the space between “1 year” and “6 months” on the renewal or new subscription slip. If the subscription is to be in a language other than English, show the language at the top right-hand corner of the slip.

It will assist the Society if all the publishers will send tlieir personal new and renewal subscriptions through the company. Care should be used to see that the handwriting is legible and that an accurate mailing address is furnished.
