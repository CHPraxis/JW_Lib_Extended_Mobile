BROOKLYN, N.Y.


Light-Bearers Begin Four-Month Offensive "Enlighten the Eyes of Men’s Hearts’ with The Watchtower


Pioneer Opportunities Expanded

New Arrangement Effective January 1, 1948


World-wide Quota Set at 300,000 Subscriptions

‘‘Keep enlightened the eyes of the heart” (Eph. 1:18,19), is the guiding thought chosen to direct our efforts during January, the first month of our present Watchtower campaign. One good reason for having the eyes of our heart enlightened is that we ‘may know . . . the exceeding greatness of his power toward us who believe’. That exceeding great power has sustained His people, held back their enemies, and now opens up the way for an enthusiastic, world-wide presentation of The Watchtower.

Every servant of Jehovah should heed the Commander’s call to field activity in this four-month offensive, thereby obediently and zealously demonstrating to Jehovah that His power is sufficient to make known the truth to others. Not only will we keep the eyes of oub heart enlightened ; we will take The Watchtower to others that their eyes too may be enlightened to Jehovah’s purposes. Since The Watchtower is available in many other languages in this country at the same subscription rate as the English, there is added incentive for the widest possible distribution.

Favorable Opportunities

The very fact that in these difficult times Jehovah has provided us with sufficient paper to meet our magazine needs is evidence that this truth must go out now. With gratitude therefor, you may order all the magazines you actually need. After studying them yourself, give all in your territory, those whom you otherwise contact, and especially everyone who has shown himself of good-will, a like opportunity for enlightenment. Offer The Watchtower for one year at the subscription rate of one dollar, together with the gift of eight booklets as a premium. Where the people already have The Watchtower, or for other reasons do not take it, then offer Awake!-, or if you believe you can place both together, you may do so. (See December Informant for alternative offers. )

Having engaged in many previous Watchtower campaigns, God's people should now be adept at its presentation. Always offer The Watchtower first, and for the next four months concentrate on getting it into as many homes as possible. Servants can build up enthusiasm for the campaign in all company meetings. Keep the matter before the publishers continually and assist them to effectively present the offer, by showing the best methods in demonstrations and discussions.

Quota

If every publisher in the world obtains at least two subscriptions, we should easily reach the worldwide quota of 300,000 subscriptions during the four-month period. Each company should set as its quota a total of double its quota of publishers. Hence, if a company has a quota of 40 publishers, the four-month quota for subscriptions will be 80. Always try to obtain full-year subscriptions.

All of Jehovah’s people realize that merely obtaining a large number of subscriptions is not the final goal. The Watchtower will not actually benefit the people unless it is studied. If it is studied it will enlighten the eyes of those of goodwill, assisting them to shun the old world and associate with God’s people. Our objective, therefore, is to aid the people in carefully studying and then acting upon the message contained in The Watchtower, for by so doing they can be helped to gain everlasting life from Jehovah.

Circuit Assemblies

We must not overlook the part that the circuit assemblies will have in this campaign. The third coverage of the circuits is now beginning, and the new lecture, “Permanent Governor of All Nations,” will be widely advertised and given. While at these assemblies, keep the presentation of The Watchtower to the fore. The instructive service meetings will high-light the most effective ways of so doing. All subscriptions obtained during circuit assemblies should be turned in through the local company organization where the assembly is held, so that the interest can be followed up and properly cared for.

So, with eyes fixed steadfastly upon the doing of Jehovah’s will, and with a clear understanding of the purpose of our efforts, let us advance together with a unified front in this four-month offensive for enlightenment.

Goal: 10 Percent of Total Publishers Enrolled as Pioneers

Already applications for pioneer service are beginning to come in, accompanied by heartfelt expressions of gratitude and joy. And why shouldn’t they? The hearts of thousands of Jehovah’s servants still beat faster when they recall Brother Knorr’s final talk at the “Song of Praise” Assembly at Philadelphia, during which he announced the new arrangement to assist more publishers to become pioneers, and, having begun, to continue without interruption.

New Quotas Make Part-Time Work Possible

The rising cost of living and the necessity of providing things decent for self and family has, with many, been one of the chief hindrances to continued pioneer service. The apostle Paul engaged in tent-making activities in order to be self-supporting and “accountable to no man”. Now it will be possible for presentday pioneers to engage in part-time employment to meet their financial needs. It is therefore suggested that each pioneer who finds it necessary obtain some sort of part-time work. He may devote one-half of each day to secular employment, or two or three full days during the week, varying according to individual needs.

And how will this be possible? Beginning January 1, 1948, quotas for general pioneers will be reduced from 150 to 120 hours per month. That will mean that an average of four hours a day, one hour of which may be travel time, will meet the minimum requirement. Undoubtedly many pioneers will find it possible to exceed this quota, as they have consistently done in the past, and they are encouraged to do so.

The yearly quota of 1400 hours will be established. Thus, if seasonal work is available and desirable, a pioneer may put in 150 or 160 hours for a few months, and then take a full month off to gather sufficient funds for several months to come. In this way the full quota will be reached by the end of the year.

Secular employment should never be allowed to overshadow the work of greatest importance, the preaching of the gospel. The pioneer should set his own quotas so that he will not fall short at the end of the year. The Society will check the records of the pioneers every six months, and if any should not be meeting the quota it will be necessary to write the pioneer about his activity. All should plan from the beginning to work toward the 1400-hour yearly requirement and attain it.

Under this new arrangement, there will be no provision for suspensions. Each person who is on the pioneer list should continue to send in his report monthly, regardless of the number of hours devoted to field service. If the report is below the minimum of 120 hours, the reason should be noted at the bottom of the report. It is understood that each pioneer will devote as much time as possible to field service each month, even though engaged in secular work, for the ministry is his vocation. Back-calls and book studies should never be neglected. If on occasions the pioneer simply cannot care for them, some other arrangement should be made to do so.

Special pioneer quotas will remain the same: 175 hours and 50 back-calls per month. Their yearly quota, however, will be 1800 hours. The service activity of special pioneers will also be checked regularly, to see that they are meeting the yearly quota. There will be no arrangement for suspension as heretofore. If, because of secular or other reasons, they do not meet the requirements, they should, however, report as usual, noting the reason on their reports. As in the past, only the special pioneers who attain their quota of 175 hours and 50 back-calls during the month are eligible for that month’s allowance.

Secondary Occupation

All pioneers have in common one primary occupation, that of preaching the gospel. Their secondary occupations, however, designed to enable them to continue in full-time service, may vary. Some are skilled at handicrafts: needlework, leather goods, and the like. Others can work as barbers; parttime stenographers, store clerks or service station attendants. The “Song of Praise” Assembly


Joyous Assemblers Acclaim Christ Jesus as The Signal

Upward of 20,000 publishers are still talking about it, at their meetings, in their homes, and with persons of good-will. And they will continue to do so, for the “Song of Praise” Assembly, held at Philadelphia, Pa., November 21-23, had far surpassed all expectations. Never, acclaimed many, had Jehovah showered down so milch in three short days. In a small way this Informant will attempt to recapture a few of the assembly’s high lights, especially for the benefit of our brethren unable to be present.

Singing praise to Jehovah: that was the theme struck by the chairman in his address of welcome. Bearing thereon were other discourses of the afternoon, including the revealing and moving presentation, “Field Service for 1947 and 1948.” ‘It is the spirit of the Lord,’ declared the speaker, ‘that has effected the 1947 increase, and that same spirit will operate for 1948 expansion as we obediently do our part in the field.’

Brother Knorr’s first talk, “Gaining Entrance into Permanent Dwellings,” on Friday evening, made crystal clear the parable of the “unjust steward”. Gain entrance into permanent dwellings by making friends by use of the mammon of unrighteousness. And who is the “faithful steward” now? The heartening results of the relief program left no room for doubt. Close to one-third of a million dollars contributed for our needy brethren in Europe, and arrangements to send more used clothing under way! And how Jehovah had favored his steward in 1947, even with a new peak of 207,552 publishers in the field! The release of the 1948 Yearbook and the beautiful Calendar came as a satisfying climax for the day.

On Saturday morning 321 symbolized their consecration; the afternoon was devoted to four encouraging discourses on endurance, pioneering, reporting and true worship; and in the evening the Society’s president addressed an attentive audience on “The Signal for the Peoples”. Not a standard, flag or banner; the “Signal” is earth’s new King, Christ Jesus. “Lift up the Signal! Behold it in all its glory and power!” A powerful resolution was then presented and unanimously adopted. Solidly back of the true Signal do we stand! We repudiate the makeshifts of men!
