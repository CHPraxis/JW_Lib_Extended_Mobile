Cultivating Gifts for the Ministry Progress to Be Made by All Publishers in September


District Assembly Blessings


Booklet Offer to Be Presented

“Cultivate these [gifts], devote yourself to them, so that everyone will see your progress.” Develop your natural abilities for the ministry ! Peter recognized the desirability of this and, after being called from the fishing profession, he willingly developed his unused aptitudes in his assigned work. Jehovah rewarded him with the gift of improved or adequate ability for his new duties as a minister. Progress showed itself. Following Paul's remarkable conversion, he, too, cultivated his gifts aright, no longer misusing them to bring persecution upon the Lord's servants. Progress again was clearly seen. Today Jehovah’s witnesses will likewise devote themselves to cultivating gifts for the ministry. They so doing, everyone will see their progress; expanded praise will be in evidence. And what better time to become more conscious of “cultivating gifts for the ministry” than now, in September, the first month of the thrilling 1950 service year!—1 Tim. 4:14, 15, An Amer. Trans.

Cultivating Gifts from the Beginning

Developing gifts for Theocratic service begins when the initial knowledge of life-giving Kingdom truth is obtained by the man of good-will. His willingness to improve brings increased knowledge and with it a desire to tell others the good things learned; and so he extends the gospel message to friends and neighbors. Questions arise. More study is required in order to locate proper answers. Aided by the holy spirit and the promptings of a willing heart, the man of good-will Is “cultivating gifts for the ministry”. Already progress is being made.

Continued improvement will be seen as the man of good-will is assisted by alert ministers to associate with the local company. Systematic presentation of the gospel to a greater number of “neighbors” in door-to-door service will be encouraged, and personal instruction given. During September door-to-door activity for all publishers, new and old, will be the presentation of any seven booklets on a 25c contribution, or three on a contribution of 10c where seven are not available.

Each booklet placed during September affords an opportunity to engage in back-call and Bible-study activity for all publishers who have placed these booklets. Do not hesitate to enjoy such refreshing and instructive work because of limited knowledge. Use the opportunity to cultivate your ability in these fields.

In making neighborly return visits, endeavor to clear away the barriers to knowledge of Kingdom truth by answering perplexing questions first. Bible studies are thus started and eventually a study of the publications is undertaken. Also, with the distribution of thousands of booklets, many booklet studies can undoubtedly be arranged. These will prove very beneficial in dispensing truth, as paragraphs in the selected booklet are discussed, questions entertained and scriptures read. Concluding the booklet study, a study in “Let God Be True” can be introduced.

The new publisher will unite with experienced ministers in doing other new things, including full support of public meeting activity, an enthusiastic share in all features of magazine day and other company service arrangements. The schedule of Kingdom proclaimers should necessarily include regular attendance at all company meetings. Full participation in the Theocratic ministry school should be outstanding, as ministers seek to improve natural abilities. Brothers not yet enrolled in the school will make every effort to take full advantage of the provision by enrolling. Students who have shown suflicient progress but who have not as yet had the opportunity of giving public talks might be used for the first time this month.

Continual Development Desired

There are no bounds for cultivating gifts for use in praising Jehovah. Pioneer service, a rounded-out ministry, offers splendid avenues for increasing praise: Gilead training, foreign missionary work and many other blessings. Doubtless many brethren can arrange their affairs to enter this branch of the ministry as the new service year begins.

For some the future may bring servant responsibilities. Develop your abilities for this service now and be in position to aid your brethren when the opportunity arises. Consecration symbolized by water baptism is the initial requirement, and then willingness to fulfill that vow and render greater service. Accept added privileges and opportunities to gain experience, however small they may be, and you will learn and advance to maturity.

Appointed servants in companies will seek to improve and perfect the gifts they possess as overseers. They will make a determined effort to do better by diligently performing their assigned duties more effectively. Finally, they will work in unity for advancement of Kingdom interests locally.

No matter what position we occupy in the Theocratic organization at present, be it man of good-will, company publisher, pioneer or servant, each of us possesses abilities that can be further developed for the ministry. September is a month for “cultivating gifts for the ministry”. Let us ‘devote ourselves to them’ and share in the month’s activity to the fullest extent.

Let Us Apply Valuable Instruction Received

Returning from the district assemblies refreshed by joyful associations and strengthened by ‘meat in season’, Jehovah’s servants truly feel that once again they have proved Jehovah and that, true to His promise, he has indeed opened the windows of heaven and poured out upon them rich blessings.

These blessings, however, were not poured out upon Jehovah’s servants solely for their own enjoyment, but also that in the coming days they might individually better carry out the resolve of the year-text “I . . . will yet praise thee more and more”. Most appropriate, therefore, was the opening discourse, “Praising More and More.” To do this effectively, we next learned, means “LTsing Your Education”, the best in the world, in every feature of the service, and, if at all possible, in the pioneer work, where one can make the most use of it.

‘Praising more’ also means “Overcoming Barriers to Company Expansion”, be such barriers a negative attitude toward expansion or more real ones such as persecution, territory and transportation problems. These barriers can be overcome “by my spirit”, by faith, by zeal and by mutual co-operation. Also, company expansion will result if “Servants Feed the Flock” by conscientiously discharging their respective duties and by setting an example, both in brotherly kindness and in field witnessing.

Particularly pertinent to the as-semblj' theme of ‘praising more’ was the discourse “Living Up to the Name”. That means ‘studying to show ourselves approved', being alert to preach ‘in season and out of season’, ‘keeping unspotted from the world’ and ‘loving one another’. And. to aid us in praising still more effectively, instruction was given on “Making the Most of Theocratic Counsel” : “During the Circuit Servant Visit,” “At the Circuit Assembly” and “By Daily Application of Counsel on Theocratic Organisation”.

Subscriber Address Changes

Daily the Society receives many notices from the post office department requesting that subscriptions be canceled because the subscriber has moved without notifying either the Society or the post office of the new address.

Henceforth, when such notices are received for subscriptions that have four or more months to run, the Society will notify the company in whose territory the subscriber formerly lived. It will be greatly appreciated if one of the local publishers will immediately check at the address given to see if the new address is available. This should be done even though the local company may not have originally sent in the subscription.

If it is found that the subscriber in question is still living at the same address, the card should be returned to the Society with a notation to that effect in the space provided on the card. In such cases it is also well to have the subscriber contact the postman to assure future delivery of the magazines. If a new address is obtained, it should be similarly forwarded to the Society.

New Service

Past Year Shows Great Expansion

With confidence we can look forward to many blessings and privileges during the 1950 service year. But before we enter the new year, let us pause and examine the past year in retrospect, noting wherein we have progressed and also wherein we need to improve. This, in order that we may plan now to make 1950 even better than last year.

Reports received for the first ten months of the past service year indicate that we have not only maintained but even exceeded the quota of a 10-percent increase in number of publishers for that period of time. All will eagerly await the report in the Yearbook to see by just how much we exceeded the quota for the year. But how does progress in your local company compare? To what extent did it contribute toward this gratifying increase of praise throughout the nation? A check of the following points will help you to determine where you have done well and also where improvement is needed.

Did the company increase 10 percent to meet the quota of publishers for the year? And how do other averages for the year, as shown on the chart, compare with the quotas?

Expired Subscription Slips

Every month the Society sends expired subscription slips to companies holding the proper territory. In event the subscribers are in isolated territory, their slips are sent to the nearest company. Therefore, when receiving these names, each company is urged to call on all it can reach, even though some may be out of your assignment. Return the slips to the Society only if it is impossible for any publisher or pioneer in the company to reach them.

The assistant company servant should compute the company’s averages, as well as the new quotas, and post these on the chart. The new quota of publishers will be 10 percent above the average number of publishers for the 1949 service year. Similarly, the new quota for total hours and total back-calls will be 10 percent over what these totals were for the previous year. The quotas of average number of hours and average number of back-calls per publisher are obtained by dividing the new quota of total hours and total back-calls by the new quota of publishers. The quota of Bible studies remains the same, 1 per publisher. It is suggested that only whole numbers be used when posting average number of publishers. Fractions of less than one-half should be dropped, but 1 whole number added if the fraction is one-half or over.
