Salvation Thro

New Subjects to Spur Public Meeting Campaign The ways of this world are ruthless and hard. It bestows its favors on those displaying the greatest degree of mental sharpness and physical prowess regardless of need. In striking contrast thereto, Jehovah, in his love and mercy, requires neither unusual mental nor physical strength of those who would receive salvation unto life eternal. In fact, that which Jehovah requires is so simple that the test falls just where he wants it to: solely on the willinff-ness of his servants. It is something that we have learned to do since early infancy, something that we cannot help doing. And what is that? Simply to talk. Yes, talk, talk, talk. So simple, so easy to do, and therefore so perfect a test of our willingness and our appreciation of his blessings. As our slogan for the month of March puts it, “Salvation Through Confession” (Rom. 10:10) is what Jehovah has decreed for human creatures that would live.

How just, how reasonable, this requirement to make confession, to preach, to witness, to talk I In order to receive salvation ourselves we must extend to others the opportunity to avail themselves of it. So obviously right and fair, is it not? Also, if we would receive life by having Jesus confess us before our heavenly Father, we must confess him before men. Why should he acknowledge us before the Almighty God. the King of Eternity, if we are ashamed to confess him before weak, imperfect human creatures?

How can we most effectively make a confession before men that will result in salvation to those that hear it as well as to ourselves during the month of March? By engaging in all forms of gospel preaching. First of all, by talking 1l’«tc7itoicer subscriptions at every opportunity, at every door. Remember, it is no great thing to receive a contribution of one dollar for a year’s subscription for The Watchtower, with a premium of a bound book and a booklet. Such a wealth of spiritual food simply cannot be measured in terms of dollars and cents. There just Is no comparison between the cost of preparation and distribution of this literature and the hope of eternal life it holds out. Therefore, let no servant of the Lord be hesitant about presenting this offer to the people.

Have Hearts Filled with Truth

To make a proper confession before men, no mere routine witness or mechanical lip-service such as religionists use will do. We must ‘believe with our hearts’, that is, have our minds filled with the truth and its goodness, so that our lips may overflow with words of knowledge and wisdom spoken with earnestness and confidence. Therefore, as we study our Watchtowers, we should make note of gems that we feel will strike a responsive chord in the hearts of those of good-will, and then remember to use them as opportunity affords.

However, to be able to make use of such we may not be hasty in our presentation. With a calm, sincere, and pleasant manner we must pave the way for the “body” of our presentation ; for to present such a matter as the campaign offer will require a little time. Having the outline of our witness clearly In mind, we shall be able to proceed with assurance, ever ready to adjust it to meet the circumstances. Much Territory

Many Counties Not Worked for Several Years

In spite of the amount of witness work that has been done in this country, it is surprising to know that 1,419 counties in the United States, or 46 percent of all the country, were not thoroughly witnessed to during the service year 1945. This represents approximately 22,000,000 people, or about 16 percent of the population, who did not have their rightful opportunity of meeting Kingdom publishers during the entire year, because of not being included in pioneer or company assignments.

While these counties are not the thickly settled ones, nevertheless they do have large numbers of goodwill people willing to receive the truth. Someone must take the Kingdom message to them. Some of this territory has not been worked for several years and is virgin territory for any pioneer who would like to have such an assignment. Much literature can be placed, many back-calls made, and many studies started with these good-will persons. Also, there are many towns and thickly settled rural sections that are ideal for putting on public lecture series. There are yet millions of people right here in our own country who are not being regularly visited with the Kingdom message, and to whom we are commissioned to go, to “make disciples of all nations” before the end comes. These people in isolated territories must receive the Kingdom witness before Armageddon begins. The field is very large.

Many brethren who enter the pioneer service, and those already pioneers, give preference to city territories which are already cared for by organized companies. Thus, some territory is worked very frequently and other territory is neglected. To offset this condition the Society urges all pioneers who can do so to apply for some of these isolated counties not being served by Kingdom publishers. Some of this territory is ideal for summer witnessing, and others are good all year round. Most of the counties that are isolated at the present time are located in the south and midwest. However, in scription from him, it is proper that those persons be called on by the publisher, who may make placements and conduct back-calls and book studies with them. As far as possible and practical the publisher holding the territory should be kept advised of any such activity in his territory. There have been reports coming in to the office of brethren being excluded from calling on such people because they do not happen to hold that particular territory assignment. This is entirely improper. Every publisher should rejoice in the fact that the people in his territory have obtained this literature, regardless of from whom it was obtained, and should co-operate fully with any back-call or study arrangement that may have been made by the publisher placing the literature. If no such back-call or study exists, then the holder of the territory should try to start one.

This must not be construed to mean, however, that a publisher should keep a list of the names of everyone in the city who obtains literature from him, and run around all over everybody else’s territory with new book releases. This has reference to legitimate cases in which the publisher, because of his acquaintance or intimate association with the individual, is situated in a more advantageous position to enliven the interest and make the placement than any other publisher.

Constantly the Society receives requests from companies that say they are unable to carry on the work effectively in their assignments and that ask for pioneers to help them. But there are not nearly enough pioneers to supply the demand. Companies should endeavor to bear their own burdens and carry on the work in their own territories and thus free the pioneers to care for this hitherto unworked territory and carry the glad news of the Kingdom to the people who are anxiously waiting for someone to call on them with the message of hope and life.

‘Imitate the Apostle Paul’

A similar condition existed in Paul’s time. The territory where he was working had been developed to the point that it was possible for the local company to care for the Kingdom interests and thus free Paul to take up foreign assignments. There was much virgin territory that was hitherto unworked, and the Lord’s spirit directed that he go to these sections, to Galatia, Macedonia, Achaia, Rome, etc. In these assignments Paul put on public lectures, made back-calls, and organized the good-will interest into companies. After organizing a company, and thoroughly instructing the brethren on how to carry on the Kingdom service in their territory, he journeyed on to another assignment to stimulate interest and organize another company. Under the direction of the holy spirit, he stated, “Be ye imitators of me, even as I also am of Christ.” (1 Cor. 11:1, Am. Stan. Ver.) Will you be an ‘imitator of Paul’ and accept an assignment in some of the isolated counties in this country ?

There is much interest to be found and many “sheep” to be fed before the final climax at Armageddon. These isolated counties offer a splendid opportunity for pioneers to develop this interest by putting on public lectures, conducting studies with them, and organizing them into active companies of Jehovah’s witnesses. Then, like Paul, you can move on to another isolated section and do likewise. This is sure to have Jehovah's rich blessing and bring you many joys, as well as the hope for life and happiness to these millions in isolated territory.

If you wish further information regarding any of these counties, feel free to write us and we will do all we can to aid you to enlarge your privileges.
