A Blessed Privilege and Obligation

The Scripture constituting the basis for the June activity, Ecele-siates 7:12 (Younf/i, is very enlightening and edifying to the servant of the Lord. “For wisdom is a defence, money is a defence, and the advantage of the knowledge of wisdom is, she reviveth her i>os-sessors.”

For more than seventy years the Lord has been creating a reservoir of Kingdom knowledge against this very day. Ue has been preparing a people for his name who would be instrumental in the distribution of this knowledge. The only reason for any company of Jehovah’s witnesses existing today is that they may be used in this mighty work of defense and reconstruction. The reviving message contained in the bound books and booklets prepared through the “faithful and wise servant” of the Most High God will never revive anyone while it lies on the shelves or is stored in cartons at the company headquarters.

In order to ‘gather out the stones and cast up a highway for the people’, the publications must be taken from the shelves and cartons and placed in the hands of the people of good-will. And the message must then be transferred from the hands of these people to their minds. This all adds up to witnessing, back-calling, and studies with the people. Actually there is no defense through knowledge until the true Kingdom information is established in the minds of the people of good-will. Then only is the possessor revived.

June Campaign Offer

During June the distribution of the three bound books from The Harp to Religion and the booklet “Be Glad, Ye Nations”, on a contribution of 50c, and the very essential and widespread distribution of “Be Glad, Ye Nations” itself, will provide both reviving and nourishing knowledge for the people, and will result to their good and to the glory of God, only if the publishers follow through by making back-calls and conducting studies with those who receive the message. We can never overlook the fact that the message must reach the mind of the person of good-will before it is a real defense to him, and we are responsible before the Lord, not only in the placing of the message in the hands of the people of goodwill, but also in aiding them to get it into their mind. Therefore, be an able minister by following through and make all of your back-calls and conduct your studies.

Many companies are running out of one or more of these bound books, and are ordering direct from the Society, instead of contacting near-by companies and finding out from them what they can do to relieve their needs. Each company servant v ill be responsible for contacting all near-by companies within a radius of approximately 100 miles, and letting them know on what books his company is short or is overstocked. This is so that you can co-operate with other companies and they can co-operate with you in disposing of all this old literature that is scattered among the companies.

The Society is able to supply only the book Salvation at the present time, but there are many companies fairly adjacent to you who may have all the literature you require, and be in position to fill your requests for combinations. Therefore, make arrangements to put into operation the above provision immediately. Please make this a local exchange either by exchanging books between one company and another or by giving a regular remittance for literature at company rates to the company from which you are obtaining the literature. It will not be necessary to make out Transfer of Stock and involve the Society in this transaction.

Pioneers will likewise try to get their literature locally the same as the company. The pioneer will remit the regular pioneer rates to the company from which he receives the literature and then sign with the company servant a joint statement of the literature taken and the company servant will forward it to the Society, who will credit the company with the difference between the company and pioneer rates.

All Jonadabs and people of goodwill who attend back-calls and book studies should be given an opportunity to complete their sets of any books of which they are short, for there is no assurance that any of these older books will be reprinted.

Write Early for Rooming Accommodations

As the time draws near for the great all-nations assembly, the hopes of all those who are for Jehovah turn to that marked time in great expectation. Everyone believes that it will be the best yet, and there is every indication to date that the assembly at Cleveland, August 4-11, will surpass all other assemblies in blessings, joy and praise.

Long ago, in Hezekiah’s time, “much people” assembled at Jerusalem to observe the passover and to praise the God of Israel. According to the Scriptural record, their hearts overflowed with joy as they praised Jehovah day by day, "singing with loud instruments unto Jehovah,” and He poured out wondrous blessings upon them. In modern times, we had a similar great gathering at St. Louis, and ever since the question has been constantly raised, ‘When are we going to have another one like that?’

For more than four years the troubled conditions of earth have prevented having such a large assembly, but now in God’s kind favor the joyful prospect lies directly before us. All the pent-up desire for a big convention can now be released in this great event for Jehovah’s witnesses. The word is spreading throughout the world, and from every direction brethren are planning to come: many different nationalities will be merged into the ‘one nation’ whose God alone is Jehovah, bound together in the basic desire for His New World of righteousness. How good it will be for brethren from lands where there are comparatively few witnesses and for the isolated publishers who do not generally have opportunities to talk with large numbers of those of like precious faith to associate with thousands of fellow publishers and rehearse the righteous acts of Jehovah '. Truly it is the Lord's provision for all of his worshipers in these postwar days. The eight days of spiritual feasting will surely build up and sustain God’s people for their continuing fight against spiritual wickedness in high places.

What to Do Now

With this Informant you receive a Room Request Form. Companies are being sent a sufficient quantity to supply each publisher and those of good-will associated with the companies. If more are needed you should write the Brooklyn Office for them. These forms are being sent at this early date so that the brethren will be able to fill them out and send them to WATCHTOWER CONVENTION, 2515 FRANKLIN BLVD., CLEVEIAND 13, OHIO, by June 1 or as soon thereafter as possible. It will be to your advantage to send in your Room Request Form early, because that will help to insure your receiving a definite assignment of accommodations. It will also be a convenience to the brethren now working, on the rooming accommodations in Cleveland. August will soon be here ami we shall have to locate many how many we do not know. There will be very limited hotel accommodations; most of the brethren will stay in private homes. Undoubtedly this arrangement will provide the best means yet of giving a witness to the people of Cleveland. Living in the homes of the people for eight days will open up many opportunities of speaking the Kingdom truth, and all should make it a point to leave a witness in each home.

Every effort will be made to give you what you ask for, but it may not always be possible to fill your requests with exactness. We feel sure everyone will appreciate the good work being done in Cleveland (especially in these times of housing shortages) to find rooms for the tens of thousands of publishers who have indicated they are preparing to go to the convention. Therefore everyone will gladly accept the accommodations as assigned. By mailing in your Room Request Form early (and we cannot emphasize too strongly the importance of writing early) you will have a better chance of getting what you prefer. And whatever you do, be sure to write for your rooms in advance. Don’t wait and take a chance on picking up accommodations when you reach Cleveland, for there may not be any rooms left. Write for rooms, and write early.

It has been possible to obtain a location for erecting tents and parking trailers. It is but a few miles from the stadium. You will help the brethren at Cleveland considerably if you send your request for space within the next few weeks. In any event, be sure to write for space before you go to Cleveland.

Time should be spent at the service meeting following receipt of the Room Request Form to explain its use.

Train, bus and car groups should now be arranged for, and, as previously advised by the Society, the brethren will co-operate with each other especially in making up groups for trains and special busses. Everyone who loves the Lord is welcome, yes, is urged to attend; so tell all the people of good-will about the benefits they will derive from attending and try to bring some along with you to share in the worship.

So write for your rooms as soon as possible and make your preparations for the journey to Cleveland. Bring your friends, bring everyone who loves the Lord, and lift up thankful hearts and voices for eight happy days in the true worship of the Most High.

Collaboration in Reconstructive Work Field of Activity Earth-wide


July Begins Intensive Campaign with Booklets

1946 sees the Theocratic Reconstruction work increasing in momentum. Literally hundreds of Watchtower Bible College graduates are now serving in foreign lands, their number steadily increasing, while at the college the seventh class is preparing itself for such service. The president of the Society, Brother N. H. Knorr, has just concluded a tour of organization in which branches were established in Central America, South America, and in the isles of the sea. The truth, the true worship, and the active organization of Jehovah are being reconstructed on every hand. What a contrast with the dark days of 1918-19, when God’s visible organization was disrupted, the true worship all but ceased, and his servants were so sorely tried in faith!

What brought about this striking change in conditions? It was the coming of the Reconstructor Christ Jesus in fulfillment of his commission and the prophecy as recorded at Isaiah 61:1-4. Releasing his followers from their bonds, he revealed to them that there was a great work to be done; and Jehovah God through Christ poured upon them his spirit of joy for the service of reconstruction. ‘Arise from slumber and the dust,’ He called; ‘shake off the ashes, cast off the sackcloth, and get busy in the great work of reconstruction!’ Zion heard and obeyed, and as a result today we see on every hand the fulfillment of our text for the month: “And strangers shall stand and feed your flocks, and foreigners shall be your plowmen and your vinedressers.”—Isa. 61: 5, A. S. V.

Far-spread and effective though the activity of Jehovah’s servants has been, such is only the beginning ; there is a tremendous reconstruction work yet to be done, both in this and in other lands. Appreciating this fact each Theocratic worker will seek to have an ever larger share in “Collaboration in Reconstructive Work ”. How can this best be done during the month of July? By bringing to the people the truth as contained in the booklets that have been permitted to accumulate in recent years. There is an important message in each one of them, and it is our obligation to see to it that it reaches someone. Many have received their first understanding of the truth by reading one of these very booklets.

Inventories show that there are upward of twelve million of these booklets in the hands of the companies and pioneers. Just think of it, twelve million! Now, by each company publisher’s placing but one combination of ten booklets or three combinations of four booklets a week—and certainly that should not be hard to do—and each full-time publisher placing but one combination of ten or three combinations of four booklets a day, more than six million of these booklets would be placed in the hands of the people during July and August, the extent of this campaign. Let each collaborator in reconstructive work accept this suggestion as his minimum quota, seeking to exceed it to the extent of his ability and opportunities. To this end each company and pioneer should arrange for evening booklet work during July and August.

To reconstruct the truth and the true worship in the hearts and lives of men of good-will we must also do a planting and building work by calling back, by answering their questions, by reminding them of the importance of the message, and by working toward a book study.

During July continue also to stress the public meeting campaign, and let each one zealously and consistently support this feature of the reconstruction work. Placing our quota (and more) of the booklets, calling back on each placement, patiently and tactfully working toward a book study, and diligently supporting the public meetings— that is the way to show appreciation for our privilege of “Collaboration in Reconstructive Work”.

Public Meetings

Public meetings are increasing with mounting momentum throughout the country. But there are still many companies that are overlooking this splendid medium of giving a public witness. All who can do so should arrange for as many public meetings as they are able to put on, particularly during June and July. August activities may be light due to the convention, but there is no reason why June and July should not be banner months for public meetings, indoors and outdoors. Many companies are finding midweek-evening public meetings very-successful. People who devote their week-ends to recreational purposes are glad to attend meetings on a midweek evening. Try it out in your community. If you don’t have sufficient qualified speakers of your own, see if some near-by company or pioneer can assist you.
