“Creator’s Remembrai

It Is Convention Month, Booklet Campaign, and Culmination

of Service Year 1946

“Hemember now thy Creator in the days of thy youth, while the evil days come not, nor the years draw nigh, when thou shalt say, I have no pleasure in them.”—Ecclesiastes 12:1.

August, the concluding month of the service year 1946, marks the culmination of a year packed with manifest leadings of the Lord Jehovah, deliverances by the score, and many blessings upon his people. Bans on Kingdom work have been lifted in many nations (to the extent that world-wide only two or three remain now) ; his people have been delivered from the Nazi-Fascist - Hierarchy - operated concentration camps, and the reconstruction of freedom of worship has gone forward at a fast pace, many branches having been opened up in foreign countries and isles of the sea. To climax this, the great convention of all nations has been arranged for at Cleveland, Ohio, and already breth-ren-delegates are pouring in from Europe, South Africa and the faraway isles. These things, meditation on them, his mighty works, stir the mind of his servants and cause them to remember him. As The Watchtower recently put it, ‘‘The best way to remember and not be forgetful hearers is to put the knowledge to work by teaching others.” Thus, Jehovah's witnesses during August will show their ‘remembrance of the great Creator’ by using their faculties to help others to see his many and mighty works, and that in the face of demonic opposition.

Your Service itinerary for August

While the nations of the world as a whole have forgotten God and the sentence of Jehovah through the psalmist is, “The wicked shall be turned into hell, and all the nations that forget God,” yet there are many people of those nations, people of good-will, and these Jehovah’s witnesses are urging to “be glad, ye nations, with his people”. Between the time you receive this Informant and the time you leave for Cleveland, you will be making arrangements to assist all of goodwill possible to get to the convention. In addition to convention attendance, August will afford much opportunity for Kingdom service activity in the field. On your way to Cleveland you will take advantage of every opportunity to witness and place combinations with those you meet, as w-ell as a “Be Glad, Ye Nations”. Arriving in Cleveland, excellent opportunity will be afforded to ‘remember your Creator’ by coming equipped to engage in every feature of Kingdom activity. This is a service convention, and plenty of provisions for service will be made. Also, you will be on hand for the various lectures and features of the program, absorbing, and taking notes for future use and remembrance.

Returning from Cleveland, make the round of all back-calls and studies where there are persons who were not able to be on hand, and give them firsthand the information received at the convention and knowledge of the many blessings and joys of fellowship together with brethren from all over the world. In view of the fact that August will be a rather crowded month, traveling to and from the convention, getting organized afterward, calling on the interested, etc., all companies are

The open Stadium will provide a great spectacle to the people of Cleveland as thousands of Christians assemble there and join in singing praises of their Maker. All who attend should come equipped to sing, by bringing their songbooks. Song sheets will not be provided at the convention. Any brethren who are professional or experienced singers and have a desire to participate in the special chorus of singers that will be organized should report to the Volunteer Service counter at the convention.

An event of such great importance as this convention should be permanently recorded. The Society hopes to provide a permanent record of the events and will therefore appreciate having those who are experienced in reporting or writing state their qualifications to the brethren at the Volunteer Service counter at the convention. Photography will also play an important part in this. There will be a special need for flashlight photography during the convention. Brethren who have flashlight cameras should bring them to the convention and enter their names on the list of photographers at the Volunteer Service counter.

All who plan to bring sound-cars to Cleveland should write to the Convention offices at Cleveland immediately and inform the brethren there concerning the type of equipment and whether it is in good condition. A number of such cars will be used during the convention at special locations.

Transportation arrangements are being made throughout the country and many special trains will be made up to accommodate several thousand from the large population centers. Thousands of others will travel on the regularly scheduled trains and busses. These latter ones who do not come on special trains or in special coaches should be sure to list their names and their expected time of departure from Cleveland at the Information counter in the arcade during the convention. It will be to your advantage to register, because this information will be compiled and due notice will be sent to the railroad and bus lines a day or two before the end of the convention so that adequate equipment can be made available to insure a comfortable return journey for conventioners. A special bulletin on transportation is being sent with this Informant for consideration at your meetings.

One feature of conventions of Jehovah’s witnesses that has always made them most enjoyable and efficient is the willingness of the brethren to co-operate in every way. A certain amount of service must be rendered to the brethren, and there have always been many volunteers. Some will be required for cafeteria work, operating refreshment stands, bookroom work, directing traffic, ushering, musical activities, first-aid service, and maintenance. Brethren who plan to volunteer for cafeteria or maintenance work should bring appropriate clothing for use during the work periods.

A number of plumbers, electricians, carpenters and tinsmiths will be required at the convention. Some will be needed a week prior to the opening of the convention. It will be appreciated very much if brethren who are able and willing to do such work will please write the Watchtower Convention at Cleveland (2515 Franklin Blvd., I’.O. Please Accept Rooms as Assigned

Many of the accommodations are obtained from persons of good-will who are looking forward to having some of Jehovah’s witnesses stay with them. The people are generally very conscientious about putting the rooms in the best possible condition for conventioners. When we have held conventions in Cleveland in the past, a few brethren have not taken the rooms to which they were assigned. That practice has caused the good householders to stay up until late hours waiting for conventioners to arrive. Some bad feelings have resulted.

When accommodations are assigned to you, a card is mailed to you showing the date the committee has given the householder for your arrival in Cleveland. Arrange your trip so you ■will arrive that day. If you cannot arrive on schedule or if you take other accommodations, please show consideration for the householder by notifying him. Let us show appreciation for the kindness of the people in opening up their homes to us. Please accept rooms to which you are assigned.
