Are You Ready?

From all over the earth, from every continent on the globe, the prayers of the Lord's people have been ascending to him, petitioning his blessings upon the arrangements for this great assembly at Cleveland and upon the assembly itself.

The hearts of God's people everywhere have been centered on that assembly with longing and desire to be there in person, and where that is impossible they will be there in spirit, living through the assembly in their mind's eye. This assembly is going to be very real to them. What would they not be willing to sacrifice to be there!

Among the more fortunate are the hundreds upon hundreds from the remote corners of the earth, Asia, Africa, Australia, Europe, South America and the isles of the sea, who will be traveling toward Cleveland by every means of transportation available; such as by camel, oxcart, river boat, freighter, tramp steamer, ocean liner, railroad, auto, bus, and airplane—by anything and everything that moves. They are coming to the greatest convention of the Lord's people that has ever been assembled upon this earth 1 They are straining every nerve, expending every resource and drawing heavily upon the Lord for direction, protection and a safe journey to that assembly. Consideration of Others

That ‘no man lives unto himself’ will be true of all that attend the Assembly. Each one privileged to go should consider himself a delegate from the Lord to the people of good-will unable to attend, obligated to bring back home to them a complete report of the Assembly and nourishing spiritual food. The Lord will do his part; his blessings will be there. Let us be on the alert and attentive to receive them so that we shall be able to pass them on to others.

Additionally, let us consider the tens upon tens of thousands of others in attendance with us at the Assembly and broaden our outlook and attitude to include them also in our planning. We should go to the convention not only with the thought of receiving abundant blessings ourselves, but also that of being a blessing to those with whom we come in contact. To do this our hearts and minds must be in tune with the occasion.

For months our brethren have been working hard, day and night, to prepare for the convention: supervisors, technicians, clerks and stenographers, pioneers by the hundreds seeking rooming accommodations, cataloguing them, writing to the householders and the brethren, handling an abundance of mail—all under the direction of the Lord to make this convention a blessed and comfortable place for you to come to, and a glory to Jehovah’s name. Let us show our appreciation of these provisions of the Lord by being content with whatever has been arranged for, remembering that, after all, the spiritual feast, both of revealed truths and privileges of service, is the main thing. Co-operate fully and obey all instructions given from the platform. Do what you can to make the assembly a success for everyone. Be also considerate of the people who have so graciously permitted us to enter their homes. In addition to giving them a proper witness of the Kingdom, let your irreproachable course of action and your thoughtfulness of their rights be a witness also. Carry out the instructions given on your room assignment form: contact them promptly upon arrival, advise them if you cannot be there on time, etc.

All such, however, is only the beginning. To fully contribute his share to the success of the convention let each one put his shoulder to the wheel by volunteering for some service, either convention work or field service; and, whatever job you undertake, stick to it. Do your assigned duty. Be dependable. Whether your task be hard or difficult or seemingly small and insignificant, put your mind to it and do it wholeheartedly as unto the Lord.
