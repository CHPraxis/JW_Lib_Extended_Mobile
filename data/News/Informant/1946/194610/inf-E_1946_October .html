A wake! Campaign Opens! “New Song” Testimony Period Features New Magazine


Wake Up More than 50,000 Persons with Awake! Subscriptions

Present world planners continue to sing their old song over and over again. They attempt a slight variation, a new key, and different lyrics, but they still produce the same old discordant and doleful tune of international collaboration. And no wonder! Only Jehovah deserves a new song! It is only what he has done that is worthy of being sung in joyful tunes. His deeds, being distinctively new to humankind, supply a new theme for the new song. No cheap man-made song is this. No. but Jehovah supplies the truths which make up the theme of the song. The theme, new in the chronicles of humankind, is that Jehovah has taken his sovereign power over the earth and is now reigning through the Kingdom. Now it is up to us to awake to the rousing call “Oh sing unto Jehovah a new song!”—Ps. 98:1, A. S. V.

Indeed Jehovah has graciously supplied the truths for the new song. It is time for the people to be awakened to such. Alert to this fact, the Lord’s servants will be awake and awaken others in October by obtaining subscriptions for the new magazine Awake! The subscription rate will be $1.00 per year. Differing from other subscription campaigns there will be no book premium given. This is strictly an Awake! campaign, and it is hoped that more than 50,000 subscriptions for this magazine will he obtained during the one month of October.

"Awake!"—What Does It Offer?

How enthused all were when Awake! was released! How impressed with the first article of the first issue, “Wake Up, World!” And as we look through the magazine we appreciate how apt it will he in showing the difference between the joyful and magnificent “New Song” of Jehovah's deeds and the cheap, shallow, worn-out song of would-be world peacemakers. Make no mistake about it. Awake! is an entirely new magazine from cover to cover. It is not an old magazine with a new face. It directs its attention to waking up the people, to showing them what is going on, and to alerting them to the vital issues of the day.

“The Mission” (on page 2) is an excellent talking point. Show the people how Atcakc! has no fetters, but is free to print facts; how it uses news channels, supplemented by its own correspondents from all continents and scores of nations. Its pages cover many fields of knowledge—government, commerce, religion, history, geography, science, social conditions, and natural wonders. It exposes subtle dangers and reflects the hope of a righteous world.

Then there is the table of contents, showing the articles of the current issue. The feature “Thy Word Is Truth”, with its Scriptural subject, will be of interest. Or “Watching the World”, with its world-wide news digest. Yes, Awake! is new, it is up to date. The publisher will never be at a loss to present its advantages for the reader.

All-out Campaign Organization

This one-month campaign requires immediate work. No waiting until the month is over before the publishers wake up to the campaign. Having ordered sufficient supplies of magazines, having lined up territory for witnessing, every publisher should be in the field with Awake! from October 1 until October 31. Those brethren who were full-time ‘singers’ during vacation should not cease their good work, but should present this offer evenings, weekends and all times. Good-will persons should be encouraged to have a few extra copies of Awake! which they will give to their friends, trying to obtain subscriptions from them, thereby joining in singing the “New Song”.

Awake! will be presented at the door throughout October. If the people are unable to obtain a year’s subscription, offer a six-month subscription for 50c. Then drop down to a copy of Awake! with a Wateh-toicer magazine and a booklet, on a 10c contribution. At least endeavor to leave a copy of Awake! in every home on a 5c contribution. Remember too, keep your House to House Record and call back on all obtain-ers of the magazine to see if they wish to have it regularly.

October should witness evening Awake! work too. New publishers should be taken in this evening work and assisted to get a magazine route started. They will call back with each issue of the magazine trying to further that person's interest, presenting the subscription offer also in this work at every opportunity.

Regular street witnessing will be stimulated by the magazine. Already brethren report an increase in placements, as a result of the people’s interest in this new magazine. Keep up their interest by being alert. Know what is going on. Don’t let street work slump into a “silent testimony" or lifeless repetition of some trite phrase. Be up to date. Use the headings of the articles for slogans. Use points from the articles to arouse interest. Make short pithy statements about some of the news found in “W a t c h i n g the World”. Tell it out about the hope of a righteous world. Be positive but tactful, using good judgment in calling out the slogans. A few suggestions are here given for the use of the brethren, in addition to the above-stated suggestions, but these should not become stereotyped in their use.

“Awake! is free to publish facts.”

“Awake! is awake to world conditions.”

“Keep awake by reading Awake!” “Awake! views behind the news.” “Awake to the hope of a righteous world.”

“World news in review.”

Further, a short announcement regarding the subscription may be made at the close of each public talk during October.

At the doors, on the streets, riding on trains or busses, or just visiting friends or relatives, urge all persons to keep awake by reading Awake! Wherever you are join harmoniously in the swelling chorus now singing the “New Song”. “Break forth and sing for joy, yea, sing praises.” Make October a banner month. Not at a later date, not at a ‘more convenient time’, but as the apostle says, “Now it is high time to awake!”

Pioneering—a Vocation for Those Who Have Faith


Kingdom Expansion Calls for More Full-Time Servants

‘The basic requirement for pioneer service is faith.’ That was the firm conviction of thousands of pioneer-minded ministers a f t er hearing Brother Knorr's stirring discourse “Equipped for Every Good Work”. ‘Does not Jehovah own heaven and earth? Does not he care for the birds of the air and beasts of the field? Has he not promised that to those who “seek first the kingdom" needed food, shelter and clothing shall be provided? Of course he has. But the publisher must believe that promise. He must have faith, and let God prove true to his word that he will sustain his pioneer servant, daily.’

Here was the answer, clear-cut, convincing. Scriptural, to those who have asked, “How much money must I save first? Do I need a car and trailer? How can I be sure of food and clothing?” Satisfied that faith in God, and not stored-up material wealth, is the thing required, hundreds of brethren at Cleveland, young and old, single and married, asked for pioneer applications. These and the scores who have written in since the Assembly, armed now with unshakable faith, are going to take God at his word and make pioneering their vocation.

Brethren should keep in mind ordering sufficient literature to make up a 100-pound shipment whenever possible. Also, pioneers should endeavor to combine their orders with the company order. In such cases, the pioneer orders should be attached to the company order and be mailed with it. This book is published primarily for the publisher, for his own study and use in preaching the gospel; also for the assistance of many persons of good-will. It is not a campaign book yet: hence it should not be presented as an offer from house to house, but only on back-calls. In most instances, the larger orders for pioneers and some companies have been cut. There is only one rate on “Equipped for Every Good "Work”, namely 50c.
