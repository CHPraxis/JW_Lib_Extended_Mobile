Claims for Damages

When brethren receive literature or records in a damaged condition, please have the postmaster or the freight agent note the damage in writing. Do not give or show to the postmaster or railroad agent any invoices, but state that the claim for damage will be made by the Watchtower at Brooklyn. Then mail to the Society a letter describing the damage, enclosing the acknowledgment of damage by the carrier employed, as well as your original invoice. We prefer to start the claim here and make collection.

The Society always replaces records and books immediately on such claims, and will not make additional charge, but we must have the proper papers to make claim.

Informants and Testimony Cards Provided for

All Publishers

The Society furnishes the Informant free to all companies according to the number of publishers in the company. The Informant should be given to the publishers, both regular and irregular. The Informant sets out all the details relative to field activity, and the only ones really interested in this field activity are those who are engaged in the fieldservice work. Other persons attending meetings, who are inactive or do not go out in the service at any time, need not have copies of the Informant. Testimony cards should be supplied to all publishers, regular and irregular. For those who profess consecration to the Lord a regular testimony card with identification printed on the back has been prepared. The consecrated should be supplied with these. For very young children and those who do not profess consecration, but want to have some part in the Lord's service, the Society has provided a testimony card that is blank on the back, and such may be had on request.

Every person who conducts a study of any kind should fill out a Study Conductor's Report (SC-3) each month. AU the information requested on the Study Conductor's Report should be given as completely as possible. The Study Conductor's Report is important because it provides information that must be reported to the Society find that is not otherwise obtainable by the company' servants from the publishers. All companies should use the latest Study Conductor's Report form, which provides space for reporting the new interest.

Actually there are two different uses of the Study Conductor's Report: (1) To rejHirt information on established studies for which the Society’ has appointed conductors: and (2) to report information on back-call book studies conducted by publishers in the field.

All Study’ Conductor's Reports must be turned in promptly at the end of each month, to the assistant company servant. The assistant company servant 1ms only three days of the following month in which to compile his report; so Study Conductor's Reports should be given to him immediately at the end of the month, if possible; otherwise, mail them to him.

Because the assistant company servant is required to turn the Study Conductor's Reports over to the back-call servant, it will be necessary’ that the assistant company-servant keep among the company reports in his care a record of the following:

Total number of book studies for which study conductors have been appointed by the Society;

Total number of back-call book studies conducted by- company publishers ;

Total number of back-call book studies conducted by pioneers working in the company territory;

Bor each of the above groups the total attendance, the total new interest, the total associated, and the total new interest attending for first time.

One study is counted for each Study Conductor's Report turned in. For example: If a study is conducted in the same home and with the same people four times in a month it should be reported to the Society as one study < and four back-calls), not four studies.

Report the New Interest

The company monthly report card (and pioneer, too) provides for reporting field serriee only. Therefore it would follow that, in reporting the number of studies and attendance thereat, the only figures to report would be those concerning field activity, that is, total back-call hook studies. Many companies have been including the established book studies conducted by' appointed conductors in the number of book studies reported on the monthly report card: but that is not field service. Established book studies are reported to the Society- in the company information form, but not on the mon fitly report card.

As to the number attending book studies to be reported, this would apply to the newly interested ones attending back-call book studios who have not commenced attendance at the company headquarters meetings regularly. Do not include the study conductor or publishers who may happen to attend back-call book studies. Just report the new interest attending the back-call book studies on your company and pioneer monthly report cards. Assistant company servants will please note that the pioneers report the number of studies they conduct, and the attend

ance, as a matter of local record only. The company should not include the pioneer activities in the company monthly report to the Society.
