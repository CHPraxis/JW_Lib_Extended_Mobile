Attend Watchtower Meetings

According to our New World calendar. the text for the month of March is. “Study to show thyself approved unto God, . . . rightly dividing the word of truth.”—2 Tim. 2:15. Never before has that admonition been so important as at the present time. For today not only must we be prepared to answer all questions relating to the doctrines of the Bible, but we must be prepared to take full advantage of the ever-in-creasing opportunities to present the issue clearly and forcefully before the rationing and other boards and before the various courts. Many golden opportunities to honor the name of Jehovah before such bodies have been lost just because the brethren were not alert and prepared to speak boldly in the defense of the Kingdom message.

To ‘quit ourselves like men’, and therefore be approved of God, we must be prepared to wield the “sword of the spirit” with skill. That requires much study. The Lord lias graciously given us many helps, and we should avail ourselves of all of them. The Watchtower should be eagerly read and then diligently studied, so as to make the points and arguments therein one’s own. The Watchtower study meeting should be faithfully attended by all. By our actions we are either saying. “I rejoice at thy word, as one that find-eth great spoil.” or are saying, “The table of the Lobo is contemptible.”

The bound books, and especially the book The New World at the present time, are a part of this “table of the Lord”, and we should not only read them, but attend a company meeting where they are being studied. In many companies, only a small number of those attending the Watchtower study attend the book studies. This should not be; for the book The New World is full of vitally important truths that are revealed nowhere else, and which we must understand to have a clear vision of the issue and the New World.

‘Feed the Sheep’

Not only should we study at home, and attend the company studies, but we should also encourage the people of good-will to study. Having placed a book with them, we should call back to quicken further interest, and then in a kind, tactful and sincere way point out to them the need of studying the Bible with this new lielp. They, no doubt, belong to one of the three hundred and more religions of this land, not one of which has the slightest inkling of what it means to ‘rightly divide the Word of truth’. So, how can these people understand the difference between the (new) world which God so loved, ami the (old) world which we are told not to love, unless we study with them? Their salvation from impending disaster depends upon their seeing the issue, and that in turn depends upon their being able to

‘rightly divide the Word of truth’, which requires study.

As The Watchtower so well stated. ‘In order to see and appreciate the issue, and to be able ministers of the Word, those in a covenant with Him must continue to study to show themselves approved unto God . . . rightly dividing the Word of truth. In order to properly divide and apply the truth one must see and appreciate that Jehovah is God and that His name is above all others; that it has been defamed and must now be vindicated; that Christ Jesus is the great Vindicator . . . that the preaching of this gospel of the Kingdom is (1) to notify the people that Jehovah is God and that His Kingdom is here, (2) to give warning and information to those who desire to be on the side of Jehovah, and (3) to “feed my sheep”.’ To understand that requires study. And the more we study, the clearer our appreciation of the issue, the stronger our faith, the better our witness, and the greater our joy. Continuing thus we shall be approved unto God.

The Magazine That Shows the Way to Life

With the world in sorrow, war and distress, the peoples of the earth need comfort. Jehovah's witnesses have the comfort wherewith others can be comforted. A great responsibility, therefore, falls upon the ' servants of Jehovah to see that the message of the Kingdom is proclaimed far and wide, and that the people are given an opportunity to hear. Tire Watchtower Campaign centers its second month in March. ; All are acquainted with the offer: The Watchtower magazine on the regular yearly subscription rate of $1.00, giving free a bound book and a booklet. Many new subscriptions have already been sent in to the Society, and these are being put through as quickly as possible so that tlie new subscribers will get the early issues. All persons on whom back-calls have been made during the past year should be called upon if you can reach them. It takes more initiative to serve the Lord now than it did in days gone by. It used to be quite easy to get the car out of the garage, get some gas, and drive out into the territory, but now maybe you haven’t the tires, nor do you have the gas, therefore you have to walk or take a streetcar or a bus. The problem faces every one of us. What will we do? Will we stay at home because of the hardships and extra work, or will we get out In a territory near by and serve the Lord, proclaiming the message of the Kingdom?

Think back to the days of the Lord Jesus. He didn’t have an automobile, but he walked from city to city and from place to place. His mode of travel was not as comfortable as is ours today, even with rationing and difficulties in getting about. The apostles did the same thing, but they did preach this gospel of the Kingdom. We must do likewise in order- to defeat persecution.

The Lord has placed in our hands the greatest message of the day. It is a message that will bring life, peace and comfort to the groaning world of mankind. That message is contained in The Watchtower. It is essential that we reach all the people that we can. These times, though hard, will help us to prove our integrity and show to the Lord that Ave are desirous of doing this one i thing, serving Him and doing the work right.

Many Shall Be Cast into Prison

Because of faithfulness unto the Lord there are many brethren who have been east into prison. These, maintaining their integrity and desirous of keeping abreast with the Lord’s truth, will be furnished The Watchtower upon their request. Most prison regulations are to the effect that the publisher of any magazine must mail the magazine to the one in prison, and that the order for the magazine must be placed through the Commissary Department. Friends or relatives of those in prison should not send in subscriptions direct to the Society, but have the arrangement made through the prison officials. In this manner you can be assured that The Watchtower will reach those who want to study the magazine.

Take Good Care of Records

The new phonograph records that the Society is now having made do not have the same high-quality material in them that was previously used in records. They wear out more quickly. This is not necessarily the fault of the manufacturer. He cannot get the supplies. If we want records we have to take what we can get these days. So that the records may last longer, this suggestion is offered, and lias been proved to be correct as tried out in our testing department: Use a medium-tone, soft needle on all records from now on, in all types of phonograph. Leave the medium-tone, soft needle in the same position in the pickup for five playings. Then take it out and put in a new one. Using a soft needle makes it so that the wear is on the needle rather than on the record. When you use the long-playing chromium needle it wears out the record but not the needle. Thus it damages the record. Very good care should be taken of your records, and be sure that from now on you use the medium-tone, soft needle. These can be obtained from the Society. The packages in which these needles are sent are marked that the needle should be changed after each playing. However, you should keep in mind that the needle should be changed after each fifth playing. In the verticaltype phonograph use the medium-tone, soft needle henceforth. Put only one record on the turntable. The soft needle is longer than the chromium, hard needle. We hope this gives longer life to your records.

Legal Advice

When brethren are convicted for violating the law, often the judge asks the defendant whether he has anything to say before judgment is passed upon him. This gives an excellent opportunity to give an additional witness. Therefore, before judgment is passed and when the invitation to speak is given by the judge, the brethren involved should take advantage of the opportunity to give as big and as full a witness as possible. This statement is generally made without limitation and cannot be interrupted and is not governed by any rules of evidence.

Time spent in jail as the result of being incarcerated on account of preaching the gospel of God’s kingdom cannot be reported as time spent in the field service. However, it is proper to indicate on your monthly report card, at the bottom, in the space for additional remarks, the number of days spent in jail because of witnessing.
