Make More Back-Calls

Conduct More Studies

It is clearly understood by all the Lord's consecrated ones that the important thing in their lives is serving the Lord; therefore the thing uppermost in their minds is how they can increase their activities and improve their service to the Most High. The report for the year 1942 shows that the Lord has crowned it with his blessing and made it the greatest year of service up to the present time. This brings great joy to all New World fighters, and none of them wish to rest on their “laurels'’ and slow down now. All are stimulated to far greater activity than ever before.

The back-call and book-study work is the most important phase of the Lord's service at this time and should be concentrated upon by all publishers of the New World. It is primarily at back-calls and book studies that the people learn of God’s purposes and are instructed and “fed”.

Consider 1943 “Yearbook”

This subject is of so great importance that the Society hereby advises every company of Jehovah's witnesses to set aside part of at least one service meeting during the month of February and consider the article “Back-Calls and Book Studies”, page 47 in the 1943 Yearhook. This assignment should be given to some capable brother in the company and he should be prepared beforehand to give an interesting and instructive talk on this subject. The speaker should study this article thorouyhIy and give it in his own words—it is not intended that the Yearbook l.e read in the service meetings The st taker could have a brief out'ino of the material he would present to the company, and give it orally. Here tire a few of the things that should be stressed by the speaker in this presentation : the necessity of making back-calls and conducting book studies, the advantages of the back-call and bookstudy work over other features of the service, the Important part that the book The New World plays in the back-call and book-study work, the results of the back-calls and book studies, and the urgent necessity of making more back-calls and conducting more studies now, particularly in connection with the present Watchtower Campaign. He may also compare the average increase in the back-call work of the country with the increase of the local company and point out where the publishers may make many improvements over last year’s activity. A few scriptures could be used to prove various important points. Additional material having direct bearing upon this work may be taken from other publications of the Society if needed for further emphasis.

Everyone who attends your book studies and upon whom you call back is a potential Watchtower subscriber. Every attention and consideration should be given these people. Every person whose name is in your back-call file should be called on with tills special offer. Do not fail to offer the Watchtower subscription to all those with whom you may come in contact.

Your Company’s Progress


New Chart Being Sent

Use Current Poster

All Theocratic publishers are very much interested in having a part in making their respective companies more progressive. We all want to see the Kingdom work increase to the honor of Jehovah’s name. There are certain features of the work for which the publishers are directly responsible and which they can control. Progressive publishers want to know whether their company is increasing in these things. So that every company publisher may know j exactly what is being done in his company each month in these vitally important features of the Kingdom service, the Society has prepared a chart, which is being mailed free of charge to each company. This chart is printed on heavy paper and will be sent separate from the Informant, in a roll.

The chart is headed, ‘'This Gospel of the Kingdom Shall Be Preached" ; which is Matthew 24: 14. There are seven columns on the chart, headed as follows: 'lotal Publishers, Total Hours, Arcrage Ilouis per Publisher, Total Back-Calls, Average Back-Calls per Publisher. Total Book Studies, and Average Studies per Publisher. These are the most important parts of the Kingdom work today. Space for posting the company quota is also allowed, as well as a place for the year totals.

Along with this chart you will find sheets of numbers, some red and some black. These numbers are i printed on gummed paper.

How to Use It

The chart should be in the care of the assistant company servant. It is in two sections and will have to be pasted or glued together at the proper point. If you visit to do so you may paste or mount it on wall board to make it more durable, having in mind, that it should last for two years. The chart should be posted in the company Kingdom Hall or headquarters meeting-place. Every company, regardless of size, will find it advantageous to use this chart. Keep your chart neat and clean and avoid smudges.

The numbers printed on the gummed sheets should be neatly cut apart in strips. Use black numbers for this year, saving the red for next year. Care should be taken in cutting these strips, so that they will all be of the same width. If the numbers are cut out properly they will all be of the same .size, and then your chart will look much neater when you paste the numbers on it.

The assistant company servant should immediately post on this chart the quota for the company for the fiscal year 1943 and all the activity reported for the months of October, November and December 1942, and January 1943. At the end I of each month hereafter when the j report for the company is made up j the assistant company servant should j post the report on this chart. In j figuring the averages it is only necessary to use tenths, the same as the Society does in the Informant. Decimal points are on the number sheets.

The Informant each month will contain a summary of the report for the United States as it does now. At least ten minutes of a service meeting once each month should be devoted to a comparison of the company activities with the report for the United States as a whole.


Whether you are keeping up with your quotas should also be discussed, using the chart. Keep up your chart and keep up your progress. Remove all other charts from the hall.
