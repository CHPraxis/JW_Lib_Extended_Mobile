How to Report Back-Calls

Keeping proper records of all people of good-will is most important. In order that the back-call records may be kept properly and accurately, and to promote uniformity in all companies in keeping these records, the following instructions are given :

When a publisher places literature in the house-to-house work he makes note of this on the “House to House Record" form and calls back on all of those people in a few days. Each one of these calls on people with whom you have previously placed literature, or w h o have shown interest and to whom you are now able to give an additional witness, should be reported as a back-call in the following manner: Right after the very first back-call, if there is evidence of interest and more calls should therefore be made, the publisher should make out one original “Back-Call” slip, and one “Back-Call Follow-Up Report". He should keep the original "Back-Call” slip as long as he holds the territory and the party is interested, but the “Back-Call Follow-Up Report” should, at the first meeting thereafter, be turned in to the back-call servant, after the necessary information is entered on the original “Back-Call" slip. Each time the publisher makes a call thereafter he will make out one “Back-Call Fol-i low-Up Report” and turn it in to the back-call servant, after entering the results of his call on the back of the original “Back-Call” slip.

Filing “New Interest”

When the back-call servant receives “Back-Call Follow-Up Reports” for names he does not have in his permanent file, he will file them in their proper order under the territory number in his permanent file, and when he receives three such “Back-Call Follow-Up Reports” he will make out a "Permanent Back-Call Record” for his file, enter the information from the “Back-Call Follow-Up Reports” on it, and destroy the Follow-Up slips. If he receives only one or two “Back-Call Follow-Up Reports” and no more for a period of two or three weeks, he should take these slips out of his file and check with the publisher to see if the prospect has lost interest. Only one original “Back-Call” slip is to be made out by a publisher for each person on whom back-calls are made. The only exception to this would be when the back portion of a slip is completely filled out and it is necessary to have another place to continue the record of calls made. The publisher will turn in all of these with up-to-date information, addresses, etc., when he relinquishes the territory. The back-call servant will take these and cheek them with those in the permanent file and make corrections on his own records where necessary, and issue them when the territory is reassigned.

Display Only One Poster

It has been noted that many companies exhibit two or more campaign posters at one time. This defeats the purpose of these posters. When the Society sends posters to the company, only the one for the current month should be displayed. At the end of each month the old one should be taken down and the new one put up in its place. Fosters are usually mailed every three months. At no time should the posters for future months be shown to anyone in the company before they are displayed to the company at the beginning of the respective month. These are confidential, and are to be treated as such until the time for their release.
