Sending Subscriptions from Large Cities

You Must Show District Number

Recently the postal authorities outlined a new system of addresses for the large cities throughout the country. This arrangement provides for the addition of a unit or district number to the name of the city; just as 117 Adams Street is in Brooklyn 1, New York. The Post Oilice Department has ruled that these unit or district numbers must be included in the addresses to make them complete.

Henceforth all publishers working in large cities will please be sure to include the unit or district number on all new or renewal subscriptions sent in. This is necessary in order to comply with the Postal Laws. It is suggested that brethren include the number when sending in their own renewals, too. If you don’t know which district you live in, ask your postman and he will be glad to inform you. Your mail will be delivered sooner if the unit number is included in the address. Brethren living in cities for which the Post Office Department has not designated a numbering arrangement will, of course, continue sending in their subscriptions in the usual manner.

Hours! Hours! Hours!

Why the 60-Hour Quota

Jehovah, through his organization on earth, has repeatedly called our attention to the importance of devoting all the time we possibly can to his service. To assist us in this regard the quota of 60 hours per month was given. Since comparatively few make the GO-hour quota, the question arises, Why was it given ?

First of all, we must remember, our occupation is that of preaching the gospel, and all other things are of secondary Importance. We are to seek “first the kingdom of God, and his righteousness”. Therefore every day we should be on the lookout for opportunities to witness. Striving for an average of two hours a day will help us to put the emphasis in our daily lives where it belongs: on serving Jehovah.

Next, the quota of sixty hours gives us an incentive, something to shoot at. The higher we aim, the higher the result will be. By aiming for 60 hours, planning and bending all our efforts toward that end, many will reach It, and all will be spending more time in the service than we would if we were striving for a lower quota. Another reason: As our text for last month so forcibly brought to our attention, we are to redeem the time because the days are evil. Never before were the days so evil as now. Our only protection from the demons is to be fully occupied in the service of the Lord, and enjoying the blessings that result from such activity.

How to Increase Our Hours

The amount of time we devote to the service depends to a great extent upon our appreciation. Do we appreciate our relationship to the great Creator and Life-giver, the privilege of sharing in the vindication of His glorious name, the great honor of being His witnesses, our obligation to warn the wicked, and the need of those who are sighing and crying because of all the abominations they see in “Christendom”? To the extent that we appreciate these all-important things, to that extent we will spend all the time we possibly can in the field service. Feeding our minds upon the truth and then acting upon it will Increase our appreciation and will result in more time in the field.

Then, too, our time in the field is helped or hindered by our planning or our lack of planning. Jehovah does not need to plan, as he is perfect in wisdom and his resources are unlimited. But our wisdom is imperfect, and our time, energy and means are very limited. The Society plans its work, and so should we. Everyone, therefore, should make a budget of his time, and stick to it. Children Should Be Returned to School

By law it is the duty of the state to provide educational facilities for the children residing in the nation. It is the duty of parents to have children of compulsory school age attend school to obtain the education thus provided.

In years past thousands of young witnesses for Jehovah have been denied the benefit of public-school education, and upon their parents has been placed a tremendous, unjust burden of educating their children at home or in private schools. This has been solely because such witnesses refused to salute the flag contrary to God’s commandments recorded at Exodus 20: 2-6.

The long and trying fight with the demonized enemy turned out in favor of Jehovah’s witnesses. The victory was given by Jehovah and his Son, the King Christ Jesus, because of the faithfulness of his witnesses to endure jiersecution and prove their integrity and unbreakable allegiance to Jehovah God and his law.

The favorable decision by the United States Supreme Court in West Virginia v. Barnette (319 U. S. --------, decided June 14, 1943), declaring the compulsory flag-salute laws invalid, should be accepted and followed by every school and school board of the nation. In order to place the responsibility upon such officials it is necessary that all children of compulsory school age be returned to the nearest school at ojiening of the next term so as to complete the prescribed course of education required by law. Each child will find it a privilege to testify of his faith in the Kingdom of Almighty God before his fellow students, and to give a witness in order that others may learn the way to everlasting life.

If the school board refuses enrollment or expels any child for refusal to participate in the flagsalute ceremonies, make a full report of the matter to the Society's legal desk, and the necessary petition forms and appropriate advice will be given.

If all school boards follow the Supreme Court decision, it should put to an end the burden that some brethren have been forced to bear in maintaining a “Kingdom School” to educate their children. The return of the children to the public school should relieve those consecrated brethren who are teaching such children, so that they can engage in full-time ministerial pioneer work from house to house.
