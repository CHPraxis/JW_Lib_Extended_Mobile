Free Education

Although stressed, this work was not started at the Assembly. The free education of the people in God’s Word has been going on for years, and must continue, and will continue, by the Lord’s grace. The talk on “Free Education for ‘Men of Good-Will’ ” brought to the fore that educational work with the Kingdom truth is the most essential instruction that can be given to them. This point should be burned into the heart and mind of everyone as he engages in the witness work.

Keep in mind always the points of the Declaration unanimously adopted at the Assembly. Briefly, they were to the effect that Jehovah God has made us free by his truth and through Christ; that our freedom will be used for no selfish purpose, but we must perform our commissioned duties by locating and educating “men of good-will” in the way to life; that our work is not subversive of present governments, but promotes full respect for law. order, and righteousness: that the textbook of freedom is the Bible; and that in the postwar era we will extend the publication of the Kingdom truth and increase the number of free Bible-study classes. “We are in favor of free education of all ‘men of good-will’ concerning The Theocracy.”

The thrilling release of the new book followed immediately, with the Society's president saying, “To everyone at this Assembly from five years old upward who will accept it in the spirit of this Declaration one copy of this book will be given free.” All of us received free copies under these conditions; therefore let us not forget the obligation which falls upon us, but let us go on with the free education of “men of good-will” in the "Textbook of Freedom”. Our book “The Truth Shall Make You Tree’’ will remind us daily to do this.

The final day of assembly stressed the freedoms and blessings promised in the New World of righteousness, which are so marvelous that the men of this “old world” dare not even promise such to the people in their so-called "new order”.

August Report

The August report is very encouraging. Some of the outstanding items only are mentioned here, because of lack of space. A new peak of full-time publishers is one of them; 6,84(5 reporting, being 85 over July, which was the previous peak. The average hours for company publishers is 18; which is the best we have had since June 1940. This shows real activity on the part of the publishers, as considerable time was taken up with activities in connection with the Assembly, which could not be reported as field service.

Its weakest point seems to be the average number of back-calls for the company publishers, which was only 2.8. If. in the 100 cities, the publishers’ devoting considerable time to locating rooms, working in conjunction with the preparation of territory, cafeteria, etc., for the “Free Nation's” Theocratic Assembly interfered to some extent with the August back-call work, then the September report should show a marked improvement because of the new interest created through Assembly activities’ being given attention by them. Undoubtedly the same stimulus indicated in the increased number of hours and of publishers will show up here from now on.
