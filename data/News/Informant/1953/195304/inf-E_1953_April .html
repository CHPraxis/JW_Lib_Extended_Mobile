Around the World with Jehovah’s Witnesses ‘Increasing and Yielding Fruit’ Increase of true worship is inevitable! Therefore we properly look for this increase and work for it. Attending Assemblies

Have you decided to attend the world assembly? Are you weighing the matter carefully? When you consider the cost, do not forget the blessings! The brothers in British Honduras attend assemblies at great effort and expense. (115) Will you have to walk 440 miles to attend the assembly, as brothers in Northern Rhodesia are willing to do? (227, 228) Do you feel that financial circumstances simply will not permit you to attend? The brothers in Italy are very poor, too, but they put forth the effort to attend assemblies. (196) Perhaps you should reconsider and plan more carefully, though it may mean a sacrifice in other directions.

Much depends on your appreciation for assemblies. Review the “Yearbook” account in the report from Tanganyika and ask yourself whether you appreciate assemblies as much as those brothers do. (231) The Kingdom work in Northern Rhodesia is growing by leaps and bounds. Undoubtedly it is due, at least in part, to the great appreciation for, and support of, assemblies. (227)

Do not underestimate the effect of assemblies, both upon those who attend and upon those who observe, hear or read about such gatherings. The report from Nigeria illustrates the point. (224) Encourage good-will interest to attend. In St Helena a boy and his father attended an assembly, their first meeting of any kind, and the boy said, “It was not as if we were on St. Helena, but in another world.” Good results followed. (265)

In faraway Chile the brothers immediately determined to be represented at the world assembly. (130) Will your efforts match those of the Canadian brothers? (128) Will you be there?


By Expanding Magazine Distribution

  • 1 Since Magazine Day was introduced in March 1949 magazines have been gradually replacing booklets. Companies were Instructed: “One day a week will be devoted to the distribution of The Watchtower and Awake! on the streets, from house to house, store to store and in other ways.” Pioneers and company publishers supported Magazine Day activity. What has resulted? The English Watchtower printing has Increased from 584,000 copies each issue to over 1,000,000 today: and the English Awake! from 545,000 to over 800,000. Has Magazine Day been successful in carrying the good news to many thousands? Most assuredly. Splendid work is being done, Kingdom publishers!

  • 2 However, there can be further expanding of magazine distribution, and all companies and individuals are invited to have a full share in this activity. Most companies are using Saturday for Magazine Day, with house-to-house or store-to-store work in the morning and street work in the afternoon and evening. If Saturday is not convenient, another day may be selected. Remember, too, store-to-store and house-to-house work, the main features of Magazine Day, may be done one day and street work another.

  • 3 Some publishers find that results of street work on Saturday afternoons are rather limited. Have you tried house-to-house distribution on Saturday afternoon? Might it not be that you will place more magazines at the homes than on the streets? Why not try it and compare results? Our object is to place magazines most effectively, and in your area working at the homes may be the way to do it Saturday afternoon.

  • 4 Some publishers, because it is not possible to get back to homes or stores every week, might find it advantageous to offer both The Watchtower and Awake! on a contribution of 10c. The two are readily placed on giving a short witness. Others have been successful in establishing routes with regular weekly visits, and these brothers may continue with such. However, when engaging in street work, feature only the latest copy of either magazine for 5c. (Both magazines may be carried, in the event they are needed.)

  • 5 Most companies have much house-to-house territory that is not frequently covered. Have you tried working some of this territory on Magazine Day with two magazines for 10c? Territory can be covered quickly and many magazines placed. The quick working of this territory with magazines can be co-ordinated and interchanged with the regular witnessing at other times so that no confusion results. Repeating on the same territory, perhaps in several months, offering two magazines again will result in placing many magazines. Rather than covering the same store area every week or two, why not branch out and include house-to-house territory? When completely covered with magazines it will be counted as a territory worked and should be marked as having been done with magazines. Territory and advertising servants can co-operate in arranging for working territory in this manner. Such magazine coverage should not interfere with the regular literature offer on Sunday and at other times.

  • 6 It is suggested that company book study groups use magazines in house-to-house work before the study, offering a Watchtower and Awake! for 10c. Why not let magazine work from house to house before the study be a regular part of the company book study arrangement? Many who cannot participate in Magazine Day activity will be able to share in magazine distribution through this arrangement.

  • 7 Whether young in the truth or a seasoned publisher, Magazine Day activity is very enjoyable and an easy avenue of service. Regularly join in offering The Watchtower and Awake! from house to house and store to store. It will increase the flow of good news to millions of potential “other sheep”.

YOUR SERVICE MEETING


The first demonstration below should be used as early as possible in April, possibly at the first service meeting. It supports striving for the 20 per cent peak. The second demonstration may be considered in connection with the notice on “1953 Vacation Pioneers’' on page four.


Worship in Holy Array’

SCENE I (4 min.) Company servant talks about recent Memorial celebration. Attendance figures are compared with number of publishers. It is evident many who attended Memorial are in need of closer association with Jehovah’s organization to join in “holy array” worship. He covers what has been done, or can be done, since Memorial night to help these new ones. This calls for love on the part of mature servants, a desire to assist men of good will to right worship. (2 Ki. 10: 15-28) April will be a busy month, because all companies are working for the 20 per cent peak. It can be reached if all servants, publishers and pioneers encourage and help one another, as well as new persons. Survey is made of company’s potential to reach the 20 per cent peak. Cards are compared with number of publishers for January, February and March. How many were irregular, did not report each month? What can be done to assist them? That is the matter for the company to work out. It requires cooperation.

SCENE 11 (7 min.) Assistant company servant raises his hand and is invited to come forward to tell what he is going to do during April. First, early in April, he will give each company book study conductor a list of publishers who did not get out in March. Then later on in April, between the 15th and 20th, another list will show all who have not turned in a report thus far for April. This will provide study conductors information with which to work. A company book study conductor then raises his hand and comes up to tell what he is going to do. On receiving the lists he will plan activity for his group, for he wants to do his part. He will be at contact point to take the lead and will call on and assist as many as he can. In fact, he makes clear this is to be done not only during April but every month, for it is his desire that all associated with his study become regular publishers. Regularity is important. (Heb. 13:15, NW) A mature publisher next puts his hand up and asks, “Brother, what can I do to assist?” He, too, is invited up and company book study conductor discusses with him how the two can work together and assist many. (Work in pioneers too, if you have them.)

SCENE Hl (5 min.) Is this arrangement practical? Company servant interviews publishers who have been assisted in this manner. Those who have become strong relate how they now arrange time each month to be regular publishers and, having been assisted, desire to assist others.—Acts 20: 35; Rom. 15: 1, 2, NW.

SCENE IV (4 min.) Company servant admonishes all publishers to make extra effort during April and months to follow to help newly associated ones, Memorial attenders and those being studied with, to come into closer fellowship and join regularly in worshiping Jehovah in holy array. More than a report is desired, as pointed out in the March “Informant”. ‘Ministers for the issue' is our goal. Join in “holy array” worship during April and assist others.

Vacation Pioneering

SCENE I (6 min.) Opens with publisher who has been assigned to prepare talk on vacation pioneering. He is talking out loud as he looks over and reviews material he has selected and prepared thus far. Before him are April 1, 1950, “Watchtower” and April 1951 “Informant”, etc. Quoting excerpts from “More and More Pioneers of Good News”, he proceeds to review briefly points covered in article dealing with urgency of times, reasons Society issues call for more pioneers. He remembers example set by Christ Jesus; recalls others. Then he relates conditions and requirements for entry, just who is urged to take up pioneer work and what must be considered before taking up pioneer work. Also discusses how divine support is forthcoming, how Jehovah provides and delivers. Next comes the matter of youthful publishers who can and will be pioneers, how they can be assisted and the benefits that come from doing this most favored work. Increases are noted in number of vacation pioneers: 1950, 858; 1951, 1,256; 1952, 1,807. What will it be in 1953? How many will come from our com-€any? To help them, perhaps it would e well to have an interview of some who were successful vacation pioneers or are prospective vacation pioneers.

SCENE It (4 mtn.) Same person is seen talking to ones selected for interview and is rehearsing with them. (Here companies that have not had any vacation pioneers may interview any who contemplate taking it up this year, or, if none, interview regular pioneers.) A series of short questions may be propounded which will permit material in Scene I to be highlighted by answers given in person’s own words.

SCENE III (5 min.) In closing, brother will ask vacation pioneers to relate experiences demonstrating blessings that come from this field of service. (If none locally, use “Yearbook”, pages 67, 96, 158, 234.) In conclusion he points out that such experiences can be the lot of those who can put in more time as pioneers. Then in summary he outlines who can enter this work: young and old, boys and girls, men and women, yes, housewives too. All are encouraged to try vacation pioneering. Vacation pioneer requirements: 1. Must be immersed, 2. Publisher for six months (before or after immersion), 3. Devote at least two months. How can this be done with a world assembly coming up in July? By planning now, starting early, striving for maximum of hours from very beginning. Many schools close in the last part of May, thus giving all of June, July and August. It can be done, and it is worth the effort. Will we have more vacation pioneers in 1953? The answer lies with you.
