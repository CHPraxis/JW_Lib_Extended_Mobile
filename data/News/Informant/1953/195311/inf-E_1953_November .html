Around the World with Jehovah’s Witnesses

Defeating Persecution

“We are persecuted, but not left in the lurch; we are thrown down, but not destroyed.” (2 Cor. 4: 9, NW) Just as this was true in the days of the early church, so it is true today. Jehovah does not abandon his people in their trials, nor can the adversary by persecution destroy them or remove them from the light of Jehovah’s favor. Persecution may come from many different sources, not the least of which will be the false religious guides. However, godly courage and persistence can defeat this persecution, as shown by the report from Belgium and India. (104, 105, 192)

Even political persecution can be turned to advantage and defeated if the brothers bear in mind that they are Jehovah’s witnesses and seize every opportunity to talk the truth to others. Our brothers in Hungary have done this. (189)

State discrimination against God’s servants and in favor of some other religious body does not discourage or stop us from making theocratic progress, as shown by the report from Taiwan. (205) Sometimes what appears to be a crushing blow at the hands of the enemy will actually prove to be a boon in the end if we patiently and calmly continue with our God-given work. (215, 216)

Persecution in one’s own family can be defeated too, often with happy results. (220) Personal bodily punishment and the seizure of personal possessions bring a great trial, but trust in Jehovah will enable one to defeat this persecution too. (262) Persecution will not destroy the New World society under the leadership of its victorious King, who himself defeated all persecution.

Modern Gamaliels

“Do not meddle with these men, but let them alone.” That was the wise advice Gamaliel of old times gave to those who would interfere with the preaching of the good news. (Acts 5'38, NW) In modern times there are also honest persons who render assistance to Jehovah’s servants by exercising a similar influence upon would-be disrupters of new-world activity. (197)

In Syria a Moslem sheik intervened in behalf of a pioneer brother who was being held in prison and used his influence to effect the brother’s release. (210)

Often the effort is made by opposers to induce the law enforcement agencies to interfere with the witness activity, sometimes with a measure of success. But there are still persons who are not afraid to stand for what is right, as shown by the “Yearbook” report from Peru. (244) The same is seen in Switzerland. (274)

At times favorable intervention to help overcome distressing problems comes from unexpected sources, as in one instance in Ecuador. (148) In Libya there are persons who regard the witnesses with a “let them alone” attitude and who are not afraid to say so despite pressure from the clergy. (152, 153) Swaziland and other African countries have tribal chiefs who have taken a stand against unwarranted interference by the clergy. (266) It is prudent for all who contemplate opposition to ponder well the warning of Gamaliel to desist from interference with these ministers of good news, “otherwise, you may perhaps be found fighters actually against God.”

YOUR SERVICE MEETING

The first demonstration illustrates how “Make Sure of All Things” can be effectively used on a back-call. The second demonstration highlights talking points that may be used in presenting the “New World Translation of the Christian Greek Scriptures” this month.


SCENE I (7 min.) Mature publisher “A” has new or less mature publisher “B” along- on back-call in home of Mr. and Mrs. Goodwill. Mr. Goodwill remarks that he has wanted to know more about Jehovah’s witnesses and their organization. He has heard others say that “they claim only they are right.” “A” responds that many persons say that without knowing facts about Jehovah’s witnesses or what the Bible has to say on God’s witnesses, and takes out “Make Sure of All Things” and reads aloud opening paragraph of the Foreword. “B” follows in his copy. “A” reasons along lines found on page 5 and suggests they find basis for religion advocated by Jehovah’s witnesses by letting the Bible speak for itself. Checking contents of “Make Sure of All Things” “Jehovah’s Witnesses” is located on page 193. “A” gives high points found in definition, points to Christ as foremost witness, selecting appropriate and key scriptures, and has “B” and Mr. and Mrs. Goodwill take turns reading. High points of scriptures are emphasized as discussion proceeds. “A” tells of prominent men in past who served as faithful witnesses. Jehovah, not men, is Founder and Christ is Leader; not a political government. (Pp. 195, 196) Moving along, “A” outlines work of Kingdom ambassadors—not conversion, but preaching —and says that each witness is a minister. (P. 197) This leads to life and many will yet join in this most important work. (P. 198) In conclusion “A” emphasizes to Mr. and Mrs. Goodwill the importance of adhering to God’s Word for complete and reliable information, and arranges to return next week to further discuss the ministry of Jehovah’s witnesses.

SCENE II (7 min.) Next week finds the two publishers ready to take up subject of the ministry of Jehovah’s witnesses not completed last time. With “Make Sure of All Things” in hand “A” turns to “Minister of God.” (P. 263) Scriptures are noted and read that tell of God’s Chief Minister and also pre-Christian ministers. (This is found under “Women” and “Children” in the “Subjects for Study” section.) Preaching is a must and there is no retirement for God’s true ministers. (P. 267) Flock is God’s, not man’s. Various forms of preaching are briefly touched on as “A” discusses added information found under “Street Work” and “Conducting Studies.” (Pp. 269, 141) He explains what comprises a congregation and why ministers use literature in their preaching. (P. 270) “B” adds that ministers of Jehovah do not profit from or peddle God’s Word, giving proof texts. Mr. and Mrs. Goodwill admit Scripturalness for all to be ministers and that they understand the preaching work of Jehovah’s witnesses better. “A” brings out “Let God Be True” and points to chapter “Who Are Jehovah’s Witnesses?” and arranges to call back next week for consideration of it.

SCENE III (2 min.) Conductor sums up value and use of “Make Sure of All Things”. It is a convenient reference to use when answering questions and clarifying doctrines. Use it always.
