Awakening the Sleepers

Arouse Slumberers to New World Alertness


Use Awake! with Premium

1 Asleep In the deep night of the old world are many millions of persons who have no knowledge of God’s Word of truth. And the same is true of the vast majority of mankind today when it comes to knowing the facts behind what goes on in the world. These persons need the truth, which will act as an alarm clock to awaken and alert them to world conditions and the cause and remedy for universal distress. It is the privilege of New World society members to actively serve in awakening the sleepers from their slumber. Everlasting sleep awaits them if they are not aroused now and helped to favorable new-world activity.—Eph. 5:16, NW.

2 During October, to make the offer of a one-year subscription on a contribution of $1 even more attractive, three booklets will be given free. Publishers will remit 90 cents for each new subscription. With such an excellent and varied magazine as Awake! to offer, it should not be very difficult for each congregation publisher to obtain at least one subscription, and pioneers, four. In the campaign last year United States publishers, 132,640 of them, obtained 107,394 subscriptions. Now, with approximately 150,000 publishers, how many subscriptions do you think will be taken? Shall we be able to make it an all-time peak? Last year, by Jehovah’s undeserved kindness, we increased our hours in the field and rose well above the previous peak of 78,888 subscriptions set in October of 1950. Over 28,500 more! Will we do better this time?

A Good Start Necessary

3 We can and will if each publisher will get off to a good start this year. Yes, hit it hard right from October 1, right from the very first door we call on, and keep stressing Awake! down to the last day of the month. Engage in house-to-house ministry every Sunday, and offer Awake! at every door.

4 Be familiar with the magazine’s contents and thus use the awakening message most effectively. In this way more and more people will become acquainted with Awake! and be alerted by its enlightening and eye-opening features. Use the material found in its pages, which you enjoy so much, to arouse the interest of the person to whom you are presenting Awake! Phrase the points found in it in your own words and see the effect as you enthusiastically and convincingly present it. Remember, you will have two issues to work with this month, designed to appeal to all persons.

5 Do we not feel and know that the old world needs to have an alarm sounded? Are not world conditions such that they practically shout aloud the sadly neglected condition of the people? Why not obey the command to “awaken the sleepers” by helping them to meet, see, obtain and read Awake! and appreciate its full value. Offer it to everyone and at every opportunity. Help them to awaken and see the light of the new-world day that leads to life. Awake! is a valuable stimulant for a drowsy world. Use it to best advantage during October.

Keeping the Goal in View

This is October, the second month of the new service year. Remember that our goal each year is to achieve a 10 per cent average increase by the end of December, and a 20 per cent peak by the end of April. This increase is to be based upon the previous year’s average number of publishers. By Jehovah’s undeserved kindness, we reached this goal last year. It will require effort and planning on the part of all servants and publishers if we are going to do so this year. Keep the goal in view! Let everyone be a house-to-house preacher!

Clean Worship

“Therefore, since we have these promises, beloved ones, let us cleanse ourselves of every defilement of flesh and spirit, perfecting holiness in God's fear.” (2 Cor. 7: 1, NW) Members of the New World society are careful to conform to the high standards, both moral and spiritual, that Jehovah sets forth in his Word. (102)

The “Yearbook” report from Costa Rica shows that there is real pleasure and blessing in bringing one’s course of living into harmony with Jehovah’s requirements. (136) There must be firmness on the part of the organization in aiding newly associated persons to see and meet these requirements, so that the spiritual health of the congregations is not impaired by contamination with old-world standards. (155) And yet there should be a kindly spirit of helpfulness in dealing with persons who are just learning of the new world and who need assistance in these matters. (179)

If anyone feels that his problems are too great to be straightened out, refer to the report from Nicaragua and see how Jehovah blessed the action taken by individual brothers in that land. (222)

We should never conclude that holding to the Bible standards will in any way retard the growth of the organization. Rather, just the opposite will be true, as shown by the report from Mexico. (211, 213) In Chile the determination to hold close to Jehovah’s standards did not hinder progress at all last year. (129) Jehovah blesses those individuals and congregations that practice clean worship. It is the only worship that has his approval. —Also see comments on pages 121, 200, 235 and 246.

Suffering for Righteousness

“For if someone because of conscience toward God bears up under afflictions and suffers unjustly, this is a thing agreeable with God.” (1 Pet. 2: 19-25, NW) This means following the example and course of Christ Jesus, which is the way of gaining Jehovah’s approval. Consider the report from Cyprus and see how our brothers there continue undaunted by violent persecution. (142, 143)

When one appreciates the great issue now before all creation and he understands that reproach from the Devil’s old world is to be expected, then he is not dismayed and discouraged by such experiences. He realizes that, in the long run, such works beneficially to himself and others of good will who observe his stand for what is right.—See the report from Dominican Republic, pages 146 and 147.

Even in violent, false-religious strongholds persecution of Jehovah's servants brings the great issue to the fore and causes many honest ones to make a right choice. It is certainly so in Greece today, all efforts to the contrary notwithstanding. (175) It must be borne in mind that opposition in modern times does not always mean “goat,” as it did not mean so in the case of Saul of Tarsus. (220, 221)

It is true, as in the days of the early church, that heated opposition that scatters our brothers often means a wider witness is given. And the courageous attitude of God’s servants in standing firm against satanic villainy sometimes moves to theocratic action those who have long stood on the sidelines and watched. (249)

YOUR SERVICE MEETING

The first demonstration below features the presentation of “Awake!” The second demonstration illustrates the use of “Make Sure of All Things” in


house-to*house ministry.

SCENE I (4 min.) Chairman discusses “Awake!” campaign, using issues of “Informant” of past few years to show what was accomplished. He stresses point that dignity of message requires clean, neat dress and thorough preparation of presentation. Lasting impressions are made by first contact of witness.

SCENE II (4 min.) Neat, clean, friendly-looking publisher is seen approaching door. Businesslike, his presentation in mind, he prepares to meet householder. Rings bell, waits patiently and then cheerfully greets householder as door opens: “Good morning. I’m Mr. ---, and I’m calling

on the people who are interested in improving their understanding of world conditions and seeing more peaceful times on earth. As an honest-hearted person, I am sure you would like to see a righteous world, wouldn’t you?” Householder responds favorably. Publisher continues: “I have found a magazine that gives much information along such lines and I would like you to see it. ‘Awake’’ is the name and it costs only $1 a year.” He produces latest copy and begins to point out how “Awake!” constantly supplies important information concerning world conditions and directs people to God’s Word for hope. To housewives it appeals with articles on food, sewing and fashions; for students, articles on science, medicine, geography and history; for everyone, stories on the wonderful things of nature and interesting customs of other peoples “But we have so many magazines we will not be able to read, it,” householder objects. Publisher tactfully shows that “Awake!” takes little time to read, yet, unlike the great majority of other magazines, offers real hope. Householder subscribes. After publisher leaves she is heard remarking on courtesy, neatness and intelligent presentation of witness. She will look forward to receiving “Awake!”

SCENE III (4 min.) Publisher is shown just concluding his weekly study with a family group. Speaking to head of the home, he asks, “Did you read the article on ‘Hypnotism’ in the last copy of ‘Awake!’ I left you?” Whlen a negative answer is received, publisher quickly brings out his copy and points out that hypnotism through conditioning can be used to induce persons to commit crimes; that confessions have been gained by the Communists through hypnotism and that it can otherwise serve the schemes of evil men. Husband and wife quickly recognize value of knowing this. Wife then mentions that she appreciated information on ineffectiveness of chlorophyll products in same issue. Schoolboy son mentions he likes “Watching the World,” ‘because it makes it easy to gather news for the current events part of his history class.’ Publisher then brings out other copies to display articles on science, nature study, etc., that would help him in school. He says, “Everyone in the family can enjoy ‘Awake!’ because it’s written for young and old alike, and it costs only a dollar to get 24 copies delivered to your home each year. It costs so little, and gives you so much; you can’t afford to be without it.” Family discuss its value and then the father subscribes.

CONCLUSION (2 min.) Summary by chairman. Emphasize making subscription presentation at every door.

SCENE 1 (3 min.) Publisher working from house to house with “Awake!” subscription offer is confronted by householder who, although having read several copies of the magazine, objects to it because it speaks against other religions. He feels that there is some good in all religions, that all are merely traveling different roads to the same happy end, and that it would be much better for all religions to work together and try to exert a good influence on people who do not go to church. He claims that “Awake!” serves to divide rather than to unite people for a good cause. Publisher states that if householder wants to believe there is good in all religions, that is his right, but the Bible shows differently. “Where?” inquires householder. “Well, I don’t know exactly, but I know there are many scriptures that show God does not approve all religions, but only the true worship,” answers publisher. But the publisher is just not prepared to give a convincing witness, and nothing worth while is accomplished.

SCENE II (6 min.) Second publisher, who is equipped with his copy of “Make Sure of All Things”, calls at a home and receives substantially the same argument from householder. He expresses himself as glad that householder has taken time to read several issues of “Awake!” and also says that he is pleased to have householder’s honest opinion of the magazine. Then he takes out his copy of “Make Sure of All Things” and turns to “Interfaith,” starting with page 187. He points out to householder that there are only two roads to travel, a right and a wrong, and inquires if householder has read these particular scriptures on the subject. He goes on to show (p. 186) that merely taking the name of God is not sufficient to get to the “same happy end.” In fact, the “many roads, one destiny” idea is a misleading principle that can keep one from true worship and is really only another way to describe the broad road that leads to destruction. (P. 186)

Jesus would not recognize religious leaders as “brothers,” nor did he overlook differences as “petty” and refrain from pointing out the shortcomings of professed servants of God. (P. 185) It is impossible to compromise by “straddling the fence,” because this would not please God. (P. 184) That is why “Awake!” must honestly state the truth regarding other religions, as well as adhere to true worship. But note that the magazine always compares religious teachings or pronouncements with the Bible, to show people the inconsistency and let them judge for themselves. A full discussion of facts never hurts those following the truth. Thus householder’s objections are answered and publisher goes on to make presentation of “Awake!” subscription.

SCENE III (3 min.) Conductor emphasizes need to be familiar with contents of “Make Sure of All Things” and have it with you in field so that it can help in overcoming obstacles and in giving a successful witness where you might otherwise not do so. It is valuable in many kinds of situations, but you have to know what is in it to use it to best advantage.

Pioneers Increasing

Since the world assembly more and more pioneers are being added to the list every day. Have you written for your pioneer application yet? Have you reviewed your position to see whether you can arrange to devote 100 hours a month to field activity? If so, then you should be on the pioneer list. Why not plan to start November 1 by requesting a pioneer application now?

When a pioneer moves from one country to another and intends to be gone for a period of six months or less, he should send his reports back to the Society’s branch office in his home country. If he is going to be gone for more than six months, he should immediately request the Society to transfer his pioneer records to the branch office in the country to which he is going. Additionally, he will write a letter to the Society in that country upon arrival, advising of his plans; and then he will send his monthly reports to that office. Missionaries, of course, always proceed as directed by the president’s office.
