“Baptizing Them’’

“Go therefore and make disciples of people of all the nations, baptizing them,’’ said Jesus; and he promised that he would back up and support such activity on the part of his followers. (Matt. 28: 19, NW) The “Yearbook” shows that this baptizing work is being done in all nations, the world over. In the Far East, for example. (132) In Latin America. (135) In the islands of the sea. (87) AU of this marks a transformation in the lives of people, for it means they set out upon a new pattern of living.

While individual congregations sometimes hold their own baptismal services, assemblies of Jehovah’s people Srovide splendid occasions for a public eclaration of one’s dedication. (151, 269) Sometimes it is the circuit servant himself who assists, either by giving the baptismal talk or actually doing the immersing. (178, 221)

Reports show, too, that a person does not necessarily have to study for a year or longer to see and appreciate the need for baptism. In the Hashemite Kingdom of the Jordan new publishers are baptized after three months of study. (209) In Colombia it is the same. (134)

Even ancient customs that allow women little freedom do not now prevent baptism on the part of new sisters. (208) Persons baptized often need continued help to keep in service regularly until they are spiritually strong and able to stand on their own feet, as shown by the report from Finland. (159, 160) Does the increase in your congregation produce candidates for baptism from time to time? Do not fail to help new ones appreciate this important step and understand what it means.

Theocratic Tactfulness

“Prove yourselves cautious as serpents and yet innocent as doves” was part of the instruction Jesus gave to his twelve disciples. (Matt. 10: 16, NW) There are all kinds of situations encountered in preaching the good news that require tact. At times it is the tactful reply that will close the mouths of those who cause us trouble, as shown by the report from Greece. (176) Again, what sometimes appears at first to be a closed mind may be opened by skillful use of tact. (114)

Witnessing in some parts of the world is very difficult because of the strong hold of false religion upon the people. Such circumstances require perseverance, consideration of the living and thinking habits of the people, and use of tact. Have you had to work under conditions similar to those our brothers cope with' in Eire? (119, 120)

Perhaps you have come across homes where servants always answer your knock. In Hong Kong the brothers have a rather novel way of overcoming the servant barrier, appealing to the curiosity of the householder himself. Perhaps it would work at times for you. (187)

When working in a strongly religious community where you meet with a constant barrage of sharp answers, do you try changing your tactics in order to achieve better results? See how the brothers in Finland were successful in this at one home. (159)

To know when to speak and when not to speak, what to say and what not to say, requires keen mental perception. We can learn much from the experiences of our brothers.

YOUR SERVICE MEETING

The first demonstration below will help publishers present the Bible and book combination in September, giving an effective short talk at the doors. The second demonstration deals with maturity and the necessity for publishers to be regular in house-to-house ministry.


SCENE I (6 min.) Publisher introduces himself to householder and presents “New World Translation of the Christian Greek Scriptures” and book offer. However, householder remarks that he has a Bible and has tried to read it, but could not understand what he read and would therefore hardly be interested in obtaining anything else. Publisher immediately sees opportunity to show outstanding value of "New World Translation” and book as an aid to this person. He tells householder that his remark about not understanding the Bible reminds him of similar incident in the Bible where a God-fearing person expressed the same thing in substance, and how Jehovah caused a servant of his to meet this person and aid him in getting a proper understanding of the Bible. By this time the publisher has opened a copy of the “New World Translation” and begins to read the account in Acts 8: 26-35 concerning Philip’s aiding the Ethiopian eunuch in understanding the prophecy of Isaiah.

Publisher shows similarity of work of God’s servants at this time and how the ''New World Translation” and book are aids toward getting a clear and correct understanding of God’s purposes. To illustrate he reads 2 Peter 3: 5-7 (NW) and then shows briefly how chapter XXI in “Let God Be True” will help in understanding. Householder appreciates point of illustration and is anxious to contribute for Bible and book combination.

SCENE li (6 min.) Publisher presents “New World Translation” and book offer to householder, whereupon householder says he has his own Bible, the “King James Version.” Publisher points out that the “King James Version” was written in 1611 in the archaic English of Shakespeare’s day. He asks householder if he finds it much easier to read and understand his daily newspaper than one of Shakespeare’s plays in Old English with its “thees,” “thous,” “thys,” and words such as “forbear,” “curst,” “giveth,” etc A comparision of the text at Matthew 5: 22 in the “King James Version” with that of the “New World Translation” is suggested by the publisher. He points out the clarity of expression and meaning in the “New World Translation” and then shows how footnote at bottom of page aids in better understanding the text. Through the footnote an additional feature, the appendix, can be evaluated by its information on the word “Gehenna,” page 766.

Publisher gives short talk on Revelation 21: 1-4 and ties it in with “Let God Be True”, chapter XXII He briefly shows scope of book and explains how it compares Scripture texts and helps in understanding. Householder agrees to take offer for $2 contribution.

SCENE III (3 min.) Summary by conductor to emphasize presenting the offer at every door.

SCENE I (10 min.) Conductor emphasizes the need for each publisher to display maturity by being a regular house-to-house publisher during the new service year. Many have failed in the past year and may need assistance in this year from the regular publishers who have overcome their problems. All can become regular publishers through the proper approach to the problem Irregular publisher in audience raises his hand and objects, “But brother how can I be a regular publisher when my secular work keeps me busy until late every day and Sunday is my only day with my family?” The question is referred to the audience and they offer such suggestions as, “Take your family with you in the service on Sunday. If they do not believe, then you will set the proper example before them by going in service yourself, and your example of faithfulness and regular worship may assist them. When we were going to church we went regularly each Sunday, or at least once or twice a month; so we should be able to spend the same time or more in proper worship Perhaps you can locate a different job so you can have more free time, etc.” Brother expresses thanks for suggestions and says he will try to do better.

A sister raises her hand and says, “I have four children and an unbelieving husband, so I find it almost impossible to get out.” Audience responds. “The sisters meet during the week; you could work with them, taking one or two children with you while the others are in school; a sister offers to stay with the very young children while she goes in service during week; perhaps husband will care for children; must be example of devotion to Jehovah, as serving him means eternal life for children as well as you; no greater heritage for children than ministry, etc.” (“Watchtower,” 1/15/52)

A third irregular publisher raises his hand and says, “I put in a few hours for four months, but the other months, I don’t know why, but I just didn’t do any service. Can you help me?” Audience responds- “Arrange a schedule of service and study and keep it, setting aside specific times to engage in service. Attend congregation book studies and work with group. Brother from his area offers to assist by calling and taking him along in his territory, etc.” All are thankful for assistance. Other practical suggestions may be offered to assist the ones suffering from irregularity.

SCENE II (5 min.) Conductor sums up the information gained from discussing the problems of three irregular publishers, pointing out how we must evaluate properly our dedication to Jehovah in relation to our secular occupations and other problems. He notes that the ones who work closely with the organization, lean on Jehovah for his spirit’s guidance and study Jehovah’s Word regularly at home and at the meetings are the ones who are spiritually healthy, mature, regular publishers. Close co-operation between the regular and irregular ones makes the congregation stronger. Real diligent effort must be put forth by all, but each one’s being a regular house-to-house publisher in the new service year will bring blessings of expansion to the organization and maturity to its members.
