Around the World with Jehovah’s Witnesses “Publicly and from House to House” To “worship Jehovah in holy array” requires his dedicated servants to bear united witness to his name. Visiting people at their homes and talking to them about the Kingdom is the primary way of doing this. The 1953 “Yearbook” shows that house-to-house preaching is vitally essential.

In Colombia a trip to an isolated town and baptism of nine new witnesses were followed immediately by two hours of house-to-house preaching, with the new ones in the forefront. (135) Brazil’s report demonstrates that new publishers take hold of door-to-door work readily. (110) Even a retired army officer in Libya does not shun house-to-house activity. (153) It is recognized as a mark of God’s true servants, as demonstrated in the Gold Coast and Hong Kong reports. (172, 186)

Pushing house-to-house preaching to the forefront brings many blessings. In Honduras the brothers find a great increase in their service averages because of this (184) With servants taking the lead in this feature of Kingdom work in Jamaica good results are forthcoming. (199) House-to-house preaching does much to stimulate interest in the truth, even compelling clergy to go from door to door in an effort to counteract the good news in Puerto Rico. (251)

For most publishers house-to-house activity is not as hard as for one brother in the Virgin Islands. (253) At assemblies, too, all publishers should engage in house-to-house activity, as the brothers in Surinam do. (269) Even those who have never had contact with the company service organization see the need for house-to-house preaching in Costa Rica (137) Indeed, how else would the good news be preached m all the inhabited earth?

“Gathering of Ourselves Together”

Fear of Jehovah as Sovereign prompts all servants to attend his table by giving diligent attention to meetings. This means recognizing, conforming to and appreciating the organization God is using. How much do you appreciate meetings? As much as brothers in Costa Rica who brave bad weather and hours of walking to reach the meeting place? (137)

If you have been in the truth a long time, do you have the same view oi meetings as our fourteen new brothers in Zacapa, Guatemala, who have a 100 per cent meeting attendance? (177) One must always guard against losing appreciation of meetings.

When you have opportunity to attend a meeting in a peaceful and quiet Kingdom Hall or other auditorium, think of our brothers in the Luangwa Valley area of Northern Rhodesia, where the roaring of lions is great enough to drown out their singing #of Kingdom songs around the campfire. (228) Do you have such threats to your meetings?

Have you ever been impressed with the importance of taking an active part in meetings you attend? Has it occurred to you how you might help others? At a company book study in Panama a man with poor eyesight called upon the brothers in attendance to comment so that he and others might be enlightened. (240) Another man of good will in Panama desired a study twice weekly, of two hours’ duration. In two weeks his questions were answered. The third week he and his family commenced attending the “Watchtower” study. By the fifth week he was going in service. His zeal rapidly increases because he prepares for meetings. (241) Do you appreciate meetings that much?

YOUR SERVICE MEETING

Analysis of the company’s past “Watchtower” campaign activity will focus attention on the goal for 1953 and what must be done to meet the quota. The discussion-interview below will provide good points to help the company. The demonstration (second below) emphasizes the proper mental attitude on the part of each publisher, with appreciation of “The Watchtower's” importance and need to present offer.


SCENE I (6 min.) The company committee, determined to make this “Watchtower” campaign the best yet, enthusiastically consider the 15th annual “Watchtower” campaign. By use of the “Informant” file, past copies of “Yearbook” and company records, they delight to note continued increase over the years. The first campaign in 1939 yielded 93,111 subscriptions, to compare with last year’s bountiful harvest of 264,159. Other points of interest are noted, with due credit given to Jehovah’s efficient educational and training organization. Attention is now directed to the local company organization. Past campaigns are considered, with emphasis on last year’s results compared with the country as a whole Did the company make its quota? How many publishers actually obtained one or more subscriptions? A study of the Publisher’s Record cards reveals, generally, that those who regularly engaged in the door-to-door work met or exceeded the quota. However, some cards showing fair hours also show surprisingly low figures for subscriptions. Why? One of committee suggests interviewing publishers who were successful and from them learn what is necessary in addition to spending time in the field to obtain subscriptions.

SCENE II (8 min.) Each one of committee selects publisher card and interviews publisher. (Factual interview on basis of card would be best here, if possible.) The following points should be brought out and established in the interviews: 1. Work from door to door and make the full offer at every door. Never get in habit of judging householder and deciding what you think should be offered. 2. Good presentation. Do not wait until at the door to decide what is going to be said. Prepare yourself for effective introduction. 3. Know your offer. Be familiar with contents so points of interest can be shown to householder to gain attention. 4. Proper mental attitude. It is the most important magazine published, a blessing to those righteously disposed. 5. Regularity in field work. Sunday morning door-to-door work supported. Put forth effort by being in field to make offer and Jehovah blesses efforts. 6. Subscription conscious. Back-calls and Bible studies also open up good avenues. Friends, neighbors, relatives often subscribe. 7. Show proper fear of Jehovah. We are his witnesses and prove love by regularly engaging in service. What offer could be better than “The Watchtower” which ‘announces his kingdom’.

SCENE III (4 min.) Committee sums up results of discussion. Agrees if all would have these seven points in mind publishers would be privileged to obtain subscriptions. Publishers encouraged to prepare individually and work closely with local company, support Sunday morning group work and other local activities. Make individual effort to do even better on subscriptions than in recent October “Awake!” campaign. “The Watchtower” is the more important instrument and should therefore receive even more enthusiastic support.

SCENE I (3 min.) Servant calls on Brother Hesitant, who was weak in last year’s “Watchtower” campaign. Hesitant places fair number of books but is low on subscriptions. Brother Hesitant says he can do all right with books, something he can place right on the spot; but to place a year’s subscription is something he just cannot seem to put across unless, perhaps, on a really good Bible study. Servant points out that his trouble is in not presenting the offer; if he did it as with books he would succeed. Also, it was noticed that since Brother Hesitant did not appreciate the “Watchtower” campaign, he neglected house-to-house work these months and engaged in other features of service. Servant says that the service meeting this week will be just the thing Hesitant needs.

SCENE II (10 min.) Brother at service meeting gives illustrated talk on “Watchtower” campaign. Shows that marvelous instruments God has given us all play their part, but “The Watchtower” is outstanding. It has served us faithfully without a break for 73 years Where would we be today without it? Being a periodical, its fresh, live material helps us meet the issues that face us every day, current issues. Then speaker recounts some history of “The Watchtower”: Its first issues appeared in July 1879, with circulation of 6,000 copies; then in 1904, 25,000 copies; 1928, 120,000; 1946, 500,000; now 1,500,000 copies of each issue in 36 languages! Enormous jump in circulation in a few years shows how hungry people are for its message. As these figures are discussed, other brothers or sisters (perhaps from small to large in stature) may appear with cards bearing the above-mentioned circulation figures for each period. (Example: size of 1879 card, 2" x 2%"; 1904, 4" x 5"; 1928, 9" x 11"; 1946, 18" x 22"; 1953, 30" x 38". At any rate, sheets of paper graduated in size will serve to illustrate circulation growth.) While these cards or sheets are being shown, the speaker can weave in remarks about outstanding truths “The Watchtower” has set forth progressively from 1879: end of “times of the Gentiles”, “millions now living will never die,” “Christ’s enthronement,” “higher powers,” “new name,” “great multitude,” “fill the earth,” “princes,” and the many vital truths of recent months showing us the way of proper living in this wicked world. Purpose of talk will be to show vital need of “The Watchtower” for the millions of people of good will today.

Travel to the World Assembly

It is very gratifying to see that many circuits, companies and individuals are responding to the privilege previously announced of making donations to the Society for the purpose of bringing to the 1953 world assembly our brothers from foreign lands who otherwise would not be able to attend. Such contributions should be sent to Watch Tower Bible and Tract Society, Treasurer’s Office, 124 Columbia Heights, Brooklyn 1, N. Y. They should not be included with any other remittance or Good Hopes donation but should be sent by separate check or money order.

All persons interested in the New World society are planning so that, if at all possible, they will be in New York July 19 to 26, 1953, for the world assembly. Such persons now prudently begin to save funds for travel to New York. Jehovah blesses the planning efforts of his servants to advance true worship in the earth, and surely individual planning and saving for participation in assemblies of his people meets with his favor. Unquestionably his chosen people of old had to plan, prepare and save to make the annual trips to Jerusalem to participate in theocratic worship. It should be so today in connection with modern assemblies of Jehovah’s people. Do not depend on others to provide for you, nor expect a miracle to make the trip possible. Plan, save and work for the privilege of attending.

In due course the Society will have suggestions on transportation.
