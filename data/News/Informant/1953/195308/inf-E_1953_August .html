The Greater Happiness of Giving Show New-World Appreciation by Service


Publishers with Full Hearts Return to Work

  • 1 Pause, take time to consider: Who upon this planet have greater cause for gratitude than Jehovah’s witnesses and their good-will companions? At the recent New World Society Assembly the great happy Giver of the universe, Jehovah, opened the windows of heaven and poured out upon his people a blessing beyond their capacity to contain. Truly there are no people on earth so blessed as we are.

  • 2 However, this bountiful blessing places a great responsibility upon us. Are we going to emulate the great and happy Giver, Jehovah, by reflecting to others that which we have seen and heard at the assembly? We know there is no salvation anywhere apart from the New World society. Into this great sheepfold Jehovah is gathering his other sheep and we have an obligation to see that they are searched out. properly received, nourished and strengthened. With this in view, at the assembly he wonderfully equipped us for the work. We will receive greater happiness by thus administering to the needs of these other sheep. Are we now living up to theocratic responsibilities that this assembly placed upon every dedicated servant of the theocracy? This question should be carefully weighed by each of us.

  • 3 During August the chief presentation to the people will be three books and three booklets, on a contribution of $1.50.   • 4 August is the concluding month of our service year. Let us make it a high note of thanksgiving and praise to Jehovah by each one's having a part in the service. Do not fail to preach from house to house. Establish a goal of one set of three books for each congregation publisher, and four sets for each pioneer.

Finish Your Territory

  • 5 Some of you have unassigned territory to finish. Others have rural territory that lias not yet been worked. Now is the time to go into such territories. Some congregations are limited to their own city territory, but they will be just as active. “There is more happiness in giving than there is in receiving.” So let each one give by being a publisher in August.

Six Months of Happy Giving

Since January, 1953, publishers in the United States have had an especially happy time in preaching the good news. We have all enjoyed great blessings at Jehovah’s hand, because, since January, we have been well above our national quota of 139,289 publishers. Starting in January we took a big jump to 141,532; then to 143,004; to 149,501 and then to 153,624. In May we had 150,660 and in June 145,345, still well above our quota. Surely we all appreciate this marvelous expansion, and the Society is grateful to Jehovah for the opportunity of serving and working with so many happy givers. May we continue together in our joyful service and, by Jehovah’s undeserved kindness, let us push the good news into every corner of this earth until our great God says It is done.

Around the World with Jehovah’s Witnesses

Unassigned Territory

Truly “there is more happiness in giving than there is in receiving/’ and publishers around the world experienced this to a large measure in working unassigned territory. (Acts 20: 35, NW) In the United States last year a tremendous expanse of territory was Worked with thrilling results. (64, 65) In Canada more than 80 per cent of available territory was assigned last summer and many persons were greatly helped. (127)

The working of unassigned territory often requires traveling over great distances, but the results achieved in scattering seed are well worth the effort. Consider the report from the missionary schooner “Sibia.” (87) In working unassigned territory do you have to travel 1,000 miles from your home congregation? They do in Australia, but the results are splendid. (93, 94)

In British Guiana brothers working unassigned territory had to wade waist deep in water to push their boat over rapids and reach the people, but they were rewarded with sincere appreciation for their efforts. (113) In France it was remarked that the brothers were working like the apostles, twelve having gone to one town. French publishers slept in barns and bartered in placing literature, but much territory was covered. (162, 163) South Africans hitchhiked and "walked to unassigned territory when no other means was available. (259)

Publishers in Austria worked overtime to earn money for trips into unassigned territory. (100) See what the brothers in Cuba did to reach their territory. (139) Are you having part in the unassigned territory work in August?

Choosing New-World Life

Everywhere people are looking toward the new world and taking their stand for it. They see the issue quickly and realize their privilege of preaching the good news about the new world. In Ethiopia newly interested persons bring others to meetings with them on the third day after they themselves hear the message. And some, having had only a few weeks’ contact with the organization, take the seed to interior parts of the country. (74) In places where heretofore there were no native preachers, they are now springing up and taking a strong stand for the truth, as in Guam. (78)

Often it takes only a reading of the publications to enable them to see that they must become part of Jehovah's organization. (79, 134) In some instances persons need only one contact with Jehovah’s witnesses to see their privilege of taking part in the preaching work. (167, 195, 208)

Even some religious leaders are seeing the necessity to abandon their former false teachings and come over to the side of the truth, as the Korean report shows. (81, 82) The same is true in the Gold Coast. (172) In one case it was the congregation chart with all its figures that stimulated a religious “elder” to take a real interest in the truth. (260)

The work Jehovah’s servants do in isolated places also bears fruit in helping persons make the right choice. (127) And special pioneers often have great joy in seeing people break away from false religion and take a firm stand for the new world, despite pressure to do otherwise. (101)

YOUR SERVICE MEETING

The first demonstration below is a refresher on the assembly and how the good things received there might be used in our service, particularly with persons of good will. The second demonstration below will present talking points on the three-book offer.


Convention Benefits

SCENE I (5 min.) What good did the convention do you? Will it help you bear good fruit in Kingdom service? Let us look at the Bible’s description of an assembly of old, with its results. (2 Chron. 30 24-31: 1) This scene may be a father speaking with his family, or a speaker may give a talk, reviewing briefly some of the blessings of the New World Society Assembly at New York, and showing the great impetus given to the advance of Jehovah’s worship by an assembly.

SCENE II (5 min.) Let us watch how two different publishers are affected. One makes a back-call. Conducting a Bible study, he ends it and the good-will person asks him how he fared on his New York trip. He then mentions how many were there, that there were some very good speeches and that it was a wonderful trip. Then he tells a little about New York sights. He promises to bring a printed report later. No real, specific truths or encouraging facts are brought out. It was just another convention to him as far as the good-will person can see.

To another publisher it was a real milestone of theocratic activity. He is anxious to close his study and tell about the convention. With his notes he gives some outstanding high lights —statements made, new truths, things the good-will person can understand and apply. He describes the harmony and the orderly operation of the various departments, the great advertising done. He shows some of the new releases. The good-will person is much encouraged and now desires to attend the congregation meetings.

SCENE III (10 min.) In order to help publishers, brothers can be used to comment on things they got out of assembly that were striking and that can be used to get good-will or immature persons to act, to help them to start associating with a congregation and to engage in service. Brothers may comment from floor, or selected ones may give brief comments from platform. All may be encouraged to bring assembly features to those who were unable to attend as vividly as possible. Assembly can be topic of all gatherings and all conversations of Jehovah’s witnesses for months, whether at congregational meetings or other times of association and fellowship.

Braille Literature

The Society has a limited number of copies of Can You Live Forever in Happiness on Earth? in Braille raised characters for reading by the blind. These are available on loan to blind persons for a period of two weeks, after which they must be mailed back to the Society for use by someone else. Return mailing instructions and a return-address label will be found inside the front cover of each Braille booklet. Care must be taken not to damage these booklets. There will be no charge for the loan of these booklets. If all the booklets are out on loan when your request is received, we will place your name on the list to receive a copy as soon as possible.
