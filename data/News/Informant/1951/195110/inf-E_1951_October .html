Through Bible Tracts

(Part One)

Competing with this fast-moving atomic age. the Christian servant at times finds himself faced with a problem: people generally are in a despondent condition and need comfort and assistance and yet, too often, they think that they are so busy they have no time to listen to the message. Realizing the importance of making known the good news of the Kingdom to everyone, he fervently and zealously7 strives to fulfill his commission from the Lord. But how is he to overcome the obstacle and present the message to the countless numbers who are so engrossed in this system of things that they7 continually give the excuse, “Sorry, but I am busy”? At the London convention the Society released something that will aid toward solving this particular problem : four fact-presenting, questionanswering Bible tracts.

Just think! A complete witness on “What Do Jehovah’s Witnesses Believe?” “Hell Fire,” “Jehovah’s Witnesses, Communists or Christians?” and “Awake from Sleep!” The question now comes to mind, How should these tracts be presented?

Being efficient ministers we will at all times consider the purpose of the tract. It was designed to give a witness by personal contact. The tracts are not to be slipped under the doors or left on the seats of buses, trains or other means of public transportation. When someone says “I am busy”, a tract or two may7 provide the brief witness that is needed.

Do not hand out the tracts as you would a handbill. Nor are they for house-to-house distribution regardless of whether people are home or not. At all times strive to make a personal presentation. The tract is not to be left with the person of good will and then forgotten. Follow this up with a back-call. The contents reveal thought-provoking information and may lead to the placing of a bound book, further questions on the subject or the opportunity to give a verbal witness.

We should at all times handle the tracts carefully, realizing that they7 curl at the ends and soil easily. Avoid folding or crushing them. Make certain that those we present to the people are attractive to the eye as well as the mind. Perhaps a small folder can be used to hold them in the pocket or handbag.

There are a variety of opportunities that provide an opening for the use of the tracts. By carrying the tracts with us at all times, we will be equipped to give a witness under any circumstance. We can use them while talking to a friend or a relative or a businessman. In striking up a conversation with a stranger on the street or the friendly companion on the Ims. the opportunity may present itself to make known the Kingdom hope.

So make known tiie good news with confidence that your territory7 can be more easily covered with the new and colorful tracts. Keep pace with this fast-moving system of things by7 letting those you come in contact with hear of Jehovah’s purposes and of the coming destruction on this rebellious world. By conscientiously7 applying ourselves in presenting the tracts we will have many opportunities to further the witness work.

The Society is ready to fill company orders for tracts, and you may begin using them as soon as you receive them. In ordering the tracts please note the one desired and indicate by7 the corresponding symbol: T1—“What Do Jehovah's Witnesses Believe?" T2—“Hell Fire,” T.‘!—“Jehovah’s Witnesses, Communists or Christians?" T4—“Awake from Sleep!" All orders should be in lots of 250. The prices on the tracts will be: 75 cents a thousand; 40 cents for 500, 25 cents for 250. There will be no reduced pioneer rate. They are to be given away free by the publishers.

After supplies of the tracts are received, 15 minutes in each service meeting for four weeks may be spent in considering each of the tracts. Discuss a different tract each week, showing how to make further use of them as a means of advertising and arousing interest.

YOUR SERVICE MEETING

Reports indicate that although there is an increase in publishers throughout the country, yet there is no corresponding increase in book placements. It is the placing of books that furnishes one of the best bases for back-calls and home Bible studies. Therefore, to assist in overcoming this weakness in book placements, the following demonstration should aid the publishers to make their presentations more effective and enable them to devote more time in the field service, especially in the house-to-house activity.


SCENE ONE: The ,company servant and assistant company servant are on the platform discussing the report of the company activity. (8 minutes)

The company servant Inquires of his assistant what an analysis of the Publisher’s Record cards reveals. The assistant explains that the book placements are low and notes the need for improvement. He also shows how the other features of activity, such as back-calls and home Bible studies, have suffered and suggests that an increase in book placements would make way for increases in these important features of activity. Many times the book placements average less than the number of publishers, and it can be shown that it is usually a minority of the publishers who make most of the book placements. (Base your discussion on analysis of Publisher’s Record cards.) Low hours of field service can also be emphasized as a contributing factor as well as the little amount of time spent in house-to-house activity. To remedy these weaknesses the company servant and his assistant decide to arrange for an interview of a few publishers who have been successful in their book placements.

SCENE TWO: An interview of publishers is conducted on the platform by either the company servant or his assistant. (8 minutes)

A number of brothers and sisters are interviewed to relate how they have been successful in their book placements. They can either demonstrate or explain how they present the books to the householders and illustrate how their presentations are varied with different persons to insure successful book placements. They can also show how these placements are followed up by making back-calls, pointing to some of their home Bible studies as a result of following up book placements. Likewise it can be shown how they devote a good portion of their time in house-to-house activity, rather than perhaps spending all their time in other features of activity.

SCENE THREE: Sunday morning out in the field service. (4 minutes)

A publisher is just finishing witnessing to a householder and places a book. The name of the householder is obtained, and the conversation closes with the householder agreeing to a return visit by the publisher. The publisher notes the householder's name and address and the book placement on his House-to-House Record form. As he looks up, he is happily greeted by two other publishers who in times past had spent most of their time in other features of activity. They joyfully tell of their successful placement of books and the good experiences ehjoyed in witnessing to the people. With this, they express their intention of continuing to put more time in house-to-house activity. They express their gratitude for being encouraged to put more time in house-to-house activity and the blessing received from the Lord for doing so.

Reporting Home Bible Studies and Public Meetings

Some servants and pioneers are having difficulty in correctly reporting their home Bible studies. As a result reports containing mistakes are sent to the Society. It is not difficult to report correctly if instructions are followed. Do not report the number of times you conduct studies during the month as the “Number of Home Bible Studies”. What is required is the number of different studies held. Before filling out your report stop and ask yourself: “In how many different homes did I conduct studies during the month?” If you were in five different homes then report five. Do not report twenty studies just because you held a study four times in each of the five homes during the month. However, each time you conduct a study, report it as a back-call.

Concerning public meetings: This column on the pioneer report card is to be used only by pioneers holding isolated territory. The pioneer who sponsors any public meeting in such isolated territory where the public is invited and a discourse is given should report such meeting. A pioneer who gives a discourse at a meeting sponsored by a company should not report the meeting, since that is to be reported by the company. Also, pioneers who advertise and attend such company-sponsored meetings should not report them. All public meetings in company territory are to be reported by the company, including funerals and meetings conducted by circuit servants. Consult Counsel on Theocratic Organization for further information on reporting properly.
