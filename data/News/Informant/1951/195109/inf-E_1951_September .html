The London, England, International Assembly

To the great Wembley Stadium they came, thousands of them, from forty nations, eagerly, knowing that their days together would n ore than justify ail the expenditure of effort, time and resources they made to be in London on the first five days of August. And why not? Had they not received through the organization of the new world society an invitation to back up by their presence a theocratic assembly? Important were previous Christian conventions in London; of outstanding importance they knew this one would be.

And it was! In its title is seen its theme: "The Clean Worship Assembly of Jehovah’s Witnesses.” Assurance, optimism, encouragement. these prevailed throughout the program. Practical were the many features relative to meetings, conduct, organization, ministry. Thrilling were the many reports from countries of Europe, Africa, Asia, the islands, the Americas. Vital were the spiritual truths set out in clarity for the first time. Devastating to demon religion was the resolving of the issues in the current fight to the finish between true and false worship.

Knorr’s first discourse was on Thursday afternoon. His subject: “Confidently Facing the End,” and at its conclusion he released four splendid little tracts, briefly outlining their use.

The immersion was held Friday forenoon in a beautiful lake, and 1,123 were baptized. YOUR SERVICE MEETING


If new and irregular publishers are to make rapid progress and become regular and effective ministers, they must be instructed in what to say and how to say it, given an assignment and shown by example how to carry it out. This Jesus did for his new apostles and disciples. He told them where to go, told them what to say, and set them a proper example in the field. (Matt. 10: 5-7; Luke 4: 43; Luke 8: 1, NW) The following demonstration should show how to follow this Christian example now in helping our brothers become efficient and productive ministers.


SCENE ONE: Early Sunday morning a mature publisher calls for the one with whom he conducts a home Bible study to go in the field service. (4 minutes)

This newly Interested one has not as yet engaged in the house-to-house work. Having this in mind, the mature publisher calls early in order that some time can be spent helping the new publisher prepare a testimony to use at the door during the morning’s field service. Tactfully he helps him decide upon a point or two in the book that can be used in its presentation. (For example, the need people have for new world conditions described on pages 2C1-2G3 of the book “Let God Be True”.) Thereafter they leave for the field to work in the mature publisher’s territory.

SCENE TWO: Both publishers are in the field. (6 minutes)

Mature publisher suggests new publisher accompany him to door for first few calls. Mature publisher makes first presentation, leaving booklet with promise to call back, and then shows the new publisher how to use House to House Record, so he will know where to call back. Thereafter new publisher is encouraged to try the next door alone using presentation previously decided upon at home. He makes presentation, copying mature publisher's example, and, using points discussed earlier, he places book, and gets person’s name and promises to call back. This scene should close with new publisher asking mature one to go with him when he makes the back-call.

SCENE THREE: Two weeks later. Both publishers are calling back on new publisher’s book placement. (6 minutes)

Before approaching the door they decide to proceed on a definite course in their discussion. They choose to use the chapter “Who Is Jehovah?” in the book “Let God Be True”. When greeted by the householder, they are asked to step in for a few minutes. After a few friendly remarks, the conversation is directed to the book and it is found that the person has not read much of it but seemed to like it. Since the householder has no Bible question of his own, the new publisher raises the question as to who Jehovah is. This catches the interest of the householder. For the answer, they turn to pages 21 to 32 of the book. The householder makes mention of never having noticed the name Jehovah in the so-called “New Testament”, and asks, “Why?” Paragraph 4 is discussed in answer. The mature publisher supports the argument with information found on pages 10-18 of the Foreword of the New World Translation. The back-call ends with the householder asking for a copy of the A’eic World Translation, and arrangements are made to call back and further consider the book in a home Bible study.
