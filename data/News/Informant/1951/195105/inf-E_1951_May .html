Circuit and district assemblies are means that the Lord is using to assemble his people for vital Kingdom instruction so necessary during these last days. A great deal of time, money, and effort are spent in arranging and preparing assemblies and, for publishers to derive full benefit, they must properly prepare to attend and conduct themselves fittingly while there. The following material emphasizes proper publisher preparation, consideration for the landlady and conduct at the assembly. To cover this material as outlined, twenty to twenty-five minutes should be devoted to it at a service meeting.

FIRST SCENE: (Part one) By pantomime illustrate the basic steps each publisher should take in preparing for an assembly.

Publisher has Room Request form and is in the process of filling It out. Care is taken to supply all requested information, such as full name and address, type of room requested and amount desired to be paid, date of arrival and length of stay, etc. Upon completion it is recheeked and mailed. Receiving the rooming assignment a few days later, time is taken to write the landlady and confirm date of arrival and intended time of arrival. Next comes personal organization. Proper clothing is selected, appropriate literature, book and magazine bags are put in order, duties requiring one's attention before leaving are cared for. room assignment card is thoughtfully put in with those things to be carried to the assembly, such as songbook, etc.

(Part two) Show arrival of publisher in assembly city at a reasonable hour on day before assembly.

Upon arrival the publisher, with suitease and book bag, goes directly to assigned room, introduces himself in a friendly manner to the landlady, and is directed to his room. Showing consideration for the landlady and wanting to be fully rested so as to get the most from the next day's assembly schedule, the publisher retires for the night.

SECOND SCENE: Witnessing to the landlady. (Arrange as a demonstration.)

Many landladies are interested in Jehovah’s witnesses, want to know what kind of work we are doing, what we believe, etc., and open their homes to Jehovah's witnesses with this in mind. Understanding this, on the following evening the alert publisher utilizes an opportunity to witness. A program is produced, future assembly activity is highlighted, particularly the public meeting, and an offer is made to have the landlady and her household attend assembly sessions with the publisher. Continuing, the witness tells of an experience had in the field service there in the assembly city. The scene will end with the publisher leaving with the landlady the particular booklet, magazine or book which covers the point of discussion raised in the experience related.
