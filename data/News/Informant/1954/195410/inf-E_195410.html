Around the World with Jehovah’s Witnesses

Means of Awakening

How will people call upon Jehovah if they are not preached to? All of Jehovah's witnesses know the answer to this question and therefore diligently arrange to share regularly in the preaching work and to present literature, knowing that through the spoken and printed explanation of the Scriptures many will be awakened to the truth.

From all walks and ways of life people of good will toward God are awaking to the truth. It is not the past that counts, but, rather, what one does upon learning the truth. You will enjoy reading what effect the truth had on a hardened criminal in Hawaii. (172)

Through the assistance of “Let God Be True” many thousands have been awakened to the truth. And it does not take long, as shown in the report from North Borneo. (245)

Even tracts can be used effectively. “Awake from Sleep!” was left with a lady in South Africa who was not too receptive. After reading the tract, what did the lady do? She immediately looked up the publisher to subscribe for “Awake!” (247)

And speaking of “Awake!” A man in Peru was so impressed with this magazine that he wrote a fine article about it and had it published in a prominent student paper. The result? Others were influenced to subscribe for “Awake!” (230)

Being awake ourselves and knowing the merits of “Awake!” we should all diligently present the subscription offer from house to house this month. It will be the means of awakening many.

Youthful Witnesses

Does one have to be old in years to understand the truth? To take a firm stand? To preach? The 1954 “Yearbook of Jehovatvs Witnesses” contains many fine reports showing that youthful witnesses are doing fine work and have Jehovah’s blessing and favor as they share in the ministry.

In France three girls, 11, 15 and 17, learned the truth and started attending meetings. What happened? Read the interesting account on page 154 of how, among other things, they exasperated the village priest with their knowledge of the Bible to the point that the priest warned all his parishioners against listening to the truth spoken by an 11-year-old girl! Another fine example is found on page 196.

At the tender age of five a youthful witness in Nicaragua effectively told her grandmother why her speechless, sightless and deaf images are -worthless. She is also able to witness alone at the doors. This is another good example of what results when parents assist their children to have a solid foundation for life in the new world. (207)

Youthful witnesses, are you regularly engaging in magazine activity? Have you tried starting a magazine route around your home? You can effectively take part in this feature of the ministry as shown by the example of other young ministers in Greece. (166)

All youthful witnesses should regularly attend meetings and be attentive during them so as to learn from them. Participate in them, too, as appropriate occasions are afforded and then be regular in the ministry by sharing in field service.

YOUR SERVICE MEETING


Know Your Magazine

The following two demonstrations are to assist all publishers to become familiar with the material in the October 8 and 22 issues of '‘Awake!” which will be used during the campaign. Consider the October 8 issue as early in October as possible and the October 22 issue two weeks later. Announcement should be made to the brothers well in advance so that all publishers may prepare to take part and benefit from the discussion.

First Meeting: A panel of “experts” on platform faces congregation, fully prepared to answer any questions propounded by audience. Questions asked should bring points to view that can be used in “Awake!” presentation. They should arouse Interest and curiosity and create a desire in others to have more information, which an “Aw’ake!” subscription will supply. Chairman will act as moderator and see that things are kept moving. He will call upon various ones so that as many as possible are given opportunity to take part. Quick and ready questions from the audience as well as prompt answers from the “panel” will allow for greatest coverage. Each “panel” member can be given a turn in rotation in answering questions, or raised hands can indicate readiness. The chairman should see to it that time spent and questions asked are properly proportioned so that each article gets some consideration. “Do You Know?” questions on page 28 of each issue may be used in addition to the ones publishers make up for this meeting. With answers brief and to the point, this meeting will be most lively, informative and beneficial to all in becoming acquainted with “Awake!” magazine used during the first part of the campaign.

Second Meeting: A brother will be assigned to prepare good, pointed questions on “Awake!” The audience will be told the week before to come prepared to answer questions on any article in the October 22 issue. A minimum of pausing will avoid loss of time and yield best results, so stress preparation. The brother should prepare some questionson each article. Interestcanbe added by calling upon the group seated on one side of the hall and then the other. With many brothers answering the questions, zest will be added to the part, making it absorbing and profitable. True and false statements can be asked periodically. Questions on detailed statistics or complex data are not necessary. Rather, use interesting points that publishers may use in pre- 1 senting the magazine. With all publishers being thoroughly acquainted with the magazines used during the campaign and by all taking a full part in the house-to-house service, this will be the best “Awake!” campaign to date.

Be Alert and Awake

October will undoubtedly be the best “Awake!” campaign to date. More publishers than ever before will take part in this campaign and all of us are better equipped than ever to present effectively this excellent magazine after giving a three- to eight-minute sermon. The keynote to successful witnessing during the month of October, as of all other months, is preparation. For best results prepare and practice your sermon before going into the field work.

SCENE I (5 min.) Mother and daughter (or son) in kitchen preparing Sunday breakfast discuss day’s activities. Both look forward to using their sermons in the field, and comment on how many good experiences they have had using them. So that all can have a part in and stay longer in the service, mother has made preliminary preparations for Sunday dinner. She comments as to how much better it is to plan ahead this way so as not to have to stay at home Sunday morning while the rest are enjoying field service privileges.

SCENE II (16 min.) Father comes in at end of discussion and compliments his industrious wife. He is glad all have arranged to spend a portion of Sunday together in Jehovan’s work. All sit down to table (conductor narrates while others pantomime consideration of text, prayer and the reading of a branch report). Then discussion turns to sermons for the day. Father asks mother what theme she is going to use; then he suggests that he act as householder and listen to her presentation. Mother gives a four-minute sermon from start to finish. Father offers a point or two of counsel and then asks his daughter to give one she has prepared. Mother acts as householder while father listens. Daughter gives a good three-minute sermon. Father makes a suggestion or two and then gives a five-minute sermon. In conclusion, as they leave the table and prepare to reach the contact point on time, father commends all for being well prepared to take part in the “Awake!” campaign.

Conclusion: (3 min.) Conductor points out the value of each one’s preparing sermon in advance and then practicing the sermon before another and receiving suggestions. Being prepared gives one greater joy in the field and makes for a more effective presentation. Endeavor to use three- to eight-minute sermons at every door in presenting the “Awake!” subscription offer.
