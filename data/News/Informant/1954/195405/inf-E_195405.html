Around the World with Jehovah’s Witnesses

Happy Giving

Regardless of surrounding circumstances or conditions, Jehovah’s witnesses joyously give of the truth freely. This may well be illustrated by our brothers in Taiwan, who, in spite of persecution, increased from 417 publishers in 1951 to 1,752 in 1953. What method of teaching is used? Oral teaching. Daily, taught ones happily talk to others, so the truth remains fresh and clear to them. Brothers who were privileged to donate material needs forwarded by the Society to Taiwan may feel especially happy as they view the increase there. (189, 190)

On bfeing contacted by a witness a Methodist Sunday-school teacher in New Zealand sought the help of three clergy to defend Methodists’ teachings, to no avail. After four studies in “Let God Be True” he was convinced he had found the truth and started teaching it to his Bible class. His name was soon struck from the church list, but now he and one of his Methodist friends are having their names recorded in Jehovah’s book of life. (205)

When no brotheis are available for servant appointments, sisters are appointed to serve and care for congregation matters. By happily giving out the truth and earing for these additional privileges, they are blessed in theii efforts, as illustrated in Puerto Rico, where a congregation increased from seven to eighteen publishers. (238)

The congregation at Sao Tome in Angola has had much happiness m giving In 1950 the congregation was started with six publishers. Now there are twenty-three publishers, all of them regular. That they appreciate the greater happiness in giving is jfhown by their field service report. Truly it is a wonderful report of integrity and love for Jehovah. (248)

More Happiness

The more you give the happier you are, said Jesus. Thus the more hours spent preaching to others, the more joyful experiences one of Jehovah's witnesses has. Since pioneers and missionaries devote more hours to the ministry they enjoy more blessed experiences.

Have you ever thought while working a particular territory: “Can there possibly be some of the other sheep in this area?” A pioneer in Cameroun working a Catholic town thought that very thing. But that did not stop him, for he knew the Kingdom message must he preached. Today a former Catholic, believed by the local inhabitants to be more devout than the pope, preaches to his neighbors and a congregation flourishes in the town. (210)

Just as a person is admonished not to judge a book by its cover, so Jehovah’s witnesses do not judge the inhabitants of a house or village by its size or appearance. A pioneer in Austria worked a village of 800 persons. And what has resulted thus far? Kight subscriptions, at least 15 persons attending studies he conducts and two publishers reporting 25 hours the first month. Yes, the next door may be the location of one of the other sheep. (95)

Does one ever become too old to pioneer or to share in happy giving as a congregation publisher For a good answer turn to page 87 and read of the 86-year-old pioneer in Argentina, who, in reporting only 145 hours one month, wrote on his report “please see letter why I have not been able to make more hours.” How evident this pioneer desired to share more and more in the happiness of giving!
