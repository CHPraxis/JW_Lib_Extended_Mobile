Are You Coming?

The Lord’s people throughout the eastern part of the United States are eagerly looking forward to the Newark convention. The opening day, Friday, October 16, is rapidly approaching, and soon a great throng of Jehovah’s witnesses and their companions, the Jonadabs, will be on their way to take part in what we hope will be the most effective witness ever given in New Jersey. Letters coming in to the office indicate that the brethren are overjoyed with the prospects. It will be a season of rich blessing from the hands of the Lord, and they are making every effort to get to the convention. Much enthusiasm is being manifest, and everyone who truly loves the Lord will be glad to have this opportunity to engage in battle with the enemy, that the “refuge of lies” of the Hierarchy may be further exposed and the name of Jehovah magnified. It seems quite evident that more than 5,000 persons will be In attendance, and everyone within any reasonable distance of Newark will make an earnest effort to get there.

We are pleased to announce that Brother Rutherford will address the convention Saturday afternoon at three o’clock, and again on Sunday, when he will deliver the public lecture on “ARMAGEDDON, THE GREATEST BATTLE OF ALL TIME”. All attending the convention will want to have some share in advertising this lecture. Every effort that can be made in advance to publicize it has been arranged for. It is hoped, by the Lord’s grace, that the crowd turning out to hear the public lecture will be so great that there will not be sufficient room in the Armory to hold the multitude.

During the three days there is a territory to be covered that has a population of more than two million people. That means a lot of work, and it will take real determination and complete devotion to the Lord to cover this territory thoroughly. Are you going to be one of the publishers in this glorious campaign? It promises to be a most happy occasion.

European Conventions

By Jehovah’s grace, Brother Rutherford has safely returned from overseas, after a survey of European conditions and a season of Intense activity in behalf of the Kingdom interests there. Every Kingdom publisher will want to read the forthcoming issue of The Golden Age, containing extensive reports of the European conventions and of the great popular demonstrations at the public addresses by Brother Rutherford, which serve as an index of what you may expect at Newark.

Referring you to the fuller reports to appear in The Golden Age, we here set out some of the noteworthyitems. You’ll see the Lord’s blessing. No matter in what country the witness is being given; no matter what language is used, the same spirit of earnest zeal is seen. Brother Rutherford was greatly used by Jehovah to give what is probably the most effective public witness and warning yet given in the European countries visited. Great opposition was manifested by the Roman Catholic Hierarchy.

Within the last decade, the city of Glasgow has increased its “Catholic population” until now there are as many Catholics as Protestants. Brother Rutherford delivered the message of warning in that city on August 2, 1936. So great was the interest that it required three auditoriums to hold the people that came. “Armageddon” was the subject used, and the message was received with greater enthusiasm than at any other time in Scotland. Over 7,000 people crowded into the auditoriums, and many were unable to gain admission. Of course, the Hierarchy was busy warning its “population” not to attend the meetings, but this only served to make a large number of honest Catholics come.

Jehovah’s witnesses attending the convention in Glasgow threw themselves into advertising the big public witness. Over 1,000 participated in the “sandwich sign” parades, 35 up to 100 in a group, walking up and down the busy streets of Glasgow announcing the public meeting in this very effective way. On Sunday, September 13, 1936, London, England, was warned of the near approach of Armageddon. About 3,000 joined in publicizing the big witness. Over a thousand joined in the “sandwich sign” parades. Crowds came to the Alexandra Palace; some got in, many heard on the outside. Powerful loud-speakers served from the roof of the building. The speaker’s voice was thus carried a great distance from the Palace into the densely populated area. It would be impossible to estimate how many people heard in this way. Never has Great Britain had such a witness in two of its largest cities.

On September 4, 1936, there gathered in the beautiful and historic city of Lucerne, Switzerland, a convention of Jehovah’s witnesses. On Monday, September 7, there was to be a large public witness. Over 3,500 publishers of the Kingdom message attended, from twenty-one different countries. One motive, one thought, dominated all: the honoring of the name of Jehovah.
