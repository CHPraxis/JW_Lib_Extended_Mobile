The inventory of the companies’ stock shows that they have approximately 5,500,000 colored-cover booklets on hand and a fair supply of self-covered booklets. Can You Use a Sound Car Equipment?

We are pleased to advise that the Society is in position now to rebuild the wet-battery portable transcription machine into No. 3 sound equipment. We can handle this work this winter so that any who desire to take advantage of the opportunity and have a No. 3 equipment instead of just the wet-battery portable transcription machine by next spring may make arrangements now. The additional equipment can be built up from your present wet-battery portable transcription machine. If your machine has a serial number lower than 451 it will be necessary to send your machine to the factory here and have it altered. It will be equipped with two-speed motor and microphone and be generally overhauled and put in first-class condition, and then the additional sound equipment will be placed on it to make it a No. 3 equipment. If your machine has a serial number above 451 it will not he necessary to return the machine to the factory, as all of that series have two-speed motor and microphone already attached.

The cost for building up your equipment is $90 where a microphone is required, or $80 if you have your own microphone. Write the office for full information regarding this matter and the Society’s arrangement for time payments on this work. Do not send your equipment to the factory, however, until you are definitely instructed to do so by the Society’s office. Present orders will require our entire output of No. 3 equipments for December and January, but during February and March, we believe, we can take care of all rebuilt jobs.

Are You Using One?

Those who have really taken advantage of the phonograph work find it the most thrilling and effective means of proclaiming the Kingdom message. Similar to all other features of the Lord's work, we must, of course, creep before we can walk or run. We cannot hope to be proficient and have the most effective method all at once. We must keep at it, become accustomed to introducing and using the phonograph so that we do it in a natural and an effective way; and when this is mastered it will be found that this is one of the best means of preaching the Kingdom gospel.

It is one of the best, first, because the phonograph is a provision made by the Lord to be used at this time to the glory of His name, and, secondly, because it is a practical and reasonable method of presenting the Kingdom message. It enables one to make a clear and concise presentation of the truth without interruption, which is something that can rarely be done by the witnesses if they try to do it themselves. No one should become discouraged because the use of the phonograph is a little awkward at first. The same is true of the witness work in general. When we first started out we were awkward and nervous and found it difficult. The same is true of our use of the testimony card and other features of the work that the Lord brings to our attention from time to time. If we had rebelled back there and refused to avail ourselves of the Lord’s provisions, -where would we be today? Not participating in the Kingdom witness work, that is sure.

Let us recognize, then, that the phonograph is the Lord’s provision for His people and that the use of the phonograph is really organization instructions, which are not discretionary, but entirely mandatory, and let us apply ourselves to its use, and the Lord will bless us with success. Care of Phonographs

Quite a few of the brethren have lost the set screw which holds the needle in the reproducer of their phonograph. In order to prevent this, so that we shall not have to replace them, we suggest that before you close up your phonograph after using it you turn the needle screw of the reproducer all the way in. Don’t turn it too tight, but just tight enough to know it can’t be turned any more. If you do this the screw will not be lost. Do not leave needles in the phonograph reproducer head when not in use.
