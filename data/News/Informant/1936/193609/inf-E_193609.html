The special offer of the three bound books on a contribution of fifty cents ends August 31. All books placed during the month, whether in tills special combination or singly, are to be reported in the regular way on the report card as total bound books placed.

Each six-book combination placed on a dollar contribution is to be reported as two three-book combinations.

What Is Your Part in This War?


World-wide Quota 600,000 Hours for Nine Days

The time is drawing near for another concerted attack upon the Devil’s organization. Jehovah has given the command, “Arise ye, and let us rise up against her in battle.” This occasion is known as the "Battle Shout”, October 3-11, 1936, and Jehovah’s faithful servants in every part of the world will join in this battle cry. No doubt you have all read Brother Rutherford’s “Personal Message” to Jehovah’s faithful servants in the September 1 Watchtower and have been moved to put forth a more strenuous effort during the coming witness than ever before. Brother Rutherford’s letter points out that we are in a real battle now, and “the time of preliminary war is here”. All those who love Jehovah should study that letter carefully and then make preparations for the greatest campaign to date to the honor of Jehovah’s name.

The book Riches will again be offered during this period, on a contribution of 25c Riches has n< w been translated and published in thirteen languages, and for the first time will be offered on a contribution of 25c no matter what language people speak. Here at the factory the presses have been running to the limit to turn out enough of these books in various languages to supply tire brethren for this special campaign. The demand for Riches continues to be exceedingly heavy, and to date over 1,700,000 copies have been printed and shipped out at Brooklyn alone. Hundreds of ihousands of copies are on their way to the branches of the Society so that Jehovah’s servants may move forward as one man with one book, Riches.

Many people will have had Riches by the time of the campaign, but that should not lessen our placements. On the contrary, it should help in placing more. Those who have read Riches will have mentioned it to their friends and neighbors, and many who turned it down the first time will now be anxious to get the book, and offering it again will increase the importance of the book in the minds of the people. Those who already have Riches should be invited to attend a study, using Riches as the text book. If in any language Riches is not available, then the latest book published should be offered. If one cannot contribute 25c and receive a bound book, then, on a contribution of 10c, offer three booklets, which should be the Choosing booklet, a selfcovered booklet, and any other colored-cover booklet.

The newly Interested who have been attending Riches studies should be invited to participate In the witness work. These people should be aided in every possible way to get started as publishers of the Kingdom.

SOUND ACTIVITY

During tliis period as many transcription meetings as possible should be arranged, using the “Separating the Nations” lecture. In many places it will be possible to play this lecture for large outdoor gatherings as well as indoor meetings. In addition, the brethren should use the phonograph records wherever possible, both in door-to-door witnessing and in making back calls. Oftentimes the playing of only one record has resulted in the placing of a book which would otherwise have been impossible. No more than one or two records should be played at any one place while witnessing. If the party is interested, arrange for a back call in the evening.

QUOTA

It is always well to have a quota of hours in mind for these special periods. Last April we reached a peak in publishers, but not the peak in hours. There were 46,209 publishers in the field, throughout the world, and these put in a total of 489,011 hours witnessing. For the coming period it is suggested that we aim at a quota of 50,000 publishers putting in 600,000 hours. That would be a nice increase over the last period, wouldn’t It? However, it is not difficult if each publisher takes the responsibility upon himself to do his share. There should be throughout the entire world 3,500 pioneers averaging 40 hours each for the nine days, 1,500 auxiliaries having an individual quota of 20 hours each, and 45,000 company publishers putting forth an earnest endeavor to average 10 hours each. If we do this we shall pass the 600,000 hours. Think what a witness can be given if each publisher puts in his share of the above quota! Remember, the individual goal is. pioneers 40 hours, auxiliaries 20 hours, and company publishers 10 hours. If each one does this, then in the United States we should expect 25,000 company publishers putting in a total of 250,000 hours, 1,000 auxiliaries with a total of 20,000 hours, and 2,000 pioneers with 80,000 hours, or a grand total for the United States of 350,000 hours. It is well to have in mind that the “Battle Shout” starts off our new fiscal year, too.

Important Notice

Send In Your September Report on the 27th

September is the last month of the fiscal year, and the year's report must be completed immediately thereafter. To get the report ready in time, it will be necessary to cut September’s work short; so end your September work with Sunday the 27th. Your report for October will include September 28 to October 31.

Each company servant, pioneer and auxiliary is to mail his report for the twenty-seven days to this office Sunday evening, September 27, and those company servants west of the Mississippi river will aid very much by sending their reports by air mail. This means that all company publishers are to get their reports to the company servant before Sunday evening, so that their work will be included in the company report mailed at that time. If it should be that there are some publishers who fall to do this, the company servant is not to hold up the report by waiting for these, but is to send the company report right on, and include, such late ones the following month.

This is very important. Let everyone mail the report promptly on September 27.

Inventory

Eacli company, pioneer, and auxiliary is required to take an exact inventory of stock as of September 30. Additionally, each one is to send in the complete report of said Inventory to the Society by October 10, on the special blank enclosed for this very purpose.

Care is to be taken to see that the inventory blank is properly filled out, particularly that the “totals” and “values” called for are figured out correctly.

The companies and auxiliaries that want credit for the change in costs on foreign bound books must make request for it by properly showing on the inventory, under the heading “Remarks”, the amount needed to adjust the account. Companies are being allowed a credit of 6c on each foreign bound book; auxiliaries, 3c. Be sure that the credit you ask for checks with the total foreign bound books reported.

Our responsibility in this battle is great. The Lord has given each one of us a definite assignment in his army, and if we are good soldiers we will, as Brother Rutherford stated in his letter, “be ready to enter the field on the first day and continue therein until the last, and as you go be joyful in the Lord.”

Soiled Literature

From time to time complaints are received because of the careless manner in which some brethren handle the literature entrusted to them on credit, and of their habit of returning to the company stock soiled books and booklets.

Tlie arrangement of allowing a publisher a limited supply of literature on credit is to aid him to have a full share in the witness. Each one who takes literature under this arrangement automatically obligates himself to remit to the treasurer for this stock as placed or, if he cannot place it, return it within a reasonable time in first-class condition.

The stockkeeper is not to take back soiled literature from anyone. Each one who soils literature is responsible for placing it and remitting in full therefor. In fairness to all, the company should see that this rule is strictly enforced.

Transfers

Whenever a transfer of stock is authorized by the Society, it is understood that the literature to be transferred is in first-class condition ; otherwise the company, pioneer or auxiliary taking over the literature can and should properly refuse to accept such of it as is damaged or soiled.
