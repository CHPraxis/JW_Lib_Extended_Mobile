NATIONAL PETITION “JEHOVAH’S Praise”

Listeners Eager for More Broadcasts


Hierarchy Threatens “More Drastic Action”

Humble persons of good will who have listened to Jehovah's Kingdom message as broadcast in speeches of Brother Rutherford frequently write the Society that they want to hear those broadcasts oftener. Three years ago the Roman Catholic press made world-wide announcement of the Hierarchy’s decision to “drive Rutherford from the air”, as they put it. They tried. They staggered in the attempt. “But the end is not yet.”

Hating the truth broadcast throughout the world last February, in “Separating the Nations”, high officials of the Hierarchy at Vatican City and in this country have conspired anew to take “more drastic action” to accomplish their announced purpose. Fresh attempts to coerce owners of radio stations are being launched. The Catholic press renews its campaign of stout protests, insisting that “Judge Rutherford misrepresents the teachings of the Catholic church” and therefore he must be stopped; or “electrocuted”, says one of the Hierarchy’s agents. Another of its so-called “fathers”, a principal (but unprincipled) representative at New York, blandly proposes deatli for all of Jehovah’s witnesses! A nd now prayers of “the faithful” during the month of August are called for by the head of the Hierarchy, ‘to save us from the infamous Judge Rutherford of the Watch Tower movement.’

Reasonable persons, including even many of the “Catholic population”, are beginning to see the inconsistency of tlie Hierarchy's position. Catholic leaders are being asked, Why plan murder? Why not openly show the people wherein Judge Rutherford misrepresents the Catholic teachings ?

At Philadelphia, in June, many of such fair-minded ones signed a petition to WIP, prominent radio station there that had been forced by the Hierarchy to discontinue broadcasting Brother Rutherford’s answers to Bible questions, after that station had refused to break its contract to broadcast “Separating the Nations” in February. Now thousands at other places in Pennsylvania and New Jersey are joining in that petition, asking WIP to broadcast a public discussion of the disputed questions, ‘one side to be taken by a high Catholic spokesman and the other side by Judge Rutherford.’

Honest Catholics, as well as others, are reporting that the demand for such an open discussion is by no means limited to the people of Philadelphia and vicinity. We agree, and also believe that throughout this entire land there are millions who are eager not Only to hear those disputed questions reasonably discussed but also to sign a petition asking that such a debate be arranged and widely broadcast.

In the public interest, therefore, and for the public convenience, petition blanks and introductory cards similar to those now in use at Philadelphia have been printed and are being contributed to all who want to use them.

ORDERLY CO-OPERATION

Any isolated pioneer or other publisher not associated with a company and who desires to assist persons that want to sign the petition will be furnished blanks and cards on request.

Companies are being supplied a sufficient quantity of cards and blanks for immediate use. When more are needed, let us know well in advance.

Let each publisher be provided with a separate container (celluloid) for the special green introductory card. Also provide each one with a substantial cardboard of same size as petition blank (8% inches by 11 inches). Upon that board two or more petition blanks can be fastened with rubber bands, to keep blanks neat and clean. Cardboard may be folded in half, lengthwise, with blanks inside (lengthwise), and thus be carried conveniently. Carry also a fountain pen or indelible pencil.

At each home or business place, FIRST present the special green card and petition.

That being disposed of, then introduce the literature, using at all times the current presentation. During this petition service, instead of using testimony card for literature, we recommend a verbal testimony, formed according to the current testimony card, either word for word or including in your own language the points shown in card. (For use wherever desirable, carry with you in a separate celluloid container the literature testimony card, so as to be ready to present that card whenever you see fit.) Let this manner of serving the petition and the literature from now on be carefully considered and clearly understood by each publisher. Note well: Make it a habit to see that each signer of the petition fills in correct address, completely, including city and state.

NAMES FOR COMPANY’S FILE

As each petition sheet is filled, let name and address of each signer be copied with care on a separate slip or card, suitable for filing, using paper of uniform size for each name. Such copying by typewriter is, of course, preferable. In due time further information will be sent as to use of copied names kept in the company’s file.

Petition sheets filled turn in promptly to the company servant.

Electric Sign

Is Doing Good Work

The one thing the Lord’s people must do is to keep the message of the Kingdom before the minds of the people. The Lord’s organization is for the sole purpose of advertising the King and the Kingdom. Report Cards Must

Be Properly Marked for Credit Allowance

During the month of August the brethren throughout the United States will have the privilege of offering the three Vindication books on a contribution of 50e. Many of the pioneers and some auxiliaries and company workers will want to offer six bound books on a contribution of a dollar. This may be done by offering Vindication 1, 2 and 3, Light 1 and 2, and Prophecy. In order to obtain the proper credit on your account because of the reduction, it is absolutely necessary to mark on the bottom of your report card the number of three-book combinations placed. If you place one six-book combination, that is equal to two three-book combinations and should appear on the report card as follows: “2 combinations of 3 books.” Adjustments will be made on auxiliary and company accounts only. The companies will be allowed a credit on their account of 15c for each combination; the auxiliaries, 18c. It is definitely understood by the Society that you do not want any credit on your account if there is no statement of number of combinations placed marked on the bottom of your report card. Credit will be made on all company and auxiliary accounts where a notation of combinations is made. The treasurer, stockkeeper and company servant should make it their business to check the report cards before they are mailed in to the Society. Send in your report card correctly made out. Companies should use their storage stock, and when auxiliaries and pioneers in company territory run out of literature they may borrow, and replace with books as they desire later. Please have in mind that If the party does not take the combination offered, then you will drop to the Riches book on a contribution of 25c, or 3 booklets on a contribution of 10c. Credit Is not given on single placements; only on combinations. Be sure that the publishers turn in this information to you on their report slips.

AUSTRALIA

“The campaign from the book Riches resulted in the placing of more than 20,000 bound books in the territory under the supervision of the Australian branch, during ‘His Strange Work’ testimony period. This has exceeded by far the previous best bound-book campaign, when 12,238 Jehovah were placed in nine days. From all quarters reports show great enthusiasm among the publishers, and it has been difficult to keep all supplied with books. At the time of writing, most of the 55,000 Riches books already supplied to this branch are either placed with the people or in the hands of the publishers.

“During this campaign it was noticeable that the opposition of the enemy is increasing and that deeds of personal violence were reported from various quarters. On the other hand, many people of good will, at the present time under the control of the Roman Catholic Hierarchy, were glad to accept the publication brought to them by Jehovah’s faithful ones and expressed their determination to get out of that wicked organization.”

CANADA

“Jehovah’s witnesses and Jona-dabs in Canada and Newfoundland entered into ‘His Strange Work’ testimony period with a zeal and determination to make it the greatest witness yet given in these countries, and by His grace the results have been most gratifying. A total of 12,010 Riches was placed in the hands of the people, the largest number of bound books placed in any nine-day period since 1930.”

JAPAN

“Devil carried a severe attack against Jehovah’s witnesses with arrests, sickness, extremely cold weather and very much snow and raining during the whole period. The Roman priests began their activity to use public press. The Osaka Asahi, the largest newspaper In Japan, carried a big article against Jehovah’s witnesses. Several pioneers were thrown into the prison during the period. All faithful ones are ever rejoicing to serve Most High and to prove their integrity under this trial condition.”

SPAIN

“After several brethren from Madrid had worked a small, near-by town, two brothers went back to an inn to collect the reserve stock left there earlier in the day. A large crowd of infuriated workmen had gathered and, suspecting our work of being Fascist propaganda under a disguise, beat them and handled them roughly. Even though the mayor himself, who happened to be a Communist like the men, interceded for the brothers and provided them with an escort of two civil guards back to the station and actually right into the train itself, they were pursued even there and received blows in the train itself.”

SWEDEN

“The arrival of Riches in Swedish on the Monday before the period started was, of course, an item of greatest interest and importance to the publishers. And although we did not receive the whole edition in one shipment, we were nevertheless able to supply almost all publishers with this grand weapon. And it went out well, too 1 Some had to wire for more already on the evening of the second day. Many reports show that the people have received this message more favorably than ever before.

“The balance of Riches edition arrived one week later and just in time to supply the service convention arranged at Stockholm for the Easter holidays. During four days an average of 138 publishers placed a total of 6,063 pieces, of which 433 were Riches.”

DENMARK

“Our last record number of publishers was 804, in February; now we had 843, which is 205 more than April 1935. Is it not fine to see the Jonadabs joining up and taking part in the work? The weather was cold and wet, but the ardor of the friends was red-hot. All were much encouraged.”

FINLAND

“The last testimony period has been the best we ever have had. The brethren were filled with joy and enthusiasm, and more than ever before took part in the work (now 777 workers; the best before, 722), using an average (now 12 hours; the best before, 103A) more time (now 9,376 hours; the best before, 7,642) than before, and, as a result of that, placing more literature (now 43,916; the best before, 38,182) in the hands of the people than ever before.”

HAWAIIAN ISLANDS

“llesponse from publishers and people called upon with Riches is best yet; praise the Lobo! Attack upon sound car by excited women furthered the witness. This is by far the best testimony period, and they seem to get better and better.”

INDIA

“Another intensive witness by eight publishers operating in two sound cars was given in the Nllgiri Hills, where many people are on holiday at this season of the year. The speech ‘Separating the Nations’ aroused the anger of the missionaries and those of their flocks who are blind, while a few more gave serious attention to the message. From other districts come reports of general opposition by the modern Philistines and general apathy on the part of the common people, but some everywhere are showing interest in the message.”

CHINA

“Two Jonadabs (Chinese) participated in the service with result of 50 booklets placed. Being Easter time, offices all closed for three days, most of people were away from Shanghai for holiday excursion. Two days had showers and heavy rain. Riches did not come on hand during the Period. The lecture ‘Separating the Nations’ was broadcast over XMHA on the 19th. About 50 orders for Riches obtained during the campaign.”

BRAZIL

“Ten persons were immersed. This is the best report we have had. Jonadabs are coming in. A greater number of publishers entered the Held this year than in the last year. The weather was the best. The doors are still open in this part of the field; praised be Jehovah!”

PHILIPPINE ISLANDS

“Many attended the Memorial supper. A few Jonadabs underwent water immersion. Since the Lord revealed to his people who are the modern Philistines, there was great zeal among the brethren here who are working in perfect unity until the Lord may say ‘It is enough’.”

SOUTH AFRICA

“This was the best bound-book effort to date. Comparing our results with the figures for the campaign when the bound book Jehovah was released we find there was an increase of 700 bound volumes and 14,000 booklets on this occasion. The figures for the sound machine work are also better than anything we have had heretofore, the increase in the hours being 90 and in the attendance 3,500. Riches was enthusiastically received by the publishers, and the public have already clearly demonstrated that they want it, too! The crew of one sound ear reports: ‘We covered 900 miles and gave lectures in the twelve main dorps of the Northern Transvaal and also on several thickly populated farms. We had visible audiences up to 500 in some dorps, and we could discern the Jonadabs among the crowds. Some bishop had the police come up to arrest us, but when the constables arrived they told us that we had full right to go on. Thus Jehovah protects his people for his name’s sake.’ ”

NYASALAND

“The work continues to go forward in the face of considerable opposition in some parts. Just lately more than one of our fellows have been rather badly handled. They have had their books snatched away and not returned, their testimony cards torn, stones, sticks and sand thrown at them, and one of the sisters was entirely disrobed. These incidents took place during the campaign. A protest has been lodged with the district commissioner, who has oversight of the district concerned.”

NORTHERN RHODESIA

"The misrepresentation and persecution which has been going on in Northern Rhodesia has not discouraged the brethren in any way. Although the greater number of the Society’s publications are at present banned, the publishers, by putting forth the best effort to date, equaled the distribution during the corresponding effort of last year. Just before the campaign began all the Europeans in Northern Rhodesia were served with a copy of GA No. 425. This special issue has since been the subject of a question in the Legislative Council. There cannot be any Europeans, and can be only a very few natives, in Northern Rhodesia who have not heard of Jehovah’s witnesses and the message of the Lord which they declare.”

SOUTHERN RHODESIA

“The proposed sedition bill and native preachers bill which are now being discussed, and which if they become law will bring conditions In this country into line with those now obtaining in Germany, did not give any of the Lord’s true witnesses cold feet in Southern Rhodesia. Judging by the time parliament is taking over the bills they appear to be in a real quandary. They are hoping that the bother in Northern Rhodesia would provide a nice background for these bills, but, the attempt to make us the scapegoat in Northern Rhodesia having failed and the secretary of state for the colonies having just decided that our request for European control in Northern Rhodesia should be granted, the legislators in Southern Rhodesia, having admitted that the bills were aimed at us, do not appear to know now which way to turn. The brethren are in good spirit and pushing on with the work.”

UNITED STATES

On the evening of April 6 throughout the land 35,172 of Jehovah’s people assembled to celebrate the memorial of Jehovah's name. Of this number, 25,435 partook. The heartcheering words of the “Courage and Victory” lecture were heard by most of the brethren at this time, strengthening them to have a share in Jehovah’s “strange work”. The Memorial attendance this year is the largest on record.

In the United States 24,441 brethren were in the field during “His Strange Work” testimony period; which is the largest on record for any special period. It is interesting to note that this number is more than double the number of publishers in the field during a similar period in 1931 (12,141 publishers), and more than three times that of the publishers for a similar period in 1928 (7,951 publishers).

The release of Riches during “His Strange Work” testimony period in the United States exceeded that of any previous bound book.

The “Separating the Nations” lecture, together with other lectures, was used to broadcast the Kingdom message. There was an increase in sound attendance of 300,000 persons, over the report for last year. It has also been reported that quite a number of Riches study classes have been started to further aid the people of good will in getting a knowledge of the Kingdom.

“His Strange Work” testimony period was certainly a season of great refreshment and joy. It was filled with strenuous activity to the praise of Jehovah's name. It was the greatest rally of Jehovah’s people up to this time in this land to raise high the standard of the Kingdom. All praise to Jehovah for continually sustaining his people and granting them the privilege of bearing his name before the nations.

Station EAQ Madrid Doing Splendid Work

Words of encouragement sent to the various broadcasting stations are a good thing. Many of the brethren in the United .States who have shortwave sets can pick up EAQ Madrid, Spain. We heartily recommend that you tune in, listen to the program, and write a letter to the station expressing your appreciation and setting out how satisfactorily the broadcast was received. The brethren in Canada, the United States and Mexico, and anywhere, for that matter, should do this. The Roman Catholic Hierarchy would very much like to stop this station from broadcasting the truth. Your letters every now and then will show that it has a good audience. What is especially true of station EAQ should also be carried out in this country among our local stations. EAQ broadcasts on Wednesday at 7:15 p.m., on Saturday at 7:15 p.m., Eastern Standard time. These two are English broadcasts. Additionally, there is a Spanish broadcast on Sunday at 6: 45 p.m.

UNTIL END OF SEPTEMBER

Begin at once, carrying on the petition work in conjunction with all regular witnessing and in special campaigns. Plan definitely to provide everyone in your assigned territory with the opportunity to sign the petition.

By the end of August, serve as nearly as possible all rural sections; and during September, all city assignments, Including business and residential. Aim to complete the work by September 30. Report in the usual manner the hours spent in such house-to-house service.

At each phonograph and transcription meeting announce the opportunity to sign; and also in connection with all sound-ear work, reading entire petition once at each location after reading the introductory card.

SEVEN MILLION

To aid each publisher to see a goal, we offer the reminder that in the United States, during the first seven months of the present service year, more than five million persons were served with literature by Jehovah’s witnesses and their companions. Much of that work was done during one of the most strenuous of winters. Jehovah sustained His servants.—Psalm 147:17.

As a goal, then, for the work from now on it is suggested that an average of 350 signatures taken by each of 20,000 publishers shows a total of SEVEN MILLION signers. That is the equivalent of about one-half of this country’s “Catholic population”, not counting babies and young children.

To increase the appreciation of all as to the importance and value of working shoulder to shoulder, it is further suggested that each one who understands that he can serve more than the proposed average of 350 persons with opportunity to sign the petition do so, and also pair together for this special work with one who, by reason of physical infirmity or other unavoidable handicap, believes he can serve less than the average of 350 persons.

THOROUGHNESS

Let thoroughness be the keynote of this petition service to fair-minded people throughout the land. Study carefully the entire outline of cooperation here shown.

Let each publisher and each company servant prepare thoughtfully, in advance, his part in the work. Then, in union with all of Jehovah’s publishers of the entire country, with joyful and fearless determination pursue the task untiringly until it is done, confidently believing that the true and living God, who gives the increase, can and will preserve and sustain His faithful servants and greatly prosper the work of their hands, for His own name’s sake.

Pioneers should give first attention to their regular work, introducing the petition when it appears to be opportune.
