Jehovah’s Purpose Shall Be Sustained


January Activities Should Increase and Prosper

“There is no furlough in war.” With this principle in mind, the members of the Free Nation and their associates press forward. Although having just finished another Testimony Period, proving themselves “more than conquerors”, there is yet much work to be done.

The offer for the month of January is the book “The Truth Shall Make You Free” and the booklet Freedom in the New World, on a 25c contribution. If your stock of this is depleted, use the latest book you have until your new supplies arrive. When making your first back-call on the book obtainers, be sure to present the Study Questions booklet and show them its advantages.

Those companies who have a supply of the 1944 Yearbook of Jehovah’s witnesses, above the needs of those associated with the company, should arrange to distribute these during January to those persons of good-will who are interested in the truth, at the rate of 50c.

Company Organization

The servants of the company should serve their brethren. Territory should be lined up, giving particular attention to that city and business territory not already worked with the latest book. January provides an excellent opportunity to thoroughly work this, especially the business. Group witnessing should be arranged for those brethren who are unable to hold an individual assignment. Group back-call arrangements should also be made to call back within the month on those book obtainers, by the same group. Perhaps demonstration at service meetings could be given to aid this, showing the presentation of the book and the directing of their attention to pages 375-370 of the book, and then the follow-through back-call, using the Study Questions booklet.

Personal Publisher Organization

In order to be assured of sustaining your purpose for the month, prepare. Do not leave matters to chance. Obtain territory near your home. See that you have an adequate supply of literature and Kinp-dom News. Your phonograph should be supplied with records and needles. Have your Testimony Card properly filled out and signed. Your Jehovah’s Servants Defended booklet should be available. Get back-call slips, followup reports, House to House Record forms, book marks, and other personal witnessing equipment, handy for use. Do not always have to ‘search’ for these before you go witnessing. Keep all instruments in ‘firing position’ and confine your ‘searching’ to the territory. Further, budget your time. Be systematic and arrange for a dependable schedule


and then follow it out. Routine accomplishes the real work.

The most important part of the personal preparation is your own study, mental preparation. Be able to discuss the message intelligently. Know the contents of the publications you offer. Have clearly in mind what you want to tell the people concerning the literature, arrange it in logical order, and then present that, brief though you may be, to the best advantage. In order that you may be an able minister, always ready for defense, a thorough knowledge and understanding of the contents of the booklet Freedom of Worship is necessary: hence, advance study.

This preparation cannot be done by any other than the individual Kingdom publisher, and we feel sure that each one will gladly do this, ever appreciating the serious work ahead. Of course, when made, this must be carried out. However, It is first realizing the purpose, and then making practical preparation for upholding that purpose, that will sustain it. This will bring Jehovah’s blessing, and the prosperity of increase will result. Concerning this, the October 1 Watchtower says:

“As Jehovah’s witnesses, in obedience to his command to preach, have gone to as many of the nations as they could penetrate by personal house-to-house witnessing, and by printed page, radio, telephone, and underground, our only Lord God has added to the nation. . . . The increasing of the Lord’s Nation has therefore not yet reached to the populousness he lias decreed for it be-


fore Armageddon finishes off the envious opposers of such increase. The increase thus far is to Jehovah’s glory.”

Conduct Your Quota of Book Studies

This is very gratifying indeed, and the brethren who conducted these studies received a rich blessing from the Lord. Theocratic Ministry Library

The “Course in Theocratic Ministry” has been in operation in most companies nearly one year. The enrollment generally is very gratifying and the progress made by the individuals applying themselves to the course is very marked, and clearly shows the Lord’s blessing upon their efforts. Our younger brethren not only are better publishers, but also excel in their school work as a result of this course.

One drawback that some brethren encountered was the lack of proper reference works. To overcome this, many companies have installed what they call a “Theocratic Ministry Library” at the company headquarters. This library is accessible to all the brethren at certain convenient times, usually Saturday afternoon and evening before and after magazine street work, and at other times mutually agreed upon.

This library usually contains a complete set of the current hooks from The Harp of God to “The Truth Shall Make You Free"; a complete set of the booklets; a file of the Watchtower and Consolation magazines (they generally take these copies from their distributors’ supply) ; a good concordance and a Bible dictionary ; as many different translations of the Bible as they can obtain ; a good dictionary; some good books on composition, outlining and grammar, and any other material that will assist the brethren in their studies. These books are marked “The Property of the ________________________

Company of Jehovah’s witnesses". Many local brethren who have such works contribute them to the library for the benefit of the company. The Society is in full accord with and thoroughly approves these arrangements.

Open Pioneer Territory

Today there are many people who are ‘seeking after God, if haply they may find him’, who have not yet been reached by the good news of Jehovah’s kingdom. This is so even in the United States, where the ratio of publishers to the population is approximately one to two thousand. True, if all the publishers were evenly distributed, and if all the pioneer and company publishers engaged regularly in the house-to-house work and made diligent efforts to meet their quotas, the accessible population of this country would come fairly close to being covered four times in six months.

But the publishers are not evenly distributed and many are not meeting the requirements. As a result, there are sections of the country (counties and small cities) that, year after year, are not receiving proper attention. Through the “special publisher” arrangement practically all of the isolated cities of four thousand or more population have been witnessed to at least four times. But for isolated counties and cities under four thousand, the witness depends upon general pioneers.

We feel sure that there are many general pioneers who would be glad to fill this breach of isolated territory so long idle, if they had more information on the matter, particularly the location and the length of time since it was last witnessed to. Many of these counties have towns with populations ranging from 2,000 to 3,500, which general pioneers with only limited facilities could easily work. For the benefit of all who would like to respond to the call for service where the need is greatest, we are beginning, with this issue of the Informant, to publish a list of counties available, showing the location and the date last covered. When making request, please give two or three selections, as others may also be considering the same.
