New Company Chart and Its Use

Most of the companies have by this time received their company chart for the service years 1945 and 194G. Detailed instructions have already gone forth to you on the sheet headed “Company Chart Instructions”. A careful study of this should be made by each company at the last service meeting in August. Thirty minutes should be set aside for this, and a capable brother should thoroughly consider this with the company assembled.

Bear in mind that this chart is prepared for your benefit. It is simple, easy to understand, and lays the facts clearly before you. That the full benefit of the chart may be derived by all, at the beginning of each month, after the previous month’s totals have been posted on the chart, a capable brother will consider the past month’s report, taking up the weak and the strong points, analyzing the activity of the company, comparing the month’s report with the previous year’s average monthly activity figure, and with the monthly quota, as well as other pertinent points relating to local Kingdom activity.

Establish Practical Individual Budget

During the coming month keep an accurate record of how you spend your time; that is, how much in Kingdom work, secular work, housework, etc. Then, at the end of the month, go over this record and see just what you accomplished; or, in other words, make a check-up on yourself. Using this month’s actual record as a basis, make a budget of your time for the coming month, striving to make it call for GO hours and no less than the average hour quota shown on the chart. Alongside this budget, keep actual record of how you have conformed to it, and, at the end of that month, again check up on yourself, and make proper adjustments for the coming month. Thus make your work progressive and systematic, and each month conform more and more to New World requirements. Carefully and conscientiously following through in this manner, we believe, will aid you to render your “reasonable service” unto the Lord.

August Report and Inventory

Included with this Informant all companies and pioneers will find an Inventory of Stock sheet. On August 28, 1944, a complete physical inventory of your stock on hand should be made in accordance with the Instructions appearing on your respective inventory sheets. Each company and pioneer should forward this inventory to the Society immediately after the close of the month’s activity. Each company publisher should see that his field reports are all turned in to the company no later than August 31, so that the company report can be compiled and forwarded as above. This would be a good time to audit all company accounts, too.

Room for More Pioneers!

The Field Is Large and the Laborers Are Few

For several years a number of pioneers carried on the list did not appreciate the points clearly defined in Organization Instructions that all Theocratic publishers do the same work; that they are grouped according to the amount of time spent in tlie field; and that the requirement for general pioneers is 150 hours a month. (Anyone unable to meet that requirement is not a pioneer in the true sense of the w’ord, regardless of what list carries his name.) As a result, the average in hours for pioneers in the United States for the year 1943 was the lowest of any country. To remedy this situation a check-up was made and all those pioneers who were unable to be pioneers in fact were transferred to other service. This largely accounts for the drop in number of pioneers and also the splendid increase in hours, etc., in recent months.

Every six months a class of special publishers graduates from Gilead, and as the way opens up for them they are sent to fields of service in other lands, thus making room for more special publishers in the United States.

While the field has ever been large, and the laborers few’, today, more than ever before, there is room for those who are willing and aide to leave all anil become “fishers of men”; to become full-time teachers giving free education in freedom to men of good-will.

True, there are trials and obstacles peculiar to such a life of service; but is it not far better to suffer these for the privilege of full-time service than to suffer the trials associated with serving some worldly master? And while there are many who w’ould i give their right eye to be in the full-time service, but who cannot because of Scriptural obligations, there are also many who are so situated as to be able to be In the full-time service but lack the perception or the ability to see that the full-time service is for them. The Territory Servant

Far from being in possession of an ‘easy’ job of merely assigning or renewing a territory when desired by a publisher, the territory servant finds himself actually with the responsibility of seeing that the entire assignment of company territory is properly mapped out, divided, and assigned to company publishers in such a manner as to insure its thorough and systematic coverage in accordance with Organization Instructions. Like all other servants, he has excellent opportunity to demonstrate proper diligence in working an assignment of territory as an example in faithful field service.

Proper Division and Assignment

Large tracts of territory that cannot possibly be cared for by the publisher will be divided into portions properly suited to frequent going over; the ideal arrangement being that the individual publisher be assigned to his own territory with full responsibility to care for all door-to-door work, back-calling and magazine distribution therein. Book-study groups will also be assigned territories by the territory servant where all attending the study meeting may co-operate in stimulating interest and directing it to the study. Likewise, the territory not assigned to individual publishers will be carefully charted and prepared for company group witnessing, care being taken by the territory servant and back-call servant working in conjunction with each other to see that all back-calls in such assignment are issued and properly followed up.

The territory servant will also see that each issue of Kingdom News is used to prepare territory by advance distribution, that portion of territory about to be covered with literature being gone over a week in advance by a distribution of Kingdom News. To have a fully tabulated picture of conditions in the territory, the territory servant will see that diligent use of the House to House Record is made, the slips properly filled out being essential to efficient performance by the individual publisher in properly working his territory.

Check Kept on All Territories

Every three months all territories will be checked by the territory servant and back-call servant to determine tlie progress being made in the various localities. The number of times worked will be shown, four times each six months being the ideal aim. Of course, reassignment of territories for all so desiring will lie made immediately after cheeking. Company territories not being covered the required four times every six months will provide the territory servant excellent opportunity to appear before the company in service meetings to devise ways for increased co-operation of all in the company to remedy the situation. The same applies to business territory; he will see that it is properly looked after, being worked either by pioneers or by company publishers.

In this connection the territory servant will check the publishers’ record cards to determine whether the amount of time spent, the number of back-calls made and book studies being held, and the amount of literature being placed, are sufficient to properly care for the territory.

A neat map clearly showing the boundaries of all territories of the company will be displayed in the hall, and the territory servant will do all possible to see that the territories are worked by those to whom they are assigned.
