Watchtower Campaign

The scope of the great command of the Lord forming our 1944 service calendar text for May is not limited to any particular section of the earth, but takes in the entire globe. His command to his disciples was, “Go ye therefore, and teach all nations, . . . teaching them to observe all things whatsoever I have commanded you.” (Matt. 28:19,20) This command applies now to the “faithful and wise servant”, the Lord's Society of his present disciples, and which Society is devoted to carrying out this command. It also applies to their companions and all people of good-will who now have a knowledge of the Lord’s purposes. The Lord's favor and blessing is evident on his organized people in that great headway is being made toward the end of expanding the campaign of free education to all nations. This is clearly shown by the Lord’s blessing on the course in Theocratic ministry and on the Watchtower Bible College of Gilead.

That the greatest number of persons throughout all nations may be fed from the Lord’s great storehouse and taught his precepts and law, the Society has used and continues to use every legitimate means of getting the teaching work done. One means of taking the message of free education to the people is that of booklets containing the Kingdom message in concise and compact form. Throughout the country there are thousands of colored-cover booklets that have been printed since about 1930 and shipped to the Lord's people for distribution but, for one reason or another, have not been placed in the hands of the people. All of these abound in the truth that the prospective “other sheep” of the Lord must receive. These publications the Lord caused to be printed for a purpose. They should not stay in storage as they have been with many companies and pioneers for some time. No, these are to be put to their intended and proper use now. The work for May is arranged for that purpose. The Lord is no waster, and he is directing that these be not wasted by deterioration simply because there is such an abundant supply of later publications on hand. Each booklet contains its own specific Theocratic message essential to a full knowledge of the Lord's purposes. Therefore it is imperative that these be placed in the hands of the people now.

Combination Offer

The offer will be three booklets on a 10c contribution. These booklet combinations will, by most companies and pioneers, be made up of two surplus colored-cover booklets and the booklet The Coming World Tiegeneration. The latter should be included in every combination. If you run low on colored-cover booklets, make it one coloredcover booklet and two self-covered. If you run completely out of coloredcover booklets it will be in order to offer three self-covered ones on the ten-cent contribution. If they do not wish the combination, stress the importance of their reading The Coming World Regeneration, stating that they may contribute a small amount for it. If they do not wish to contribute, but will promise to read it, leave it with them.

Assist New Publishers

May will afford a splendid opportunity for the servants, regular publishers and book study conductors to assist the new publishers, particularly those reporting activity in April for the Memorial special service week-end, and also the ones attending Memorial that expressed definite interest and wish to have part in Theocratic service. No doubt you have territory that needs covering with the new Kingdom News prior to the regular current offer. Give these new publishers the opportunity of covering a section of the territory with Kingdom, News, and point out how they have the privilege of calling back on the people in such territory a week later with the current booklet offer. Show them how to present the literature, and then suggest that they try witnessing alone in the territory. Provide them with the necessary forms for reporting: “Publisher’s Field Service Report” slips, “Back-Call” slips, “Back-Call Follow-Up” slips, “House to House Record” forms, and instruct them as to the proper use of such. Also, the blank testimony cards can be furnished the very new ones, such cards being obtainable on request at the Society’s headquarters in Brooklyn. Suggest the use of the phonograph and the doorstep setup if the phonograph is available.

The company servant should see that group-witnessing arrangements are made to cover territory not assigned individually and isolated urban (thickly populated) sections. The servants, study conductors and regular publishers should take the initiative and see that definite arrangements are made so that all associated and new interest desiring a part in the work will have the opportunity to participate. By special concentration, making personal affairs conform to increasing Theocratic requirements, along with the assistance that the new publishers will give in covering the territory, each territory in the company should be covered approximately one time during May.

Essential to Able Ministry

The remarkable evidences of the Lord’s favor and blessing upon those who have earnestly embraced the provisions of the Theocratic ministry course during the past year are innumerable. Young and old alike have been blessed. The fluent and the halting have found it indispensable. Those whose native tongue is English and those who have been accustomed to speaking some other language alike find that they cannot get along without it. This is the Lord's provision to make one and all more able ministers, and no one can afford to do despite to it.

Our brethren speaking other than English have probably been blessed most of all, because this course proved an invaluable aid in acquiring knowledge of English and in arranging and presenting it in the most effective manner possible. The Theocratic ministry study is an integral part of the Theocratic requirements for the Lord’s people. Every company, regardless of size, should by now be conducting the course. Everyone in the company should attend these meetings, and all male brethren, young or old, educated or of only limited education, should enroll. Even the illiterate have been great!y assisted.

With the March statement the Society sent each company two letters and two schedules outlining in detail the course in Theocratic ministry for the coming year. This schedule commences June 4, 1944, giving three subjects for each week. Where only one speaker is scheduled for each week, the first subject should always he used. Whore two speakers are available, the first and either the second or the third subject may be used. If three speakers are scheduled, each one of the speaking assignments will be used.

Schedules

One of the schedules is to be posted on the company information board of each company headquarters, and the other retained by the Theocratic ministry instructor in his book. Of the two letters, one should be retained by the company servant for his files and the other is to be given to the Theocratic ministry conductor for his book.

Letter of Appreciation to Pioneers

With the crumbling and passing away of the present old world affairs, the service of the incoming New World of righteousness takes on ever-increasing importance and delight to those who love Jehovah. In review of six months of the pioneer activity, the Society has sent a letter of appreciation to those fulltime servants who have given themselves freely to the service of God, rejoicing with them in the many evidences of Jehovah’s favor upon their activities.

Hours, back-calls, and book studies have all been increased by the pioneers, in keeping with the advancement of the mighty campaign of free education now being carried forward under the supervision of our great King, and it is apparent that many more brethren are desirous of sharing in these blessings by devoting themselves to full-time service. These realize that this is not an easy way out of present world difficulties; in fact, times may be harder while engaging in pioneer action; but where else can the joy, peace, and evidence of God’s favor that comes from full-time service be equaled? May Jehovah bless and guide all who contemplate devoting their entire time to His service.

Back-Call Servant

The continuous expansion of the educational activity of God’s able ministers causes the duties of the back-call servant to occupy a position of much responsibility in the company organization. The gathering together or assembling of the “other sheep” with those of Zion depends to a large extent upon the faithfulness of each company in carrying forward the back-call service to acquaint those of good-will with the way to the Kingdom.

For these reasons the back-call servant must have a clear vision of the timeliness of diligent back-call activity, take the lead in this feature of service, and keep it continually before the company for its advancement. He will check the publishers’ record cards with the intent of aiding all not doing back-call work to see Its urgent necessity and have a share in it.

All Essential Forms Provided

Many forms have been provided and a complete system developed by the Society for keeping the record of all interest located in the different territories. The back-call servant must see to it that these forms are utilized by all in the company. Always alert to discern good-will interest, the publisher will record the names of all definitely interested. After making the first back-call, a follow-up slip is promptly made out and given to the back-call servant. After it has been established by three back-calls that genuine interest exists, the back-call servant will then make out a permanent record slip and keep it in his permanent file. In order to see that all names are being properly cared for he will then check all the follow-up slips received each month with the permanent back-call slips on hand. If some names are not being followed up, he should ascertain the reason. If the person is still interested, the back-call servant will make all effort possible to see that the good-will person is regularly called on. The permanant slip should be destroyed only if interest no longer exists.

Proper System of Filing

Advancement of the back-call activity will result from continual checking by the back-call servant with all study conductors and publishers in the company, and keeping all back-call information neatly and accurately filed. The first essential, then, is to get all publishers back-call-minded and engaging freely in this work, and to exercise much care in keeping the records thereof. A working back-call file will be kept with all the names located in unassigned territory, which names will be handed to publishers whenever the territory is taken out. In this connection there must be close co-operation between the back-call and territory servants. Permanent back-call slips will be filed according to territory, arranging such in alphabetical order within each territory. This will be the permanent or duplicate file for the company's records.

The whole company can be thoroughly instructed in back-call activity by frequent appearance of the back-call servant on the service meeting programs. Demonstrations can be made to illustrate the development of good-will interest from its first location until definitely associated with the organization. To assure that new interest is being properly tied In with the local organization the back-call servant should co-operate closely with all study groups.
