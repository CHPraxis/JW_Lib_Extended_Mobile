“Freedom Now!” Testimony Period


Not Entangled by Yoke of Bondage


Grand Opportunities to Present New Book

The title of December’s Testimony Period definitely places special emphasis on the fact that note freedom prevails among God's people. No one devoted to Jehovah will neglect to avail himself of such freedom. This condition exists chiefly because the power of God exercised in behalf of his people has prevented the enemy from closing in on them, and. secondly, because His witnesses have zealously pressed ahead in using the privileges thus extended and have fought valiantly to maintain this favored position.

The issue of loyalty to the New World or to the “present evil world” becomes more finely drawn each passing month, and the need of preserving the stranger's rights to learn the way to life becomes more urgent. Education of Jehovah’s servants to serve as willing and able ministers has equipped them for efficient performance, the organization of the Lord’s people for unrestrained activity is now highly developed, and the freedom to utilize all this lays the matter squarely before every individual as to his reaction. Can any fail or refuse to heed Christ’s commands to ‘preach this gospel of the Kingdom and teach all nations’ under the present prevailing conditions? The “Freedom Now I” Testimony Period provides the opportunity to meet this challenge in favor of wholehearted Kingdom activity, which activity includes the offering to all men of good will the way to real freedom.

Kingdom Gospel — New Offer

The time eagerly looked forward to ever since the United Announcers’ Theocratic Assembly has now arrived. Every sincere announcer will greatly rejoice in the opportunity to present the new book “The Kingdom Is at Hand” and the booklet “The Kingdom of God Is Nigh,”, on a contribution of 25e, which is the offer for door-to-door activity during December. This makes all territory ‘new’, and the thrilling, soulstirring message presented in the current offer brings before all men their only hope and truly makes every bearer of the message a Kingdom ambassador in the fullest sense.

The month of December usually brings the brethren face to face with definite wintry conditions which, unless overcome, will curtail their participation in announcing the Kingdom. The Informant has stressed many times the need of properly dressing for winter activity. Of course, and above everything else, the will and determination to share in the "Freedom Now !” Testimony Period regardless of any obstacle is what will result in continued or even increased service. Where the will to serve exists, the cold weather, the flat tire, the muddy roads, or any other hindrance, does not become an excuse to ease up and quit, but presents merely another obstacle to be overcome ; and from this angle the work for December opens up grand opportunities for everyone.

Every Feature Can Be Advanced

Instead of merely standing on a cold, wind-swept corner while engaging in the street work, the publisher will keep much warmer by-moving around, entering the stores, and talking to people in cars and on the streets, and thus be enabled to continue in this line of activity in spite of cold weather.

As far as back-call and book-study work is concerned the winter is the ideal time. People that in summertime might make the claim that they were busy with farm or garden work and wanted to be out of doors are now to be found in the house, and, as the Kingdom book is helpfully accompanied with a question booklet, wherever good interest is found it can be immediately developed and nourished by calling back and starting a book study. A fine opportunity now exists to call back upon those with whom studies may once have been held and who for one reason or another saw fit to discontinue them. Seed once planted may germinate and in time grow, and, if such people could not grasp the truth when first studied with them, perhaps the desire to learn has been stimulated by now, and surely no subject or presentation of the Scriptures could appeal to honest men more than that offered in the new book. Thus, in every feature of field activity, December’s offer and opportunities afford privileges and favors that must thrill every publisher both in anticipation and participation.

Breaking Away from Bondage

Wicked men have in many cases restrained the Lord’s people from freely serving him. This is bondage hard to be borne and causes much distress, but the most terrible bondage is that entered into by any of God’s people through negligence or indifference toward the Kingdom interests. Failure to be wholly submissive and willing to do the Lord’s bidding, and yielding to the demands of this evil old world, is a yoke of bondage that can and will be avoided by all those who now stand fast for freedom. The purpose of that ‘freedom wherewith Christ makes us free’ is shown in Isaiah 42:7, the monthly text for December: “To open the blind eyes, to bring out the prisoners from the prison, and them that sit in darkness out of the prison house." A glorious commission, everything needful provided in the way of literature and equipment, an army of willing and freedom-loving publishers who pledged themselves in the Resolution adopted at the United Announcers’ Assembly to carry on the present joyful work of announcing the Kingdom regardless of enemy action, and freedom now in which to use the great privileges afforded. Such is the happy picture for December. Every free servant of God will gladly respond.

Infoi’mation on Singing

Many companies have written to the Society inquiring whether or not it is in harmony with instructions to sing at company meetings other than the service meeting. There is no objection to the brethren’s singing if they wish to sing before or after the meetings. However, the singing should in no wise take up any of the hour set aside for the meeting itself.

“Kingdom News” No. 14

Again the Lord has furnished his Theocratic announcers of the evangel with another Kingdom News: “Overcoming Fear of What Is Coming upon the Earth.” What a timely and encouraging message! Clear and to the point, without false optimism, not mincing words but hewing to the line, it shows that though statesmen may profess that they have nothing to fear but fear, yet the very fact that they hold one conference after another and propose so many conflicting remedies speaks louder than words and gives proof that Jesus’ words are being fulfilled regarding ‘men’s hearts failing them for fear and for looking after those things that are coming upon the earth’.

And that while men fear the threatening and uncertain future, those who have obtained a correct acquaintance with the truth and are living in harmony with it rejoice in such indications that the end of Satan’s world is near; for these know that this does not mean the end of the globe upon which they live, but the end of Satan’s demon rule and of his wicked organization on earth, and that in its place will be Jehovah’s government, orderly, righteous, and beneficial.

All territory is now virgin soil for this new Kingdom News. An effective way to cover it is: Each week, after completing the door-to-door work with the literature offer, cover with the Kingdom News as much territory as one expects to cover the following week w’ith the literature presentation.

Be sure to have the Kingdom News with you wherever you go, including the magazine work and on your back-calls, as well as in your regular house-to-house work. Also, as opportunities to give a witness arise while engaged in other pursuits. be prepared to leave a Kingdom News with them. The truth has helped us to overcome fear. Let us not withhold from the “stranger” his right to also overcome fear and to worship God and gain freedom. Leave a Kingdom News in every home at every opportunity.

The Theocratic Ministry Instructor

Must Be Apt and Able Minister

Long ago Christ Jesus gave assurance to his brethren, as recorded in Luke 21 : 15, that they would be equipped to meet their adversaries without being gainsaid. The Theocratic ministry course is one of the chief means of so equipping the servants of the Lord; therefore the Theocratic ministry instructor will do all in his power to fully equip himself as an instructor. His aptitude and ability to Instruct and counsel will have much to do with the success or failure of the course in his particular company.

Counsel and Co-operation of Students

The advancement of the Kingdom interests and the preparation of the students to be properly fitted for that work is his first concern, and the brethren putting forth time and effort to improve themselves as able ministers can be greatly encouraged or discouraged by the manner in which their efforts are received and handled by the instructor. Disparagement of a student's efforts, indulging in personalities or partialities, will result in difficulties, and eventually a lack of Interest and an unwillingness on the part of the students to take their assignments. This would hinder the real purpose of the course. Where mistakes and failings appear they will be corrected solely from the angle of seeking to measure up to as high a standard as possible, and where an instructor gives evidence of knowing his duties and possessing the ability to help others advance he will gain the cooperation of all concerned, and the whole company will profit accordingly.

A library for the use of all in the company for reference purposes will be of great aid, and the instructor will encourage all in the use of it. He will likewise encourage everyone in the company, students and others, to make every effort to profit by the review of the lesson or instruction talk given in the previous week. The privilege of becoming able ministers extends to all in the company, and the ability to answer the review questions will show that the previous lesson has been learned and applied. Advanced students will be used by the instructor to give instruction talks, but the instructor will give all the needed counsel at all times. Counsel or criticism will not be asked of nor given by publishers attending the course; and for this reason the instructor must be thoroughly able and competent to aid and instruct the students in all features of the ministry course. The student giving the instruction talk on an occasion will, of course, conduct the review on such talk the following week.

Maintaining a Profitable School

There is not much good accomplished by dropping members off the roll, and such should not be done unless the student willingly absents himself and fails to share in the benefits of the course. These benefits are so essential and so much needed by every Theocratic minister that the real aim of the instructor will be to make the course so interesting and profitable that everyone will want to share in it and be very unwilling to be dropped.

Co-operation between the instructor and the students and all in the company for the greatest good of all concerned is the goal; and where the spirit of the Lord is permitted to direct and guide the efforts there will be brought forth or developed from this course a united company of able ministers, meek and apt to teach, usable by Jehovah to His praise.
