House-to-House Work Backbone of Field Activity

Our calendar for September directs our attention to the Course in Theocratic Ministry. “Give instruction to a wise man, and he will be yet wiser: teach a just man, and he will increase in learning.”—Prov. 9:9. As called to our attention at the recent Assembly, our big job as ministers is to preach the gospel from house to house, place literature, and call back and endeavor to aid those who have received the literature in their study of it.

Why Provided?

It is just for this very work that the Course in Theocratic Ministry was provided by the Lord. Many are inclined to take a much too narrow view of it, seeing only what appears at the weekly study meeting, and fail to associate with it its real purpose. Yes, to limit one’s conception of it thus is to miss the point of the course entirely, which is not merely to develop a handful of platform speakers, but to make every one of Jehovah’s servants, old and young, male and female, more able ministers in the field service. And to realize this purpose we must make conscious, diligent and consistent efforts to apply in our field service the lessons learned in the ministry course.

In the House-to-House Work

First of ail, in our house-to-house presentation. As records wear out and new publishers join the ranks who are unable to procure phonographs it becomes more and more imperative that we start right. During the month of September we shall continue to present the offer of the book “The Truth Shall Make 'You Free”, the colored-cover booklet Religion Reaps the Whirhcind, and the self-covered booklet The Coming World Regeneration, on a contribution of 30c. In presenting the Kingdom message at the door we will be as careful as if we were on the platform addressing a company or a unit. We will endeavor to have poise, greet the householder with a smile, and in a relaxed and conversational tone state the purpose of our call, having definitely in mind what we intend to say for our opening sentence. As we note the response of our “audience” we will use Theocratic tactfulness, presenting Scriptural arguments and logic in such a manner as to help them to see the truth and make it easy for them to let go of error. Thus we demonstrate that we are “apt to teach”. Should they show a contrary spirit, we will not lose control of our spirit, our mental disposition, but will endeavor ‘in patience to teacli those that oppose themselves’. If, try as we may, we have been unable to make our point, and we depart without seeing any tangible result of our efforts, we will immediately reflect on our manner of handling the situation and try to see where, either in what we said or how we said it, we could have done better, and will make a mental note of it for the next such instance. Doing thus, our presentation will become more and more effective.

Not Easily Discouraged or Sidetracked

Because we have been repeatedly counseled not to get into heated and long-winded arguments with those whom we meet at the door, some have gone to the other extreme of following the line of least resistance by accepting every expression of indifference or opposition as final. Instead, here again we should use what we have learned in the course regarding arousing interest and tactfully overcoming opposition. The question form of Introduction is a good one to use in such cases, doing this in meekness, and in a kind and sincere tone of voice, “a soft answer.”

Failure to follow such a course of action has caused some to become discouraged in the house-to-house work, who, as a result, are now limiting their activity to the street magazine-work. This is a big mistake ! The house-to-house work lays the foundation for the back-call and book-study work, and is the ideal method for approaching the people. It is the real hand-to-hand grappling with the enemy, and, while the most strenuous, it is also the most stimulating and holds the most surprises for the publisher, as every house is a question mark.

In Back-Calls and at Book Studies

The same, of course, applies to the making of back-calls and the conducting of book studies, only much more. Here again we must be relaxed and manifest a friendly, sincere and meek disposition. As able ministers we will be well equipped as to the doctrines of the Bible, be well acquainted with our Bibles, and know how to find the information needed in either Theocratic or non-Theocratlc helps. And especially should we have the subject matter well in mind and be able to show the connection between the scriptures cited or quoted and the argument being presented. We will have looked up all unfamiliar words so as to know their meaning and how they should be pronounced. Thus fully knowing whereof we speak, we shall instill confidence into those whom we are endeavoring to teach that what we present is indeed the truth; for we shall be teaching with authority and not as the religionists. Doing thus, we shall be making full proof of our ministry.

The New Releases Our New Weapons

The joyful expression on the lips of all Theocratic announcers, after attending this year's Assembly, is “What a convention!” The themes


there developed still ring through our minds: Seek ye first the Kingdom ! This gospel of the Kingdom must be preached! The kingdom of God is nigh! While thus rejoicing and giving thanks to Jehovah for the many blessings received the sober question presents itself: “What shall I do now to faithfully fulfill my commission in preaching this gospel of the Kingdom?”

One of the main purposes of the convention, of course, was to imbue God’s people with an absolute and wholehearted appreciation of the importance of the Kingdom, their responsibility of discharging their commission as its announcers, and of enduring faithfully for it. The Resolution adopted on the afternoon of August 12, after the stirring presentation of our necessity to preach the gospel of the Kingdom, puts every consecrated servant of God as definitely committed to an unswerving course of faithful activity in Kingdom service.

This Resolution also clearly shows that, in spite of any postwar attempts of the old world to hinder such activity, God’s zealous servants will maintain integrity and never slack in preaching this gospel down to the end of Satan’s visible organization. As demonstrated during the session entitled “Faithful Endurance for the Kingdom”, some of God’s faithful remnant have served more than fifty years in Kingdom activity, and still are not tired or pining to leave the scene of service. If these are eager to continue in the fight until and through Armageddon, shall their companions, who have served comparatively few years, grow lax just because it may be a few more years than they onee thought?

Publications for Our Aid as Announcers

Lasting aids and provisions were brought forth at the Assembly to enable the Lord’s people to fulfill their preaching commission. The first was the Kingdom Service Song Book. The opportunity to now make melody in our own hearts, and to sing the songs of Zion in unison, will greatly aid in efficient singing of the Song of Moses and the Lamb in field activity. The new songbook is Theocratic throughout, no sad melodies, no religious words, no creature worship, but entirely Kingdom service and praise to Jehovah. Who have more cause for joy and singing than Jehovah's servants? For this reason the release of the songbook was hailed with great joy, and all now rejoice in this gracious gift from God.

Thursday was devoted to field-service features, and, appropriately at its close, our choicest instrument yet, for use in our ministry, the Watchtower edition of the American Standard Version Bible, was released. Yes, due to the Course in Theocratic Ministry, Jehovah’s ministers were becoming more and more “version”- and “translation”-minded. No longer were they content merely to quote from the popular King James version; but if another more modern translation expressed the thought better, it was used. What could be more fitting, therefore, than that one of the best, if not the best, of modern versions of the Bible should now be published by the Society? It is a version that puts the name of Jehovah where it belongs: in the foreground as the most prominent name of the Hebrew Scriptures. With its boldface type, its freedom from archaisms in spelling, punctuation, and grammar, and its comprehensive 95-page cyclopedic concordance, it certainly will prove to be the most valuable instrument Jehovah’s ministers have yet received.

New Campaign Equipment

Saturday afternoon was the occasion of another grand surprise when the bound book “The Kingdom Is at Hand” was released, and all present who understood and were in favor of the Resolution there passed were each given a free copy. The teaching of the Kingdom being the paramount Bible theme, how valuable a work which traces that theme through the many pages of the Bible, from Genesis to Revelation ! In a style that is a happy combination of beautiful and forceful Scriptural idiom and pointed modern expressions, it traces the Theocratic theme from the “first Kingdom promise” to the time when “Jehovah reigns” as undisputed ruler of the universe. What an instrument to revel in, to equip us to better preach this gospel of the Kingdom, and then to pass on to others! And so that no time might be lost in aiding each one to study this book, the Question booklet for it was released the same day, in the evening.

More Diligent Field Service Will Result

Sunday afternoon saw the final release when, at the end of the forceful delivery of the masterful argument in behalf of the announcement “The Kingdom of God Is Nigh”, the booklet containing the lecture was distributed free to all in attendance. Pulling no punches, it exposes to all the folly of world rulers, political and religious, who presume to do that which only God’s kingdom can do.

Returning home from the Assembly, the Lord’s servants find themselves faced with the necessity of pressing on in individual action against many foes, difficult conditions, and various obstacles. However, fully supplied now with ample spiritual food, possessed of a complete array of service and field equipment, and filled to overflowing with gratitude for these bounties from our heavenly Father, all will remove every obstacle, ‘sell all that they have,’ so that everything may now be devoted to the Kingdom service.

Advertising Servant

Because one of the basic operations of God’s visible organization is the advertising of His Kingdom, the advertising servant occupies the position of aiding the entire company in this respect, (1) by being himself an exemplary and zealous advertiser and (2) by arranging all affairs and matters of this nature so that everyone in the company may operate to the greatest advantage as efficient advertisers of The Theocracy.

Use of Sound Equipment

While the sound-machine activity has been somewhat hindered by world conditions, there still remain thousands of phonographs and records that may be used. The advertising servant will diligently strive to have all in the company realize the value of this line of activity and put the phonographs to effective use.

Through demonstrations in the service meetings tlie advertising servant, with others in the company, can show how to properly employ the phonograph in the door-to-door work, and also how to bring the recorded lectures to people as part of the educational or back-call work so vital now. This will necessitate the careful attention of the advertising servant in seeing to it that all broken records are deleted and replaced as far as possible, and that by the company's having a good variety of records on hand those willing to hear- may be given the benefit of listening to many subjects for their instruction. The Society’s Sound Equipment Record form will be used to keep track of records or equipment borrowed and returned by the various publishers.

Magazine Advertising

A zealous advertising servant will, of course, be in the forefront of magazine activity, diligently advancing the continuous use of The Watchtower and Consolation. Complete organization is very necessary in this connection, so that all in the company may enthusiastically engage in street-corner work, distributing magazines from store to store, building up routes for regular ob-tainers, and using magazines in the field distribution. Every publisher will be encouraged to use the magazines to the utmost, registering as magazine publishers, and ordering a steady supply of each issue, and using these regularly so that they will not accumulate. Territories for street-corner and store-to-store activity will be issued by the advertising servant. He will also watch carefully to regulate the supply of magazines to the needs of the company, and by seeing that regular monthly remittances are made by the company servant the magazine account will be kept in proper order and not be allowed to build up a deficit.

Other Advertising Agencies

Bookmarks, book folders and Kingdom News are also of vital need in advertising, and the advertising servant will see that all publishers can obtain an ample supply that they may freely use in covering their territories. If a company is still able to use a sound-car or transcription machine, the advertising servant will direct the use thereof. Kingdom Halls and billboards offer other possibilities for advertising too. An attractive display in the windows of a Kingdom Hall may be worked out between the company servant and advertising servant, and, by changing the display once a month, much can be accomplished in directing the attention of the people to the Kingdom message. A faithful advertising servant occupies a position that may be used greatly to the benefit of the entire company in fulfilling their advertising commission.
