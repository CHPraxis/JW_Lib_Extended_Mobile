He had zeal for the Lord’s cause and righteousness and, above all, unity in action to the advancement of the kingdom interests. Jehovah gave Ehud a job, and he set out to fulfill his commission. We see on every side practically every nation of the Devil’s organization closing down the Lord’s work, except England and the United States. The attaining of any great quota this year depends almost wholly upon the publishers in these two countries, and primarily the Theocratic servants in the United States. What are you going to do? Are you going to make this the greatest year ever in the witness of the Kingdom? By the Lord’s grace it can be done.

It would be well for each publisher to refer to the November Informant on how to make these setups with greater efficiency. Use the special Watchtower record “Instruction” in introducing this magazine in this special offer. Have with you the Testimony Card, which has been consigned to.all companies. Learn to talk subscriptions now, and keep it up for three months. Remember that during this campaign if you meet a person who is already receiving The Watchtower, he may obtain the Consolation magazine on the same terms.

Campaign Will Gain Momentum

February may be a cold, hard month in which to work, but we hope that at least 50,000 publishers will be engaged in the field service with the Watchtower magazine, Religion, and Satisfied. As spring comes on conditions will be somewhat better and more of the brethren will be able to get into the field and, without question, should be able to put in more time. The Lord’s people are not looking for good weather and for better conditions under which to work, because we know that eventually conditions may be far worse than they are now, and as long as we have work to do and are assigned by the Lord to do it we must go ahead; but in this campaign beginning with “Ehud’s Sword” Testimony Period we may naturally expect that as the days, weeks and months go by greater momentum and force will be backing this campaign, not only in the increased hours put in the field work, but in the number of back - calls made, model studies begun, use of the records “Religion” and “The End”, more book studies organized, and more publishers coming into the field organization.

By April, at which time we will celebrate the Memorial, the greatest number of publishers in the field for 1941 should manifest themselves. In April the special offer will be The Watchtower subscription, with a book and these two new booklets, on a contribution of one dollar; dropping to the book and two booklets on a 25c contribution, and finally, if these offers are not taken, to three booklets on a 10c contribution or either one of the new ones on a 5c contribution. Consignments are now being made to companies, so that all the publishers of the Kingdom will be supplied. What a witness against the Devil’s organization! and what a joy to the Lord’s people in hammering home the truth I The Watchtower campaign Is not one that becomes easier as time goes on, but one more strenuous right up until the end, culminating with “The Sign” Testimony Period.

Quota

In times past the Society set a quota for all the world. 100,000 new subscriptions, and placed over a million bound books and 12 million booklets. In view of so few reports coming in from nearly all parts of the world now it seems advisable to change this quota and set one for the United States alone, where we still have freedom to move ahead in the proclamation of the Kingdom.

A three-month quota for each company publisher should be 3 Watchtoioer subscriptions, 15 bound books, and 90 booklets. Pioneers should set their quota much higher, due to the fact that they put in many more hours; namely, a three-month quota of 21 subscriptions, 120 bound books, and 750 booklets. By the Lords grace and an extraordinary effort put forth by the servants of The Theocracy during February, March and April, we might expect to place in the United States at least 100,000 subscriptions for the magazines, one million bound books, and 6,750,000 booklets. This will mean a diligent effort on the part of every publisher. And you will note a change from January Informant in your individual quota.

How to Handle Subscriptions

When you take a subscription for a magazine, always remember that the name of the publisher and the name of the company should appear on each subscription slip that is sent to the Society. The subscriptions should be sent in to the office at least once a week, with the remittance. A Subscription Record Sheet should accompany these subscription slips. The secretary or one assigned to look after this work should be careful to check each subscription slip, see that it is written plainly, and address is correct, and also check the Subscription Record Sheet, so that no errors will be made. In this way time will be saved and complaints be reduced. The company should also keep its records complete and accurate.

Mailing of Informant

The Informant is sent as first-class mail so that the information contained therein will be given to the company organization only. So as to save on postage we never mail a company more than one ounce. Small companies do not require as many copies of the Informant as larger companies: therefore, to companies where there are 1 to 5 publishers we mail 1 Informant; 5 to 15 publishers we mail 3 copies of the Informant. Companies of 16 to 30 publishers receive 5 copies. All the larger companies arc mailed 8 copies of the Informant. When it becomes necessary for the Society to send a letter or some other information in the same envelope, an Informant is omitted, to save postage, thus making it 7 copies to the larger companies.

Two copies of the Informant should always be kept in the permanent files: one in the files of the company servant and one in the files of the secretary. Two of the other copies of the Informant can be cut up, giving the various articles to the various servants to whom they apply. The main point of the Informant as discussed at service meetings should be given to study conductors to use at the book studies and the Watchtower meetings.

It is essential that all associated with the company attend the serves. In addition to taking it up in this manner at the service meeting it should be read periodically at the Watchtower studies and at the book study centers.

Company Servant, Treasurer, Stockkeeper, Advertising

Servant, Please Take Note

Once each month the Society sends all companies and pioneers a statement showing- the standing of their book account. Those having magazine distributors’ account also receive a statement showing the balance on that account. These statements are valuable, and each company and pioneer should keep a file of statements for reference.

Companies and pioneers are continually requesting itemized statements of their accounts, particularly the magazine distributors’ account. This should not be. When you receive your statement. check the figures, comparing the invoices received during the month and the number of magazines received, as w^ll us the credits issued; and if satisfactory, file it for future reference. If companies and pioneers properly care for these statements it will be unnecessary to send copies.
