“The Sign” Testimony Period, April


Are You Doing Your Part ?


How Are Your Company and Individual Quotas?

“The Sign” Testimony Period is the name that is used to designate the activity of the Theocratic publishers during the month of April, 1941. What does “The Sign” mean to us? We know that to the enemy it will be an indication of false security. They think that everything is all right with them because they have accomplished certain things in opposition to the kingdom of the Lord God; but what does “The Sign” mean to us?

The Scriptures clearly indicate it will mark a time when the servants of the Most High God must render an account of their stewardship to Jehovah. The prophet Ezekiel says, in this respect, that before the six men with the slaughter weapons went into action the man with the writer’s inkhorn had to render a report. “I have done as thou hast commanded me.” Jesus says “this gospel of the kingdom shall be preached in all the world for a witness unto all nations; and then shall the end come”. The prophet Isaiah has this testimony: “Then said I, Lord, how long? And he answered. Until the cities be wasted without inhabitant, and the houses without man, and the land be utterly desolate.” These and many other scriptures clearly depict the responsibility resting upon the servants of the Most High now. They are to push the battle to the gate with greater vigor as this momentous event draws near.

The Battle

The instrument the Lord has assigned to us for “The Sion” Testimony Period is The 'Watchtower, the book Religion, and the booklets Theocracy and God and the State, on a contribution of one dollar. This combination is to be introduced with the phonograph and the “Instruction” record. If we are unable to use the phonograph or do not have one, of course, we are to use the testimony card provided. These are the particular instruments for this campaign. This is to be the primary offer and the way it is to be presented. We have no more right to change this arrangement than Jael, Ehud or David had to reject the instruments provided by the Lord and use instruments of their own choosing. It is very important that each and every feature of the Lord’s work now be carried on according to organization instructions. If some feature of the work does not come up to expectations and we have not been obeying organization instructions, then we must accept the responsibility for the failure of accomplishment of that purpose.

Your Quota

Up until February 28 we have less than 12,000 subscriptions. Our original quota for that month was more than 33,000. In other words, we are about two-thirds behind our quota, and we are inclined to believe that much of this is due to the fact that not all the publishers use with enthusiasm and faithfully the Instruments that the Lord provides and in the way that He directs. Each and every one should give this matter very careful consideration. Now it has pleased the Lord to extend this campaign for another month. Let us one and all check up on our activity and see if we can increase our effectiveness in utilizing these provisions that the Lord has arranged for this campaign and in that way increase the number of subscriptions.

The newspapers of the country have had their special witness by mail. The Protestant clergy, school boards and politicians have received their warning by having the message served upon them by the local publishers. If this is not complete, steps should be taken to see that it is completed as soon as possible.

Special Letter on Pioneer Service

A special letter to all Theocratic publishers regarding the importance of pioneer activity, which field has reached a new peak of 3,877 fulltime workers, is being mailed out to all pioneers, removed pioneers, companies, zone servants and regional servants. This letter should receive the prompt and serious consideration of each and every one. In all companies it should be read at the first service meeting following its receipt. It should be read again at the conclusion of the Watchtower study following this service meeting above mentioned, and at the second service meeting after it has been received fifteen minutes should be set aside for a general discussion of the letter and its contents.

Company servants, zone servants and regional servants should personally aid all individuals who are interested in having a part in the pioneer service and should help them to see their privilege in that respect, particularly those who are attaining the 60-hour quota or close to it. If you are contemplating pioneer service, write the Society for more Information and a questionnaire.

Memorial Report

Each company should prepare for and conduct the Memorial service this year as outlined in the notice in The Watchtower.

The Society desires an accurate report of the attendance. Care should be taken that no duplicate reports are submitted. Each company is to show, at the bottom of their report card for the month of April, the total Memorial attendance, and the total partaking of the emblems. These totals should Include everyone in attendance regardless of whether associated with the local company organization or not. Companies that go to some other company for the Memorial service should mark their April report card “Attended Memorial service with ... ”, giving the name of the company, but do not show any figures. Pioneers who are associated with companies will, of course, be included in the company’s report and they are not to make a separate Memorial report. Where pioneers partake of the Memorial separate from company organizations, one of such group should send in the report of the total attendance on his or her report card. They are to report pioneers and any other good-will interest who may be assembled with them. Isolated pioneers who partake of the emblems by themselves will report such on their report card.

Old-Style Phonographs Out of Stock

The Society does not have any phonographs in stock at the present time, either the old or the new model; so please do not place orders. The new-model phonograph is coming along very well, but because of the emergency measures in the United States it has been very difficult to get certain parts. However, it is hoped that within the next month or two a very definite announcement can be made on the new-model phonograph.

Back-Calls and Model Studies


You Can Do It!

Once again Brother Rutherford has called our attention to our responsibilities with regard to the back-call work. The portion of his letter of January 8, 1941, on “Back-Calls” should be considered at your service meeting. There is now no feature of the Kingdom service more important than the back-call work.

In January 1940 the matter of increased back-call activity was brought forcefully to the fore. Brother Rutherford pointed out that “back-calls are one of the best means of informing those who are somewhat interested of their privileges and the necessity of service” and showed that twelve back-calls a month is a reasonable quota. This had a stimulating effect upon the work, and the report for January 1940 showed a marked increase, the publishers averaging 3.7 back-calls for the month, which was the peak for the fiscal year 1940. October, 1940, the first month of the present fiscal year, saw a new peak reached of 4.6 back-calls per company publisher; however, by January 1941 the publishers averaged only 3.3 back-calls each, a drop of 1.3 below the peak and a decrease of .4 from January 1940. The pioneers have never surpassed their peak of 26.9 back-calls attained ’way back in November 1939, averaging only 22.1 in January 1941. This would seem to indicate that the brethren are not giving the proper attention to the back-call feature of the Kingdom interests. It may be that some of the newer publishers have not been properly instructed in the carrying on of this work. Perhaps some have thought themselves incapable or too inexperienced. What requisites are necessary? Who is qualified to make back-calls and conduct model studies?

Everyone Qualified

If some special training were necessary, if only a few were qualified to make back-calls, there might be an excuse for a decline in the average number of back-calls. This, however, is not the case. Everyone who engages in the regular door-to-door witnessing and who keenly appreciates the Kingdom and his position as a servant of The Theocracy In a covenant to unselfishly obey Jehovah’s will is qualified to carry on the back-call work. Publishers of the companies and study groups can set aside an evening each week for back-call work; show the new ones how to proceed in this work.

Efficiency

Each publisher holding an individual territory assignment should have a personal back-call file. These slips should be retained in the territory envelope and kept up-to-date by recording each call made, literature placed, records played, etc. Every individual territory requires 12 back-calls a month and one model study a week. Are you making this quota? Is your territory being given the proper attention?

In addition to keeping your personal back-call file in order, a Back-Call Follow-Up Report should be turned In at your local company headquarters for each back-call made and each time you conduct a model study. Other forms should also be employed, and it would be well to discuss in the service meeting the proper use and filling out of these various forms, for the mutual benefit of the company.

Increased activity is the order of the day. The back-call and modelstudy work should now be stressed as never before. Company servants should take the lead. Each publisher should look after his individual quota. The time is short. People of goodwill must learn of The Theocracy, the place of safety. Therefore let us continually keep in mind the admonition by Brother Rutherford, “The responsibility is laid upon the witnesses of the great THEOCRAT to see that the people have opportunity to receive such knowledge. Do your part."

Information for Company Servants, Sound Servants, and Pioneers

Please note the following when ordering any parts for phonographs:

First give the number of your phonograph; second, give complete name of the part desired. If you are not sure of the name of the part wanted, then give a detailed description or a rough drawing showing where the part goes. When ordering carrying handles, state whether they are fastened to your phonograph by a lengthwise or a crosswise clip. Please state whether the winding cranks are of the slotted type or threaded type. Make distinction between crank clips and crank supports. When ordering any kind of spring give a definite description, such as governor spring, winding shaft retaining spring, or mainspring for motor. Please do not take it for granted that we know what parts have been broken or write stating “Send a spring for my phonograph.” Be specific. This will eliminate mistakes in shipping parts to you, which in some cases had to be returned and other parts sent because of not being clear. In ordering any parts, literature, or supplies, do not use symbols, but write out plainly what you require.
