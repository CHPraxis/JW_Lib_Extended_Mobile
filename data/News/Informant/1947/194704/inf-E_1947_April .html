Another Peak of Publishers Expected

The matter of shining forth in the kingdom of Jehovah is no negative, inanimate stand, but a living, active, burning brilliance, reflecting the glory of Jehovah. This is clearly implied by the concluding words of our April text: “He that hath ears, let him hear.” (Matt. 13 : 43, A.8.17.) If the Kingdom message of our day finds a responsive echo in the heart, and impels to Theocratic action, that is proof that we are hearing the message of the Lord with understanding. Such hearing amounts to a command to those who are so blessed to “arise and shine”. If we hear, iee must shine, reflect, the glory of Jehovah. If we do not, it is an indication that we are not hearing with understanding and may be drifting toward the ‘dull of hearing' class.

In the midst of the bedlam, confusion and darkness of the Devil’s organization, and the weeping and wailing and gnashing of teeth of the wicked, the servants of the Lord, as light-bearers of Jehovah, “shine forth as the sun in the kingdom of their Father” ; a privilege appreciated and enjoyed by the remnant and reflected by their faithful companions, who are now dwelling in the light of the presence of the King. The many features of service for the month of April give ample opportunity for ail to reflect this light.

There is the climax of the subscription campaign, increased back-call activity, and the start of the new public meeting talks. And not only will all publishers share in these features, but April will witness arrangements to extend the share in reflecting this light to many persons of good-will who not only have ‘ears to hear’ but desire to say to others ‘Come and take of the water of life freely’.

“Watchtower”-“Awake!” Offer Climaxed

During April the Watchtower and Awake! magazines, with a premium of two bound books, will be presented on a $2.00 contribution. Present this with conviction, knowing that the benefits the people will derive from it will be far in excess of any amount they may give for it. Every publisher knows that the message contained in these magazines means life to the people. Our job is to make the people appreciate this point too. Make your presentation so clear and forceful that they will feel the need of having a New World messenger coming to their home every week.

Whole-hearted participation in all features of the Watchtower campaign includes, in addition to obtaining the complete subscription, the “establishing” of all the subscribers. Assist them by calling back upon them, arranging for home Watchtower studies, and, where-ever possible, taking them from house to house and in any branch of able to the new interest that they will want to continue. A discussion of the many interesting points of each witness and plans to cope with each problem you encounter as you go from house to house is very helpful. Let each regular publisher make it a point to take at least one newly interested person or one irregular publisher into the service with him during April, particularly the weekend of the 5th and 6th. By thus aiding one another we are encouraging our brethren and the people of good-will who desire to serve the Lord to have some part, either directly or indirectly, in shining forth in the kingdom of their Father.

The circuit assembly is a provision of the Lord for the expansion of The Theocracy. It should be vigorously supported when held in your circuit. Go to it and joyfully support all its activities. Make it your business to see to it that all goodwill back-calls and new interest are advised about it and encouraged to attend. All subscriptions taken at circuit assemblies are to be turned in at the assemblies with the name of the company holding that territory as the company sending them in (not the company with which the publisher who obtained the subscription is associated). However, each subscription should be reported to the company with which the publisher is associated, and will be included in only that company's report to the Society.

At circuit assemblies, at Kingdom halls and at all other meeting places, Kingdom light-bearers will always conduct themselves in a manner that will aid and encourage good-will interest as well as our brethren in Kingdom activity. Shine fortli in the Kingdom reflecting the glory of your Father.

Important: Expirations Require Prompt Attention

To companies throughout the country the Society is mailing slips containing names of persons whose subscriptions for the Watchtower and Awake! magazines have expired. These people should be called upon promptly and an effort made to get them to resubscribe. At one time they were interested enough to subscribe. Try to ascertain why they permitted their subscription to lapse. That will be your cue on which to work. Show them the importance of having the magazine coming to their home regularly, as well as studying the premium book “Let God Be True”. Offer to call back and help them with their study of the magazine. These slips have been run early. In some cases it may be known to the publisher that per- | sons have already renewed their subscription. Don’t ignore the slips, nor should a complaint be sent in. Call back on such; show an interest in them; let them get to know you and your desire to help them.

Let us further shine by putting forth every effort to make the public meeting campaign, beginning April G. the start of the mightiest public witness to date. Let the spirit of the “Memorial” continue to surge through us individually and collectively, to the glory of the Lord. Aid all regular and irregular publishers and inactive good-will to regular and active participation in these meetings. By precept and example aid everyone to have some part in this service, if it is only a matter of putting out handbills for an hour or two, or by going on the street or from house to house with you.

Evening Work

Regular participation in the evening work and handbill distribution is another very effective means of shining forth. We have many letters from brethren everywhere informing us of tlie blessings they are receiving in this work all the year round. People are at home and usually in a relaxed, receptive mood, and willing to talk about the Kingdom. We heartily counsel all companies to inaugurate this feature of the Kingdom service. Like all other Kingdom joys, it cannot be fully appreciated until one participates in it.

Effective Group Witnessing

Plan in Advance;

Assist Weaker Publishers

The inauguration of the public meeting campaign not only opened to all companies a new avenue of witnessing, but also brought with it emphasis on group witnessing. And with the improved and expanded instructions regarding advertising the public meetings, discussed in the March Informant, this method of witnessing comes in for even more attention. Why this emphasis on group witnessing?

To begin with, group witnessing is the ideal method of completely covering a certain territory in connection with the advertising of a series of public meetings. Also, by means of it territory that might otherwise be neglected is given the needed witness. And most important, group witnessing provides opportunities for servants in the companies, pioneers and other experienced publishers to show a personal interest in the new, weak, youthful, irregular or somewhat negligent publishers by giving them practical demonstrations and individual counsel as to how to carry on the various features of the work, in actual field service.

Service Meeting Arrangements

For group witnessing to be effective and accomplish the greatest amount of good, however, it must be taken seriously and given adequate attention beforehand. The specific territory to be witnessed is selected, of course, at the time the public meeting series is planned. Toward the end of the service meeting preceding the first public meeting of a series time should be devoted to the assigning of publishers to groups under captains previously chosen and provided with territory. If at all possible such groups should continue throughout the series, making it unnecessary to arrange new groups each week. As far as possible, publishers should equip themselves with literature and other witnessing supplies on the service meeting night or prior to the Sunday group witnessing.

For at least the first Sunday of each public meeting series it would be well for all to come together in the morning at some convenient meeting place, preferably the hall at which the public meeting is to be held. A suitable program should be arranged for, the reading of the day's text and comments, service instructions as to the literature offer and its manner of presentation, and concluding prayer, after which all will depart to the field. This should be an interesting and instructive meeting from twenty to not more than thirty minutes in length and will be arranged by the company servant. While it may not be convenient for all to meet in this manner every Sunday, each group should arrange to assemble for field service at some convenient place each week.

Keep Up the Good Work!

Good work! With February showing a drop in activity? Yes, good work, for after a comparatively mild winter, February saw this country swathed in blizzards and snowstorms, from one end to the other, such as had not been seen in years; and yet, in spite of this, the report shows over a thousand more publishers tliis February than any previous February.

Four months in a row, November, December, January and February, each has witnessed the highest number of publishers for its respective month on record. Keep it up.

Summer Vacation Pioneers

With the approach of the summer months schools will be closing for their annual vacation. The invitation is extended to youthful witnesses of Jehovah to pioneer for at least two months, during their summer vacation period. This arrangement affords a splendid opportunity to ‘remember your Creator in the days of your youth’ and to exercise that same faith mentioned by Paul. Further, by participating in the summer work, the youthful minister is laying a solid foundation for future service to Jehovah. Tn the years to come, when he has launched out on the road of the “regular” pioneel- publisher, it will be a heartwarming joy to view in retrospect the hours wisely expended in the summer-vacation service, to realize that it was there that he began the unequaled lifetime occupation, and that he had faithfully spent his time in that which was to be to his future welfare, rather than squandering it in idleness and the ways of this world. Y’es, it is a grand opportunity. Plan now for summer activity; write for your application.

Back-Call Group Witnessing

As noted in Organization Instructions paragraphs 20 to 23 inclusive, group witnessing is not merely for the purpose of covering territory. House-to-house group witnessing should be followed through by evening back-call group activity, calling back on all placements and where interest has been found. And if there is need for training publishers how to present the message of the Kingdom from house to house, and there is, how much greater the need of training them in bow to make back-calls and how to start book studies.

To this end let each mature publisher, wherever possible, come to the Kingdom Hall or book-study meeting point on back-call nights and arrange to take along a publisher needing training in the back-call and book-study work. Thereby you will be aiding your brother at the same time that you are feeding the "‘other sheep”. And keep it up. Do not expect them to acquire ability and confidence to make back-calls and conduct book studies after just one or two evenings. And as they show appreciation of this feature of the work let them make a call or two on their own, and perhaps turn over a book study to them. Then look around for another publisher that needs help in this feature of the work and train him.

We spend much time in ‘hunting’ and ‘fishing’ for the “other sheep” and then in feeding them. Let us not neglect those who have taken their stand and who may need help. Remember, “no man lives unto himself” also applies to our field service. Tako part in group witnessing.
