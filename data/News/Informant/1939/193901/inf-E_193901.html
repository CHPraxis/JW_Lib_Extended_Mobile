Our Equipment

If at all possible, each time you engage in the field service carry a phonograph along with you and at least the record “Miracles” and "Instruction”, P-153, P-154. Take with you the special Testimony card provided for this campaign, a few samples of the current Watchtoioer, some bound books, and a good supply of Face the Facts booklets. Also, take some Watchtoioer subscription blanks, back-call slips, and cards or slips bearing the name and address of the meeting place where the Watchtower studies are held.

Offer the Entire Combination

The special combination to be stressed at each call, if possible, is a year’s subscription for The Watchtower, the book Enemies (or any other bound book), and the booklet Face the Facts, all on a contribution of one dollar. With each person taking this offer leave a sample copy of the current Watchtower, a book, a Face the Facts booklet, and a card bearing the name and address of the hall where the Watchtower studies are held; also a duplicate copy of the subscription slip signed by the publisher, which is the subscriber’s receipt.

To persons unable to subscribe for a year, endeavor to place a six-month subscription, on a contribution of 50c, with a Face the Facts booklet free. With all who so subscribe leave a current issue of The Watchtower, a Face the Facts booklet, the address of the Watchtower study, and (as the subscriber’s receipt) a duplicate copy of the subscription slip.

If a subscription for The Watchtower cannot be obtained, then try to place Enemies (or any other book) and a Face the Facts booklet, on a contribution of 25c. Where not even this is possible, then leave Face the Facts for anything they can contribute, or even free if they promise to read the booklet. In all homes visited wherein the people show any interest whatsoever we must try to leave at least the Face the Facts booklet—in order, first, that they may have the message, and. second, that we may meet our individual and world-wide quota.


Time the Main Requisite With a Watchtower, a Book and Face the Facts

In every’ land throughout the entire world, commencing Sunday, January 1, and continually every day and evening thereafter for four months, till April 30, Jehovah’s faithful remnant and their companions will obediently and enthusiastically march forward in the “hottest” world-wide campaign that has ever been prosecuted byr Christians on earth. In Germany and like dark places where God’s true people are cruelly oppressed and hindered in every conceivable way’, they will be united with us to the extent they are able, so that, in reality, all who know of and love the Lord will be found diligently doing with their might what their hands find to do during every hour of this Watchtower Campaign.

This Watchtower Campaign is the most stupendous ever attempted— not merely because within that time, by the Lord’s grace, we confidently expect to have the largest number of publishers in the field, putting in the highest number of hours in field service and back-call work, and placing the largest number of pieces of literature, but for reasons more far-reaching than these. This campaign is going to result in bringing the Watchtower magazine regularly into the homes of at least 100,000 more families, which magazine stresses the great issue of serving Jehovah, the great Theocrat, and live, or serving the cruel dictator Satan and his wicked organization, and die. Also, in Face the Facts, which is going to be read by millions, is sounded the warning. And there arc going to be placed a million bound books which outline Jehovah’s gracious provision for oppressed mankind at this crucial time prior to Armageddon. Thus, this campaign has as its objective the bringing to millions timely facts which they must learn in order to know about Jehovah’s theocratic organization and the course of action they must take now for their protection and safety.

New Publishers

The publishers must assist the newly interested ones to become “laborers” by showing them just what they can do in advancing this Watchtoicer Campaign.

At the last service meeting held in December approximately* 70.000 persons, we believe, heard the lecture “Fill the Earth”. Many of these have never gone out into the field, yet they sincerely love the Lord and are anxious to serve Him to the best of their knowledge and ability. What have you done to aid these newly Interested persons to see their priv


ileges and responsibilities in this Watch tower Campaign? Have you personally invited them to attend the Watchtower studies in your Kingdom hall? Have you arranged for them to regularly receive a copy of the Informant? Have you shown them how they can participate in the Watchtower Campaign by at least going along with you in the witness work or by placing Face the Facts booklets among their friends and neighbors and from door to door? Are they reporting regularly? All these things must be done if the witnesses are to discharge their full obligation to the Lord. Not only is it essential that the witnesses themselves do all they can in the field service, but they must also aid the “other sheep” to accompany them in this Kingdom service. Pioneers, particularly, can be very' helpful, because they can start new companies or, if in company territory, take the newly interested to the Kingdom hall.

One of the big objectives of this campaign is to have at least 35,000 publishers in the field service. This means an increase of 15 percent over our last peak of publishers. All our records show that this can be and should be exceeded; for there are at this present time 60,000 associated with the companies throughout the United States, and, of this number, 28,000 are regular publishers and 15,000 irregular. If all who have gone out at all in the past witness regularly during this campaign, and if, in addition, even 15 percent of the others who are associated with the companies join with them, we shall easily have 35,000 publishers. The zone servant, the company servant and the other servants of the company must see that the importance of increase in publishers is given consideration at the service meetings from time to time. The newly interested must be ‘built up’, that they may become active and thus enjoy the full favor and protection of the Lord.

Time in the Service

It is very essential that each publisher put in much time in the field service each month during this Watchtower Campaign. To do so it will be necessary’ to brave the cold winds, snow*, and chilly rains. Yet all who keenly appreciate the importance of the Kingdom issue will overcome these obstacles by properly clothing themselves and taking pains to work the most protected and sheltered portions of their assigned territory. And when the weather is good, full advantage will be taken of it by workins as long as possible, and not being satisfied with putting in three hours, but five and more.

Each morning and each evening

In every case where people show any willingness to listen, even if at the time you can leave only the Face the Facts booklet, try to arrange for a back call with the speeches “Face the Facts”, “Fill the Earth,” and “Fascism or Freedom”.

Individual Quota

The “My Daily Kingdom Service” record sheet sets out the quota made for each company publisher and pioneer, which must be met if we are to reach the world-wide quota. This quota for each company publisher is 4 new Watchtower subscriptions, 20 bound books, and 250 of the Face the Facts booklet; for each pioneer it is 20 new Watchtower subscriptions, 200 books, and 1,500 of the Face the Facts booklet.

Servants of the Company

The success of each company In this campaign depends much on the zeal and leadership of its various servants. If each and every one of these servants does his share in the field service and looks after the other interests committed to him, the witness work of the company will be greatly stimulated and advanced. Be sure that the territory is so assigned and worked that your entire assignment will be covered at least once during tne campaign —and twice where possible.

Make a Good Start

The Watchtower Campaign begins at a time when most of the publishers can put in full time during the first two days, namely, Sunday, January 1, and Monday, January 2.

Use New Record “Miracles”, “Instruction” By January 1, it is hoped, all the publishers will each have one of the new records P-153, P-154 to use in the service. We are swamped with orders, and working day and night to get them filled. Run this record at every place possible in your door-to-door work. One side of this disc, “Miracles,” tells about the two speeches of Brother Rutherford from London and why the person should hear them. The other side, "Instruction,” explains why one should read and study The Watchtower. Run both of these records, if possible, when you visit the people the first time in the house-to-house work, then try to place the special offer as well as to arrange for a back call. Even if the party refuses to give you a subscription, you may be able to place a book or booklet and arrange an appointment to run “Fascism or Freedom” or “Face the Facts”.

Another place to uso the new disc, P-153. P-154, is on back calls. Some publishers have already obtained subscriptions by doing this. Those persons on your back-call list who are not yet subscribers for The Watchtower should be called on. The record “Instruction” will help you to take their subscription, and “Miracles” will stimulate their interest to hear the new lecture series.

December 1 Watchtower Article to Be

Carefully Studied

The December 1 Watchtower, on page 364, In the article “Kingdom Activities”, brings to our attention the importance of devoting more time to the field service. Everyone having in mind the importance of the Kingdom will surely put forth every possible effort to devote at least two hours a day, on the average, to the field service, in obedience to the King's command to preach this gospel of the Kingdom in all the world for a witness. Suggestions are here offered as to how a publisher can put in more time in the field.

First, publishers should get personal assignments of territory near their homes. Sucli territory can be so situated that it takes in four blocks each way from one’s home. The advantages of getting near-by territory can readily be seen. Persons who have domestic duties to perform can slip out an hour or two each day and do some witnessing work. Practically no time, then, will be spent in traveling to territory. One may find it more convenient to devote an hour or two in the morning; others, two hours in the afternoon. After working in this near-by territory for several days with the phonograph, one will have names and addresses of interested persons to call on when rainy or severe weather makes it almost impossible to go from door to door. On such calls a publisher can spend an hour or an hour and a half, running records, answering questions and assisting the people in Bible study. In the case of married people, the wife can assist the husband to put in more time in the field by witnessing in the territory during the day, finding those who show interest, and turning these names and addresses over to the husband publisher' for back calls an hour or two in the evening.

Having a local territory, those who get home early from their daily work can put in an hour or two before suppertime. Those persons whose work does not begin until after dinner, because of varying shifts in the industry in which they are employed, can put in a few hours in the morning. Since the 40-hour week is in effect, many factories and offices are closed on Saturdays, giving many of the publishers all day Saturday- off. Considerable of this time could be devoted in field service work, either in local territory or in a witnessing party. Those who have only a half day off from their secular work on Saturday can put in at least three or four hours.

Sunday has always been a big day for the publishers, and should continue to be the big day, and bigger, if at all possible, as far as the Kingdom interests are concerned. Many of the publishers are arranging now to put in five to seven hours rather than the usual three, eating lunch in the field. Four to five hours can be spent in door-to-door work, and the remaining time in back calls. Always carry a full set of “Face the Facts”. “Fascism or Freedom” or some other series with you in the car, so that you will have them on hand for back calls in the afternoon. If you have no car. carry a set to your territory and leave it with some interested person, preferably the person on whom you intend to make a back call. In this way you let him know in advance that you intend to be there at an appointed time.

Attending Zone Assemblies

The zone and regional servants' reports coming to the office indicate that many of the brethren do not attend the zone assembly on the occasion of the regional servant’s visit. Apparently some do not appreciate the importance of the new organization. Usually such brethren are unable to get effective results in their own territory, and they wonder why. These assemblies are a part of Jehovah's Theocratic Organization for the conduct of his Kingdom service. The instruction provided there is essential for each one in order to properly do the work assigned. Let everyone who possibly can, therefore, attend these assemblies regularly.

Can You Arrange for Full Time?

The December 1 Watchtower announces the discontinuance of the Auxiliary branch of the Kingdom service. (See article “Kingdom Activities”.) While many of the auxiliaries enrolled as pioneers on January 1, having made definite arrangements to Increase their time in the work, many more pioneers are needed to properly care for the Kingdom work in the United States at this time. It will be remembered that many previously serving as pioneers are now active as zone servants.

There is much territory that requires the witness. As a result of the world-wide convention in September, with the accompanying radio broadcasts, many are asking for additional information and instruction. These people must be called on, and there are not sufficient pioneers to do this.

In addition to much isolated territory (in which no company organization exists), a number of small companies throughout the country, composed, in the main, of newly interested persons, require the assistance of live, active pioneers to instruct them and aid them in covering their assigned territory.

The experience that the auxiliaries have had in the field, taking the lead in using all the instruments the Lord has provided, using the phonograph consistently, carrying on model studies, making back calls, etc., equips them remarkably for this work. The great majority of those enrolled as auxiliaries will wish to increase their service to the King and enroll as pioneers. Let us hear from you immediately ! A pioneer application will not be necessary in your case; merely say, "Transfer me from auxiliary to pioneer service.” Company publishers will write for application and information letter.

Some of those already enrolled as pioneers will wish to assist those now entering. Some may have space in their trailers and will welcome a partner in the Kingdom work. We should like to hear from such, advising us just what accommodations they can offer for these new Kingdom pioneers.

The Messenger

Due to late orders and a very heavy demand for The Messenger, a second edition was put on the press. It is believed that The Messenger will be of real assistance to newly interested persons to appreciate the magnitude of the work done by Jehovah's witnesses. Therefore, extra copies of The Messenger were printed on the second edition, and will be offered at 5c a copy (remittance to accompany order) when five or more are ordered to one address. These can be placed with the newly interested people at 5c a copy, and we know that they will get a rich blessing from it.

Pecans

The December Informant had a notice with reference to the sale of pecans to aid pioneers. There seems to have been a misunderstanding about this notice. It is the privilege of anyone to sell his products to anyone he desires, but the purpose of that notice is to inform others that the Society is not either directly or indirectly engaging in selling pecans, and that neither the Society's name nor that of the pioneers in its service should be used to induce others to purchase. To be sure, anyone can buy pecans as he pleases, but this matter should not be connected with contributions to the Society’s pioneers.
