100-Pound Shipments

A large majority of companies could order 100-pound shipments if the company servants and stockkeepers did a little planning in ordering. Please have in mind that it costs just as much for the Society to send a 75-pound shipment of books by freight as it does 100 pounds. To send several small orders a month means the Society spends more money to get the literature to you than if you would order one large shipment a month and make it 100 pounds or more. The Society does not encourage small companies to order 100 pounds, but they should place an order about once every two months for the supplies they require and at the same time make remittance for the literature placed during the previous period, so as to keep their accounts in good condition. Where there are pioneers in small companies, or even large companies, the pioneers can be asked to turn In their orders at a certain time, to be mailed to the Society in the same envelope, so that all of this literature can be shipped out as one shipment, thus taking advantage of the 100-pound freight rate. The literature can be distributed to the pioneers at the company headquarters when received. When these joint orders are shipped as one shipment the various packages are marked on the sealing tape as to whom they are for, thus making it unnecessary to open the carton to see what is in it. Cooperation on the part of company servants and groups of pioneers ordering 100-pound shipments will save the Society considerable money which can be used for other interests of the Kingdom.

Do Not Ship Literature to Foreign Countries

Brethren having friends or relatives in other countries should not send literature to those countries. If you want these friends to get literature, order it through the Brooklyn office, and we in turn will forward your order for filling by the Branch in the country where you want the order delivered.

For example, if some Polish brethren in the United States want books sent to Polish people in France, send your order to the Brooklyn office with the proper remittance and the Brooklyn office will forward your order to the French office to fill. Government authorities have confiscated considerable literature sent from one country to another by the brethren. It is best to do this through the organization that the Lord has provided.

Bibles

Many of the brethren throughout the country are obtaining Bibles from different sources to place with the literature. This is quite all right, and there is no objection to their doing so. However, care should be taken that these Bibles do not contain comments that are contrary to the truth and confusing to the people. Only Bibles should be used that have no comments In them beyond the marginal rendering and concordance.

Discontinue Information Marches

Information marches throughout the country have given a good witness. But the witnesses will discontinue information marches for the present time. After October no more information marches are to be held by any of the companies until further advised by the Society.

The material you have for information inarches can be stored until further notice.
