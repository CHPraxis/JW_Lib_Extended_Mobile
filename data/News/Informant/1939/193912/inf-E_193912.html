Don’t Forget Subscriptions

January a Bound-Book Month Are You Ready? Have You Supplies?


Most Unusual!

3 Bound Books on a 35c Contribution

To follow the December campaign, “War of Vindication,” the Society has arranged for a wide distribution of bound books in January, 1940. These will be placed on a contribution of 35c for any three of the Society's bound books from The Harp of God to Jehovah inclusive.

The brethren will recall our experience last year in the distribution of the three books Vindication on a similar contribution. Let us profit by the experiences of that campaign. At that time almost all companies and pioneers underestimated the result of the campaign (many of them running out of their Vindication stock before the campaign was half over; others, when it was two-thirds over), and very few of them had enough stock on hand to last them until the end of the campaign. There were many contributing causes which are familiar to the brethren and servants in each company ; therefore all should watch and see to it that they do not make the same mistake again. To do so would show negligence and indifference toward the Kingdom interests committed into our care.

To the enlightened child of God it is clearly evident (from the ballyhoo in the press, on the radio, and from the religious and political pulpiteers everywhere) that the people are perishing, not for bread or for water, but for the hearing of the word of the Lord. The books we have to offer to the people contain Jehovah’s life-giving food for a perishing world. We are obligated before the Lord to raise up a standard for the people, and, at the contribution on which this combination is offered, the Lord has made it possible for practically everyone to obtain at least one combination. Let us, therefore, by the Lord’s grace, accept this grave responsibility whicli Jehovah has placed upon us and go forward with zeal and thanksgiving carrying this life-giving food to a perishing world during this campaign.

The Offer

All of the Society’s bound books from The Harp of God to Jehovah may be offered during this period, 3 on a contribution of 35c, 6 on 70c contribution, 12 on $1.40 contribution, etc. Foreign-language books are also to be used in this campaign ; any 3 to be offered on a contribution of 40c, 6 on 80c contribution, etc. Note that Riches, Enemies and Salvation are not to be included in this combination offer.

The enemy is closing down on the Lord’s work throughout the earth. In America we still have the privilege of service. Let us, therefore, work ‘while it is day ; for the night cometh, when no one will be able to work’. Each individual publisher, each company servant, zone servant, and regional servant, should start planning and preparing for this campaign now, each in his respective position seeing that every necessary detail is taken care of. Pioneers also, in their field, are to participate in this campaign, so that on January 1 the entire organization will roll into action as one man.

We learned from the Watchtower Campaign, last January to April, that if an offer is attractive enough and is presented with zeal and conviction the people will take it. Therefore there should be no backwardness or timidity about presenting a 35c combination to the people, but it should be done with the conviction that what you have to offer to them is far more valuable than any amount of money they could possibly contribute for It.

During the much-underestimated Vindication campaign last December we placed 290,516 bound books. With proper advance planning and preparation, with from 10.000 to 15,000 more publishers in the field all averaging about 6 hours more each month, it would not surprise us if that figure were almost doubled this year. “According to your faith be it unto you.” Let each one do his part with faith and confidence, and the Lord will give the increase.

Details

On November 13 the Society sent a letter to each company and zone servant regarding this campaign. This letter should be read to the company. In addition to other things, it contains a basis for carrying on this campaign. The following figures are recommended: 12 bound books for each company publisher. That amounts to one combination a week for the month. This is a very reasonable expectation and should be attained without difficulty. This estimate applies to all companies, large and small. Detailed instructions regarding the manner of obtaining the stock, and other details, were also contained in this letter of November 13. The instructions therein should be followed out carefully by all the servants. In addition, the company servant is responsible for cheeking up at the last service meeting in December, seeing to it that each publisher has the required combinations, territory and other details arranged for, so that all can start right into the work at the beginning of the month.

During this period individual territory adjacent to the publishers’ homes is particularly recommended, and, for group work, territory in the vicinity of each study group. The study conductors should arrange to take tlie study group out at least once each week in this territory in addition to the individual witnessing done by the brethren themselves. By working such convenient territory repeated back-calls can be made at any time on those who obtain the literature and manifest interest. Arrange for back-calls on each one obtaining a combination. The real purpose behind leaving the literature with the people is to have them read it. Therefore, call back and see how they are progressing, and show an interest in assisting them, and the advantage of having a model study. Arrange for such model studies. In fact, the procedure should be that at each door where the publisher comes there is a prospective obtainer for the combination; each obtainer is a prospect for a back-call ; each back-call is a prospect for a model study; and each attendant at the model study should be viewed as a prospective active publisher and should be invited to the company meetings as soon as possible.

The back-call servant should see to it that all interested ones on his files are called upon with this special offer. This is a work that new publishers can participate in without difficulty. Assign some names to them. The people they call upon are acquainted with the work, in sympathy with it, and will receive them gladly. The person should be given the advantage of this special offer because of previous interest.

How Companies Are to Get Their Books and Rebate

As set forth in detail in our letter of November 13, the companies with insufficient stock are to get the books they need from any near-by company which has an oversupply. This should be done at once, and the Society should be notified of such transactions on the regular Transfer of Stock blanks, which blanks are to be duly signed by the respective company servants. The blanks are then to be sent in to the Society and an adjustment will be made on your accounts. Only one blank is to be sent in covering each transaction; do not send in two notices about the same transfer: otherwise, duplicate charge and credit will be made.

However, if there is no company near by to supply the books needed, then send in your order immediately to the Society for the “total quantity of books” needed. That is, say, order 120 books, and leave it up to us to send you whatever books we find it

The Pioneers’ Part in the January Campaign


Many of the pioneers during the Vindication campaign barely got going before they had to stop, due to the fact that they didn’t supply themselves with sufficient Vindication or that they got started too late. Notwithstanding all this the pioneers averaged 53 books each during that campaign. Knowing that there are sufficient books for all, and looking forward to the pioneers’ starting January 1, we strongly urge the pioneers to immediately check their stock and send in their orders to the Society for what additional books will be needed. Surely it is reasonable to expect the pioneers to average at least one combination of three books a day. You should order accordingly.

Pioneers should send in their orders as soon as possible after receiving this Informant. Where there are two or more pioneers working in the same city, they should arrange to send in their orders together, in the same envelope, with instructions to send the orders to one address. How Publishers Are to Get Their Books and Rebate The publishers are to get their books for the January campaign from tlie stockroom at the usual cost. Each day the publisher engages in the service he should make out a Publisher’s Field Service Report slip, showing on the bottom of the report slip the number of 3-book combinations placed at 35c. The publisher is to turn in these reports to the stockkeeper or treasurer, who in turn will give the publisher a rebate of 25c (either in cash or in literature) for each set of 3 books reported placed. Each report slip so handled is to be signed by either the stockkeeper or the treasurer, to show that a rebate has been made. These report slips are to be kept on file for two months or until such time as it seems certain all the publishers are satisfied they have received proper rebate for the sets placed and reported.

The company will be reimbursed for such rebates by credit on its account. The sets reported placed, and shown on tlie bottom of the company’s January report card Io this office, will determine the credit allowed.
