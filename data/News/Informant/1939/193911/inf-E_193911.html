Continue “Theocracy” Offer During November

Salvation

Has Excellent Distribution

During ‘'Theocracy” Testimony Period our offer to the people was Salvation and Government and Peace on a contribution of twenty-five cents. We are sure that all are anxious to supply as many as possible in their territory with these two important pieces of literature; and so the same offer will continue during the entire month of November.

All the publishers who engaged in the Kingdom service during October should be busily engaged therein during November, and, in addition, there should be others seeing their privileges. Note that last November there was a slump in number of publishers; so let each one determine to give a good account of himself. While there are only four Sundays in the month, many will be off from secular work on Thanksgiving Day and can utilize that day by really showing their thanks to the Fathei- by passing on to others that which will point them the way to salvation.

Comparing the first two months’ placements of Salvation with that of Enemies, an Increase of 40,000 books is shown, which is gratifying. But it must be remembered that considerably more publishers were working with Salvation; so it behooves all to make a greater effort in the placing of this book. Familiarize yourself with it, so you can show the people the importance of obtaining it; use all the aids provided : “Snare and Racket” record, testimony card, Kingdom News, Government and Peace booklet, and (in preparation for the next visit) Salvation folders.

And remember, leave a copy of the new booklet at every call, if at all possible.

Therefore the title must inspire everyone on the Lord’s side to determine, by His grace, to devote as much time and energy as possible to this campaign during the month set aside for that purpose.

Commence planning now, so that the greatest amount of time will be given and the most effective co-operation rendered by you. Today every agency of the Devil’s organization is assembled against the great Theocracy, and it is the privilege of the anointed and their companions, in the strength of the Lord, to go forward against this array and push with greater vigor than ever before in this War of Vindication, absolutely confident of their ultimate victory. Are the People of Good Will Attending Your “Salvation” Studies?

Are Studies Conveniently Located?

The book Salvation Is the Lord’s provision for instructing the people of good will in the way of salvation. One main study should be held at the headquarters of each company; and, in addition to this, at strategic points throughout the company territory, there should be established Salvation studies which would be convenient to a number of publishers as well as to people of good will. The publishers should always attend the study in their area.

The study conductors should also arrange to hold the territory in the immediate vicinity of the study, for those attending. This territory should be worked regularly as outlined in the new form forwarded to the companies recently. The study conductor should lead the publishers into the field, thoroughly witnessing to the people in that vicinity, making back-calls, conducting model studies, and inviting the people of good will to attend that meeting. The study conductor also should have all back-call names in that particular area and see that these people are properly called upon and invited to the study, or have other assistance as may be necessary provided for them.

These Salvation studies, however, should not be used as an excuse to discontinue the model studies in that particular vicinity. Back-calls should be made as usual, and each back-call should be developed into a model study, and as the people progress in the model study they should be invited to the Salvation study. In fact, it is not necessary to wait until the conclusion of the model study before inviting the people to the Salvation study. They should be attending both studies for a while, and, of course, when they are attending the Salvation study they should also be invited to attend the headquarters’ Watchtower study and service meeting. In this way the various features of the organization can co-ordinate in developing the interest all over the territory, gradually working it into the company organization.

Please Give the Office Time to Fill Your Orders

It is strongly urged that all the companies and pioneers send in their orders for literature far in advance of the date they wish it, so that the orders can be shipped the most economical way and yet reach you in time. We also request that you make your orders for 100-pound shipments and do everything possible to avoid “rush” orders. Remember, there are over 2,500 companies and over 2,000 pioneers, as well as branches, placing orders for literature.

You will appreciate the necessity of “giving us time”, when you see the great volume and visualize how your orders are handled. On receipt of your order, which took a few days to get to the office, it is turned over to the bookkeeper for checking against the account, then invoiced; invoice is checked over to see that all items you ordered have been listed, and labels are made; order is then figured as to weight and cheapest way to ship, finally filled from the nearest depot, and then turned over to the shipping concern, from which it comes to you. You must allow at least two weeks for the above, including the time it takes freight shipments to points within 600 or 800 miles from the depots, and at least three or four weeks for more distant points (especially when shipped by boat), from the time the order reaches this office.

Orders for Phonographs and Records

Many apparently do not understand the Society’s arrangements regarding supplying phonographs and records to those who are unable to pay the full amount at the time of ordering. The Society is glad to cooperate with and aid all the brethren who want to take advantage of this arrangement to serve the Lord and advance the Kingdom interests, by assuming part of the burden for a period. However, all orders for phonographs and records should be accompanied by a reasonable down payment unless your account has a credit balance to cover the cost of the equipment. If only part payment is made with the order, the balance should be sent in as soon as possible.

We do not open accounts for the different individuals in the companies. All orders should be sent to the Society through the company organization. The orders should be turned over to the company servant or stockkeeper with the down payment. He will then put through the order in the usual way. The order will be filled and shipped and charged to the company’s account, and all subsequent payments, until the entire amount is taken care of, should be made to the company, the company in turn remitting to the Society.
