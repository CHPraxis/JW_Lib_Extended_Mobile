Around the World with Jehovah’s Witnesses

Pioneer and Missionary Blessings

In reply to Peter’s question. “Look’ we have left all things and followed you: what actually will there he for us?” Jesus gave the assurance that all who followed such a course would be amply rewarded and that even in this present life they would receive a hundredfold. That Jehovah God in our day makes good this promise of his Son to all who engage in the fulltime service is very apparent as we read the 1952 “Yearbook’’.

During our subscription campaigns company publishers do not average one subscription in two months; but a pioneer in Bolivia in a two-month “Awake!” subscription campaign obtained 210 subscriptions’ And whereas company publishers average but a half study each, this pioneer had so many studies that he could not take care of them all but had to turn some of them over to others who did not have any. And what feature of the service is more enjoyable than conducting a home Bible study? (91)

A missionary in Ethiopia reported that “it is a strange and wonderful experience to have . . . people coming to the missionary home every day requesting that someone study with them”. Some days as many as 20 come, walking as much as two hours to get there and many want to study two or three hours at a time.(66)

The Gilead district servants in Nigeria travel by plane, car, truck, canoe, bicycle and often, like* Jesus, by foot Reports one “On Saturday morning about 50 men, women and children followed me around to record with their own eyes the unbelievable sight of a white man preaching the gospel in their homes through an interpreter.” For one meeting 1,000 came, although advertised only by the advertising servant going around the streets at five o’clock in the morning announcing the lecture by means of a trumpet! Being Faithful in That Which Is Least

Not all can serve as pioneers, special pioneers, missionaries or at some Bethel home. And some may not even have much time for the regular features of field service. But the least we can do is to be faithful in using the opportunities that come our way to give the witness.—Luke 16:10.

Two energetic publishers in the Sudan, Africa, make many opportunities for themselves to witness at their place of business simply by discussing the truth before they transact their business. As a result a company has been formed and some former Moslems are now conducting Bible studies with other Moslems (130)

Some of our brothers in Nyasaland who are fishermen likewise put the preaching of the good news ahead of selling their fish. Customers are spreading the news of how they got their fish and what was said on the lake shore by the witnesses. (196)

Not only at places of business but also at home one can show faithfulness in that which is least. A sister in Ecuador makes good use of the patio of the apartment house where she lives to give the witness. The women folk gather there to knit, sew, weave and talk, and so she joins them with a supply of socks needing mending, booklets and a Bible. By being alert and tactful she has been able to start five home Bible studies, with the prospect of two or three more. (127)

Our own circumstances may not be exactly like any of the foregoing, but if we are desirous of being faithful in that which is least we will find opportunities to witness at our home or place of business.

    COMPANY ORGANIZATION


The Service Meeting

Lively, enthusiastic service meetings are a vital necessity to a healthy, active company. They help the Lord’s people to learn the most practical and effective ways of carrying out the commission to “make disciples of people of all the nations". The company servant is responsible for planning successful service meetings.

Upon receipt of the Informant he should outline the service meetings for the month. All Informant articles should be considered during the month. Whenever possible the article dealing with work for the current month on the front page should be considered at the first service meeting in the month. The article appearing on the back of the Informant considering activity of the following month is to be considered at the last service meeting of the current month. Other articles may be worked into the program in accord with the local company's arrangements. The company servant should look for and encourage other servants to offer suggestions in planning meetings, but the company servant makes the final choice.

A Scriptural theme or a particular objective should be determined for each meeting. The first meeting in the month could have the monthly text as a theme and subsequent meetings other appropriate themes. Often the daily text can be woven in as a theme for the meeting. After the Informant material is apportioned, the company servant, aware of the local problems or needs, should schedule portions on the program that will offer helpful suggestions to remedy the weaknesses.
