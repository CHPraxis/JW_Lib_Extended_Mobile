‘Wisdom Crieth Aloud In the Street’

“Go forth!” must also be said on the streets, especially since that is the only place where many of the prisoners can be reached. (Prov. 1:20-23, AS) The very fact that misguided religious zealots become so incensed when they see us engaged in this feature of the work should help us to appreciate its Importance. For example, in the British West Indies such fanatics have jerked the magazines out of the publishers’ hands and have torn them up and then thrown them into the streets. How one such fanatic fared in the hands of the law is told in our “Yearbook” on page 101.

By continuing in this work regularly we not only help those who are opposed to mark themselves for destruction but we also help those of good will to mark themselves for salvation. In Pakistan, where the Moslem religion has such a strong hold on the people, a government official, after watching a publisher on the street for two years, summoned up enough courage to ask him for a home Bible study. Another Moslem, after being witnessed to on the street, called at the Kingdom Hall expressing the desire to know more. (199)

To properly help the people to mark themselves for or against the Theocracy by means of the street work we must do more than merely stand mute with the magazines in hand, or mechanically call out the slogans. We must make it a point to speak to the people individually and then follow through. One publisher in Rangoon, Burma, inquired of a person: “Have you ever read the ‘Watchtower’ magazine?” Receiving an affirmative reply, he was able to place a magazine and obtain the address of the interested one. Following up this placement resulted in five taking their stand for the Theocracy. (104-105)

‘The First Love’

Jehovah foretold that his people would be volunteer servants in the day of his power. He also foretold that in this day ten men would take hold of the skirt of him who is a real Jew and say, 'Come, let us go with you.’ Time and again the zeal of the new interest shames those who have been acquainted with the truth for some time. A publisher in El Salvador moved to an isolated spot and went to sleep as far as the service was concerned. He was found by the most zealous of the new publishers recently baptized, who told him: “If you were only saying one word a day, that would be something, but you are not doing anything; you’re dead.” While perhaps not the most tactful thing to say, it did succeed in waking up the sleeping one, who is now publishing regularly. (133, 134)

The report from Singapore tells of the eagerness of a young Chinese whose family is Buddhist. He soon began to witness and in six months joined the pioneer in the field service, impressed with the “urgency of the work due to the nearness of Armageddon”. He already has been instrumental in others’ taking their stand, one of whom joined him in vacation witnessing. The two of them are conducting six studies and have already started two others in the service. (214) Are we manifesting a like zeal?

PIONEERING

SCENE 1 (6 min.) Discussion.

Serving God is man’s greatest privilege. The more service rendered, the greater our privilege and joy. A servant (or pioneer) and two publishers discuss the advantages of and great need for more pioneers. Sound admonition is found at Ephesians 5:15, 16 during this time of the end when the Devil, like a roaring lion, is out to devour all he can. Just look at the great increase in public praisers; wouldn’t you like to have a greater share in this increase? You can, by pioneering! (2 Cor. 9:6) The abundance of local territory in the company is mentioned in harmony with figures on the recent circuit servant’s report. The sheep are there to be found, but the territory is not being worked adequately. More workers are needed. But from where will they come? Can we expect others to come in and work territory for which we are responsible? Do other companies have too many pioneers? No! Then we must fill the need from right within our own ranks of company publishers. The need throughout the world is for more pioneers.

SCENE 2 (4 min.) Experiences.

Pioneers will express their joys of service and many rich blessings by relating some of the experiences they are having in this full-time work. If there are no pioneers associated with the company or if local experiences are lacking for this part of the demonstration. pioneers from nearby companies may be requested to participate in this meeting or, if necessary, pioneer experiences from the “Yearbook" (pages 117, 118 and 153, 154. etc.) may be enthusiastically presented by local publishers. This as well as the entire demonstration should be prepared and practiced before presentation to the company.

SCENE 3 (5 min.) How to do it!

Consider the material and ideas presented in the “Informant” for October, 1950, December, 1951, and August, 1952. Show what some pioneers are doing to engage in pioneer service, or what some company publishers might do to get into this service. Why cannot vacation pioneers continue? Can we expect others to pioneer when we, who could pioneer, are not doing so? Hardly!

Yes, we have good reason to be grateful. Just review the timely instruction and counsel that we received. Maturity, consistency in the King’s service and increased activity were stressed in the stirring talks delivered by the brothers. This is a tremendous increase to compare with 93,305 for the district assemblies in 1951. Also, 2,534 persons symbolized by water immersion the dedication of their lives to do Jehovah’s will.

Thus, with our minds braced for activity, let us push ahead “always having plenty to do” in the service of the King, proclaiming that world unity is not just a dream, but will soon be an established reality by theocratic direction.
