Increased Activity

To properly care for our Godgiven responsibility of ‘feeding the sheep’ group contacts should be well organized. It has been found that 9:30 a.m. Sunday is the best time for the publishers to assemble for group service. Discussion of the daily text and a consideration of the activity for the day should start promptly at the appointed time. Brief quizzes, discussions, experiences, demonstrations, or reviews, based on previous service meetings, may be prepared and presented for refreshment and stimulation before going afield. The entire meeting need be no longer than 15 minutes, thereby making it possible for all to be in the field by 10:00 a.m. for a full day of service.

Many publishers have found it practical to start the day’s activity with a couple of hours of house-to-house activity, eat a lunch while still in the field and then continue into the afternoon by calling back on persons who previously displayed interest and starting and conducting home Bible studies.

So prepare for and take an active part in Theocracy’s increase now by uniting yourself with your brothers world-wide in this great witness work.

Theocracy’s Increase

Jehovah’s prophet Zechariah, foretelling Theocracy’s increase, said that “Jerusalem shall be inhabited as towns without walls for the multitude of men and cattle therein”. (2:4) Why without walls? Because walls would Interfere with the expansion of Jehovah’s organization. The aptness of the simile and the various ways in which this prophecy is being fulfilled today are forcibly brought to our attention in the “Yearbook”.

The advertising of public meetings plays a vital part in Theocracy’s increase. Said a Swedish newspaper: “One day in the spring an energetic gentleman appeared in this community and started to invite people to a lecture ... It did not [take] long until the lectures had to be held out of doors for lack of space.” (231)

And the part circuit assemblies play in Theocracy's increase is noted in the report from El Salvador. As a result of a circuit assembly at San Miguel, “The hall overflowed for every public talk, so more walls had to come out, and to date we have removed three walls, and each time the hall has filled right up to its new capacity as if by miracle.” (132)

Missionary activity is another vital factor in Theocracy’s increase as seen by the report from Uruguay. “To accommodate the many new attenders seine good people have opened up their private homes for company meeting places. One brother tore out a brick wall . . . and ... is going to do away with another wall to make [the room where the meetings are being held] still larger. Six missionaries in a small city . . . now find that they have to enlarge their dining room and use it to accommodate the people.” (238, 239) Truly, literally as well as symbolically, Zechariah’s prophecy is finding fulfillment in our day, ana the various kinds of activity are all having a part therein.

YOUR SERVICE MEETING


Now that two demonstrations are presented here each month, two different points can be stressed. This month, the first demonstration shows how the two new uses of the S-10 form, that is, the procedure in following up new subscriptions and obtaining renewal subscriptions, will be put into operation. The second one demonstrates the need of training for the preaching work.

SCENE I: Calling back on the new subscribers.


Part A (7 minutes) Company servant, Bible study servant, accounts servant and at least one company book study conductor arc engaged in a lively discussion of the new procedure. Company servant, explaining system, emphasizes that accounts servant will make out S-10 in triplicate, stressing need for double-checking for accuracy and legibility. He will note on it names of publishers who placed subscriptions and give one copy to Bible study servant. About six weeks after receiving S-10, Bible study servant will make out lists of subscriptions received along with names of publishers who placed them. He will give lists to company book study conductors. Study conductors will check with publishers who placed subscriptions to see that back-calls are being made.

Part B (3 minutes) A study conductor, after company study, is checking with publishers and brie fl y explaining why this is done and importance of calling back on subscribers. Publishers express appreciation for reminder even though they had been calling back with each current issue of magazine. Study conductor mentions that he will have to check with one publisher on back-call day since he was not able to be here.

Part C (2 minutes) Publisher continues to follow through by calling on subscriber with current issue again even though six weeks have passed since subscription was placed This should be very brief, and only the introduction need be given.

SCENE II: Calling back to get renewal subscriptions.

Part A (5 minutes) Above-mentioned servants continue discussion started in first scene, taking up use of S-10 in obtaining renewals. One or two months before subscriptions expire, Bible study servant makes out subscription blanks and gives them to study conductors. For this year, since in some cases the names of publishers who placed subscriptions are not available, Bible study servant can see if expiring subscriptions belong to those with whom Bible studies are conducted. If so, conductor's name is noted so that he will be approached concerning the call. If publishers have back-calls on some whose names are listed, they should call for the renewal.

Part B (3 minutes) Company study is just closing. Study conductor is giving out subscription slips handed to him by Bible study servant. One slip is given to a publisher who conducts a study with the subscriber. Another is given to a publisher who obtained subscription almost a year ago. Some slips are left over because a publisher moved and others because ones who placed subscriptions could not bo determined. These slips are given to various publishers. A publisher recognizes the name on an unassigned slip and it is given to him. Some unassigned slips still remain to be used at contact points.

Part C (2 minutes) The final part of demonstration shows a publisher just leaving a home after obtaining a renewal. He is assuring householder that when the renewal notice appears in his magazine he may ignore it and that he will call again as arranged.

“Seeing Ourselves as Others See Us”

A brother with a microphone (or facsimile) announces, “I am your sidewalk radio reporter. An item of interest in this town is the ‘Watchtower’ people, whom I always see on the street. 1 am going to devote tonight's program to interviewing our citizens concerning this magazine, ‘The Watchtower.’ ” People (prepared with answers beforehand) are walking by, and he stops some and asks them questions, as, “What do you think of the ‘Watchtower’ people?” or “Do you take ‘The Watchtower’?” To their answers, he asks, “Why?”

One man answers he never took a magazine because he didn’t know the Witnesses wanted him to take one; the way they stand just holding up the magazines like a signpost, he thought they were merely advertising it. Another says he approached one Witness who didn’t try to tell him what was in the magazine, and even hesitated about handing him one to look at. A third says a Witness came to his door, but what a shape the magazine was in! All marked up and wrinkled, besides, all the Witness seemed interested in was getting subscriptions, he couldn’t tell him anything of interest in the magazine; it looked like an ancient copy anyway. One says he is a subscriber to “The Watchtower”. He took it because he noticed the Witnesses so diligently and consistently on the street, and they seemed to have such belief that “The Watchtower” would help the people. Another subscriber explains how a Witness approached him while he was sitting in his car and enthusiastically showed ,<that here was something he would find to be absorbing reading while waiting; he did, and on approaching the Witness two weeks later for another copy, the subscription offer was made and he accepted it. A lady says she took “The Watchtower” at her home. Why? My! the interesting features the Witness showed her; how he referred from it right to the Bible since then he has come back and, with each issue, shows her things she can tell her whole family, until she has got them all reading it now. A man complains that he subscribed but never got a copy, until some other Witness came months later and found that the subscription slip had been filled out wrong; it certainly seemed an unbusinesslike and slipshod way to do.

The reporter questions several, showing the way the people view us as we do the work in a right or in a poor, unprepared manner. He breaks the conversations up by interjecting remarks, as a sidewalk reporter does. Afterward he finds a Witness doing street work. Approaching him, he asks why the ones who manage his local organization do not train all their ‘ministers better, or use the good ones to go with the less-trained ones. The Witness answers that this is done, but that lie believes the citizens’ views certainly show the need of more such training.

COMPANY ORGANIZATION

Pioneer Publisher

The pioneer is a publisher who devotes at least 100 hours a month, or 1,200 hours a year, to Kingdom preaching work. Privileges of pioneer work are open to company publishers who have regularly published for at least six months after their baptism. If necessary, a pioneer may engage in part-time work to provide things essential for his livelihood. (Acts 18:2,3; 20:33,34; 1 Cor. 4:12, AW) However, such work must always be viewed as secondary to his pioneer service.

The primary responsibility of a pioneer is to devote his time to the preaching work, and this by regularly engaging in house-to-house, back-call, Bible study and magazine activity. He should use the current offer when witnessing, obtaining his literature through the company when at all possible. Each pioneer will direct interested persons to company meetings and endeavor to aid these and other publishers in the field service. This he can do by working in co-operation with the company’s weekly service arrangements at the Kingdom Hall and company book studies.

Due to his consistent field activity and practical field experience he can be of real help to the company in its ’service arrangements and programs. By taking personal Interest In company activity and putting forth a real effort to Improve his ministry, as well as that of his fellow servants, he will be exercising understanding and maturity. Servants in the company will show appreciation for this assistance by not imposing various duties on the pioneer that may interfere with his field service work. In exercising love and showing enthusiasm for his work, the pioneer will be an example in service and godly conduct and prove to be a stimulating inspiration to the entire congregation.

The pioneer endeavors to exercise patience and tact at all times and will work for the upbuilding and unity of the local organization. He wisely appreciates that regular attendance and oral participation at company meetings and enrollment (if a brother) In the theocratic ministry school are a real encouragement to the company publishers, and it will benefit him personally and prepare him for his dally ministry. Many opportunities and privileges are open to him to expand his ministry by accepting assignments to companies in need of pioneer assistance.

By doing this a pioneer Is able to contribute much toward the increase and progress of a company.
