NEW YORK CONVENTION!


Have You Completed Plans to Attend?

Imagine if you can the greatest convention of modern times! That will be the International convention of Jehovah’s witnesses in New York city. Tens of thousands from the ■wide world over assembled for eight days of bounteous spiritual feasting, and YrOU will be there! Do you realize that this month automobiles, trailers, trains, busses, airplanes and boats will be striking out for New York with their loads of joyful con-ventioners? Indeed, the time is at hand; here are the eleventh-hour questions:

Have you completed your convention preparations in harmony with the information given in the May and the June "Informant” and other instructions sent out l>y the Society? If not, do not hesitate any longer to do so. Are you helping all people of good will in your territory to know about this great convention and realize the benefits they will receive by attending? They will surely thank you after the convention for your consideration and express amazement and satisfaction, as did the queen of Sheba, when she said: “The half was not told me.” New York is planning for your arrival; are you planning an arrival in New York?

If anyone has been holding back because of thoughts that there might not be sufficient accommodations, he should immediately disperse such ideas. There are plenty of rooming accommodations available in private homes and hotels. A trailer and tent camp location has been contracted for, and there should be room for all who want such accommodations. Here is some last-minute information on the convention:

Rooming

The rooming accommodations are being obtained in sufficient quantities to accommodate all who will be coming to the convention. The rates are very reasonable too. It is expected that the Rooming Committee will be able to mail out most of the room assignments to the convention-ers who have written in already. However, to those who write later it may not be possible to send assignments in time to reach their home address before they leave for New York. In such cases we shall hold the assignment cards, to be picked up by the delegates upon their arrival. So if you do not receive your assignment card before you leave home, call for it at the Rooming Committee. Up to and including July 28 the Rooming Committee will be at 557 West 157tli street, near Broadway, in Manhattan. From July 29 on, the Committee will be located under the bleacher section of Yankee Stadium, West

161st street at River Avenue in the Bronx.

Brethren should make note of the name of the publisher at the top of the room request form that they sent, because that is the way your assignment will be filed. As soon as the publisher receives his assignment, he should write down the address and put it in his wallet or purse, thus avoiding being unable to locate the place where he is staying. Sometimes in the past brethren have lost this Information during the convention and then didn't even know where they left their luggage.

If you arrive in New York between the hours of eleven o’clock at night and eight in the morning, please do not try to contact your landlady then, but wait until a reasonable hour. It is advisable to arrange for a daylight arrival so rooming accommodations, etc., can easily be located.

Needy pioneers will be given the benefit of the most reasonably priced rooms, but all accommodations offered free are being assigned to the brethren from foreign countries who cannot get currency exchange. Most of the pioneers have had time to do secular work and should be in a position to pay some rent.

At all times brethren should keep in mind the need for Christian consideration of others. If you have mailed in a request for rooms, then Theocratically accept your assignment and show your consideration and appreciation to the landlady for her hospitality. We would suggest that you do not obtain and hold a room assignment from the Rooming Committee just in case you can’t find something better upon arrival. Many times landladies go to considerable expense and inconvenience to prepare accommodations for the brethren, so you can see the importance of utilizing the assignment you have received from the Rooming Committee. As soon as your plans are made, notify the landlady concerning the time you expect to arrive; and if this is changed, show enough consideration to let her know about it. Remember, brethren, many of these landladies are good will persons already progressing in the truth, while others are rendering their first Christian gestures toward getting acquainted with Jehovah’s witnesses. You will have an excellent opportunity to witness to the landladies, so accept your responsibility of bringing them a spiritual blessing which is very great.

Hotel Accommodations

Many hotels will hold a reservation until 6 p.m., but in most cases no later unless they are advised of a definite later time of arrival or receive an advance deposit of at least one day’s rent. In some cases the check-in time is even earlier.

Brethren would do well to advise the hotel of their expected time of arrival.

Trailer and Tent Camp

It is a pleasure to announce that a trailer and tent camp will be set up at the Butterworth farm, Old New Brunswick Road, near New Market, New Jersey. If you are traveling toward New York on U. S. Route 22, near Plainfield you will notice a large sign saying “Turn Right to Dunellen”. Make the right turn and drive through Dunellen to the camp. You will find special signs along the way to direct you to “J. W. Camp”. If you are traveling toward New York on U. S. Route 1, just after you leave New Brunswick turn left at the sign that says “To Camp Kilmer”. From there on you will see “J. W. Camp” signs to guide you to the camp. The camp is about five miles from either of these routes, being easily reached over surfaced roads.

Companies of Jehovah’s witnesses have been sent special forms to be filled out by those who want to use the facilities of the camp. If your company has none left, request additional copies from the Watchtow’er Convention Rooming Committee, 557 West 157th street, New’ York 32, New York, and they will be sent. Please use the form when requesting parking space. It will be necessary to make a small charge to those using the camp to cover the expenses of renting the ground and setting up the camp.

To reach Yankee Stadium from the camp, a distance of 40 miles, you should travel by U. S. Route 1 or 22 to either the Holland or Lincoln Tunnel (probably Lincoln Tunnel will be less congested) or you may use the Erie Ferry, near the Holland Tunnel. When you reach Manhattan take the West Side Express Highway (no trucks permitted) to the 158th Street exit. The bridge at 155th street will take you across the Harlem river1 to Yankee Stadium. There are no parking facilities right at the stadium, so you will have to park a distance away from the stadium.

Although programs from Yankee Stadium will be transmitted by telephone lines to the camp for the benefit of those on duty there, nevertheless, to get the real blessing from the convention, it is suggested that all campers make it their business to be at the Yankee Stadium daily.

Information

The information department is planning to make you as familiar wnth New York as possible during your stay here. The first problem you are probably wondering about is how to find your room assignment when you arrive. We believe those from New England are familiar with New York and will not have difficulty locating their rooming accommodations, using the parkways and express highways when entering the city. If you are traveling from the West or South by car, and your rooming assignment card shows a Brooklyn address, it would be to your advantage to enter New York via the Holland Tunnel on U. S. Brethren will be on duty 24 hours a day from July 28 to 30 to show you how to get to your room and give you a map of the city as well as a subway map. “Convention Information for Jehovah’s Witnesses” signs will identify the station for you.

These stations will be identified by signs, and there will be brethren there as at the Holland Tunnel.

Plan now for the work that will follow the convention so that you and the new ones you are privileged to aid may share in the Increase of Jehovah’s Theocracy as it grows to fill the whole earth.

Volunteer Service

To make the convention a success many departments must be organized. Volunteers will be required for cafeteria work, operating refreshment stands, bookroom work, directing traffic, ushering, playing music, first-aid service, and maintenance. Brethren who plan to volunteer for cafeteria or maintenance work should bring appropriate clothing for use during the work periods.

A number of plumbers, electricians, carpenters, and tinsmiths and other skilled craftsmen will be required at the convention. Some will be needed a week prior to the opening of the convention. It will be appreciated very much if brethren who are able and willing to do such work and can come early will please fill out a Volunteer Service form and address it to the Watchtower Convention, 557 West 157th street, New York 32, New York, immediately and state their qualifications, as well as the date they plan to arrive in New York.

A large staff of cooks and persons familiar with cafeteria work will be required, and we invite such to volunteer at the convention. Doctors and nurses may wish to serve in first-aid work.

A small orchestra will be organized, and publishers who are experienced musicians and wish to share in orchestral activities should report to the volunteer service department. Bring your instruments.

Baptism

Arrangements are being made to hold a baptism. Those who plan to be immersed should be prepared with bathing suits and towels. This matter should be announced to the companies at meetings during the last two weeks of July as a reminder to the delegates.

Other Details

For the benefit of those who have never been to New York we wish to state that there is no reason to be frightened on account of the largeness of the city. Everything is well regulated here. The people may be in a little more of a hurry than in other cities, but they are generally very helpful when you ask them for information. New York has a large police force and they are trained to help visitors, so always feel free to ask them for directions or information.

An ordinance in New York requires car drivers to always remove keys from the car when parking on streets. It is a good practice to always lock your car when you park it, and we suggest that you never leave luggage, cameras, clothing or anything of value visible in your car even though it is locked. It is best to lock your personal belongings in the trunk. Be sure to observe all parking and traffic regulations. And in crowds it is always good to be aware of the possible presence of pickpockets and thieves, for the stadium will be open to the public.

Many of the brethren will be coming by automobile. Inquiries have been received about the parking facilities at Yankee Stadium. There is really no parking space at the stadium, so we suggest that wherever possible you leave your car at your rooming location and come to the stadium by subway. New York has a very efficient subway system, which is not only the fastest way to get around in New York, but the cheapest, and there are two subway lines with stations at the entrance to Yankee Stadium. It is possible to transfer from busses and other subway lines to the lines that pass the stadium.

If your room is In a hotel, the hotels usually provide garage space for their patrons; but this service is $1.00 to $2.00 per 24 hours per auto. It is prohibited to park your car on the streets overnight or during business hours in mid-town Manhattan; check with the doorman or reception desk on parking regulations.

There is a 5-percent city tax levied on all hotel accommodations, so brethren should be prepared to pay this when they pay their hotel bills.

No automobile signs will be provided by the Society for this convention.

Mail

If you desire to receive mail while in New York, please give your correspondents your personal room address. Do not plan on receiving any mail at the stadium.

Toilet Facilities

Toilet facilities are always crowded at conventions. Yankee Stadium will be no exception. If you travel to the stadium by subway, you may find it convenient to use the subway public toilets before you enter the stadium.

Deaf Mutes

For the benefit of our deaf brethren, an interpreter will be located in the box seat area of Section 24 in the lower stands.

Photography

All official pictures for the convention will be taken by the photography department. No others will be allowed to take pictures on the field, but it will be all right to take any pictures you wish from the grandstand.

Recording

Many have asked about the facilities for personal wire recorders. Arrangements will be made to accommodate the brethren, and information on this may be obtained at the information counter at the stadium.

Sound Cars

All who plan to bring sound cars to New York should write to the convention offices immediately and inform the brethren here concerning the type of equipment and whether it is in good condition. The Convention Committee will notify you if your sound car is needed.

Visit Headquarters

Take this opportunity to visit the Bethel Home, the Watchtower factory, WBBR transmitter at Staten Island, and Gilead (South Lansing, New York). The home and factory will be open for inspection following the convention.
