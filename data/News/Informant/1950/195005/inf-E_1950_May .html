Rural Work with Public Meetings

May brings warmer weather and the opportunity to expand our singing activity into the rurals. By a systematic arrangement all of the rural territory of the company can be covered thoroughly with the Kingdom message. Just consider the ways this can be done. A particular area, preferably that surrounding a small village or rural community in the territory, can be selected, and a group of publishers assigned to concentrate on this by a house-to-house coverage, presenting the offer and advertising the public talk. This can be given in the local community hall, schoolhouse or even outside in a park, on a lawn or other accessible ground.

Remember, too, that where it is inadvisable to hold a complete series of talks you may arrange for two or even one, depending on the circumstances or the available halls, etc. Conduct a campaign in one area with the public meetings while the territory is being covered, and then move on to another one. Interested persons found should be revisited and fed regularly.

Servants should plan now for an organized coverage of the rurals during the spring and summer months. In larger companies two or more groups can be arranged for rural campaigns. When going into the rurals the groups should arrange to spend a full day in the service.

With so many ways of swelling the taunt-song against Satan in May, certainly none of Jehovah’s servants will neglect to add their voices to the chorus of singers. Satan has about had his day; he is reaching the summit of his ambitious climb to rival the Most High, and this enslaving tyrant is doomed to certain destruction in this generation. With this truth ringing out world-wide, many, many persons now in slavery to Satan will begin now to serve Jehovah, before Satan’s destruction along with all who refuse to take up the tauntsong.

Request Convention Rooms Early

Trailer Camp Unlikely

All of the Lord’s servants are now keenly looking forward to the International convention scheduled for July 30 to August 6, 1950. Since you have made up your mind to attend the assembly, one of the questions of prime interest to you is, “Where am I going to stay while in New York?” Now is the time to attend to this matter, and to assist you the Society is mailing, with this Informant, the Room Request Forms which you have been waiting for. Additional copies may be had by company servants upon request.

Instructions have been prepared by the Society to help you provide all the pertinent information required by the Convention Committee to facilitate assignment. Therefore, study thoroughly the instructions attached to the forms at your next service meeting. It will be much better to fill out the form carefully and completely than to write letters endeavoring to describe accommodations you wish.

It would be well to mail the Room Request Forms as soon as possible to Watchtoweb Convention, 557 West 157th St., New York 32, N. Y. This will advise the committee of the number of rooms necessary. Be sure to designate whether you want accommodations in private homes or in hotels. This time there is a good supply of hotel rooms and they will be assigned as long as they last. None are within walking distance of the convention grounds, so subways must be used. The hotel rooms will be a little higher priced than in other cities. Bear in mind, too, that there is no overnight parking permitted on streets in most hotel areas, and it will cost an additional $1.50 to $2.00 a night to put your car in a hotel garage or in a parking lot

Up to this time we have not found a suitable place for a trailer camp, and It is doubtful that one can be located within twenty-five miles of New York city. If any location is found you will be notified later, but do not plan on a trailer camp.

Accept Rooms Assigned

Even though thousands of hours will be required to obtain the necessary additional rooms in private homes, the publishers of the New York area are happy to do this on behalf of their visiting brethren. When such rooms are assigned they should be accepted. Do not ask for a room in a private home “just to be safe”, with the intention of looking for something better when you arrive. Transportation Circular Provided

For many months the Society’s Convention Transportation Desk has been gathering information and making arrangements for brethren to travel to the 1950 international convention. New world servants world-wide will assemble in New York city July 30-August 6 to enjoy the “feast of fat things” Jehovah has prepared for his servants in these last days, to strengthen and encourage them for the battle yet ahead. Less than three short months from now the convention starts I Have you arranged for your transportation?

To aid you in your travel arrangements the Society is sending with the May Informant a transportation circular, which contains valuable travel information. One copy of the circular should be put on the company information board and one copy given to each family of publishers. Companies who have extra copies may give them to people of good-will planning to attend the convention. If your company does not have extra copies and you know of individuals planning to attend the convention, be sure to discuss the information contained in the circular with them.

The information contained in the circular should be considered individually and in group study. At the first or second service meeting in May time should be allowed for consideration of all written material and the special train information relative to the local company. The circular will be considered again at the first service meeting in July, so the brethren will have a clear picture of travel before leaving for the convention. Local transportation servants should be thoroughly familiar with all information contained in the circular and local arrangements, so that maximum assistance can be rendered to the brethren. Where no transportation servant has been appointed, the company committee should designate someone for this service.

Arrange for an Early Arrival

The Society encourages all who possibly can, whether traveling by auto, bus, regular train, airplane, or other means, to arrive in New York city the morning of July 29. This will afford an opportunity to find rooms during daylight hours, to get acquainted with surroundings and to have a full night’s rest in order to be ready to receive full benefit from the 8-day feast. The Bethel home and factory will be closed during the convention, but both will be open for brethren to visit from Monday, August 7, on. Brethren desiring to see these places can arrange for departure on Tuesday, August 8.

Purchasing Special Train Tickets

After making a careful study of the circular and selecting the special train serving your area, those desiring to ride special trains may purchase railroad tickets. Be sure to have your ticket routed over the railroad(s) to be used by the special train en route to and from New York. Gifts and Wills

It is only natural that servants of God do not wish others to dictate what shall be done with their material possessions in the event of death. Not for the purpose of solicitation, but to answer your many questions relative to disposition of property, the Society’s legal counsel has prepared a Memorandum on Gifts and Wills, which is available to those who make request for it.

In writing the Society concerning gifts and wills, please give the full details of your problem, and the Society’s legal counsel will be glad to furnish whatever additional information may be required, along with a copy of the Memorandum. Address your inquiries to the Legal Office at 124 Columbia Heights, Brooklyn 2, N. Y.
