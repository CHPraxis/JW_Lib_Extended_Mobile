Ministers for the Issue


Serving Watchtower Subscribers


Enter First Month of 1951

Know the Offer;

Present the Offer

Who is the Supreme One? Who shall rule the universe? In this great issue is locked the destiny of all humankind. For those who decide in favor of Satan and his system there will be but one end—death. Conversely, the wise who side with Jehovah and his Theocratic government will be rewarded with an unexcelled gift—eternal life. To proclaim this issue of universal domination God has raised up his ministers, ministers for the issue. Here are men, women and children positively upholding God’s side of the universal question! Here are men, women and children who “bless . . . the Lord” and “do his pleasure”.—Psalm 103: 21.

To “do his pleasure” indicates action on the part of Jehovah’s ministers. And action it will be as ministers for the issue swing into the twelfth annual Watchtower campaign beginning January, 1950.

The Offer

During 1949 The Watchtower upheld the issue in over a score of different languages to the number of 23,190,737 copies. Doers of God’s pleasure realize the effectiveness of this instrument in making known the issue and during January these ministers will offer a year’s subscription for The Watchtower together with a free eight-booklet premium on a $1 contribution. Awake! without a premium may be offered to those already having The Watchtower: or the 8 booklets may be left for 25c with those unable to subscribe.

The advantages of obtaining and reading The Watchtower are many. Primarily, it provides “meat in due season”, enabling truth-seekers to know Jehovah, his purposes and his Word. The Watchtower does not claim to be inspired, but It is awake to unfolding Bible prophecies. It is impartial and adheres strictly to the Bible as authority for its comments. Free from old world ties, The Watchtoieer stands fearlessly for Jehovah’s side of the vital universal issue.

Though the ever-present theme of Jehovah’s supremacy is noted in each issue, the subject matter of The Watchtower is varied. In 1949 ministers of God, as well as men of good-will, enjoyed and benefited from such articles as “Bible Study, a Royal Requirement”, “Messiah’s Presence,” “Love’s Expression,” “Israel,” and many others. Articles on various Bible characters provided groundwork for future Bible study. Highly informative reports on world travel in connection with the ministry interested Watchtower readers, as did published experiences from ministers upholding the issue in the field. Indeed, Watchtower readers are well-informed.

Supplementing its spiritual value,

Watchtower Campaign

The Watchtower offers other features to aid the reader in getting the most from the magazine. Timesaving questions search out the main thoughts in each paragraph. When necessary, explanatory footnotes appear to clarify and document information presented. A systematic study schedule is published in each issue for use by companies of Jehovah’s witnesses as well as individual subscribers. Dozens of Scripture texts are cited and quoted to support material presented, thus obviating the need of spending many hours of individual search to locate such scriptures. Watchtower subscribers also have the advantage of considering Scripture texts in the light of various Bible translations and versions, eliminating the necessity’ of gathering all of these many’ versions and translations for private study. In fact, since its first publication in 1879 The Watchtower has cited or quoted from more than 40 English versions of the Bible or portions of the Bible. Backed by painstaking research and with information presented in exemplary grammatical style, The Watchtower is indeed a magazine for the issue.

Additional Aids

In presenting the offer some may find it advantageous to utilize the December 15 issue of The Watchtower, which carries the Scripture Index for the Leading Articles and the Subject Index for the preceding year. Using this, one can briefly point out some of the varied subjects treated and how they are of extraordinary value in gaining a knowledge of the Scriptures.

The service meeting can also add impetus to the 1950 Watchtower campaign by considering the importance of the four-month activity, the advantages of engaging in it, ways and means of presenting the offer, the progress of the campaign in your company and how you are meeting the quota.

In view of the importance of The Watchtower and the predominant part it plays in making known the issue, let us unite in the 12th Watchtower campaign, praising the Lord and preaching his Word. Or, as the psalmist so aptly expressed it: “Bless ye the Lord, all . . . ye ministers of his, that do his pleasure.” —Ps. 103: 21.

Follow Up Placements

“Then shall the King say unto them on his right hand, Come, ye blessed of my Father, inherit the kingdom prepared for you from the foundation of the world.” Would you like to be invited to “come” and live in the new world forever under the King, Christ Jesus? If so, you must now get on his right side and have a part in the separating work he is directing, because only those who do will receive his favor and the invitation to life.—Matt. 25: 33, 34.

The nations, without exception, are on his left side. They will not change their position, for they refuse to hear the Kingdom message which makes known how to do so; yet within the nations are multitudes of good-will persons who desire the King’s favor. For their benefit the reigning King sends his trusted servants to bid these sheeplike ones to separate themselves from the nations, take their stand on his right side, serve him and live. All Theocratic ministers are invited to share in this blessed dividing work during the “Separating to the Right” Testimony Period in February by presenting a year’s subscription for The Watchtower for $1, and the free premium of eight booklets.

Back-Calls an Avenue for Subscriptions

By the time February’s activity begins thousands of subscriptions will already be pouring into the Society’s office. But that will be only the start! For many, many more persons will undoubtedly recognize the benefits of The Watchtower and subscribe if given the opportunity.

Those who have previously manifested interest in the Kingdom message are obviously the most likely to avail themselves of the benefits of The Watchtower. For example, there are those who obtained “Let God Be True" during November and December, individuals who have received sample copies of the magazine, those on magazine routes, persons who formerly subscribed but who have allowed their subscriptions to expire as indicated by the expiration slips sent to the company by the Society, and, of course, those upon whom we regularly make back-calls and with whom we conduct Bible studies. All of these persons of good-will need to regularly receive The Watchtower. We can reach them by making back-calls. Remember, the back-call work is a provision of the King to advance others to his favored side.

Following Up Subscriptions

Getting a subscription is only the first step, for the subscriber must usually receive further help to separate to the right. Back-calls are necessary even after the subscription is obtained. Show the person that you are interested in more than simply taking a subscription, by delivering copies until his magazines start coming in the mail. To be sure that the subscriber not only receives The Watchtower but also understands it have in mind starting a study as soon as possible. This requires preparation: studying the current issue before making the call, so that an interesting point can be discussed. For example: One might call attention to the varied means of preaching employed by religion in contrast with the method used by Jehovah's witnesses. After raising the question, “Which is the proper way?” the Watchtower article “Preach the Word” could be used to provide the Scriptural answer.

The Bible study servant can improve the following-up of subscriptions if he will examine the Publisher's Record cards from time to time and note those publishers obtaining subscriptions but not making back-calls. He can then give them practical aid in making effective Watchtower back-calls. It is essential to call back on these subscribers. You may be sure that the Devil will endeavor to minimize the importance of the magazine in the mind of new subscribers and will work through neighbors, religious ministers, members of the family and others to keep the magazine from being studied. Yes, Satan wants to keep them on his side, which is the wrong place for any who would live in the new world. Regular calls pointing out the King’s commands as they are called to attention through the columns of The Watchtower will defeat the wily one.

Be diligent, then, in planning your service during February so that all avenues of obtaining subscriptions are used. Be prepared to point out vital information as you call back on subscribers. The Watchtower is the most important magazine on earth, but only to those understanding its life-giving message. Use it well, that you may have the privilege of aiding others to separate to the King’s right hand.

The Catalogue

Are You Using It in Field Service?

The catalogue is an instrument which the Lord has provided as a specific aid for his servants. It can be very effectively used in acquainting persons of good-will with the Bible helps supplied by the Society. The catalogue, placed with those showing a hearing ear, will point them to these publications containing life-giving information. Have you been using it regularly? If your supply is depleted you may obtain more by ordering from Brooklyn.

When engaging in the house-to-house activity, introduce the catalogue wherever particular interest is found. The brief summary of the contents of each publication listed therein will often arouse the householder’s desire to have other publications and will reduce much of the publisher's explanation. A catalogue so placed provides an opportunity to make a back-call.

Similarly, when placing The Watchtower or Awake! on street corners you may give a catalogue to those persons obtaining magazines and who show marked interest. This same procedure can be applied on your magazine route. When placing magazines with regular obtained, leave a catalogue and acquaint them with these Bible helps.

On back-calls the catalogue can be used to good advantage also. Some of the listed Bible helps may appeal to the person and he can obtain them to further aid him in his study. It might also be suggested that these good-will persons acquaint their friends and neighbors with the literature listed in the catalogue. At the close of a public meeting, the catalogue can be given, along with the other booklet, to strangers who display interest.

In all avenues of the Lord’s service, the catalogue is of real value. It is an instrument to aid good-will persons to know of the availability of important Bible information. Use it well. that all are prepared. Do not wait until the circuit servant arrives before making these and other preparations. If you are not sure about something, go ahead to the best of your ability and the circuit servant can offer suggestions to avoid the difficulty in the future.

Much of the valuable counsel given in the various talks by the circuit servant is designed to fit the needs of the individual company being served. Each publisher should therefore attend all of these meetings and seek to personally apply the suggestions given.

It is also profitable for the company to review from time to time the circuit servant’s report on the company, a copy of which is retained in the company file. This will aid the company to overcome any weaknesses before the next visit of the circuit servant. Do not neglect any opportunity to profit from the circuit servant’s visit. It is important to expansion and progress.

Companies Ordering for Pioneers

Companies ordering supplies which will be used in part by pioneers should always indicate the number of pioneers in the company in the space provided on the order blank. Failure to do so may result in the order’s being reduced to cover the expected needs of the company publishers only.

From time to time pioneers may request certain items which the company does not have in stock and which must therefore be ordered from the Society especially for the pioneers. If the company orders such items the Society will charge the company at the regular company rate even though it is stated that the items are for pioneers. The Society does not charge any item on a company invoice at pioneer rates. The company should request the difference between the amount received from the pioneer and the amount which it is charged by the Society by using the Literature Credit Request form. This difference in rates is then credited to the company’s account.

In some instances it may be more convenient for the pioneer to order such special items on a separate order blank sent in with the company order. When this is done the items are charged to the pioneer at pioneer rates, as the company is not involved in the transaction. They will usually, however, be shipped with the company order. It should be kept in mind that a remittance must accompany cash item orders by pioneers unless the pioneer’s account has sufficient credit balance to cover.

Whenever pioneer and company orders are sent in together with a remittance to cover, separate remittance forms should be included. It is not necessary to send separate checks or money orders, although this may be done if desired; but in every instance it will be necessary for the Society to know how much of the remittance is to be applied to the company’s order and how much to that of the pioneer.
