“Theocracy’s Increase’’ Testimony Period Spread Convention Enthusiasm


End Service Year with a Fitting Climax

Abel had faith in it, Abraham hoped for it, Isaiah foretold it, and Jehovah established it in 1914. What was It? The Theocracy! Today, thirty-six years removed from the Theocracy’s establishment, we behold the increase Isaiah foretold when he said, “Of the increase of his government [Theocracy] and peace there shall be no end.” (Isaiah 9: 7) What a privilege and joy it is to have a small part in announcing Jehovah’s Theocracy and to see it increasing to the ends of the earth'.

August will undoubtedly be a great month in announcing that kingdom, as it begins with the “Theocracy’s Increase” Assembly in New York city. The effects of this great assembly will not be limited to New York city, but will be felt throughout the entire earth as servants of the Theocracy, refreshed by the feast of fat things, return to their individual countries, cities and companies, there to share the abundant blessings of the assembly with those who could not attend. The information carried back will be a driving force to bearer and hearer alike for energetic and enthusiastic service yet ahead.

August Ends 1950 Service Year

With the month getting off to a good start with the assembly, August should be a pinnacle month of activity in the eventful 1950 service year. Can it be? It can if each publisher accepts his responsibility before the Lord to participate in the field service and to report this to his company. Remember, August 1949 saw an all-time peak, up to that time, of 91,463 publishers enjoying field service privileges in the United States. The October, 1949, Informant carried that most pleasing report and asked: “With diligent effort on the part of all, is it possible that there will be 100,000 publishers in the United States [by convention time] ?” Through our diligent effort and Jehovah’s blessing, over 100,000 publishers were active long before the convention arrived. Now, with each publisher spurred on by the convention and expressing his appreciation for this great provision, what will the number of publishers be in August, to fittingly close the eventful 1950 service year?

Reaching a climax for August will require sincere effort on the part of each publisher to get in some time during the month without fail and then report it. There will have been unlimited opportunities to announce the Theocracy’s increase at the convention, to the people in whose home you lived while there, en route, and at home. Opportunities to preach are plentiful both for those who went to New York and for those who remained at home. So everyone should have a report to turn in. Brethren who will not be back to their local company by the end of August should send their field service reports to the company servant before the month’s end so that a complete and accurate company report can be sent to the Society by the 3rd of September. The offer for August Is one bound book, on a contribution of 35c.

Resume Local Activity

As soon as possible after arriving home arrangements should be made to devote portions of the service meeting to reviewing the high lights of the “Theocracy’s Increase” Assembly. This will present a splendid opportunity to share Jehovah’s goodness with the brethren who could not attend. The meetings should be well planned and a real effort put forth to invite newly interested persons on whom we call and others interested in the Theocracy’s Increase to attend. These meetings will be stimulating and beneficial to all in attendance.

Arrangements for resuming all features of service activity will be put into operation as soon as conventioners are back. Organized group activity from the Kingdom Hall and company book studies will let the community know that you are back and better equipped than ever to declare the way to life to all who will listen. Every publisher desiring to swell the Theocrat's praise will respond whole-heartedly to the arrangements for house-to-house work, street magazine witnessing, store-to-store activity, advertising public meetings and supporting other company arrangements. In addition, back-calls and Bible studies will be resumed, as well as our incidental preaching of truth every man to his neighbor, including the baker, grocer and all others with ears to hear.

Undoubtedly some who are beginning to appreciate the Theocracy will be so enthused by having attended the assembly or hearing the results from you that just a helpful “come with me” will start them in the service. Complete your assistance to them by aiding them to turn in their field service report. With every company publisher, special, regular and vacation pioneer lending full support to the company arrangements, aiding new publishers and encouraging others ready to publish, August will conclude, in a very fitting way, the grand 1950 service year.

Better equipped than ever before and having the definite assurance that the Theocracy’s increase will continue until it fills the whole earth, Kingdom publishers worldwide will intensify their faithful planting and persistent watering while God continues to give the increase.

Theocratic Ministry School Essential


Everyone Contributes to Its Success

An indispensable aid to efficient service! How true this is of the Theocratic ministry school! Would you be without the knowledge and training it has given you since April, 1943? Reflect for a moment. Think how your appreciation of God’s Word grew as you studied concerning the origin and authenticity of the Bible, the growth of its canon, the geography and history of Bible lands, following this with a book-by-book examination of this marvelous library. Then, how thankful you were for the lessons on Theocratic tact, argumentation, composition and delivery, Bible helps and translations. Yes, you can only conclude that the Theocratic ministry school has been very necessary to you and all Theocratic ministers.

Now we are entering the final section of Theocratic Aid to Kingdom Publishers. Have you considered how practical and useful will be the information you will receive during the next few months? The history of false religion, its fusion with apostate Christianity and the subsequent birth of the Roman Catholic Hierarchy, the Dark Ages and Reformation, plus a brief review of many present-day religions, climaxed by Jehovah’s vindication over demon religion. Certainly, armed with this knowledge, you will be better able to approach people from a position of vantage, and know how to tactfully refute their religious contentions.

Everyone Shares and Benefits

Viewing the ministry school from its relation to Kingdom service, each one will want to contribute his share toward its success. How can this best be done? First, attend regularly. Study your lesson beforehand and come prepared to take part in the oral review. This also prepares you for the written review, which should be taken by all. Good-will persons can gain much from attending the school, as they will receive early training for their future work of preaching.

Secondly, let all brothers enrolled as students set a good example. Do not take the school lightly, but accept each speaking assignment you receive. Prepare your student talks well and at the appointed time deliver them, keeping in mind previous counsel. Counsel given is for your benefit; seek continual Improvement by endeavoring to Improve upon the weak points brought to your attention. Instruction talks especially require much preparation and should be given extemporaneously, covering thoroughly the information in the lessons. All those on the program should make the most profitable use of the allotted time as well as stay within their time limit.

Role of the School Servant

The Instructor, too, plays an important part in making the school an instructive and beneficial meeting. He should prepare himself in advance to counsel by becoming familiar with the material to be treated each week. In offering counsel he must be guided by love, understanding, kindness, and a sincere desire to aid the students to improve. To that end counsel given will be constructive, including suggestions on how to improve, referring to Theocratic Aid to Kingdom Publishers when fitting. The same consideration should be given when grading the written review papers.

The company servant can schedule various features of the school on the service meeting program from time to time. Through demonstrations, forum discussions, and talks illustrate the value of Theocratic ministry, how to prepare for the written review, how to use in the field the Information gained, etc. This practical application of what is being taught will aid to equip the publishers for proficient service.

The Theocracy is advancing. The Theocratic ministry school is a gracious provision contributing to Its increase. Are you keeping pace with it? You cannot afford to miss out, so form the good habit of studying, attending and participating in the Theocratic ministry school, and strive to apply all that you learn.

Pioneer Reports

A few pioneers are improperly reporting public meetings and Bible studies. Though sometimes held by pioneers, public meetings in company territory should always be reported by the company itself. When pioneers sponsor a public meeting in isolated territory only one pioneer should report it. (See paragraph 41 of Counsel on Theocratic Organization.) The number of studies on the report card means the number of places you hold a study, not the number of times you call at each place. Each time a study is held, however, a back-call should be counted. Thus, if you held studies at five locations and conducted each one four times in a month, you would have 20 back-calls but still only five studies to report. Do not put messages on your report cards other than remarks about your service; otherwise there will be a delay In handling or replying.

Subscriber Address Changes

Are you notifying the Society in advance each time you change your address? Not all subscribers do. Every month thousands of magazines are returned to the Society by the post office with the notation that the subscriber has moved, cannot be found, or for some other reason the magazine cannot be delivered. At present, the Society must pay 2c postage for each magazine returned, and this charge is soon to be raised to 5c a copy. It can readily be seen that this represents a substantial outlay of money that could better be used otherwise.

The importance of sending the Society notice of your address change in advance cannot be overemphasized. Even though you notify the post office of the change, magazines will not be forwarded to you, since they are second-class mail. When one of your magazines is returned by the post office and your address is changed from the information that accompanies it, there are probably two more magazines yet in the mail which were sent to your old address and which will therefore be returned at a cost of 5c each to the Society.

For each magazine returned the Society sends the local company a card (L-47) requesting the correct address of the subscriber. Since only about 20 percent of these cards are returned, it would seem that a more careful check should be made by the company or the publishers to whom the cards are assigned. Each such card represents a person who has subscribed but who is not getting his magazine. Some of the subscribers may have moved, some have died, the post office is sometimes in error, or the address may have been wrong in the first place. But in every instance a thorough check should supplies can be obtained from nearby companies that are overstocked.

Actual-Count Stock Inventory

In harmony with paragraph 120 of Counsel on Theocratic Organisation, an actual-count inventory of stock on hand is to be taken on March 1 and September 1 of each year. The count taken on September 1 is to be sent to the Society on the forms provided. Two of these “Stock Inventory” forms are being sent to each company and each pioneer with this Informant. Record the count of your inventory on these forms accurately and completely, send the original to the Society not later than September 5, and retain the duplicate for your files.

This inventory should include only literature which is clean and in proper condition to place in the field. Note on the inventory any literature in stock in especially large quantities. All foreign literature should also be listed, although that which was obtained free from the Society should not be included when figuring the value of literature on hand.

The completed forms should show the amount of literature on hand, amount of cash on hand (book money), and the value of literature held on credit by the publishers. The company servant should check to see that the stock servant has filled out the forms properly, and should have the report and statement of the company’s indebtedness to the Society on that date read to the company.
