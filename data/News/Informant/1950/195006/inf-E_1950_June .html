‘living Up to the Name” Testimony Period Offer of Three Books Continued


Advertise the International Assembly

One of the things that particularly identifies the Lord’s consecrated servants today is the name they bear as Jehovah’s witnesses. What a lofty privilege it is for insignificant man to bear the majestic name of the glorious Creator who alone is Jehovah ! Those that accept the name, however, must also accept the responsibility that such a privilege places upon them. They cannot be witnesses in name only; they must live up to the name.—Isa. 43: 10, A. S. V.

How do Jehovah’s witnesses live up to the name? By living exemplary Christian lives, which are built around concentrated activity in field service. Out on the busy streets and to the homes they go, praising Jehovah and magnifying his name before all who will hear. While in this service of the King they deport themselves as befitting ambassadors of his heavenly kingdom. Their speech at the doors is pleasant, graced with good things about Jehovah. Their conduct in the homes is irreproachable so as not to stigmatize or cast reflection on his holy name and cause some to stumble. In all their service they display Theocratic tact and the attributes of the God they worship.

During June part of living up to the name will be offering to the people three bound books on a contribution of $1.00. In most cases the three latest books will be used, but any of the campaign volumes in stock may be presented. Demonstrations should be planned around the literature you will be using. The value of these various publications cannot be overstated, for they make known the name of Jehovah, how to serve him and live up to his name. Isn't this the people’s greatest need?

There are yet thousands of goodwill persons who will accept Jehovah's name. Before these can live up to the name they must be found and instructed. A house-to-house search must be made for them. In view of this we cannot ignore this important feature of service. These initial contacts furnish the basis for by far the majority of our future back-calls and home Bible studies. Those living up to the name should make it a point to engage regularly in the house-to-house activity ; and group witnessing for this purpose should be arranged from the company book-study locations and the Kingdom Hall.

Stress Back-Calls and Talk Convention

As you extol Jehovah’s name in your door-to-door witnessing be sure to make a record of all placements and where interested ones live. Not only should you inform these prospective praisers that you will return, but keep your word and call back. Jehovah is a dependable God; those living up to his name must likewise show dependability as well as patience and loving consideration toward interested ones. The sheeplike ones who invite you into their homes look to you as a minister of God. Their future progress will be influenced by the example you set for them.

The international assembly in New York city will make an excellent talking point on your back-calls and Bible studies. Review past assemblies for them. Whet their appetites for these joyous spiritual feasts which Jehovah serves to those bearing his name. Though the Society does not have any in stock, it may be that publishers have personal copies of The Messenger from previous conventions, which can be reviewed to arouse interest for this grand event. Many of them will desire to attend, and there is yet time for them to make plans. Perhaps they can be assisted in this regard.

Also, let us not neglect to cal] on the regular subscribers to the Watchtower and Awake! magazines. Keep their interest alive, and aid them to renew their subscription before it expires. Expiration slips for those subscriptions which do expire will, of course, provide an additional source of back-calls, and you should continue to contact these as their names are sent to your company. Since assemblies play such a vital part in magnifying Jehovah’s name, an enthusiastic discussion about the international convention may be just the stimulus needed to reawaken interest in The Watchtoiver or Awake! and in the Kingdom. Make the most of these opportunities.

Live Up to the Name at All Times

Living up to the name is not restricted to oui- direct service activities. It extends further than that. It is a full-time obligation. Though field service reports show that publishers, on the average, spend less than one hour each day in actual preaching, we, nevertheless, bear Jehovah’s name all day long. Advance Convention Preparations Begin


President Opens Rooming Work

On Saturday night, May 13, at St. Nicholas Arena, in the heart of New York city, 4,837 publishers from the city and surrounding companies assembled together to discuss their share in the 1950 International Convention. Gladly and with great enthusiasm they accepted the role of hosts. Up until the time of that meeting they had been looking ahead, but with it came a first taste of the convention Itself. The president of the Society, Brother Knorr, opened the meeting, describing the program to be accomplished and showing the responsibilities of it all upon the local publishers. Instructions were given as to how to select rooming accommodations, what to look for and how to eliminate the undesirable ones.

Since there is very little hope of a trailer camp’s being established, many more rooms in private homes will have to be found. It is true there are many more hotels in New York city than in other cities, but most of the hotels are keeping permanent guests and not so many rooms are available. The Convention Bureau helped by having its member hotels pledge rooms, and so at the time of the meeting there were 8,700 rooms pledged for convention use, which might accommodate approximately 20.000 people. Circuit Servant Visits Discontinued During Convention

On the week-end of July 23, 1950, all circuit servants will discontinue visiting the companies. Their work with the companies will be resumed on Tuesday, August 15. This will allow a three-week period to attend the international convention in New York and enjoy a vacation from the regular routine of the circuit work.

Circuit assemblies, which have been suspended since March 26, will be started again on Friday, September 1, 1950. Undoubtedly the circuit assemblies and the visits of the circuit servants will do much to emphasize the blessings of the international convention and further strengthen the publishers for the important work of gospel-preaching yet ahead.

If your company is notified that the circuit servant's visit or the circuit assembly will closely follow the convention, you should make as much of the preparation as possible before you leave for New York.

The Call for Pioneers Continues

Gratifying, indeed, is the response to the call for pioneers. Why, in one week alone over 400 applications for pioneer service were sent out! True, it may be that not every one of these publishers will realize his goal to enter the pioneer service, but it is evident that they are thinking, planning and working toward that end. There is every reason to believe that the majority of these brethren will continue on, with Jehovah’s help, to complete this forward step. But there are undoubtedly many others with the same heart's desire and whose circumstances might be altered sufficiently to make pioneering possible. To these the call for pioneers continues.

With this issue of the Informant another pioneer letter is being sent. There is a personal copy for each publisher, so that it can be prayerfully considered in private study in addition to being discussed at the service meeting.

Do not conclude that pioneering is only for others. Have you calmly and dispassionately reviewed your circumstances before the Lord? Can you foresee adjustments which might be made to overcome those things which prevent you from being a pioneer? If, as a result of this personal examination of your circumstances, you believe you can embark on the wise course and heed the call for pioneers, send now for your application. Having taken that step, continue to work positively toward being a pioneer, looking to Jehovah for guidance and help.

Order Public Meeting Handbills Early

The factory will be closed during the convention, July 30 through August 6. No orders will be filled nor correspondence answered during this time. Handbills for public meetings which are now being planned for August and September should be ordered by July 1, in order to be printed and mailed in time for your use. Letters containing orders for handbills should be plainly marked HANDBILL ORDER on the outside of the envelope. Any other matters requiring immediate attention before the convention should be marked RUSH on the envelope.

Magazines and Mail During Convention

During the latter part of July and the first part of August, the majority of the publishers will be away from their home companies to attend the international convention in New York. Arrangements should be made in advance to take care of personal and company mail during this absence so that it will not be returned to the Society. If you will carefully note the suggestions given below much expense and inconvenience can be avoided.

Pioneers and Individuals who receive The Watchtoicer and Awake! direct in individual rolls or by subscription should arrange for a friend, neighbor or another witness, who is not attending the convention, to pick up and care for their magazines and other mail regularly. It might be well to mention to your postman that you will be gone for a short while but that your mail should still be delivered and you have arranged to have it cared for.

If the one who regularly receives the distributors’ copies is to be away, please see that someone is appointed to take care of them and arrange for their distribution in the field. Company servants and others who may be expecting literature shipments or other mail from the Society should leave definite instructions as to how it is to be cared for to avoid return of mail or costly storage charges on literature shipments. Please do not leave these matters until the last minute and then forget to care for them, as it will mean much needless expense and inconvenience.
