DECEMBER, 1950   “Preach the word.”—2 Tim. 4: 2,___________________________________BROOKLYN, N. Y.

“Preach the Word” Testimony Period Strive for Efficient Use of the New Book


Follow Up All Interest —Start Studies

  • 1 The apostle Paul was nearing the end of his long, faithful ministry. His trials had been many and some very difficult, but in bonds of imprisonment he counseled the young man Timothy, impressing upon his mind the most important work for him as one of Jehovah’s witnesses. Was that to seek financial security? Gain worldly prestige? Or climb the social ladder of success? No! Paul’s inspired words pointed to the highest vocation and the most important thing for one desiring everlasting life. He instructed, “Preach the word I”—2 Tim. 4: 2,

  • 2 Timothy did preach, but Paul’s words pointed forward especially to our day when the life of every living creature depends upon the choice of the right way based on God’s Word. Urgent it is for us to “preach the word”. And this we have been doing. Reflect: As we look back over the past year’s activity, we see Jehovah’s rich blessing upon the word preached. Increase after increase has resulted, circuit assemblies have accentuated our joy, and the climax was reached at the wonderful assembly in New York city. At the public meeting 123,707 in attendance attested to Jehovah’s blessing upon the word preached. The world may call this preaching impractical if it wishes, but the results show the opposite. Gratefully we go forth in December, the last month under this yeartext.

Effective Preaching

  • 3 December brings us to the second month of presenting “This Means Everlasting Life”, on a contribution of 35c, and endeavoring to leave a copy of Can You Live Forever in Happiness on Earth! in every home. Present the book enthusiastically and with conviction. Work for efficient presentation. Chapter 2, for example, presents excellent talking points. This age of science and advance in human wisdom tends to exalt man. He can peer millions of light-years into space, probe the secrets of atomic energy and erect great buildings. His theories lead him still farther until the layman looks to man and his greatness and becomes subject to such leaders to do their will. Great men, however, when seen from God’s viewpoint are very little. They have made advances, yes, yet the Lord says man is as a grasshopper, the nations as dust in the balance! God alone is great and he is the Giver of life. Should we not humble ourselves and learn of his requirements for life?.

  • 4 What kind of life has God promised those who will meet his requirements? Chapter 27 points out that it is God’s purpose to restore to this earth the paradise of Eden with all its blessings. That means everlasting life with peace, freedom and happiness. What a prospect for righteous, God-fearing persons today ! To learn of this righteous new world is of greatest importance.

  • 5 Many other presentations may be used, but, in all cases, “preach the word” as commanded and then offer the book as an aid to study. The holiday season brings added opportunities for presentation. Since it has been impossible to fill all domestic and foreign orders, companies that do not have supplies of “This Means Everlasting Life” may use “Let God Be True".

“Preach the Word” by Following Through

  • 6 The brief witness and placing of the book is only the start. Hearing and learning are required, as John 6:45 points out. That means back-calls. Perhaps you make the back-call and they say they haven’t read the book. What then? Words to this effect might be used to strike their interest: ‘I realize that many things come up that prevent us from doing all we would like to do. However. you will agree that if you don’t read the book it will be of little benefit to you. I desire to aid you to learn the precious contents of the book you have. It has brought much comfort and understanding to me and I’m sure it will bring this to you also. The chapter on the Author of Life is very interesting . . .’ etc. Or if they seem indifferent about the book, you might ask if they have any Bible questions they would like answered. If not, you ask one; “Do you believe there will be people that will live on the earth forever in peace and happiness?” You might go to chapter 27 for your discussion. In this way you start them talking and the way is opened for you to preach the Word. Be friendly. Talk to the people as your neighbors, which they are.
