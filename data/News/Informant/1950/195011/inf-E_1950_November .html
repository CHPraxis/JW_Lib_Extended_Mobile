Working with a Purpose

  • 1 Jehovah is a God of purpose. From this record we get some idea of how he does things and how efficiently his organization is equipped for handling the work he sets out to be done. All things are done In order and in his own time and manner.

But being imperfect we are inclined to not always keep clear in our minds the real reason for our work. Sometimes a lack of purpose in our work becomes apparent and we find ourselves doing work which is not as effective as it could be. If this continues over a period of time we find that our work becomes somewhat arduous and the real burning desire to aid those of good will is cooled to some degree. Hence, the need to keep the truths of Jehovah’s Word firmly fixed in our heart and to always keep the purpose of our work clearly in mind.

1950 Pioneer Hour

  • 1 The pioneer activity is close to the hearts of all the brethren. The pioneers themselves are most keenly interested because it personally affects them. Then, too, the company publishers are concerned about it, for many of them expect to be pioneers in the near future, while to those who cannot be pioneers it is an encouraging example of devotion and service.

  • 2 Just as it is good to periodically examine ourselves in an effort to improve our service, so likewise it is profitable to check on the pioneer activity as a whole. This we have done in the pioneer hour check-up for the 1950 service year. This checkup is encouraging, yet it reveals room for improvement. Sixty-five per cent of the pioneers met or exceeded the minimum hour requirement. Nine per cent came somewhat short of this requirement and were given opportunity during the coming service year to improve, while three per cent, because of their reports and circumstances, were transferred to the company publisher ranks. The remainder are new pioneers put on the list during the last few months of the service year. All but eight of the special pioneers met the minimum hour requirement.

  • 3 In dealing with the pioneers the Society endeavors to be fair to all, allowing each one full opportunity to measure up to the requirements of the pioneer work. This it does by giving consideration to individual circumstances. Nevertheless, the Society must maintain the established and proper standards for such service.

Individual Check-up

  • 4 Although this check-up was to find out how the pioneers have done in respect to meeting their hour requirement. the check-up should not end there for the pioneer. He should be concerned with improving in every respect as a full-time publisher. Ask yourself: Is my presentation at the door effective? Am I following up the interest that I find, by making back-calls and conducting studies? Do I engage in the magazine work, putting to good use the magazines the Society sends me?

  • 5 In Bible study these persons gain the knowledge which furnishes the foundation for understanding. Here working with a purpose is very essential too, since one must be ever alert to instruct as the sheep advance to maturity and take their places in the chorus of praise singers. Sometimes being alert to do the little things to aid does much to impress and build up those with whom you are studying. For example, when points come up that seem hard to understand, a little extra research on these points to bear out the correct understanding and meaning will be appreciated. This demonstrates that we are sincere and that the knowledge we have gained by regular study at the Kingdom Hall and privately has not been gained to hoard up but to share unselfishly with others.

  • 6 As we preach, let us do it with a purpose. The Word needs to be preached more and more, and the sheep need to be fed. If we keep this commission clearly in mind and continually work to become more adept in carrying it out in harmony with Jehovah's organization, we will be working with a purpose.

3 To lie a pioneer and successfully meet the requirements year after year calls for effort, study, and planning. Especially is this true since the provisions for suspension have been replaced by the decided reduction in the yearly hour requirement. Just as any business poorly managed will fail, so will the activity of a pioneer gradually slacken if pursued without purpose or plan. Schedule your time wisely, allowing for field service, study and meetings, meals, secular work, etc. Then carry out this schedule with determination. So today, let publishers and pioneers alike be of the same mind, joyfully using their time in the witness work. Besides, more time in Jehovah’s service leaves less time for the ensnaring things of this world. Hence, the safe choice is to spend more time in His service.
