APRIL, 1950                                       “Preach the word.”—2 Tim. 4:2.                                 BROOKLYN, N.Y.

“Announcing Messiah’s Presence” Testimony Period Watchtower Campaign Enters Final Month


Pioneering, a Work with a Future

Are You Arranging Your Affairs to Be One?


Memorial Week-End Sets Stage for 100,000 Goal

In 1914, amid world-shaking events, Jehovah God asserted his universal sovereignty and sent forth his Messiah-Son to rule in the midst of his enemies. Christ’s invisible reign means everlasting life to all who believe and obey. But how can they believe unless they first hear? and how can they hear unless his presence is announced? And who is there today announcing Messiah’s presence? You are, if you really believe the signs that prove that Christ is now present and ruling. —Matt. 24: 37, Roth.

Just pause a moment to consider what a unique position and privilege we have in daily telling others that Christ Is present, ruling in the heavens, and how his Kingdom will shortly “satisfy the desire of every living thing”. Of all the world’s millions, Jehovah’s witnesses alone are engaged in this ministry of salvation. One of the best instruments we have to aid us in ministering to “this generation” is the Watchtower magazine. Did not The Watchtoicer help us to see with our eyes of faith that Christ is present and ruling? Logically, then, it is the magazine we should use to assist others likewise to appreciate this all-important truth. Therefore, during April, we will continue to offer the Watchtoicer subscription and a gift of eight booklets on a contribution of one dollar.

As we enter this final month of the Watchtower campaign let us redouble our efforts to meet our individual quota of two subscriptions per publisher for the four months. Reports coming into the office thus far show we are surpassing last year's figures for this same period. Continue your diligent efforts by calling back on last year’s subscribers, whose expiration slips are regularly mailed to each company. Rekindle the spark of interest they once had in The Watchtower, and demonstrate its practical benefits for them. Also, be sure to remember those who subscribed during the early months of the campaign. Visit them. Aid them to understand and study The Watchtower. Help them to realize that The Watchtower is “meat in due season” for them.

Follow Up

Memorial Week-End Activity

The Memorial week-end should give an enthusiastic Impetus to all our April activities, setting the stage for a new peak in publishers. No doubt then many new ones will be assisted Into the field, and also many more will eagerly participate during the remainder of the month, if given the proper encouragement and loving assistance by zealous publishers. Remember not lo neglect these once they start publish

ing, but continue to work with them and assist them until they become regular "announcers”, able to bear their own burdens. As they engage in field service, encourage them to report their activity. Also visit delinquent publishers, infirm and sick ones to obtain their reports of time spent in preaching.

Our goal is 100,000 publishers by convention time, announcing Messiah’s presence. We have already reached the 96,000 mark, so the desired quota is by no means an impossibility. It can be reached if we do not relax in our service. If all publishers are diligent and regularly report the time profitably spent in preaching, both “In season” and “out of season”, a great stride will be made toward reaching a new peak and our goal of 100,000.

Public Meetings Aid in Announcing

Another very effective means of' announcing Messiah’s presence is | through public meetings. The new series should now be under way, and will undoubtedly give renewed force to this feature of service. Brand-new subjects! What striking titles! “It Is High Time to Awake!” “Have Christendom’s Religions Failed?” “Hell Used as a Scare.” What vital, absorbing issues! “The Pathway to Peace,” “Who Is Your God?” “Preserving Godliness amid World Delinquency.” What comfort and assurance! “The Truth Shall Make You Free,” “Surviving Global War.” Yes, what a joy it will be to announce such good news.

True, for these talks to fulfill our expectations, those assigned to deliver them must make proper advance preparation. Their real success, however, will depend on the energetic support of each publisher. The company servant, together with the advertising servant, should ascertain the number of handbills needed for the company and order accordingly. The wide distribution of leaflets plays an Important part in announcing and advertising. Advertise, too, by inviting your back-calls and home Bible studies. Are you doing your part?

Once you have advertised, then attend the public meeting and show genuine interest in the strangers present. Help them to further understand and appreciate the truths they have heard, by discussing with them any question they may have and by arranging to call on them at their homes at some convenient time.

Let us bring this, the twelfth Watchtower campaign, to a climactic close. The prospects are bright. Memorial week-end has set the stage, and by further zealous endeavor, making the most of our opportunities, the way will be opened for new peaks and countless joys and blessings. So forward ! announcing Messiah's presence.


Watchtower Article to Be Considered at Service Meetings

Consider, if you will, representative types of work in which you might engage. After each put down the brightest future you might expect to result from devoting all of your ability and energy to It. You may find on your list such things as wealth and ease, social position, security or fame; perhaps even the satisfaction of benefiting humankind to some degree by discovery or invention.

Now record the future prospects that pioneer service holds out to those who diligently strive to succeed therein. These will include “the joy of the Lord”, the full satisfaction of really aiding your fellow creatures to lasting benefits, countless blessings from the hand of a provident Creator both in richness of life and sufficient to meet present physical needs, a new world full of bounties just ahead and, best of all, for those who maintain integrity, everlasting life!

Study your lists and thoughtfully make comparison. Do you honestly feel that the transient rewards of any secular position can in anywise compare with those of the pioneer work? Or, are they as sure of fulfillment? Why, it even seems a waste of time to make the comparison ! Truly, the pioneer work is the work with a future!

The Pioneer Service Is for All

Surely you, like all other conscientious servants of God, have considered more than once these blessings that accompany pioneer service ; but then you have dismissed the prospects from your mind. True, pioneering presents remarkable opportunities for others, but it cannot be for you, you conclude. You have good reasons. Well, list your reasons or excuses. Consider each one. Then know for a certainty that whatever your hindrance may be, there is somebody on the pioneer list who has overcome that same problem. If one can overcome it, doesn’t it seem possible that by placing complete faith in Jehovah you too can overcome it?

Now is the time for each servant of God to carefully and prayerfully consider his own circumstances, and work toward the goal of entering the pioneer service. Many who have already been planning and working toward that end will be able to send for an application immediately and enjoy the added blessings of attending the 1950 convention as a pioneer. But do not let inability to begin before the convention move you to “put off” pioneer service Into the indefinite future. Send for your application anyway, fill it in and return it, letting us know what month you wish to start; whether immediately, or soon after the convention. Then you will be able to begin at the time you have planned, without delay.

Some may wish to sign up for the pioneer work while at the convention. In that case, it would be wise to bring with you the letter of recommendation from the company servant, which must accompany all pioneer applications. Then your application can be considered without unnecessary and time-consuming correspondence.

Vacation Pioneering

A large number of those now enjoying full-time service have been at one time or other vacation pioneers. This in itself proves that vacation pioneering is the best possible way for children to spend their vacation days, for, by busying themselves in Kingdom service, they grow in appreciation of their responsibility to Jehovah and are protected from undue influence by the world. But vacation pioneer service is not restricted to young people alone. Anyone who can remain in the pioneer work for at least two months at any time of the year and who can meet the necessary requirements of having been immersed and a publisher for six months can qualify for vacation pioneering. For the [last two years we have had new [leaks in the number who have enjoyed this service. This summer may we not hope to exceed last year’s 832 vacation pioneers?

“Watchtower” Stresses Pioneering

So important is pioneering now to the welfare of the servants of God and to the expansion of gospelpreaching that The Watchtower has devoted the April 1, 1950, issue to this subject. The second article of this issue, entitled “More and More Pioneers of Good News”, because of its service theme, will be considered at the service meetings instead of at the regular Watchtower study. It will be taken up during the last three service meetings in April, by questions and answers in the same manner in which The Watchtower is customarily studied. Not more than thirty minutes should be spent on each of these studies, which will be apportioned to cover paragraphs 1-10 at the first meeting, paragraphs 11-21 at the second and paragraphs 22-31 at the last.

All publishers should make every effort to be present at each of these service meetings, to benefit from these studies. Careful study of the article beforehand and enthusiastic participation will enhance the value of the material to you.

Remember, then, pioneering isn’t only for somebody else. Pioneering is also for you. Make your decision now to be a pioneer, and plan for the future. Send for an application blank, fill it out, and return it with a letter of recommendation from the company servant. Then, at the time you have decided upon, enter the pioneer service, the work with a lasting future.

Pioneer Assignment Cards

Assignment cards held by pioneers should always coincide w’ith the territory in which they are working. This is not always the case at present, for different reasons. In some instances the name of the company or unit may have been changed, because of a shift in the location of the company headquarters or the formation of new units. Hence, even though the pioneer is still working in the same general territory or with the same group, his card may not agree with the official name of the unit or company. Other pioneers may have started to work with other companies, overlooking the necessity of being assigned to that territory by the Society.

It is therefore requested that all pioneers immediately check their assignment cards. If the card does not show the name of the company you are now with, please return the card to the Society immediately and request an up-to-date assignment card. Be sure to state where you wish to be assigned to avoid unnecessary correspondence. Circuit servants can assist in this respect by emphasizing this point at the time of the pioneer interview.

Furthermore, many reports were made by the Creator himself. He recorded in remarkable detail events from creation to the ultimate destruction of wickedness, and coming Kingdom blessings for those who now obey. His report is a loving provision for the comfort of all who love him.

Bethel Applications

Recently a number of brothers were Invited to come to Bethel and Kingdom Farm because they had filed applications some time ago. But now their circumstances are changed, so they found it impossible to accept the invitation.

In order to keep the Bethel application file up to date, all Bethel applications now on hand at Brooklyn will be canceled as of April 1. Those who have had applications on file for some time and who wish to keep their names on file must make new applications. Those who have not previously applied for Bethel service may do so now, if they wish.

Single men and women between the ages of 18 and 35 may qualify for Bethel service. They must be in good health and seriously interested in permanent full-time service. Another requirement is that each person must have been consecrated and active in the service for a full year. Pioneers and company publishers may apply.

Send requests for application blanks to: President’s Office, 124 Columbia Heights, Brooklyn 2, New York.

When blanks are filled out and returned to the President’s Office, they will be put on file for consideration when openings occur for additional servants at Bethel.
