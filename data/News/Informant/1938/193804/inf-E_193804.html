Brother Rutherford’s personal letter mailed to all Watchtower subscribers is of such importance that it is reprinted here.

January 31, 1938

To the witnesses of Jehovah

throughout the earth.

Dear Brethren and Companions:

A world-wide witness campaign to the name and glory of Jehovah and his King begins April 9, 1938. That campaign specially features the message of testimony of the Lord set forth in the books “Riches” and “Enemies” and the booklet “Cure”. Such contains a warning from the Lord’s Word to “Christendom” and a message of hope to those who are looking for consolation. The obligation to get this message to the people is laid upon everyone who has covenanted to do the will of God.

It appears from the Scriptures that the people of God are now entering upon a period of unusual importance, during which time every one of the anointed and companions must do his, or her, duty. It is my privilege and duty to call your attention to the importance of the hour and to the message of the Lord that must now be delivered to the people. All witnesses must now be at complete unity in action, dwell in peace, and work harmoniously in righteousness.

Will you put forth your best endeavors to carry this testimony to those who have a hearing ear and to give warning to others?

Never in times past has there been such an opportunity to sing forth the praises of Jehovah and speak of his glorious name as now. He has commanded all who are of his organization to rise up against the enemy in war, and our part in that warfare is to bear testimony to his name and sing the praises of Jehovah and his King. Be of good courage and do your part, and may the God of peace and righteousness shield and protect you and richly bless you as you go forth. To you the Most High speaks as at Isaiah 51: 16.

The interest of the Kingdom is of paramount importance and is the only thing worth while.

With much love and best wishes,

Yours for the Kingdom Interest,

New Model Phonograph

The Society is pleased to announce a new model of the small portable phonograph. The new model is very similar in every respect to the small model previously manufactured, except that it is 11/16 of an inch deeper than the older model, which permits the tone arm and reproducer to be left in the playing position when no more than three discs are being carried in the machine. There is a small coil spring in the lid of the case to hold the records on the turntable when carrying the machine. The removable tone arm feature is retained so that on making back calls the tone arm and reproducer can be placed in the bottom of the case as before, thus leaving room for carrying up to 10 or 11 discs in the phonograph.

The Society is nearly 2500 machines behind in tilling orders, but we hope to be able to 1111 all of these orders in the next three months. All of these orders will be filled with the new model which we are now producing.

Convention

A convention of Jehovah’s witnesses and companions will be held at San Antonio, Texas, April 15-17 inclusive. Both the Spanish- and English-speaking people of the Lord will be served at that convention. The convention will open at the meeting place, 323 Matamoros Street, at 8: 30 a.m. on Friday the 15th. All brethren should bring their phonographs, with the necessary records. Those desiring accommodations will please address J. D. Carter, 517 Stonewall St., San Antonio, Texas. A joint meeting of English and Spanish will be held on the evening of the 15th to celebrate the Memorial.
