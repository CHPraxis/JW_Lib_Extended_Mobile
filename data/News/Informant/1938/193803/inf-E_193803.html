The Start of the Biggest Witness of the Year


One Back Call a Week

Each Company Publisher’s Quota

In order to properly build and plant’, we must bring to the people of good will the Kingdom message in such an effective way that these “other sheep” may be encouraged to break away from the Devil’s snare and take their stand alongside of Jehovah's witnesses. When we come in contact with people favorable to the Kingdom message, we now see that our obligation does not end with merely leaving with them the Kingdom truths in printed form, but we must go a step further, if we are to carry out oui- commission fully. That forward step is making back calls and thus aiding the interested ones to arrive at the point where they can and will be able to make an intelligent decision.

As organized units of Kingdom publishers throughout the earth, our individual privileges and responsibilities in the advancement of this back-call work are clearly defined. Service committees: Their obligation Is to stress this important feature of the work and to arrange that the back calls turned in are promptly attended to by the willing publishers. Company publishers: Each one should set as his quota an average of one back call a week, and be diligent in seeking and using every means available towards reaching this goal. Auxiliaries: Enjoying increased opportunities, these should set and reach a quota of two back calls a week. And finally, the pioneers: Because of their privilege of being able to be continually In the field (and this particularly In the case of the special pioneers), these should set and make their quota an average of five back calls a week.

To aid in reaching the quota set, each one should set aside a definite time for making back calls, as far as possible and practicable. Companies should set aside one or two evenings a week as back-call periods. Pioneers can use evenings and daytime for this particular work. If we plan our back-call work as we have planned and do plan our regular witnessing work, we shall enjoy the same degree of increased opportunities and blessings from the Lord’s hand.

When making back calls, be prepared always to offer at least an assortment of four bound books. And, furthermore, be on the lookout to make each back call grow into a model study meeting. Remember your quota. One back call a week.

“Companions” Testimony Period “Cure” Being Shipped by the Millions Great Preparations Under Way


Are You Ready to Do Your Part?

The importance of the "Companions" testimony period, April 9-17. cannot be overstated. It commences the greatest campaign that the Lord has entrusted to his people to date.

The campaign is international in its magnitude, and its objective is the distribution of ten million of the booklet Cure and one million bound books, Enemies or Riches, and 100,000 Consolation subscriptions. The “Companions” testimony period should see this objective well on its way and a good percentage of the work accomplished.

Concurrent with the announcement of this special campaign comes the Watchtower carrying the "Jonah” article, emphasizing above everything else the need of absolute obedience to organization instructions. Some of the significant statements in The Watchtower are quoted here to refresh our memories and show us their close relationship to tills campaign:

“During the World "War there was a side-stepping of duty which God laid upon his people.”

“To be silent about the kingdom and especially God’s announced vengeance against all of Satan's organization was also an effort to flee from the presence of the Lord.”

These quotations indicate the tremendous responsibility resting upon each one of the anointed and their companions in connection with this and all other campaigns which the Lord inaugurates through his organization. No one can escape it. No one can get someone else to substitute for him. All must bear their responsibility before the I,ord, individually and as an organization.

Organization

As an organization, the responsibility rests upon the service committee and company servant to make the necessary preparations. Have you. as a committee, carefully studied the February Informant and started planning what territory is going to be used for this campaign and how you are going to work it? Have you ascertained the available publishers and taken the necessary steps to help those publishers who are hindered by obstacles which they need assistance in overcoming? Have you checked your stock of Enemies. Riches, phonographs, records, and have you seen io it that the necessary orders for an adequate supply have been forwarded to the Society? Have you planned for the necessary sample copies of Consolation? Have you seen to it that all names of good-will persons in your files are properly arranged so that they can be distributed to the publishers holding the various assignments, to make back calls upon them with this special offer, and the numerous other Ihings for which you, as a service committee and company servant, are responsible, in order to make this campaign a success in your locality?

Publishers

As an individual publisher, are you planning now for this campaign? Is your personal equipment arranged for phonographs and records? Are you planning your time, budgeting it so that you have definite periods set aside each day for participation in the Kingdom service—days which are to be devoted to the field service, and nothing permitted to Interfere with them?

All of these things are details for which each and every one of us is responsible before the Lord, and we manifest our dependability by carefully looking after them. The Lord has entrusted us with our biggest job to date. Can he depend upon us to do our part? The companions, both anointed and Jonadabs, will answer “Yes”, and mean it, because they are trustworthy. Are you ready to be one of the 30,1)00 publishers in action during the April campaign and present to the nations the real CURE?

What We Want to Do

We have our job outlined for us by the Lord through his organization. Our commission from the Lord is clear-cut and definite. We must carry the warning to the people; then the responsibility is theirs. The question facing each one, therefore, is. Are we. like Jonah, going to flee from the presence of Jehovah, or are we going to be like the class described by the prophet Isaiah who joyfully say, ‘Here am I, Lord; send me’?

We know that the latter describes the spirit and zeal of the Lord’s true people now. and we are confident that the United States will reach, and probably exceed, the 30,01)0 publishers for which we have been looking forward during this period. Let each one do his part, looking to the Lord to direct the issue. And now, as we come to attention in the Lord's army, what is to be our personal quota for this campaign?

The average number of publishers per month for this three-month period was 23,863 in 1937, and 22,355 in 1936. This is an increase of 1,508 publishers per month, which is splendid and just as we should expect. The great multitude is now being gathered by the Lord. When these people receive a knowledge of Jehovah and an understanding of his purposes they want to do the same thing that you are doing. Thus the ranks of the publishers increase. However, we have not yet reached the 30,000 publishers for which we are striving. Shall we do it by next April?

In order to find out how many different individuals were in the field during the three months we have gone through your reports and taken the highest number for each company during the three months, and totaled these, and by so doing we find there were 26,250 different persons in the field this fiscal year. However, the average was only 23,863 regularly in the field. This means that nearly 3,000 publishers failed to get into the field at least once a month. Every publisher should make it his business to report as a publisher at least once a month, if only for one hour; but report.

The Peak

The greatest number of publishers ever engaging in the field service in the territory of the United States and countries under the jurisdiction of the Brooklyn office was 27,004. This total was reached in April, 1936. What will it be in April 1938, two years later? Shall we have an increase of more than 3,000 publishers and pass that 30,000 quota and establish a new peak? We should. The work is increasing, and more publishers are needed to do the work.

The witness must be given, and every company organization should now begin planning how it can aid and assist the newly interested ones attending its meetings to get into the field service also. No one should be forced or coerced to go into the field work, but in many Instances the service committee can make it more convenient for the brethren in the company to get into the field work. They can point out by the Scriptures their responsibility and stress the Importance of studying the Society’s publications, particularly The Watchtower. After feeding upon this food for a while they will see that their responsibility to the Lord is that of being a witness for the Kingdom. Special pioneers, pioneers, auxiliaries and company publishers should now begin to assist the newly interested persons in the witness work and show them how they, too, can be publishers for the Kingdom.

A good way to do this is for the brethren who live in the vicinity of the interested to make arrangements to call on them some Saturday afternoon or Sunday, or during the week, and take them along into the field and show them how it is done. Loan them your phonograph and a few books, take them from door to door for a few homes, and then let them do it for themselves.

When shall we reach the 30,000 in number of publishers? What is your local company doing toward reaching that mark? Have you increased in number of publishers?

Special Call

A call is going forth for more volunteers for this special pioneer service. The requirements are 160 hours a month in the field witnessing, as minimum time. The quota of every special pioneer is 200 hours a month, which means 50 hours a week of actual witnessing. The work to be done is particularly in the northern part of the United States all the year round. The work is in big cities. Each special pioneer tries to make 15 or more setups a day with the phonograph, and from these setups makes as many back calls as possible in evenings or at other times during the day. The Lord has greatly blessed the special pioneer service, and those that are in it are averaging more than 160 hours a month. As spring comes on tins little band of faithful warriors will gradually increase their hours toward the 200 mark, which is the goal set for them.

This call goes forth to pioneers, auxiliaries or company publishers. Consider it. Take it to the Lord and see if you can Increase your interests in the Kingdom to the extent of being a special pioneer. Write the Society for more information. Why not be a special pioneer in March and be ready for the world-wide campaign in April with Enemies, Cure and Consolation?
