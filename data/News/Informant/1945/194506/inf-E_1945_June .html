JUNE, 1945                           “Go ye therefore, and make disciples of all the nations.”—Matt. 28: 19, A.S.V.                   BROOKLYN, N. Y.

“This Gospel” Testimony Period

June to See Marked Expansion of Public Meeting Campaign


‘The Meek Inherit the Earth,’ to Be Featured

While the nations assemble (as at San Francisco) and seek human remedies for world distress (a ‘just and lasting peace' by means of a world security organization), Jehovah calls attention to Uis remedy by commanding, “This gospel of the kingdom shall be preached in the whole inhabited earth for a testimony unto all the nations.” (Matt. 24:14, A.K.V., margin) The carrying out of that command is the most important work that is being done on the earth today.

Jehovah always does his part. By setting his King upon his holy hill of Zion, he created the basis or foundation for “this gospel”. He put his spirit upon his people and commissioned them to go forth and preach “this gospel", and by his spirit or active force he sustains them in that work. And so that his servants might press on with joy and zeal and increased faith and appreciation, he provided the special company meetings.

At those meetings the servants of Jehovah were refreshed and edifled as to the privilege of presenting their bodies (including all its members and faculties) as living sacrifices. How they might perform their rational divine service was clearly and forcibly brought to their attention. They were thrilled as they heard the graphic portrayal of the part that Jehovah’s witnesses play in the great conflict for supremacy as the people for the issue, specially created for that occasion. There they heard with rapt attention the timely and comforting message “The Meek Inherit the Earth”, and received it in printed form with enthusiasm for their use in further preaching "this gospel”. But above all, those special meetings will be remembered as the occasion on which Jehovah, through his “faithful and wise servant”, provided his surprised and delighted evangelists with the new instruction book Theocratic Aid to Kingdom Publishers. And what an instrument that is! What an aid in our endeavors to measure up to the exacting terms of our commission! Yes, Jehovah always does his part I

Availing Ourselves of the Theocratic Aid

Now the issue facing us is that of applying these divinely provided instructions in the field. Throughout the month of June, all of the Lord's servants will make use of its counsel while preaching from house to house (see Lesson 50), offering the bound book “The Kingdom lx at Hand” and the new booklet ‘The Meek Inherit the Earth’ on a contribution of 25c. An earnest endeavor should be made to place one of these new booklets in each home. Where the new booklet has not yet been received, use the booklet One World, One Oorernment until it arrives. (The factory has worked extra time so as to get as many of the new booklets as possible into the hands of the publishers in time for the June campaign.) This month brings with it warmer weather and longer days, thus providing additional opportunities to preach “this gospel" by engaging in the evening house-to-house work, presenting either the regular offer or the booklet alone.

Not only will the servants of Jehovah do their part by going from house to house with “this gospel”, but they will also show their appreciation of his tender compassions by calling back on all such as have shown interest and aiding them to appreciate the issue and the Kingdom. In going from house to house we are merely looking for the meek, the “other sheep”. Being meek, they are teachable; and therefore, having found them, wre must also teach them. And having started studies with these, we must exercise care if we would have them continue. It is not enough merely to go through a routine procedure of asking the questions and then having the paragraphs read, but we must put our heart into it; teaching with am thority and conviction, making the lesson clear and interesting, and at the same time being ever alert to use Theocratic tactfulness in dealing with the many questions and Incidents that arise. That is one of the main reasons why the new book Theocratic Aid to Kingdom Publishers was provided. Study and apply its lessons, and your back-calls and book studies will increase.

Preaching “This Gospel” Publicly

Doing our part includes also pushing forward in the public meeting campaign. Improved weather should see a decided “stepping up” in this method of preaching “this gospel". In fact, June is the ideal month for this, being neither too warm nor too cool. Therefore, do not wait any longer, but arrange now for openair meetings in parks, pavilions, bandstands, ball parks, and where-ever suitable places can be found. Bring the lectures to the people, and thus reach many who failed to come to the lectures. At such places it would be well to attract attention by playing musical selections on a transcription machine (if one is available) for ten to fifteen minutes before the assembly begins. During this time the brethren can go around handing out leaflets and personally inviting those in the vicinity to attend the public meeting.

Where the company has no further places available at which to put on the series, it would be well to arrange for a return series at the Kingdom Hall and other places used earlier in the year. At such places the same lectures could again be given, or, especially where the first series was limited to four lectures, the company may use new subjects for the lectures by now putting on lectures numbers 5 to 8 in this manner: “ ‘Thy Kingdom Lome’ When?” “The Bible Spotlight on Religion"; “Religion’s Harvest”; “Education for Life.” Under these circumstances it is left to each company to decide whether to start again with the first talk and run the entire series or just use the last four mentioned here. Indeed there is still plenty of work for all, in the public meeting campaign.

With each and every publisher having clearly in mind what Jehovah has done in connection with the preaching of “this gospel”, and seeing the many opportunities that lie before us as co-workers with Him, there is no reason why the June Testimony period should not see a new peak in hours for all ranks of Kingdom publishers. Jehovah always does His part. Will you do your part?

Your diligent study of the course in Theocratic ministry has greatly assisted you in performing your covenanted work of preaching "this gospel” to others. You clearly discern the Lord's blessing on your efforts. Now many of you are relieved from school work for the summer months. You have time on your hands. The question arises, What are you going to do with the time? As a young man he proved his faith by works, his utter dependence upon the Lord’s guiding, active force, when he walked out without fear of man and met the challenge of the Devil’s representative, Goliath. Many years later, when he was an old, grayhaired man, David said, “For thou art my hope. O Lord Gou: thou art my trust from my youth.” He had devoted himself to the glorious service of making known Jehovah’s judgments, and had been richly blessed and led of the Lord God.

The Lord Jesus preached the truth when but a lad of tender years. Kingdom Service Expanding

Soon all the meek of the earth will pass through the perils of Armageddon and enter into the cleansed, refreshed new earth ready for unhindered Kingdom service throughout eternity. Before that glorious prospect is realized, however, Satan will employ every means of deception and opposition within his power against us with increasing pressure as his inescapable doom approaches. Parallel with his avalanche of demonic propaganda goes the invincible proclamation of the “Gospel of the Kingdom”. Those who are truly meek will not hesitate to separate themselves from the course of the demonized, doomed world and devote their time, energy and resources to advancing the Kingdom interests. Thus they heed the admonition of the apostle Paul to the Corinthians at 1 Corinthians 15:58, Rotherham: “So, then, my beloved brethren, become ye steadfast, immovable, superabounding in the work of the Lord at all times.”

The Christian steadfastly and immovably seeks ways and means of enlarging his privileges in Kingdom service at the sacrifice of personal selfish interests. He carefully considers how he spends every moment of his time, every bit of energy, and how his mind is being occupied, and strives diligently to divert those resources now being used for other purposes to advancing the Kingdom service. The offer in July will be a continuation of the June combination of “The Kingdom Is at Hand" and ‘The Meek Inherit the Earth', on a 25c contribution. This booklet should be left in every home, where-ever possible. Make sure every person whose name is on the back-call file gets a copy immediately, then distribute them far and wide throughout your entire territory.

Increasing Public Meeting Activity

As the gospel preaching steadily and surely expands toward the ends of the earth our privileges and opportunities in connection therewith must similarly be enlarged to keep pace with it. We believe that nearly every company has, by this time, had at least one series of public lectures. However, this is only the beginning. These public lectures will continue to be held on a greater scale in the future. All companies, as soon as one series of public lectures is ended, should be prepared to begin another series in a different section of territory. When all the territory has been covered with the series once, then it will be time to begin covering it again.

It appears that some of the publishers still fail to appreciate the great privileges they have of advertising these public lectures. The apostles, who accompanied Jesus in His public meeting campaign throughout Judea, Galilee and Samaria always faithfully supported him in every way possible. They entered public squares and streets and made known the way of gaining life to all who desired to hear. They spread the good news far and wide and gained the attention of all who would listen and receive the invitation. The faithful publisher today will not try to do differently. He will willingly deprive himself of everything else in order to give full, 100-percent support to the presentday public meeting campaigns.

Notice

The United States Post Office has set forth the arrangements that will now govern their acceptance in the mails of newspapers and other periodical publications addressed to the personnel of the Navy, Marine Corps and Coast Guard overseas.

Individual copies, addressed via the fleet post offices at New York, N. Y., or San Francisco, Calif., shall be accepted only when they are being sent in fulfillment of written requests sent or initiated by the addressees for subscriptions or renewals. Relatives and friends may pay for the subscription or renewals provided the addressees have made requests therefor.

This means, therefore, that each subscription or renewal for magazines from this office must be accompanied by a written request by the service man for whom it is intended.

This same ruling applies to Army subscribers also.
