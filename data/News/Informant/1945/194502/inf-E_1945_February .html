Seeking First the Kingdom Help the “Stranger” to Become a Publisher


Memorial, March 28

Today, as never before, Satan is doing all within his power to turn all mankind away from Jehovah God and to stop the work of preaching this gospel of the Kingdom, the evangel that directs all men to their Creator. On the one hand this adversary uses pressure, threats, mob action, and imprisonment, and on the other hand he uses the temptation to lay up earthly treasures and to put one’s confidence in the uncertainty of riches. Some of Jehovah’s servants who have stood firm under all manner of persecution are now weakening as to this temptation, apparently taking the viewpoint that when conditions are less favorable to the accumulation of this world’s goods they will again give the Kingdom interests more attention.

Even if the Kingdom work were not so pressing, were there not so much to do and so few to do it, there still could be no excuse for this attitude. Kingdom publishing has always pressed for fulfillment, and now the most stupendous task ever set before imperfect human creatures is placed upon all who have undertaken to serve Jehovah. Therefore now, more than ever before, it is imperative that they be "seeking first the Kingdom”, our text for the month of March.

During this month all Theocratic servants will seek first the Kingdom by pushing forward in the Watchtower subscription campaign (which should be going in high gear in this, the third month), by working in their individual territory from house to house, offering the year’s subscription for The Watchtower (at the regular subscription rate), with the book “The Kingdom Is at Hand" and the new booklet One World, One Government. As previously mentioned in the Informant, where the householder is unable or unwilling to subscribe for the entire offer, efforts should be made to leave the book and the booklet. However, at every home and every interview leave a copy of the new booklet One World, One Government. This important message should be gotten into the hands of every person in your company territory. Servants and all publishers should make arrangements to this end. All publishers will also continue to take care of their back-calls and conduct their book studies.

They will also put the Kingdom interests first by giving enthusiastic and wholehearted support to the public meeting campaign, advertising it on the streets and from house to house on Saturday with the leaflets, and on Sunday in connection with the literature presentation. They will also attend the public meetings, bringing their good-will interest with them, and will be on the lookout to welcome the “stranger” and make him feel at home.

Peak of Publishers

However, that is not all. They must also follow in the footsteps of their great Exemplar in seeking first the Kingdom in another respect. And what is that? In assisting new ones to become publishers. Yes, He not only conducted a public meeting campaign and taught the people in their homes, but also took beginners with Him, teaching them by precept and example how to preach, and then sent them forth to ‘disciple all nations’. So this month each evangelist will put forth a special effort to help others to get started in the preaching work.

March should see a peak in publishers. It is the ideal time for this, as the Memorial comes during this month. Therefore invite your goodwill interest not only to attend that meeting but also to have a part in the field service. Start them out in the leaflet distribution work, both on the streets and from house to house, and then take them with you when presenting the literature. And when doing so use the time to good advantage by counseling the new publisher as to the best way to meet the various objections and to answer the many questions one encounters while engaged in the preaching work. Such suggestions, together with a real joy in the service being apparent on your part, will go far toward making your new publisher an able and zealous one.

Nor will we limit our assistance to such. Continually new ones are coming into the organization, symbolizing their consecration, and starting out in the field service, and yet it has been two years since we have had a peak in publishers. Why is there no increase? Is it because some are growing weary in well-doing or are becoming weak in faith, and as a result are publishing irregularly or not at all? If so, these too we must help. If we are willing to spend hours and hours in helping those of good-will by conducting book studies in their homes, certainly we should be willing, yes, anxious to spend some time and effort with those who have already taken their stand but for one reason or another have relaxed and failed to appreciate their privilege of service.

Therefore, this month let every regular publisher work for a peak by assisting others to be publishers, not only those just beginning but also those weak and irregular publishers. Thus doing we not only shall be “seeking first the Kingdom” ourselves, but shall also help our brethren to do likewise.

‘Discipling All the Nations’

Glorious Opportunity

for Full-Time Servants

Those present-day faithful servants of the Lord who are seeking to keep Christ’s command to ‘preach the Kingdom gospel’ are thus described in the 1945 Yearbook: “Some call at farmhouses, others at apartments, and still others from house to house. In some lands it means visiting people in their adobe huts and their lean-to’s; but wherever people live, that is where Jehovah’s witnesses are directed to go: ‘Go ye therefore, and make disciples of all the nations.’ ”

The same general picture is conveyed by the striking illustration and text on the Society’s 1945 Calendar. In equatorial heat, or the bitter polar cold, the busy city streets, or the lonely continental plains, in every land, to every people, the message will go until all who live shall bow the knee to Him.

What a glorious prospect 1 What a desire for full-time activity is kindled in contemplation of the service privileges thus presented. Many willing ones in this day of Jehovah's power have put aside the things of the old world and taken up full-time service, and many more would do so except for the fear of being unable to support or maintain themselves as pioneers. There were those in Jesus’ day also that were hindered from following him because possessed of the same doubts, but Jesus declared that if men would seek first the Kingdom interests his Father would provide all things needful. The work of discipling all the nations includes the drawing of men from the things of the old world to God’s New World. How, then, in all consistency, can the Lord’s servants be concerned with such things as the Gentiles seek?

Referring to the position now occupied by Jehovah’s witnesses in this glorious ‘discipling’ work, The Watchtower for December 15, 1944, states: “Blessed is the lor of those who are sent forth and who endure in service to the end.” This statement is especially true of the one who through sheer love for God and a desire to do His will makes the decision to devote all his time and energy to serving the Lord. Tn the ranks of Jehovah’s full-time servants individuals from every condition and stage of life appear. They dwell in all parts of the earth, some in difficult, others in easier circumstances. Some are sickly, others robust; some very young, some very old: some with money, many without much money. They go forward day by day not in their own strength nor looking to the things of this world for sustenance. They would not endure unto the end if they did.

Their strength and support come from the Lord, who declared. ‘If the birds and even the grass of the field are provided for. how much more you, O ye of little faith?’ Yes. the lot of all sent forth as Jehovah's witnesses is blessed indeed, but manyfold more blessed is that one who becomes a full-time pioneer servant. The way is now open for all who will to “disciple all the nations" as full-time servants of the Lord. Enroll and partake of God's favor and blessings to the fullest extent.

Information on Reporting Publishers, Pioneers, and Servants,

Study Carefully

As the man ‘clothed in linen with the writer’s inkhorn by his side’, after finishing his Theocratic assignment, reported the matter, saying, “I have done as thou hast commanded,” so Jehovah’s Theocratic servants today make reports to the Theocratic organization via their local company organization, and the pioneers direct with headquarters, as to the work they have done which the Lord has commanded them to do, to “go ye therefore, and make disciples of all the nations”.

Set forth below is information on the proper reporting of time for various features of Kingdom activity. and what should be reported on your field service report; also information on reporting attendance at book studies. Please study this carefully and conform to same from and after February 1, 1945.

Time. Report time while engaged in house-to-house distribution ol handbills, folders, radio announcements, Kingdom News, magazines, books, booklets, and other Kingdom literature. Also, report time spent advertising the local public meeting with information signs, and passing out handbills; time spent in operating sound equipment, thus heralding the Kingdom message far and wide over the territory; also time spent in witnessing to owners of buildings, lots, etc., when arranging to secure such lots, buildings, parks, pavilions, schoolhouses, bandstands, etc., for use in connection with the public meeting campaign. Of course, time spent in regular features of the work nol mentioned above, such as back-call and book-study activity, will be reported as heretofore.

Publishers assigned to work wiih a particular public meeting group may include in their service report the time spent in attending and supporting the public meeting, provided they have participated in advertising that public meeting to the best of their ability. Also, the speaker delivering the talk may count the time spent doing such, but not the time spent in preparing it. This is witnessing publicly and the time in bearing the witness is thus counted. This rule concerning the publisher’s and speaker’s counting time applies only for the public meetings, and not for regular studies and meetings in the company.

Travel Time. Henceforth, travel time may not exceed one hour a day for each day spent in the service by company publishers and pioneers.

On Being Thorough

The House to House Record form now employed by the publishers is an instrument by which thorough coverage of territory may be accomplished. When a person is too busy to receive the witness or no one is at home, notation is made on tlie form. It has been found that at about 35 percent of the homes a witness is not given the first time a territory is worked; and that is considerable. These homes should be called on again the very next time the publisher is in the territory, else the territory is really only partially worked. Many wonderful opportunities of free education are enjoyed by the publishers who are thorough. One such publisher reports placing as many as seven books in one day while calling upon those who were not at home when he first worked the territory. It is always well to go back to homes where you have not had a chance to give the Kingdom witness. Being thorough in your service will produce the most fruitful results.
