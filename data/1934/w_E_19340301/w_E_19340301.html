FOR centuries theologians have been puzzled over how Jesus eould he born of a woman who was a descendant of the sinner Adam and yet himself be “holy, harmless, undefiled, separate from sinners”, as the sacred Scriptures (Ileb. 7:26) testify. Joseph was espoused to Mary, Jesus’ mother, and before they were married she was found to be with child. The record of Matthew, chapter one, verse eighteen, reads: “Now the birth of Jesus Christ was on this wise: When as his mother Mary was espoused to Joseph, before they came together, she was found with child of the holy [spirit].” Mary was a virgin, yet she was about to give birth and did give birth to the babe Jesus.
